<p><b>Nadya Zhexembayeva</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">author</a>, <a href="/wiki/Educator" class="mw-redirect" title="Educator">educator</a> and <a href="/wiki/Business_owner" class="mw-redirect" title="Business owner">business owner</a>. Nadya has written two and contributed to three books about business, strategy, and sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-About_Nadya_Zhexembayeva_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About_Nadya_Zhexembayeva-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2009, Nadya co-authored the concept of "embedded sustainability",<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in 2015 introduced the term "Chief Reinvention Officer".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadya_Zhexembayeva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Zhexembayeva grew up in Almaty, Kazakhstan (then part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>), at the time she claims the country was "going through immense deterioration".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> While still at high school, Nadya started her business career selling insurance and later worked as a trainer at Association of Young Leaders, then went on to earn a <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Support_Act" title="Freedom Support Act">Freedom Support Act</a> scholarship that allowed her to complete two <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> degrees in Management and then Psychology from <a href="/wiki/Hartwick_College" title="Hartwick College">Hartwick College</a>, where she was named a Faculty Scholar and <a href="/wiki/John_Christopher_Hartwick" title="John Christopher Hartwick">John Christopher Hartwick</a> Scholar - "the highest honor the College can confer upon a student".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2008, Nadya obtained her <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> degree from <a href="/wiki/Case_Western_Reserve_University" title="Case Western Reserve University">Case Western Reserve University</a>, where she served as associate director of the Center for Business as an Agent of World Benefit at <a href="/wiki/Weatherhead_School_of_Management" title="Weatherhead School of Management">Weatherhead School of Management</a>, where as of 2016, she continues to serve on the Board of Advisors.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nadya Zhexembayeva served as the Coca-Cola Chaired Professor of Sustainable Development at <a href="/wiki/IEDC-Bled_School_of_Management" title="IEDC-Bled School of Management">IEDC-Bled School of Management</a>, an executive education center based in the Slovene Alps, where, as of 2016, she continues to teach courses in leadership, strategy, change management, design thinking, and sustainability. In addition to IEDC, Nadya has taught in other business schools, including CEDEP (France)<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/IPADE" title="IPADE">IPADE</a> Business School (Mexico).</p>
<p>As a speaker, Nadya has shared her insights with audiences worldwide through keynotes, panel presentations, and workshops. She has delivered three TEDx talks in Austria,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Slovenia<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the US.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Nadya co-founded WE EXIST Reinvention Agency. Based on Zhexembayeva's work with the Coca-Cola Company, ENRC Plc, IBM, CISCO, Erste Bank, Henkel, Knauf Insulation, Vienna Insurance Group on reinventing products, processes, and leadership practices In Ventures magazine called Nadya ‘The Reinvention Guru’,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> while TEDx Navasink named her ‘The Queen of Reinvention.’<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nadya_Zhexembayeva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nadya Zhexembayeva has written two books and contributed to three others that focus on strategy, innovation, and sustainability.</p>
<p>Author:</p>
<ul>
<li>Overfished Ocean Strategy: Powering Up Innovation for a Resource-Deprived World (2014)</li>
<li>Embedded Sustainability: The Next Big Competitive Advantage (2011)</li>
</ul>
<p>Chapter contributor:</p>
<ul>
<li>Practicing Organization Development: Leading Transformation and Change (2015)</li>
<li>Positive Design and Appreciative Construction: From Sustainable Development to Sustainable Value (2010) (Full citation: Nadya Zhexembayeva (2010), A whole new value: Driving innovation, sustainability, and prosperity through appreciative inquiry, in Tojo Thatchenkery, David L. Cooperrider, Michel Avital (ed.) Positive Design and Appreciative Construction: From Sustainable Development to Sustainable Value (Advances in Appreciative Inquiry, Volume 3) Emerald Group Publishing Limited, pp.&#160;77 – 96)</li>
<li>Handbook of Transformative Cooperation: New Designs and Dynamics (2007)</li>
</ul>
