<td class="mbox-text plainlist">Look up <i><b><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/time_scale" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:time scale">time scale</a></b></i> in Wiktionary, the free dictionary.</td>

<p><b>Time scale</b> may refer to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Time_standard" title="Time standard">Time standard</a>, a specification of either the rate at which time passes, points in time, or both</li>
<li>A duration or quantity of time:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude_(time)" title="Orders of magnitude (time)">Orders of magnitude (time)</a> as a power of 10 in seconds;</li>
<li>A specific <a href="/wiki/Time#List_of_units" title="Time">unit of time</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Human_time_scale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Human time scale (page does not exist)">human time scale</a>, timescales comparable to human lifetimes</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Geological_time_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Geological time scale">Geological time scale</a>, a scale that divides up the history of Earth into scientifically meaningful periods</li>
</ul>
<p>In <b>astronomy and physics</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dynamical_time_scale" title="Dynamical time scale">Dynamical time scale</a>, in stellar physics, the time in which changes in one part of a body can be communicated to the rest of that body, or in celestial mechanics, a realization of a time-like argument based on a dynamical theory</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nuclear_timescale" title="Nuclear timescale">Nuclear timescale</a>, an estimate of the lifetime of a star based solely on its rate of fuel consumption</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thermal_time_scale" title="Thermal time scale">Thermal time scale</a>, an estimate of the lifetime of a star once the fuel reserves at its center are used up</li>
</ul>
<p>In <b>cosmology and particle physics</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Planck_time" title="Planck time">Planck time</a>, the time scale beneath which quantum effects are comparable in significance to gravitational effects</li>
</ul>
<p>In <b>mathematics</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Time-scale_calculus" title="Time-scale calculus">Time-scale calculus</a>, the unification of the theory of difference equations with differential equations</li>
</ul>
<p>In <b>music</b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhythm" title="Rhythm">Rhythm</a>, a temporal pattern of events</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Time_scale_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Time scale (music)">Time scale (music)</a>, which divides music into sections of time</li>
</ul>
