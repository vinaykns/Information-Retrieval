<p><b>Pasture</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>pastus</i>, <a href="/wiki/Past_participle" class="mw-redirect" title="Past participle">past participle</a> of <i>pascere</i>, "to feed") is land used for <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pasture lands in the narrow sense are enclosed tracts of <a href="/wiki/Farmland" class="mw-redirect" title="Farmland">farmland</a>, grazed by domesticated <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> or <a href="/wiki/Swine" class="mw-redirect" title="Swine">swine</a>. The vegetation of tended pasture, <a href="/wiki/Forage" title="Forage">forage</a>, consists mainly of <a href="/wiki/Grasses" class="mw-redirect" title="Grasses">grasses</a>, with an interspersion of <a href="/wiki/Legume" title="Legume">legumes</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Forb" title="Forb">forbs</a> (non-grass herbaceous plants). Pasture is typically grazed throughout the summer, in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Meadow" title="Meadow">meadow</a> which is ungrazed or used for grazing only after being <a href="/wiki/Mowing" class="mw-redirect" title="Mowing">mown</a> to make <a href="/wiki/Hay" title="Hay">hay</a> for animal <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">fodder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Pasture in a wider sense additionally includes <a href="/wiki/Rangeland" title="Rangeland">rangelands</a>, other unenclosed <a href="/wiki/Pastoralism" title="Pastoralism">pastoral systems</a>, and land types used by wild animals for <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Browsing_(predation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Browsing (predation)">browsing</a>.</p>
<p>Pasture lands in the narrow sense are distinguished from rangelands by being managed through more intensive agricultural practices of <a href="/wiki/Sowing" title="Sowing">seeding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, and the use of <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizers</a>, while rangelands grow primarily native vegetation, managed with extensive practices like <a href="/wiki/Controlled_burn" title="Controlled burn">controlled burning</a> and regulated intensity of grazing.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">Soil</a> type, minimum annual <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> are important factors in pasture management. Sheepwalk is an area of <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> where <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a> can roam freely. The productivity of sheepwalk is measured by the number of sheep per area. This is dependent, among other things, on the underlying rock.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Sheepwalk is also the name of townlands in <a href="/wiki/County_Roscommon" title="County Roscommon">County Roscommon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Fermanagh" title="County Fermanagh">County Fermanagh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.</p>
<p>Unless <a href="/wiki/Factory_farming" class="mw-redirect" title="Factory farming">factory farming</a>, which entails in its most intensive form entirely trough-feeding, managed or unmanaged pasture is the main food source for <a href="/wiki/Ruminants" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruminants">ruminants</a>. Pasture feeding dominates livestock farming where the land makes crop sowing and/or harvesting difficult, such as in arid or mountainous regions, where types of <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antelope" title="Antelope">antelope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yak" class="mw-redirect" title="Yak">yak</a> and other ruminants live which are well suited to the more hostile terrain and very rarely factory farmed. In more humid regions, pasture grazing is managed across a large global area for <a href="/wiki/Free_range" title="Free range">free range</a> and <a href="/wiki/Organic_farming" title="Organic farming">organic farming</a>. Certain types of pasture suit the diet, <a href="/wiki/Natural_selection" title="Natural selection">evolution</a> and metabolism of particular animals, and their fertilising and tending of the land may over generations result in the pasture combined with the ruminants in question being integral to a particular <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
