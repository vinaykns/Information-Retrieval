			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Greenhouse gas removal</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_gas_remediation&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse gas remediation">Greenhouse gas remediation</a>)</span></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Greenhouse gas removal</b> projects are a type of <a href="/wiki/Climate_engineering" title="Climate engineering">climate engineering</a> that seek to remove <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse gases">greenhouse gases</a> from the atmosphere, and thus they tackle the root cause of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>. These techniques either directly remove greenhouse gases, or alternatively seek to influence natural processes to remove greenhouse gases indirectly. The discipline overlaps with <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">carbon sequestration</a>, and some projects listed may not be considered to be geoengineering by all commentators, instead being described as <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Mitigation of global warming">mitigation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_sequestration">Carbon sequestration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_gas_removal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Carbon sequestration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage">Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_negative_fuel" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon negative fuel">Carbon negative fuel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">Carbon sequestration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_removal" title="Carbon dioxide removal">Carbon dioxide removal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_air_capture" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon dioxide air capture">Carbon dioxide air capture</a></div>
<p>A wide range of techniques for <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">carbon sequestration</a> exist. These range from ideas to remove <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a> from the atmosphere (<a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_air_capture" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon dioxide air capture">carbon dioxide air capture</a>), flue gases (<a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a>) and by preventing carbon in <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a> from re-entering the atmosphere, such as with <a href="/wiki/Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage">Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage</a> (BECCS).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chlorofluorocarbon_photochemistry">Chlorofluorocarbon photochemistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_gas_removal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chlorofluorocarbon photochemistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Chlorofluorocarbon" title="Chlorofluorocarbon">chlorofluorocarbon</a> (CFC) removal is an idea which suggests using <a href="/wiki/Lasers" class="mw-redirect" title="Lasers">lasers</a> to break up CFCs, an important family of greenhouse gases, in the <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methane_removal">Methane removal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_gas_removal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Methane removal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">Methane</a> potentially poses major challenges for remediation. It is around 20 times as powerful a greenhouse gas as CO<sub>2</sub>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Large quantities may be outgassed from <a href="/wiki/Permafrost" title="Permafrost">permafrost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clathrates" class="mw-redirect" title="Clathrates">clathrates</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as a result of global warming, notably in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are existing climate engineering proposals.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Methane is removed by several <a href="/wiki/Methane#Removal_processes" title="Methane">natural processes</a>, which can be enhanced.</p>
<ul>
<li>Chemical decomposition — reaction with <a href="/wiki/Hydroxyl_radicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydroxyl radicals">hydroxyl radicals</a> produced from <a href="/wiki/Photochemical" class="mw-redirect" title="Photochemical">photochemical</a> decomposition of <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">ozone</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere" title="Stratosphere">stratosphere</a>.</li>
<li>Biological decomposition — by <a href="/wiki/Methanotrophs" class="mw-redirect" title="Methanotrophs">methanotrophs</a> in soils and water.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
