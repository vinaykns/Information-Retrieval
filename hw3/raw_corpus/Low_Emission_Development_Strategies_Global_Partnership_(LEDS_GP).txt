			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Low Emission Development Strategies Global Partnership (LEDS GP)</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p>The <b>Low Emissions Development Strategies Global Partnership</b> (LEDS GP) aims to advance climate-resilient low emission development and support transitions to a <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_economy" title="Low-carbon economy">low-carbon economy</a> through coordination, information exchange and cooperation among countries and programs working to advance low-emission <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>. The partnership was launched in 2011 and brings together more than 160 governmental and international institutions. The implementation, knowledge management, and outreach of LEDS GP is coordinated by a co-secretariat of the <a href="/wiki/Climate_%26_Development_Knowledge_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate &amp; Development Knowledge Network">Climate &amp; Development Knowledge Network</a> (CDKN) and the <a href="/wiki/National_Renewable_Energy_Laboratory" title="National Renewable Energy Laboratory">National Renewable Energy Laboratory</a> (NREL).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>LEDS GP delivers support through its three regional platforms, the Africa LEDS Partnership (AfLP), the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://ledslac.org/">Latin America and the Caribbean Partnership</a> (LEDS LAC) and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://asialeds.org/about-us">Asia Partnership</a> (ALP).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It aims to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Strengthen support for low-emission climate-resilient development in all regions,</li>
<li>Mobilize capacity and advance peer-to-peer learning and collaboration on low emission climate-resilient development across countries, international institutions and practitioners, and</li>
<li>Improve and support coordination of low-emission climate-resilient development LEDS activities at the country, regional and global level.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_Groups">Working Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Low_Emission_Development_Strategies_Global_Partnership_(LEDS_GP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Working Groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LEDS GP delivers support through six technical working groups on the following topics.</p>
<ul>
<li>Agriculture, Forestry and Land Use (AFOLU)</li>
<li>Benefits Assessment of LEDS</li>
<li>Energy</li>
<li>Finance</li>
<li>Subnational Integration of LEDS</li>
<li>Transport<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_Assistance">Technical Assistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Low_Emission_Development_Strategies_Global_Partnership_(LEDS_GP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Technical Assistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Through its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://ledsgp.org/assistance/?loclang=en_gb">Remote Expert Assistance on LEDS</a> (REAL) service, the LEDS GP provides virtual technical assistance to developing country government agencies or initiatives—as well as their consultants, technical institutions, and <a href="/wiki/Non-government_organizations" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-government organizations">non-government organizations</a>—working on LEDS plans and implementation.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellowship_Program">Fellowship Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Low_Emission_Development_Strategies_Global_Partnership_(LEDS_GP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fellowship Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2016, LEDS GP announced the launch of its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://ledsgp.org/2016/05/apply-2016-fellowship-program/?loclang=en_gb">Fellowship Program</a>, which aims to embed individuals or teams of practitioners in leading institutions to learn practical lessons that they can apply to low emission development strategies (LEDS) and/or <a href="/wiki/Intended_Nationally_Determined_Contributions" title="Intended Nationally Determined Contributions">Intended Nationally Determined Contributions</a> in their home countries.</p>
