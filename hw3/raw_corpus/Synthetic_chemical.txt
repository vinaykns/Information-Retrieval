<p><b>Chemical synthesis</b> is the purposeful execution of one or more <a href="/wiki/Named_reactions" class="mw-redirect" title="Named reactions">named reactions</a> to obtain a <a href="/wiki/Product_(chemistry)" title="Product (chemistry)">product</a>, or several products. <sup id="cite_ref-vogel_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogel-1">[1]</a></sup> In modern <a href="/wiki/Laboratory" title="Laboratory">laboratory</a> usage, this tends to imply that the process is reproducible, reliable, and established to work in multiple laboratories.</p>

<p>A chemical synthesis—<i>synthesis,</i> in its present meaning, begins with the selection of chemical product target, which often possesses academic, industrial or therapeutic interest to the broader aims of the research effort. Secondary practical concerns also come into play, including researcher availability, availability of material resources (necessary equipment, chemical building blocks), and research budget.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A research grant proposal is often submitted to a funding agency (such as <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> or <a href="/wiki/National_Institutes_of_Health" title="National Institutes of Health">National Institutes of Health</a>), which describes the background/proposed synthesis and research plans, and secondary concerns. For instance, a prior developed reaction methodology may highlight particular man-made or natural compounds that would serve the purposes of the effort, in highlighting the breadth of the new methodology and possibly providing facile access to complex natural products.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Once the target or targets are established, the next critical phase begins, that of <i>synthetic design</i>, typically in modern efforts, in the area of <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a>, using <a href="/wiki/Retrosynthetic_analysis" title="Retrosynthetic analysis">retrosynthetic analysis</a>, as championed by <a href="/wiki/E.J._Corey" class="mw-redirect" title="E.J. Corey">E.J. Corey</a> and others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>An eventual step is selection of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">compounds</a> that are known as <a href="/wiki/Reagent" title="Reagent">reagents</a> or reactants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_reactions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of reactions">Various reaction types</a> can be applied to these to synthesize the product, or an intermediate product.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This requires mixing the compounds in a reaction vessel such as a <a href="/wiki/Chemical_reactor" title="Chemical reactor">chemical reactor</a> or a simple <a href="/wiki/Round-bottom_flask" title="Round-bottom flask">round-bottom flask</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Many reactions require some form of <a href="/wiki/Work-up_(chemistry)" title="Work-up (chemistry)">work-up</a> procedure before the final product is isolated.<sup id="cite_ref-vogel_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vogel-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (March 2016)">page&#160;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The isolation (purification) of the product then proceeds via a variety of methods.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The amount of product in a chemical synthesis is the <a href="/wiki/Yield_(chemistry)" title="Yield (chemistry)">reaction yield</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (March 2016)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Chemical_synthesis#Dubious" title="Talk:Chemical synthesis">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> Typically, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_yield" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical yield">chemical yields</a> are expressed as a weight in <a href="/wiki/Gram" title="Gram">grams</a> (in a laboratory setting)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (March 2016)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Chemical_synthesis#Dubious" title="Talk:Chemical synthesis">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup> or as a percentage of the total theoretical quantity of product that could be produced. A side reaction is an unwanted chemical reaction taking place that diminishes the yield of the desired product.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Many strategies exist in chemical synthesis that go beyond converting <a href="/wiki/Reactant" class="mw-redirect" title="Reactant">reactant</a> A to reaction product B in a single step. In <b>multistep synthesis</b>, a chemical compound is synthesised though a series of individual chemical reactions, each with their own work-up. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#What_information_to_include" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="A more detailed citation is required. (March 2016)">full citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For example, a laboratory synthesis of <a href="/wiki/Paracetamol#Synthesis" title="Paracetamol">paracetamol</a> can consist of three individual synthetic steps. In <a href="/wiki/Cascade_reaction" title="Cascade reaction">cascade reactions</a> multiple chemical transformations take place within a single reactant, in <a href="/wiki/Multi-component_reaction" title="Multi-component reaction">multi-component reactions</a> up to 11 different reactants form a single reaction product and in a <a href="/wiki/Telescopic_synthesis" class="mw-redirect" title="Telescopic synthesis">telescopic synthesis</a> one reactant goes through multiple transformations without isolation of intermediates.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">Organic synthesis</a> is a special branch of chemical synthesis dealing with the synthesis of <a href="/wiki/Organic_compound" title="Organic compound">organic compounds</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Total_synthesis" title="Total synthesis">total synthesis</a> of a complex product it may take multiple steps to synthesize the product of interest, and inordinate amounts of time. Skill in organic synthesis is prized among chemists and the synthesis of exceptionally valuable or difficult compounds has won chemists such as <a href="/wiki/Robert_Burns_Woodward" title="Robert Burns Woodward">Robert Burns Woodward</a> the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_for_Chemistry" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobel Prize for Chemistry">Nobel Prize for Chemistry</a>. If a chemical synthesis starts from basic laboratory compounds and yields something new, it is a purely synthetic process. If it starts from a product isolated from plants or animals and then proceeds to new compounds, the synthesis is described as a <a href="/wiki/Semisynthetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Semisynthetic">semisynthetic</a> process.</p>
