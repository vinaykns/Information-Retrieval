			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">World Health Assembly</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>The <b>World Health Assembly</b> (<b>WHA</b>) is the forum through which the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (WHO) is governed by its 194 <a href="/wiki/Member_state" title="Member state">member states</a>. It is the world's highest <a href="/wiki/Health_policy" title="Health policy">health policy</a> setting body and is composed of <a href="/wiki/Health_minister" title="Health minister">health ministers</a> from member states.</p>
<p>The members of the World Health Assembly generally meet every year in May in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, the location of WHO Headquarters. The main tasks of the WHA are to decide major policy questions, as well as to approve the WHO work programme and budget and elect its Director General.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members_and_observers">Members and observers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Members and observers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original membership of the WHA, at the first assembly held in 1948, numbered 55 member states.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The WHA has, currently, 194 member states.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition, seven agencies have <a href="/wiki/Observer_status" title="Observer status">observer status</a> at the WHA - the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Vatican</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian Authority">Palestinian Authority</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Malta" class="mw-redirect" title="Order of Malta">Order of Malta</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">International Committee of the Red Cross</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Societies" title="International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies">International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Parliamentary_Union" title="Inter-Parliamentary Union">Inter-Parliamentary Union</a>, and The <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Health_(Republic_of_China)" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Health (Republic of China)">Department of Health</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a>, commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, was invited on 28 April 2009 to participate in the WHA 2009 as an observer for the first time since losing its China seat in <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> in 1971. The invitation was extended to "the Department of Health, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resolutions">Resolutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Resolutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main international policy frameworks adopted through WHA <a href="/wiki/Resolution_(law)" title="Resolution (law)">resolutions</a> include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Health_Regulations" title="International Health Regulations">International Health Regulations</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Code_of_Marketing_of_Breast-milk_Substitutes" title="International Code of Marketing of Breast-milk Substitutes">International Code of Marketing of Breast-milk Substitutes</a>, adopted in 1981</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Framework_Convention_on_Tobacco_Control" class="mw-redirect" title="Framework Convention on Tobacco Control">Framework Convention on Tobacco Control</a>, adopted in 2003</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Health_Human_Resources#Global_Code_of_Practice_on_the_International_Recruitment_of_Health_Personnel" class="mw-redirect" title="Health Human Resources">Global Code of Practice on the International Recruitment of Health Personnel</a>, adopted in 2010</li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, the WHA has endorsed through resolutions a number of WHO action plans dealing with different areas to improve <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> around the world, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Worldwide eradication of <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a>, first endorsed in 1959, and then declared to have been won in 1980<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Worldwide eradication of <a href="/wiki/Poliomyelitis" title="Poliomyelitis">polio</a>, first endorsed in 1988,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> recently re-affirmed in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-WHA2011_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHA2011-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Control of human <a href="/wiki/Hookworm" class="mw-redirect" title="Hookworm">hookworm</a> infection through regular deworming of at-risk school children, endorsed in 2001</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/WHO_global_action_plan" title="WHO global action plan"><i>WHO global action plan for workers' health</i></a>, endorsed in 2007</li>
<li>Control of <a href="/wiki/Passive_drinking" title="Passive drinking">harmful use of alcohol</a>, endorsed in 2010</li>
<li>Enhanced global actions for the prevention and control of <a href="/wiki/Non-communicable_disease" title="Non-communicable disease">non-communicable diseases</a>, endorsed in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-WHA2011_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHA2011-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The WHA is also responsible for the endorsement of the <a href="/wiki/WHO_Family_of_International_Classifications" class="mw-redirect" title="WHO Family of International Classifications">WHO Family of International Classifications</a>, a series of internationally standardized medical classifications, including the <a href="/wiki/International_Classification_of_Diseases" class="mw-redirect" title="International Classification of Diseases">International Classification of Diseases</a> (ICD) and the <a href="/wiki/International_Classification_of_Functioning,_Disability_and_Health" title="International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health">International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health</a> (ICF).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_Assemblies">Annual Assemblies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Annual Assemblies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016:_Sixty-ninth_WHA">2016: Sixty-ninth WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016: Sixty-ninth WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 69th World Health Assembly took place 23-28 May 2016.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Sixty-eighth_WHA">2015: Sixty-eighth WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015: Sixty-eighth WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 68th session of the World Health Assembly (WHA) took place in Geneva 18–26 May 2015. The Health Assembly is the supreme decision-making body of WHO. It is attended by delegations from all WHO Member States. Its main functions are to determine the policies of the Organization, supervise financial policies, and review and approve the proposed programme budget. <a href="/wiki/Jagat_Prakash_Nadda" title="Jagat Prakash Nadda">Jagat Prakash Nadda</a> assumed new presidency of WHA. India assumed the presidency after a gap of 19 years. During the assembly the WHA agreed to the Global Malaria Strategy and Programme Budget for 2016-2017, polio, International Health Regulations, strengthening surgical care, WHO's reform of its emergency and response programme, antimicrobial resistance, immunization gaps, malnutrition, air pollution and epilepsy. Annual health awards were given by the Director-General of WHO and the President of WHA.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_Sixty-seventh_WHA">2014: Sixty-seventh WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014: Sixty-seventh WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 67th WHA took place in Geneva on 19–24 May 2014. Among the more than 20 resolutions adopted by the Assembly included ones concerning strengthening of national drug management systems to address <a href="/wiki/Antimicrobial_resistance" title="Antimicrobial resistance">antimicrobial resistance</a>; implementation of the <a href="/wiki/Minamata_Convention" class="mw-redirect" title="Minamata Convention">Minamata Convention</a> to protect human health and the environment from effects of exposure to mercury and mercury compounds; and improving access to <a href="/wiki/Essential_medicines" title="Essential medicines">essential medicines</a> worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Also endorsed was a global monitoring framework for maternal, infant and child <a href="/wiki/Human_nutrition" title="Human nutrition">nutrition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the 67th WHA, the WHO's Director-General Dr <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Chan" title="Margaret Chan">Margaret Chan</a> was criticized by the Association of Correspondents Accredited to the United Nations (ACANU) for not having spoken directly to the media during the course of the Assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Sixty-sixth_WHA">2013: Sixty-sixth WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013: Sixty-sixth WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In her address to the 66th WHA in May 2013, WHO Director-General <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Chan" title="Margaret Chan">Margaret Chan</a> traced a brief history of revisions to the <a href="/wiki/International_Health_Regulations" title="International Health Regulations">International Health Regulations</a> following the <a href="/wiki/SARS" class="mw-redirect" title="SARS">SARS</a> outbreak in 2002-3, the "first severe new disease of the 21st century." She observed that the two new diseases WHO is dealing with in 2013 are the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East_respiratory_syndrome_coronavirus" title="Middle East respiratory syndrome coronavirus">novel coronavirus</a> (MERS), from the same family as SARS, detected in 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, and the first-ever human infections with the H7N9 avian influenza virus reported in China in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-WHA2013_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHA2013-14">[14]</a></sup> She attributed the positive report by the <i>World Health Statistics</i> (May 2013) on dramatic improvement in health in the world's poorest countries from 1993-2013, to the emphasis placed on poverty alleviation by the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WHA2013_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHA2013-14">[14]</a></sup> She announced the emergence of global action plans for noncommunicable diseases, mental health, and the prevention of avoidable blindness and visual impairment calling for a life-course approach which includes "equity through universal health coverage," preventive strategies and "integrated service delivery."<sup id="cite_ref-WHA2013_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WHA2013-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dr Margaret Chan declared at the Assembly that <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_Property" class="mw-redirect" title="Intellectual Property">Intellectual Property</a>, or patents on strains of new virus, should not impede nations from protecting their citizens by limiting scientific investigations. Following the 2012 MERS outbreak in Saudi Arabia, Deputy Health Minister Ziad Memish raised concerns that scientists who applied for a patent would not allow the MERS-coronavirus to be used for investigations by other scientists and were therefore delaying the development of diagnostic tests. Ten of the 22 people who died and 22 of 44 cases reported were in Saudi Arabia.<sup id="cite_ref-coronavirus_patent23may2013_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coronavirus_patent23may2013-15">[15]</a></sup> Saudi Arabia–based microbiologist Ali Mohamed Zaki reported the first known case, a 60-year-old Saudi man who got sick in June, 2012 on ProMed-mail, a public health on-line forum<sup id="cite_ref-sciencenews27feb2013_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sciencenews27feb2013-16">[16]</a></sup> then published more details including the virus’s genetic makeup and closest relatives.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencenews27feb2013_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sciencenews27feb2013-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zaki8nov2012_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zaki8nov2012-17">[17]</a></sup> The Erasmus Medical Center "tested, sequenced and identified" a sample provided by Ali Mohamed Zaki.<sup id="cite_ref-sampledisputeCTV23May2013_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sampledisputeCTV23May2013-18">[18]</a></sup> Erasmus MC and Dr. Zaki strongly refuted all allegations concerning a presumed lack of willingness to cooperate in research into the new MERS coronavirus, making diagnostic tests and virus specimens freely available to all research institutions around the globe.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012:_Sixty-fifth_WHA">2012: Sixty-fifth WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2012: Sixty-fifth WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Among other actions, the 65th Assembly endorsed the Rio Political Declaration to address the <a href="/wiki/Social_determinants_of_health" title="Social determinants of health">social determinants of health</a>, intended to spearhead support for all countries to adopt inclusive ‘<i><a href="/wiki/Health_For_All" title="Health For All">Health For All</a></i>’ approaches to health promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It also endorsed the first <a href="/wiki/World_Immunization_Week" title="World Immunization Week">World Immunization Week</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009:_Sixty-second_WHA">2009: Sixty-second WHA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Health_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2009: Sixty-second WHA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In her role as global patron of The <a href="/wiki/White_Ribbon_Alliance_for_Safe_Motherhood" title="White Ribbon Alliance for Safe Motherhood">White Ribbon Alliance for Safe Motherhood</a>, and chair of the Maternal Mortality Campaign, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jane_Brown" title="Sarah Jane Brown">Sarah Brown</a> gave the keynote speech at the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="World Health Organisation">World Health Organisation</a>'s 62nd WHA, alongside <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General_selection,_2006" title="United Nations Secretary-General selection, 2006">United Nations Secretary-General</a> <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> asking "Where is the M in MCH?’ [maternal and child health]" in an echo of <a href="/wiki/Allan_Rosenfield" title="Allan Rosenfield">Allan Rosenfield</a>'s landmark <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lancet" title="The Lancet">Lancet</a></i> article of 1985 - and highlighting that the numbers of women dying in pregnancy and childbirth were still the same 14 years later.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
