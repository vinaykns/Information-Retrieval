<p>The <b>Decade of Education for Sustainable Development (DESD) 2005-2014</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Education_for_Sustainable_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="Education for Sustainable Development">Education for Sustainable Development</a> (ESD) initiative of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. The Decade was delivered by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> as lead agency, and gave rise to <a href="/wiki/Regional_Centres_of_Expertise_(RCE)" title="Regional Centres of Expertise (RCE)">Regional Centres of Expertise (RCE)</a> networks, and the <a href="/wiki/Global_Universities_Partnership_on_Environment_and_Sustainability" title="Global Universities Partnership on Environment and Sustainability">GUPES</a> universities' partnership.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="About_the_Decade">About the Decade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Nations_Decade_of_Education_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: About the Decade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Based on proposals by Japan and Sweden, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a>, at its 57th Session in December 2002, adopted Resolution 57/254 to start the DESD,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> following the Johannesburg Plan of Implementation which emphasised that education is an indispensable element for achieving sustainable development.</p>
<p>UNESCO was designated as lead agency for the Decade and developed a draft International Implementation Scheme] for the DESD.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along with the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a> (MDGs) process, the <a href="/wiki/Education_For_All" title="Education For All">Education For All</a> (EFA) movement, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Literacy_Decade" title="United Nations Literacy Decade">United Nations Literacy Decade</a> (UNLD), the DESD also aimed to achieve an improvement in the <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a>, particularly for the most deprived and marginalised, fulfillment of <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> including <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">gender equality</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty reduction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Active_citizenship" title="Active citizenship">active citizenship</a>. Whereas the MDGs provide a set of tangible and measurable development goals within which education is a significant input and indicator; EFA focuses on ways of providing educational opportunities to everyone; and the UNLD concentrates on promoting the key learning tool for all forms of structured learning, DESD was concerned particularly with the content and purpose of education. In concept and design, ESD challenges all forms of educational provision to adopt practices and approaches which foster the values of sustainable development.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to the DESD, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_University" title="United Nations University">United Nations University</a> (UNU) called for the development of networks for the promotion of ESD, as well as being expertise centres for the research development of ESD. This was the birth of <a href="/wiki/Regional_Centres_of_Expertise_(RCE)" title="Regional Centres of Expertise (RCE)">Regional Centres of Expertise (RCE)</a> supporting education for sustainable development. Dr. <a href="/wiki/Betsy_Boze" title="Betsy Boze">Betsy Boze</a> was instrumental in drafting and approval of the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Universities" title="International Association of Universities">International Association of Universities</a> "Iquitos statement on Higher <a href="/wiki/Education_for_Sustainable_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="Education for Sustainable Development">Education for Sustainable Development</a>."</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">United Nations Environment Programme</a> (UNEP) has facilitated the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Universities_Partnership_on_Environment_and_Sustainability" title="Global Universities Partnership on Environment and Sustainability">Global Universities Partnership on Environment and Sustainability</a> (GUPES) in 2012 as a flagship contribution to the UN DESD. The focus of GUPES is around three pillars: Education, Training and Network,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and is based on the experience and scaling up of the successful Mainstreaming Environment and Sustainability in African Universities (MESA) project.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
