			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Green Seal</h1>

<p><b>Green Seal</b> is a non-profit <a href="/wiki/Environmental_standard" title="Environmental standard">environmental standard</a> development and certification organization. Green Seal's flagship program is the certification of products, services, restaurants, and hotels. The certification is based on Green Seal standards, which contain performance, health, and sustainability criteria.<sup id="cite_ref-nrdc_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrdc-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Green Seal is an ecolabel used by product manufacturers and services providers.<sup id="cite_ref-ecolabelindex_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ecolabelindex-2">[2]</a></sup> The Green Seal Certification Mark is registered with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Patent_and_Trademark_Office" title="United States Patent and Trademark Office">United States Patent and Trademark Office</a>.</p>
<p>Green Seal is a U.S. member and co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Global_Ecolabelling_Network" title="Global Ecolabelling Network">Global Ecolabelling Network</a> (GEN), which consists of 28 international ecolabeling programs, including Germany's <a href="/wiki/Blue_Angel_(certification)" title="Blue Angel (certification)">Blue Angel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/EU_Ecolabel" title="EU Ecolabel">EU Ecolabel</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_swan" title="Nordic swan">Nordic swan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Green Seal meets the Criteria for Third-Party Certifiers of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the requirements for standard development organizations of the American National Standards Institute,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the principles for environmental labels of the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO 14020 and 14024).<sup id="cite_ref-ecolabelindex_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ecolabelindex-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Seal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since its founding in 1989, by Rena Shulsky David, Green Seal has developed environmental standards for hundreds of categories of products and services.<sup id="cite_ref-nrdc_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrdc-1">[1]</a></sup> Green Seal published a series of buying guides for purchasers in the 1990s (the Choose Green Reports) and at that time began providing technical assistance to Federal, State, and local governments and other institutions' environmental purchasing, operations, and facilities management.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the early 2000s, the certification program focused primarily on building maintenance. In 2006, the US Green Building Council LEED rating system included several Green Seal standards in their criteria.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Practice GreenHealth,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> the AASHE STARS program,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and the Green Ribbon Schools Program of the U.S. Department of Education,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> among others, reference Green Seal standards.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> According to a 2010 study by the Responsible Purchasing Network, the Green Seal was recognized by 95% of purchasers and used by 76%.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Standards_and_certification">Standards and certification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Seal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Standards and certification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Products and services must meet the requirements in Green Seal standards in order to achieve certification. The standards are based on a life cycle approach, considering such impacts as those from raw materials extraction, manufacturing, use, and re-use or disposal.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The evaluation process includes review of data; assessment of labeling, marketing, and promotional materials; and on-site auditing.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Products or services become certified by Green Seal after these evaluations are completed. Regular compliance monitoring is required to maintain certification.</p>
<p>Green Seal has standards and provides certification for products and services in the following categories:<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Building &amp; Construction Products</li>
<li>Cleaning Products &amp; Services</li>
<li>Company Certification</li>
<li>Hotels &amp; Lodging</li>
<li>Household Products</li>
<li>Institutional Products</li>
<li>Paint &amp; Coating Products</li>
<li>Paper Products</li>
<li>Personal Care Products</li>
<li>Restaurant &amp; Food Services</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Institutional_greening_programs">Institutional greening programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Seal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Institutional greening programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Green Seal works and worked with government agencies, healthcare facilities, universities, and other institutions and companies on sustainable purchasing, operations, and facilities management.</p>
<ul>
<li>Green business projects have included the City of Los Angeles Green Business Program and Green Lodging Program,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> the City of Chicago Green Hotels Initiative,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and the Pennsylvania Green Hotels Program.</li>
<li>Green purchasing projects have included assisting in green procurement for the State of California Procurement Division;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> City of Los Angeles Green Purchasing Action Plan;<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Environmentally and Socially Responsible Procurement Program;<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Los Angeles County Green Procurement Manual;<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> National Park Service Green Purchasing Manual;<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> State of Colorado Green Purchasing Evaluation and Training;<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Multi-State Department of Transportation Environmentally Preferable Purchasing Guide;<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and the World Bank Purchasing Program.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li>Green building operations and maintenance projects have included specifications for the City of Philadelphia;<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> an operations and maintenance manual for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania;<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> environmental evaluations for the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) and the World Bank headquarters facilities; a green facilities plan for the Susquehanna Association for the Blind and Visually Impaired (SABVI);<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> an environmental facilities assessment of the three University of Miami campuses;<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and an operations and maintenance manual for Public Housing Authorities developed in conjunction with Siemens Industries.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Seal_publications">Green Seal publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Seal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Green Seal publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<ul>

<li>“NACo Green Purchasing Toolkit”<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
