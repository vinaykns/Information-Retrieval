<p>The <b>American Public Health Association</b> (<b>APHA</b>) is a Washington, D.C.-based professional organization for public health professionals in the United States. Founded in 1872 by a group of physicians, including Dr. <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Smith_(surgeon)" title="Stephen Smith (surgeon)">Stephen Smith</a>, Dr. Henry Hartshorne, APHA has more than 25,000 members worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Associations defines itself as: "APHA champions the health of all people and all communities. We Strengthen the public health profession. We speak out for public health issues and policies backed by science. We are the only organization that influences federal policy, has a 140-plus year perspective and brings together members from all fields of public health." It defines its mission as: "Improve the health of the public and achieve equity in health status."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_APHA_Awards">National APHA Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National APHA Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The accomplishments of public health leaders are recognized through an awards program. APHA presents its national awards during its annual meeting.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Section_Awards">Section Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Section Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Public Health Education and Health Promotion section recognizes individuals in six award categories. The awards include the Distinguished Career Award, Early Career Award, Mayhew Derryberry Award for contributions of behavioral scientists to health education, Mohan Sing Award for humor in health education, Sarah Mazelis Award for health education practitioners, and Rogers Award for public health communication.</p>
<p>The Statistics section offers the Mortimer Spiegelman Award to a statistician under the age of 40 for contribution to public health statistics.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsored_Awards">Sponsored Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sponsored Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rema_Lapouse_Award" title="Rema Lapouse Award">Rema Lapouse Award</a> – sponsored by the Mental Health, Epidemiology, and Statistics Sections, this award is granted to an outstanding scientist in the area of psychiatric <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">epidemiology</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The American Public Health Association publishes periodicals and more than 70 public health books. Several of these are the reference source for their specialty within public health practice. Some publication titles include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Control_of_Communicable_Diseases_Manual" title="Control of Communicable Diseases Manual">Control of Communicable Diseases Manual</a></li>
<li>Standard Methods for the Examination of Water and Wastewater</li>
<li>Landesman's Public Health Management of Disasters: The Practice Guide.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Journal_of_Public_Health" title="American Journal of Public Health">American Journal of Public Health</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Nation%27s_Health" title="The Nation's Health">The Nation's Health</a></li>
<li>Public Health Newswire</li>
</ul>
<p>Additionally, they are an active partner in the release of <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Health_Rankings" title="America's Health Rankings">America's Health Rankings</a>; working with United Health Foundation and Partnership for Prevention.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Annual_Meeting">Annual Meeting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Annual Meeting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The APHA Annual Meeting and Exposition is the largest meeting of public health professionals in the world. The meeting draws more than 13,000 attendees, offers 700 booths of exhibits and features more than 1,000 scientific sessions. Presentations cover new research and trends in public health science and practice.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Public_Health_Week">National Public Health Week</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Public_Health_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: National Public Health Week">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National Public Health Week is an observance organized annually by APHA during the first full week of April. The week’s activities are designed to highlight issues that are important to improving the public’s health.</p>
