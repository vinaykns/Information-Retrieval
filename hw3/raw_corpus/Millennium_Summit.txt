			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Millennium Summit</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>The Millennium Summit</b> was a meeting among many world leaders lasting three days from 6 September<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to 8 September 2000<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_headquarters" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations headquarters">United Nations headquarters</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Its purpose was to discuss the role of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> at the turn of the 21st century.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCagenda_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCagenda-3">[3]</a></sup> At this meeting, world leaders ratified the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Millennium_Declaration" title="United Nations Millennium Declaration">United Nations Millennium Declaration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This meeting was the largest gathering of world leaders in history as of the year 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCagenda_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCagenda-3">[3]</a></sup> It was followed by the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">World Summit</a> five years later, which took place from 14 to 16 September 2005.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Goals">Goals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Goals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MDGs.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/MDGs.svg/200px-MDGs.svg.png" width="200" height="391" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/MDGs.svg/300px-MDGs.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/MDGs.svg/400px-MDGs.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="327" data-file-height="640" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MDGs.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Official logos for each of the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/UNGA" class="mw-redirect" title="UNGA">General Assembly</a> <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations General Assembly Resolution">Resolution</a> that decided upon this summit stated that it attempted to seize "a unique and symbolically compelling moment to articulate and affirm an animating vision for the United Nations."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In this summit, 189 member states of the United Nations agreed to help citizens in the world's poorest countries to achieve a better life by the year 2015. The framework for this progress is outlined in the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>. Also known as the <i>MDGs</i>, these goals were derived from the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Declaration" class="mw-redirect" title="Millennium Declaration">Millennium Declaration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This summit was focused on various global issues, such as <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, and how to share the benefits of <a href="/wiki/Globalisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Globalisation">globalisation</a> more fairly.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCmideastpeace_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCmideastpeace-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Delegations">Delegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Delegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_delegates_to_the_Millennium_Summit" title="List of delegates to the Millennium Summit">List of delegates to the Millennium Summit</a></div>
<p>On 5 September 2000, delegates around the world began to travel to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for the Millennium Summit. The <a href="/wiki/Delegation" title="Delegation">delegation</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> was inspected at <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt International Airport">Frankfurt International Airport</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> airline officials during a stop in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> personnel demanded that the members of the delegation and their belongings be searched. In response to these demands, the North Korean government withdrew its delegation from the Summit. As <a href="/wiki/Diplomats" class="mw-redirect" title="Diplomats">diplomats</a>, the officials should not have been subject to search.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over 150 world leaders participated in the discussion, including 100 <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">heads of state</a>, 47 <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">heads of government</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">crown princes</a>, five <a href="/wiki/Vice_President" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice President">Vice Presidents</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy Prime Minister">Deputy Prime Ministers</a>, and 8,000 other <a href="/wiki/Delegate" title="Delegate">delegates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Group_of_77" title="Group of 77">Group of 77</a> was also present to discuss the changes the United Nations faced at the turn of the 21st century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Summit">The Summit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Summit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg/170px-Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg" width="170" height="254" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg/255px-Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="334" data-file-height="500" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">President of Russia</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> giving a speech at the Summit on 6 September 2000.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Finland" title="President of Finland">President of Finland</a> <a href="/wiki/Tarja_Halonen" title="Tarja Halonen">Tarja Halonen</a> and the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Namibia" title="President of Namibia">President of Namibia</a> <a href="/wiki/Sam_Nujoma" title="Sam Nujoma">Sam Nujoma</a> co-chaired the Millennium Summit. This was due to the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="President of the United Nations General Assembly">Presidency over the General Assembly</a> of <a href="/wiki/Theo-Ben_Gurirab" title="Theo-Ben Gurirab">Theo-Ben Gurirab</a> in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifty-fourth_session_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fifty-fourth session of the United Nations General Assembly (page does not exist)">fifty-fourth session</a> and that of <a href="/wiki/Harri_Holkeri" title="Harri Holkeri">Harri Holkeri</a> in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fifty-fifth_session_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fifty-fifth session of the United Nations General Assembly (page does not exist)">fifty-fifth session</a>. Therefore, the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Head of State">heads of state</a> of Finland and Namibia were chosen to preside over the summit.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Wednesday, 6 September 2000, the Millennium Summit was opened by <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Secretary-General" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Secretary-General">Secretary-General</a> of the United Nations. Before moving into the summit, Annan called for a minute's silence for four United Nations workers who were killed in West Timor by pro-Indonesian militiamen. <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Russia" title="President of Russia">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> delivered a plea for <a href="/wiki/World_peace" title="World peace">world peace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disarmament" title="Disarmament">disarmament</a>. Sixty-three other speakers spoke for five minutes each. In the duration of the summit, Bill Clinton held separate meetings with <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel's</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Israel" title="Prime Minister of Israel">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Ehud_Barak" title="Ehud Barak">Ehud Barak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian people">Palestinian</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Yaser_Arafat" class="mw-redirect" title="Yaser Arafat">Yaser Arafat</a>, calling on them to reach a peace agreement among the two nations,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCagenda_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCagenda-3">[3]</a></sup> although no actual progress was made in doing so. Both sides were still committed to reaching such an agreement, however.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCmideastpeace_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCmideastpeace-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Thursday, 7 September 2000, various heads of state discussed peacekeeping issues. They discussed these issues at a round-table meeting of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a>. Seventy speakers were scheduled for this day during the summit, including <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People's Republic of China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_South_Africa" title="President of South Africa">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Thabo_Mbeki" title="Thabo Mbeki">Thabo Mbeki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lankan</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Sri_Lanka" title="President of Sri Lanka">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Chandrika_Kumaratunga" title="Chandrika Kumaratunga">Chandrika Kumaratunga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Yoshiro_Mori" class="mw-redirect" title="Yoshiro Mori">Yoshiro Mori</a>, and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Sierra_Leone" title="President of Sierra Leone">President of Sierra Leone</a> <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Kabbah" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahmad Kabbah">Ahmad Kabbah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCagenda_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCagenda-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final day of the Millennium Summit, Friday, 8 September 2000, ended after 60 world leaders said their speeches for five minutes each. The speakers included <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Indonesia" title="President of Indonesia">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Abdurrahman_Wahid" title="Abdurrahman Wahid">Abdurrahman Wahid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwean</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Zimbabwe" class="mw-redirect" title="President of Zimbabwe">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mugabe" title="Robert Mugabe">Robert Mugabe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigerian</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Nigeria" title="President of Nigeria">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Olusegun_Obasanjo" title="Olusegun Obasanjo">Olusegun Obasanjo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_India" title="Prime Minister of India">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Atal_Behari_Vajpayee" class="mw-redirect" title="Atal Behari Vajpayee">Atal Behari Vajpayee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCagenda_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCagenda-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_East_peace_negotiations">Middle East peace negotiations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Middle East peace negotiations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak called for Yaser Arafat, the Palestinian leader, to reach an agreement with him. During the summit, Barak stated:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"The opportunity for peace in the Middle East is now at hand and must not be missed. Jerusalem, the eternal capital of Israel, now calls for a peace of honour, of courage and of brotherhood. We recognise that Jerusalem is also sacred to Muslims and Christians around the world and cherished by our <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian people">Palestinian</a> neighbours. A true peace will reflect all these bonds."<sup id="cite_ref-BBCmideastpeace_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCmideastpeace-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Yaser Arafat responded to Ehud Barak's comments by saying the Palestinians have already contributed to the peace efforts by making significant sacrifices towards a compromise between the two countries.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCmideastpeace_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCmideastpeace-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peacekeeping_forces">Peacekeeping forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Peacekeeping forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a> urged the overhaul of the United Nations' <a href="/wiki/Peacekeeping" title="Peacekeeping">peacekeeping</a> forces. He called for the creation for a military staff to supervise the operations.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> American President Bill Clinton also stressed the importance of these peacekeeping missions.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Millennium_Declaration">The Millennium Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Millennium Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Millennium_Declaration" title="United Nations Millennium Declaration">Millennium Declaration</a> was adopted during the Millennium Summit by the world leaders who attended, striving to "free all men, women, and children from the abject and dehumanizing conditions of <a href="/wiki/Extreme_poverty" title="Extreme poverty">extreme poverty</a>." By the end of the Summit, the Millennium Declaration's eight chapters were drafted, from which the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>, originally developed by the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_for_Economic_Co-operation_and_Development" title="Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development">Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development</a> (OECD), were particularly promoted in the years following the summit.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The delegates at this summit agreed on the following eight chapters:<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>Values and Principles</li>
<li>Peace, Security and Disarmament</li>
<li>Development and Poverty Eradication</li>
<li>Protecting our Common Environment</li>
<li>Human Rights, Democracy and Good Governance</li>
<li>Protecting the Vulnerable</li>
<li>Meeting the Special Needs of Africa</li>
<li>Strengthening the United Nations</li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Follow-up">Follow-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Millennium_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Follow-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Additional summits are to be held every five years after the Millennium Summit to assess the progress of the United Nations in reaching towards the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>. The first follow-up to the Millennium Summit was held in the year of 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Summit" title="2005 World Summit">2005 World Summit</a>.</p>
<p>The United Nations summit for the adoption of the <a href="/wiki/Post-2015_Development_Agenda" title="Post-2015 Development Agenda">post-2015 development agenda</a> will be held from 25 to 27 September 2015, in New York and convened as a high-level plenary meeting of the General Assembly.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Proposed 6 themes for Interactive Dialogues are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Eradicating poverty in all its dimensions and addressing inequality</li>
<li>Tackling climate change and achieving more sustainable lifestyles</li>
<li>Building strong, inclusive and resilient economies</li>
<li>Promoting peaceful societies and strong institutions</li>
<li>A renewed global partnership and adequate means of implementation</li>
<li>Reviewing progress on SDG commitments; universality and differentiations</li>
</ul>
