			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Biophysics</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Biophysical&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Biophysical">Biophysical</a>)</span></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Biophysics</b> or <b>biological physics</b> is an interdisciplinary science that applies the approaches and methods of <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> to study <a href="/wiki/Biological_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological systems">biological systems</a>. Biophysics covers all scales of <a href="/wiki/Biological_organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological organization">biological organization</a>, from molecular to organismic and populations. Biophysical research shares significant overlap with <a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">biochemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physical_chemistry" title="Physical chemistry">physical chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">nanotechnology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioengineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioengineering">bioengineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computational_biology" title="Computational biology">computational biology</a>, biomechanics and <a href="/wiki/Systems_biology" title="Systems biology">systems biology</a>.</p>
<p>The term <i>biophysics</i> was originally introduced by <a href="/wiki/Karl_Pearson" title="Karl Pearson">Karl Pearson</a> in 1892.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Glaser2012_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glaser2012-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Molecular biophysics typically addresses biological questions similar to those in <a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">biochemistry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molecular_biology" title="Molecular biology">molecular biology</a>, but more quantitatively, seeking to find the physical underpinnings of biomolecular phenomena. Scientists in this field conduct research concerned with understanding the interactions between the various systems of a cell, including the interactions between <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a>, <a href="/wiki/RNA" title="RNA">RNA</a> and protein biosynthesis, as well as how these interactions are regulated. A great variety of techniques are used to answer these questions.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fluorescent" class="mw-redirect" title="Fluorescent">Fluorescent</a> imaging techniques, as well as <a href="/wiki/Electron_microscopy" class="mw-redirect" title="Electron microscopy">electron microscopy</a>, <a href="/wiki/X-ray_crystallography" title="X-ray crystallography">x-ray crystallography</a>, <a href="/wiki/NMR_spectroscopy" class="mw-redirect" title="NMR spectroscopy">NMR spectroscopy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atomic_force_microscopy" class="mw-redirect" title="Atomic force microscopy">atomic force microscopy</a> (AFM) and <a href="/wiki/Small-angle_scattering" title="Small-angle scattering">small-angle scattering</a> (SAS) both with <a href="/wiki/Small-angle_X-ray_scattering" title="Small-angle X-ray scattering">X-rays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Small-angle_neutron_scattering" title="Small-angle neutron scattering">neutrons</a> (SAXS/SANS) are often used to visualize structures of biological significance. <a href="/wiki/Protein_dynamics" title="Protein dynamics">Protein dynamics</a> can be observed by <a href="/wiki/Neutron_spin_echo" title="Neutron spin echo">neutron spin echo</a> spectroscopy. <a href="/wiki/Conformational_change" title="Conformational change">Conformational change</a> in structure can be measured using techniques such as <a href="/wiki/Dual_polarisation_interferometry" class="mw-redirect" title="Dual polarisation interferometry">dual polarisation interferometry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Circular_dichroism" title="Circular dichroism">circular dichroism</a>, <a href="/wiki/SAXS" class="mw-redirect" title="SAXS">SAXS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Small-angle_neutron_scattering" title="Small-angle neutron scattering">SANS</a>. Direct manipulation of molecules using <a href="/wiki/Optical_tweezers" title="Optical tweezers">optical tweezers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Atomic_force_microscopy" class="mw-redirect" title="Atomic force microscopy">AFM</a>, can also be used to monitor biological events where forces and distances are at the nanoscale. Molecular biophysicists often consider complex biological events as systems of interacting entities which can be understood e.g. through <a href="/wiki/Statistical_mechanics" title="Statistical mechanics">statistical mechanics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thermodynamics" title="Thermodynamics">thermodynamics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemical_kinetics" title="Chemical kinetics">chemical kinetics</a>. By drawing knowledge and experimental techniques from a wide variety of disciplines, biophysicists are often able to directly observe, model or even manipulate the structures and interactions of individual <a href="/wiki/Molecules" class="mw-redirect" title="Molecules">molecules</a> or complexes of molecules.</p>
<p>In addition to traditional (i.e. molecular and cellular) biophysical topics like <a href="/wiki/Structural_biology" title="Structural biology">structural biology</a> or <a href="/wiki/Enzyme_kinetics" title="Enzyme kinetics">enzyme kinetics</a>, modern biophysics encompasses an extraordinarily broad range of research, from <a href="/wiki/Bioelectronics" title="Bioelectronics">bioelectronics</a> to <a href="/wiki/Quantum_biology" title="Quantum biology">quantum biology</a> involving both experimental and theoretical tools. It is becoming increasingly common for biophysicists to apply the models and experimental techniques derived from <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_and_theoretical_biology" title="Mathematical and theoretical biology">biomathematics</a>), to larger systems such as <a href="/wiki/Tissue_(biology)" title="Tissue (biology)">tissues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Organ_(anatomy)" title="Organ (anatomy)">organs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Population_biology" title="Population biology">populations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecosystems" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecosystems">ecosystems</a>. Biophysical models are used extensively in the study of electrical conduction in single <a href="/wiki/Neurons" class="mw-redirect" title="Neurons">neurons</a>, as well as neural circuit analysis in both tissue and whole brain.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Medical_physics" title="Medical physics">Medical physics</a>, a branch of biophysics, is any application of <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> to <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> or <a href="/wiki/Healthcare" class="mw-redirect" title="Healthcare">healthcare</a>, ranging from <a href="/wiki/Radiology" title="Radiology">radiology</a> to <a href="/wiki/Microscopy" title="Microscopy">microscopy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nanomedicine" title="Nanomedicine">nanomedicine</a>. For example, physicist <a href="/wiki/Richard_Feynman" title="Richard Feynman">Richard Feynman</a> theorized about the future of <a href="/wiki/Nanomedicine" title="Nanomedicine">nanomedicine</a>. He wrote about the idea of a <i>medical</i> use for <a href="/wiki/Biological_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological machine">biological machines</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Nanomachines" class="mw-redirect" title="Nanomachines">nanomachines</a>). Feynman and <a href="/wiki/Albert_Hibbs" title="Albert Hibbs">Albert Hibbs</a> suggested that certain repair machines might one day be reduced in size to the point that it would be possible to (as Feynman put it) "<a href="/wiki/Biological_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological machine">swallow the doctor</a>". The idea was discussed in Feynman's 1959 essay <i><a href="/wiki/There%27s_Plenty_of_Room_at_the_Bottom" title="There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom">There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></i></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some of the earlier studies in biophysics were conducted in the 1840s by a group known as the Berlin school of physiologists. Among its members were pioneers such as <a href="/wiki/Hermann_von_Helmholtz" title="Hermann von Helmholtz">Hermann von Helmholtz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Heinrich_Weber" title="Ernst Heinrich Weber">Ernst Heinrich Weber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_F._W._Ludwig" class="mw-redirect" title="Carl F. W. Ludwig">Carl F. W. Ludwig</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Peter_M%C3%BCller" title="Johannes Peter Müller">Johannes Peter Müller</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Franceschetti2012_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Franceschetti2012-4">[4]</a></sup> Biophysics might even be seen as dating back to the studies of <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Galvani" title="Luigi Galvani">Luigi Galvani</a>.</p>
<p>The popularity of the field rose when the book <i><a href="/wiki/What_Is_Life%3F" title="What Is Life?">What Is Life?</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Schr%C3%B6dinger" title="Erwin Schrödinger">Erwin Schrödinger</a> was published. Since 1957 biophysicists have organized themselves into the <a href="/wiki/Biophysical_Society" title="Biophysical Society">Biophysical Society</a> which now has about 9,000 members over the world.<sup id="cite_ref-RosenGothard2009_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RosenGothard2009-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some authors such as <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rosen_(theoretical_biologist)" title="Robert Rosen (theoretical biologist)">Robert Rosen</a> criticize biophysics on the ground that the biophysical method does not take into account the specificity of biological phenomena<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Focus_as_a_subfield">Focus as a subfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Focus as a subfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While some colleges and universities have dedicated departments of biophysics, usually at the graduate level, many do not have university-level biophysics departments, instead having groups in related departments such as <a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">biochemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cell_biology" title="Cell biology">cell biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molecular_biology" title="Molecular biology">molecular biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neuroscience" title="Neuroscience">neuroscience</a>,<a href="/wiki/Pharmacology" title="Pharmacology">pharmacology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a>. Depending on the strengths of a department at a university differing emphasis will be given to fields of biophysics. What follows is a list of examples of how each department applies its efforts toward the study of biophysics. This list is hardly all inclusive. Nor does each subject of study belong exclusively to any particular department. Each academic institution makes its own rules and there is much overlap between departments.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">Biology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molecular_biology" title="Molecular biology">molecular biology</a> – Almost all forms of biophysics efforts are included in some biology department somewhere. Typical examples include: <a href="/wiki/Gene_regulation" class="mw-redirect" title="Gene regulation">gene regulation</a>, single <a href="/wiki/Protein_dynamics" title="Protein dynamics">protein dynamics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioenergetics" title="Bioenergetics">bioenergetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patch_clamp" title="Patch clamp">patch clamping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biomechanics" title="Biomechanics">biomechanics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virophysics" title="Virophysics">virophysics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Structural_biology" title="Structural biology">Structural biology</a> – Ångstrom-resolution structures of proteins, nucleic acids, lipids, carbohydrates, and complexes thereof.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">Biochemistry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a> – biomolecular structure, siRNA, nucleic acid structure, structure-activity relationships.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">Computer science</a> – <a href="/wiki/Neural_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Neural network">Neural networks</a>, biomolecular and drug databases.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Computational_chemistry" title="Computational chemistry">Computational chemistry</a> – <a href="/wiki/Molecular_dynamics" title="Molecular dynamics">molecular dynamics</a> simulation, <a href="/wiki/Docking_(molecular)" title="Docking (molecular)">molecular docking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quantum_chemistry" title="Quantum chemistry">quantum chemistry</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bioinformatics" title="Bioinformatics">Bioinformatics</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sequence_alignment" title="Sequence alignment">sequence alignment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Structural_alignment" title="Structural alignment">structural alignment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Protein_structure_prediction" title="Protein structure prediction">protein structure prediction</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a> – graph/network theory, population modeling, dynamical systems, <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetics" title="Phylogenetics">phylogenetics</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">Medicine</a> – biophysical research that emphasizes medicine.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Neuroscience" title="Neuroscience">Neuroscience</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neurophysics" title="Neurophysics">neurophysics</a> – studying neural networks experimentally (brain slicing) as well as theoretically (computer models), membrane permitivity, gene therapy, understanding tumors.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pharmacology" title="Pharmacology">Pharmacology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a> – <a href="/wiki/Channelomics" class="mw-redirect" title="Channelomics">channelomics</a>, biomolecular interactions, cellular membranes, <a href="/wiki/Polyketide" title="Polyketide">polyketides</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">Physics</a> – <a href="/wiki/Negentropy" title="Negentropy">negentropy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stochastic_processes" class="mw-redirect" title="Stochastic processes">stochastic processes</a>, and the development of new physical <a href="/wiki/Techniques" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Techniques">techniques</a> and <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">instrumentation</a> as well as their application.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quantum_biology" title="Quantum biology">Quantum biology</a> – The field of quantum biology applies <a href="/wiki/Quantum_mechanics" title="Quantum mechanics">quantum mechanics</a> to biological objects and problems. <a href="/wiki/Quantum_decoherence" title="Quantum decoherence">Decohered</a> <a href="/wiki/Isomers" class="mw-redirect" title="Isomers">isomers</a> to yield time-dependent base substitutions. These studies imply applications in quantum computing.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Agronomy" title="Agronomy">Agronomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Biophysical_techniques" class="mw-redirect" title="Biophysical techniques">biophysical techniques</a> are unique to this field. Research efforts in biophysics are often initiated by scientists who were traditional physicists, chemists, and biologists by training.</p>
