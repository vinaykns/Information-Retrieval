<p>The <b>Chief Joseph Dam</b> is a concrete gravity <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">dam</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a>, 2.4&#160;km (1.5&#160;mi) upriver from <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Washington" title="Bridgeport, Washington">Bridgeport, Washington</a>. The dam is 877&#160;km (545&#160;mi) upriver from the mouth of the Columbia at <a href="/wiki/Astoria,_Oregon" title="Astoria, Oregon">Astoria, Oregon</a>. It is operated by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Corps_of_Engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army Corps of Engineers">USACE</a> Chief Joseph Dam Project Office, and the electricity is marketed by the <a href="/wiki/Bonneville_Power_Administration" title="Bonneville Power Administration">Bonneville Power Administration</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chief_Joseph_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The dam was authorized as Foster Creek Dam and Powerhouse for power generation and irrigation by the <i>River and Harbor Act of 1946</i>. The <i>River and Harbor Act of 1948</i> renamed the project <a href="/wiki/Chief_Joseph" title="Chief Joseph">Chief Joseph</a> Dam in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Nez_Perce_tribe" class="mw-redirect" title="Nez Perce tribe">Nez Perce</a> chief who spent his last years in exile on the <a href="/wiki/Colville_Indian_Reservation" title="Colville Indian Reservation">Colville Indian Reservation</a>. Because of its lack of <a href="/wiki/Fish_ladder" title="Fish ladder">fish ladders</a>, Chief Joseph Dam completely blocks salmon migration to the upper Columbia River system.</p>
<p>Construction began in 1949, with the main dam and intake structure completed in 1955. Installation of the initial generating units was completed in 1958. Eleven additional turbines were installed between 1973 and 1979, and the dam and lake were raised 3&#160;m (10&#160;ft), boosting the capacity to <span class="nowrap">2,620 MW</span>, making Chief Joseph Dam the second largest hydroelectric power producer in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Type">Type</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chief_Joseph_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Type">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chief Joseph Dam is a <a href="/wiki/Run-of-the-river" class="mw-redirect" title="Run-of-the-river">run-of-the-river</a> dam which means the lake behind the dam is not able to store large amounts of water. Water flowing to Chief Joseph Dam from <a href="/wiki/Grand_Coulee_Dam" title="Grand Coulee Dam">Grand Coulee Dam</a> must be passed on to <a href="/wiki/Wells_Dam" title="Wells Dam">Wells Dam</a> at approximately the same rate. With 27 main generators in the powerhouse, it has the hydraulic capacity of 6,030&#160;m<sup>3</sup>/s (213,000&#160;cu&#160;ft/s).</p>
<p>In the event more water flows to Chief Joseph Dam than could be used for power generation, the spillway gates would be opened to pass the excess water. With an average annual flow rate of 3,058&#160;m<sup>3</sup>/s (107,992&#160;cu&#160;ft/s), the Columbia River seldom exceeds the powerplant’s capability to pass water, and spilling of water is infrequent at Chief Joseph Dam.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reservoir">Reservoir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chief_Joseph_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reservoir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Reservoir" title="Reservoir">reservoir</a> behind the dam is named Rufus Woods Lake, and runs 82&#160;km (51&#160;mi) up the river channel. <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport_State_Park" title="Bridgeport State Park">Bridgeport State Park</a>, on the lake, is adjacent to the dam.</p>
