<p>This is a <b>list of energy storage projects</b> worldwide. Many individual <a href="/wiki/Energy_storage" title="Energy storage">energy storage</a> projects augment <a href="/wiki/Electrical_grid" title="Electrical grid">electrical grids</a> by capturing excess electrical energy during periods of low demand and storing it in other forms until needed on an <a href="/wiki/Electrical_grid" title="Electrical grid">electrical grid</a>. The energy is later converted back to its electrical form and returned to the grid as needed.</p>
<p>Another energy storage method is the consumption of surplus or low-cost energy (typically during night time) for conversion into resources such as hot water, cool water or ice, which is then used for heating or cooling at other times when electricity is in higher demand and at greater cost per kilowatt hour (kWh). Such <a href="/wiki/Thermal_energy_storage" title="Thermal energy storage">thermal energy storage</a> is often employed at end-user sites such as large buildings, and also as part of <a href="/wiki/District_heating" title="District heating">district heating</a>, thus 'shifting' energy consumption to other times for better balancing of supply and demand.</p>
<p>Some notable energy storage projects include <a href="/wiki/Sir_Adam_Beck_Hydroelectric_Generating_Stations" title="Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations">Sir Adam Beck Hydroelectric Generating Stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bear_Swamp_Hydroelectric_Power_Station" title="Bear Swamp Hydroelectric Power Station">Bear Swamp Hydroelectric Power Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seneca_Pumped_Storage_Generating_Station" title="Seneca Pumped Storage Generating Station">Seneca Pumped Storage Generating Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salina_Pumped_Storage_Project" title="Salina Pumped Storage Project">Salina Pumped Storage Project</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carters_Dam" title="Carters Dam">Carters Dam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smith_Mountain_Dam" title="Smith Mountain Dam">Smith Mountain Dam</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Matanoagawa_Dam" title="Matanoagawa Dam">Matanoagawa Dam</a>.</p>
