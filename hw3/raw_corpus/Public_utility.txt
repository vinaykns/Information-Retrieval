<p>A <b>public utility</b> (usually just <b>utility</b>) is an organization that maintains the <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Public_services" class="mw-redirect" title="Public services">public service</a> (often also providing a service using that infrastructure). Public utilities are subject to forms of public control and regulation ranging from local community-based groups to statewide government monopolies.</p>
<p>The term <b>utilities</b> can also refer to the set of services provided by these organizations consumed by the public: <a href="/wiki/Electric_utility" title="Electric utility">electricity</a>, natural gas, <a href="/wiki/Water_utility" class="mw-redirect" title="Water utility">water</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Water_industry" title="Water industry">sewage</a>. <a href="/wiki/Broadband" title="Broadband">Broadband</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> services (both <a href="/wiki/Fixed-line" class="mw-redirect" title="Fixed-line">fixed-line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mobile_broadband" title="Mobile broadband">mobile</a>) are increasingly being included within the definition.<sup id="cite_ref-merriam-webster1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merriam-webster1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-investorwords1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investorwords1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-answers1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-answers1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cpuc.ca.gov_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cpuc.ca.gov-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_utility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, public utilities are often <a href="/wiki/Natural_monopoly" title="Natural monopoly">natural monopolies</a> because the infrastructure required to produce and deliver a product such as electricity or water is very expensive to build and maintain.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As a result, they are often government monopolies, or if privately owned, the sectors are specially <a href="/wiki/Regulation" title="Regulation">regulated</a> by a <a href="/wiki/Public_utilities_commission" title="Public utilities commission">public utilities commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-merriam-webster1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merriam-webster1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-investorwords1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-investorwords1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-answers1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-answers1-3">[3]</a></sup> The first public utility in the United States was a grist mill on <a href="/wiki/Mother_Brook" title="Mother Brook">Mother Brook</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dedham,_Massachusetts" title="Dedham, Massachusetts">Dedham, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-growth_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-growth-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Developments in technology have eroded some of the natural monopoly aspects of traditional public utilities. For instance, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">electricity generation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_retailing" title="Electricity retailing">electricity retailing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">telecommunication</a>, some types of <a href="/wiki/Public_transit" class="mw-redirect" title="Public transit">public transit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mail" title="Mail">postal services</a> have become competitive in some countries and the trend towards <a href="/wiki/Liberalization" title="Liberalization">liberalization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deregulation" title="Deregulation">deregulation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of public utilities is growing, but the network infrastructure used to distribute most utility products and services has remained largely monopolistic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Public utilities can be <a href="/wiki/Private_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Private company">privately owned</a> or <a href="/wiki/Government-owned_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Government-owned company">publicly owned</a>. Publicly owned utilities include cooperative and municipal utilities. <a href="/wiki/Municipal" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal">Municipal</a> utilities may actually include territories outside of city limits or may not even serve the entire city. <a href="/wiki/Utility_cooperative" title="Utility cooperative">Cooperative utilities</a> are owned by the customers they serve. They are usually found in <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a> areas. Publicly owned utilities are non-profit.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Private utilities, also called <a href="/wiki/Investor-owned_utility" title="Investor-owned utility">investor-owned utilities</a>, are owned by <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investors</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and operate for profit, often referred to as a rate of return.</p>
<p>Public utilities provide services at the consumer level, be it residential, commercial, or industrial consumer. In turn, utilities and very large consumers buy and sell electricity at the wholesale level through a network of <a href="/wiki/Regional_transmission_organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Regional transmission organization">RTOs</a> and ISOs within one of three grids, the eastern grid, Texas, which is a single ISO, and the western grid.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_utilities_commissions">Public utilities commissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_utility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Public utilities commissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Each US state has their <a href="/wiki/Public_utilities_commission" title="Public utilities commission">public utilities commission</a> which regulates the privately owned electric, <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>, telecommunications, water, railroad, rail transit, and/ or passenger transportation companies. For example, the <a href="/wiki/California_Public_Utilities_Commission" title="California Public Utilities Commission">California Public Utilities Commission</a> (or CPUC) is an operating in the state of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cpuc.ca.gov_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cpuc.ca.gov-4">[4]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Public_Utility_Commission_of_Texas" title="Public Utility Commission of Texas">Public Utility Commission of Texas</a> which regulates the state’s electric and telecommunication utilities, implements respective legislation, and offers customer assistance in resolving consumer complaints. Over the years, various changes have dramatically re-shaped the PUC’s mission and focus, shifting from up-front regulation of rates and services to oversight of competitive markets and compliance enforcement of statutes and rules.<sup id="cite_ref-puc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-puc-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_Ireland">United Kingdom and Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_utility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: United Kingdom and Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, the state, private firms, and charities ran the traditional public utilities. For instance, the <a href="/wiki/Sanitary_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Sanitary District">Sanitary Districts</a> were established in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> in 1875 and in Ireland in 1878.</p>
<p>The term can refer to the set of services provided by various organizations that are used in everyday life by the public, such as: <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">electricity generation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_retailing" title="Electricity retailing">electricity retailing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_utility" title="Electric utility">electricity</a> supplies, <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas_utility" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural gas utility">natural gas</a> supplies, <a href="/wiki/Water_utility" class="mw-redirect" title="Water utility">water</a> supplies, <a href="/wiki/Sewage_treatment" title="Sewage treatment">Sewage works</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_industry" title="Water industry">sewage</a> systems and <a href="/wiki/Broadband" title="Broadband">broadband</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> services.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They are regulated by <a href="/wiki/Ofgem" class="mw-redirect" title="Ofgem">Ofgem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ofwat" title="Ofwat">Ofwat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a>. Disabled community transport services may occasionally be included within the definition. They were mostly privatised in the UK during the 1980s.</p>
