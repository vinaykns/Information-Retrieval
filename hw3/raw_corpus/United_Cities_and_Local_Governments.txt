<p><b>United Cities and Local Governments</b> (<b>UCLG</b>) is an umbrella organisation for cities, local governments, and municipal associations throughout the world. United Cities and Local Governments was founded in 2004, when the existing local government organisations - the International Union of Local Authorities (IULA), the United Towns Organisations (UTO), and Metropolis – united their respective global networks of cities and national associations of local governments in a single organisation. United Cities and Local Governments' headquarters, the World Secretariat, is based in Barcelona, Spain. UCLG is the largest local government organisation in the world and understands itself as the united voice and world advocate of democratic local self-government, de facto representing over half the world's population, the cities and association members of UCLG are present in over 120 UN Member States across seven world regions:</p>
<ul>
<li>Africa (UCLGAfrica)</li>
<li>Asia-Pacific (UCLG-ASPAC)</li>
<li>Europe (<a href="/wiki/Council_of_European_Municipalities_and_Regions" title="Council of European Municipalities and Regions">CEMR</a>),</li>
<li>Eurasia (UCLG EuroAsia)</li>
<li>Middle East and West Asia (<a href="/wiki/UCLG_MEWA" title="UCLG MEWA">UCLG MEWA</a>),</li>
<li>Latin America (FLACMA)</li>
<li>North America (UCLG North America)</li>
</ul>
<p>UCLG has also a dedicated "regional" Section for large metropolitan Areas, Metropolis.</p>
<p>Over 1,000 cities are direct members of United Cities and Local Governments, as well as 112 national associations which represent all the cities and local governments in their respective countries. Members vary, from small cities to national local governments associations and regional associations.</p>
<p>On May 8, 2004, United Cities and Local Governments (UCLG) adopted the <a href="/wiki/Agenda_21_for_culture" title="Agenda 21 for culture">Agenda 21 for culture</a> as a reference document for its programmes on culture and assumed the role of coordinator of the process subsequent to its approval.</p>
<p>Its President since November 2010 is Mayor <a href="/wiki/Kadir_Topba%C5%9F" title="Kadir Topbaş">Kadir Topbaş</a> (Istanbul). He replaces Mayor <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Delano%C3%AB" title="Bertrand Delanoë">Bertrand Delanoë</a> (Paris). Accompanying the <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Istanbul" title="List of mayors of Istanbul">Mayor of Istanbul</a>, as Co Presidents are <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Costa" title="António Costa">Antonio Costa</a> of Lisbon, Portugal; <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Araya_Monge" title="Johnny Araya Monge">Johnny Araya</a> of San Jose de Costa Rica; <a href="/wiki/Wan_Qingliang" title="Wan Qingliang">Wan Qingliang</a> of Guangzhou, China; <a href="/wiki/Muchadeyi_Masunda" title="Muchadeyi Masunda">Muchadeyi Masunda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Harare" title="Harare">Harare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Ilsur_Metshin" title="Ilsur Metshin">Ilsur Metshin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kazan" title="Kazan">Kazan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a>. Ted Ellis, Mayor of <a href="/wiki/Bluffton,_Indiana" title="Bluffton, Indiana">Bluffton Indiana</a> USA is the Treasurer.</p>
<p>The city of <a href="/wiki/Rabat" title="Rabat">Rabat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Kingdom of Morocco</a> hosted the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.rabat2013.uclg.org/es/">4th World Congress of UCLG</a> in 2013. During the world congress in Rabat, <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, was voted to host the 2016 United Cities and Local Governments congress.</p>
<p>Since September 2014, UCLG has provided oversight to the <a href="/wiki/Compact_of_Mayors" title="Compact of Mayors">Compact of Mayors</a>, a global coalition of mayors and city leaders dedicated to fighting climate change locally.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Committees_and_Working_Groups">Committees and Working Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Cities_and_Local_Governments&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Committees and Working Groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Committees prepare and implement policies within priority areas, as defined by the Executive Bureau and the work programme for the current period. Each Committee can establish one or more Working Groups. The Working Groups develop proposals and/or cooperation initiatives between local governments and their associations in order to enrich the discussions within UCLG.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>The standing Committees are:</b></p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Decentralization" title="Decentralization">Decentralisation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">Local Self-Government</a></p>
<p>- Local Finance and Development</p>
<p>- Culture</p>
<p>- Development Cooperation &amp; City Diplomacy</p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Social_Inclusion,_Participative_Democracy_and_Human_Rights" title="Committee on Social Inclusion, Participative Democracy and Human Rights">Committee on Social Inclusion, Participative Democracy and Human Rights</a></p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">Gender Equality</a></p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Interregional_Commission" title="Mediterranean Interregional Commission">Mediterranean Interregional Commission</a></p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a></p>
<p>- Peripheral Cities</p>
<p>- Urban Mobility</p>
<p>- Digital and Knowledge-Based Cities</p>
<p>- Urban Strategic Planning</p>
<p><b>The four working groups are:</b></p>
<p>- Capacity and Institution Building (CIB-ACB platform)</p>
<p>- Migration and Co-Development</p>
<p>- The local dimension of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Civilizations" title="Alliance of Civilizations">Alliance of Civilisations</a></p>
<p>- <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">Responsible Tourism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">Sustainable Development</a></p>
