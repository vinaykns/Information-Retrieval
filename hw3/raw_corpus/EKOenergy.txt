<p><b>EKOenergy</b> is an ecolabel for electricity. It is a not-for-profit initiative of the EKOenergy Network, a group of more than 40 environmental organizations from 30 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> EKOenergy started in 2013 in Europe. Its secretariat is based in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Nowadays, EKOenergy is the only international ecolabel for <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable electricity</a>. It is available all over the world and its material is available in more than 30 languages.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Every member organization (all <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">not-for-profit</a> organisations) appoints one person to the EKOenergy Board, the Network’s highest governing authority. The Board takes decisions in consultation with the Advisory Group, which consist of experts from different stakeholder groups, including electricity industry, consumers and environmentalists, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Label">Label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:EKOenergy_logo.jpg" class="image" title="thumbnail"><img alt="thumbnail" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/EKOenergy_logo.jpg/220px-EKOenergy_logo.jpg" width="220" height="77" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/EKOenergy_logo.jpg/330px-EKOenergy_logo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/EKOenergy_logo.jpg/440px-EKOenergy_logo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1181" data-file-height="413" /></a></div>
<p>Other language version of the label can be found in various countries. E.g. EKOénergie in France,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> EKOenergie in Germany<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> or EKOenergia in Finland.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria_of_the_ecolabel">Criteria of the ecolabel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criteria of the ecolabel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Only electricity from renewable sources can be sold as EKOenergy. But EKOenergy is more than just green electricity. EKOenergy also sets criteria for the following aspects:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Consumer information: Consumers get information about where and how the electricity has been produced.</li>
<li>Sustainability: EKOenergy takes into account the impacts of electricity production on <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a> services, <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> of species. Firstly, it does this by excluding the most environmentally degrading and problematic electricity production methods. Secondly, it finances river restoration through an Environmental Fund.</li>
<li>Climate: per every <a href="/wiki/MWh" class="mw-redirect" title="MWh">MWh</a> sold, minimum 10 euro cents will be paid into the EKOenergy Climate Fund.</li>
<li>Tracking of the electricity: The criteria are based on the European best practice, and on the recommendations of RE-DISS/EPED.</li>
<li>Auditing and verification: All EKOenergy claims are verified by independent auditors.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Users_of_the_Label">Users of the Label</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Users of the Label">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Well known sellers of EKOenergy certified electricity include <a href="/wiki/Fortum" title="Fortum">Fortum</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vattenfall" title="Vattenfall">Vattenfall</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Ekosähkö<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and Gesternova.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Also sellers of electricity tracking certificates can help consumers to get EKOenergy: E.g. ECOHZ<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and South Pole Group.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> A full list of licensed sellers can be found on EKOenergy’s website: <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.ekoenergy.org/buying-ekoenergy/licensees/">http://www.ekoenergy.org/buying-ekoenergy/licensees/</a>. An increasing number of EKOenergy consumers uses the label in its own communication:<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> e.g. Otava Printing<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and Saimaan juomatehdas.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_Fund">Climate Fund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate Fund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For each MWh of EKOenergy sold, the seller pays a minimum of €0.10 to EKOenergy’s Climate Fund. This money is used to finance climate projects that would not have happened without the contributions. These projects are managed by experienced organisations. All projects are selected in an open process, with sellers, buyers and independent experts actively involved.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Examples of funded projects:</p>
<ul>
<li>Solar panels for <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzanian</a> schools<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>Solar panels for schools in <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="EKOenergy.E2.80.99s_Environmental_Fund_and_nature_conservation">EKOenergy’s Environmental Fund and nature conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: EKOenergy’s Environmental Fund and nature conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whenever <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a> is sold with the EKOenergy label, €0.10/ MWh go to the Environmental Fund, to finance river restoration projects.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Examples of earlier funded projects:</p>
<ul>

</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EKOenergy_mentioned_in_other_environmental_standards">EKOenergy mentioned in other environmental standards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: EKOenergy mentioned in other environmental standards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="LEED">LEED</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: LEED">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The European versions of the <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">LEED</a> Standard explicitly recommend the use of EKOenergy labelled electricity.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Buildings aiming at LEED certification can get extra points if the electricity used in that building is EKOenergy certified. The text “LEED 2009 BD+C Supplemental Reference Guide with Alternative Compliance Paths for Europe”<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> gives EKOenergy the same status as Green-e certified <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Energy_Certificate_(United_States)" title="Renewable Energy Certificate (United States)">RECs</a> in the US. They write: "The EKOenergy electricity certification scheme represents the best available pan-European option for the sustainable and additional consumption of renewable electricity within Europe. EKOenergy certifies renewable electricity that goes beyond the regulations of European directives and national governments of Europe."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenhouse_Gas_Protocol">Greenhouse Gas Protocol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Greenhouse Gas Protocol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Greenhouse Gas Protocol is a worldwide standard for <a href="/wiki/Carbon_accounting" title="Carbon accounting">carbon accounting</a>. It is a joint product of the <a href="/wiki/World_Resources_Institute" title="World Resources Institute">World Resources Institute</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development" title="World Business Council for Sustainable Development">World Business Council for Sustainable Development</a>. In January 2015, the Secretariat of the Greenhouse Gas Protocol published the Scope 2 Guidance, which gave advice about carbon accounting. The Guidance refers to EKOenergy several times. Chapter 11, which encourages companies to go one step further, refers to EKOenergy’s Climate Fund.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CDP">CDP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EKOenergy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: CDP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Carbon_Disclosure_Project" title="Carbon Disclosure Project">CDP</a> works with 5000 of the largest corporations in the world to help them calculate their <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a> and to help them develop effective carbon emission reduction strategies. On page 15 and 16 of its technical notes for accounting of scope 2 emissions (i.e. emissions related to the production of purchased electricity), CDP explains how companies can do more. “Ecolabels are a way for companies to do more with their purchases. EKOenergy, mentioned by the GHG protocol Scope 2 guidance, is such an option: it is a mark of quality which comes on top of tracking certificates. Electricity sold with the EKOenergy label fulfills strict environmental criteria and raises funds for new renewable energy projects. Involvement, transparency and ‘deeds not words’ are important principles of EKOenergy’s work.”<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
