			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sustainable Energy for All</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Sustainable Energy for All</b> (SE4ALL) is a global <a href="/wiki/Initiative" title="Initiative">initiative</a> led by the <a href="/wiki/Secretary-General_of_the_United_Nations" title="Secretary-General of the United Nations">Secretary-General of the United Nations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> to achieve <a href="/wiki/Energy_poverty" title="Energy poverty">universal energy access</a>, improve <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, and increase the use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>. It was launched to coincide with the designation of 2012 as the <i>International Year of Sustainable Energy for All</i> by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">UN General Assembly</a> in December 2010.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_Energy_for_All&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>UN General Assembly <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.un.org/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/65/151">resolution 65/151</a>, which was passed on 20 December 2010, designated 2012 as the International Year of Sustainable Energy for All. This was in recognition of the growing importance of energy for <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> and <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">climate change mitigation</a>. It also attempted to correct what many working on energy and development issues had for many years argued was a major error in not including action on energy poverty in the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.</p>
<p>Ban Ki-moon made sustainable energy one of the five priorities that would guide his second five-year term. In June 2009 he established the Secretary-General's Advisory Group on Energy and Climate Change (AGECC), which published its final report on 28 April 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The recommendations from this report form the basis of the Sustainable Energy for All initiative launched in 2011.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">Objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_Energy_for_All&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Sustainable Energy for All initiative is intended to attract global attention and public and private commitments to meeting three objectives by 2030:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ensuring universal access to modern energy services</li>
<li>Doubling the rate of improvement in energy efficiency</li>
<li>Doubling the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_Energy_for_All&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A number of activities have been initiated, both within and outside the UN system. These include:</p>
<ul>
<li>National Coordinating Committees: led by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Programme" title="United Nations Development Programme">United Nations Development Programme</a>, national committees and country focal points will be established to drive forward activities and programs on the ground</li>
<li>Energy Access Practitioner Network: led by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Foundation" title="United Nations Foundation">United Nations Foundation</a>, this global network will bring together practitioners working to improve access to modern energy services.</li>
<li>Energy+: led by the Government of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, this international energy and climate initiative will support SE4ALL by coordinating practical actions on the ground, with an emphasis on results-based approaches.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Paris-Nairobi Climate Initiative: led by the Governments of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, this initiative was launched in April 2011 with a focus on energy access.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
