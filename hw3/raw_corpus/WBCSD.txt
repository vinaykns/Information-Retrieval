<p>The <b>World Business Council for Sustainable Development (WBCSD)</b> is a <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a>-led, global <a href="/wiki/Advocacy_group" title="Advocacy group">advocacy association</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> of some 200 <a href="/wiki/Multinational_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Multinational company">international companies</a> dealing exclusively with <a href="/wiki/Business_development" title="Business development">business</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hunter_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunter-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its origins date back to the Rio de Janeiro <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a> of 1992,<sup id="cite_ref-WBCSD_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBCSD-1">[1]</a></sup> when <a href="/wiki/Stephan_Schmidheiny" title="Stephan Schmidheiny">Stephan Schmidheiny</a>, a Swiss business entrepreneur, was appointed chief adviser for business and industry to the secretary general of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_Environment_and_Development" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Conference on Environment and Development">United Nations Conference on Environment and Development</a> (UNCED).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He created a forum called "Business Council for Sustainable Development", which went on to become <i>Changing Course</i>, a book that coined the concept of <a href="/wiki/Eco-efficiency" title="Eco-efficiency">eco-efficiency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The WBCSD was created in 1995 in a merger of the Business Council for Sustainable Development and the World Industry Council for the Environment<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and is based in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> with an office in New York and New Delhi.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Council provides a platform for companies to explore sustainable development, share knowledge, experiences and best practices, and to <a href="/wiki/Advocacy" title="Advocacy">advocate</a> business positions on these issues in a variety of forums, working with governments, non-governmental and <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organizations</a>.</p>
<p>It works on a variety of issues related to sustainable development. While its focus is on the overarching areas Energy &amp; Climate, Development, Ecosystems and the Role of Business in Society, it also executes sector specific projects on <a href="/wiki/Cement" title="Cement">cement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_infrastructure" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban infrastructure">urban infrastructure</a> initiative, tires, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Corporate_reporting&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Corporate reporting (page does not exist)">corporate reporting</a>, water, <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a> in buildings, forest solutions, and electricity utilities.</p>
<p>The Council’s objectives are to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Be a <i>leading <a href="/w/index.php?title=Business_advocate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Business advocate (page does not exist)">business advocate</a></i> on sustainable development</li>
<li><i>Participate in <a href="/wiki/Policy_development" class="mw-redirect" title="Policy development">policy development</a></i> to create the framework conditions for business to make an effective contribution to sustainable human progress</li>
<li>Develop and promote the <i>business case for sustainable development</i></li>
<li><i>Demonstrate the business contribution</i> to sustainable development solutions and share leading edge practices among members</li>
<li>Contribute to a <i>sustainable future</i> for <a href="/wiki/Developing_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Developing nation">developing nations</a> and nations in transition.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_influence">Impact and influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact and influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 2003 <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>/<a href="/wiki/International_Finance_Corporation" title="International Finance Corporation">IFC</a> commissioned study identified the WBCSD as one of the "most influential forums" for companies on corporate social responsibility (CSR) issues.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> A 2004 <a href="/wiki/Globescan" class="mw-redirect" title="Globescan">Globescan</a> survey found the WBCSD as the second most effective SD research organization.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The 2006 survey by the same company reports that 54% of all surveyed experts believe the WBCSD will play a "major role" in advancing sustainable development. Only the European Union received higher approval (69%).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2007's <a href="/wiki/Ethisphere_Institute" title="Ethisphere Institute">Ethisphere Institute</a> list of the 100 Most Influential People in Business Ethics, WBCSD President Bjoern Stigson has been ranked 9th, which made him the 2nd most influential NGO leader.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="WBCSD.27s_10_messages_by_which_to_operate">WBCSD's 10 messages by which to operate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: WBCSD's 10 messages by which to operate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ol>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">Business</a> is good for sustainable development and sustainable development is good for business.</i> Business is part of the sustainable development solution, while sustainable development is an effective long-term business growth strategy.</li>
<li><i>Business cannot succeed in societies that fail.</i> There is no future for successful business if the societies that surround it are not working. Governments and business must create partnerships to deliver essential societal services like energy, water, health care and infrastructure.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">Poverty</a> is a key enemy to stable societies.</i> Poverty creates political and economic instability, a big threat to business and sustainable development. By contrast, businesses can lift living standards and eradicate poverty.</li>
<li><i>Access to markets for all supports sustainable development.</i> Sustainable development is best achieved through open, transparent and competitive global markets.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Good_governance" title="Good governance">Good governance</a> is needed to make business a part of the solution.</i> Supportive frameworks and regulations are needed for business to contribute fully to sustainable development.</li>
<li><i>Business has to earn its licence to operate, innovate and grow.</i> The way business acts and is perceived is crucial to its success. Accountability, ethics, transparency, social and environmental responsibility and trust are basic prerequisites for successful business and sustainable development.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">Innovation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technology_development" class="mw-redirect" title="Technology development">technology development</a> are crucial to sustainable development.</i> They provide key solutions to many of the problems that threaten sustainable development. Business has always been, and will continue to be, the main contributor to technological development.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Eco-efficiency" title="Eco-efficiency">Eco-efficiency</a> – doing more with less - is at the core of the business case for sustainable development.</i> Combining environmental and economic operational excellence to deliver goods and services with lower external impacts and higher quality-of-life benefits is a key sustainable development strategy for business.</li>
<li><i>Ecosystems in balance – a prerequisite for business.</i> Business cannot function if ecosystems and the services they deliver, such as water, biodiversity, food, fiber and climate, are degraded.</li>
<li><i>Cooperation beats confrontation.</i> Sustainable development challenges are huge and require contributions from all parties — governments, business, civil societies and international bodies. Confrontation puts the solutions at risk. Cooperation and creative partnerships foster sustainable development.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Membership of the WBCSD is by invitation of the Executive Committee to companies committed to sustainable development. Among its members<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> are well-known companies such as <a href="/wiki/General_Motors_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="General Motors Corporation">General Motors</a>, <a href="/wiki/DuPont" title="DuPont">DuPont</a>, <a href="/wiki/3M" title="3M">3M</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a>, <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jain_Irrigation_Systems" title="Jain Irrigation Systems">Jain Irrigation Systems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Royal Dutch Shell</a>. On January 22, 2013 the organization announced that <a href="/wiki/Monsanto" title="Monsanto">Monsanto</a> had joined.<sup id="cite_ref-Monsanto_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monsanto-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Member companies pledge their support and contribution to the WBCSD by making available their knowledge and experience, and appropriate human resources. They are asked to publicly report on their environmental performance and to aspire to widen their reporting to cover all three pillars of sustainable development - economic, social and environmental.</p>
<p>A key element is the personal commitment of the Chief Executive Officers (CEOs), acting as Council Members. They are influential advocates for the WBCSD’s policy positions, and they co-chair the working groups. They also organize support for the WBCSD’s work program and ensure the adoption of sustainable management practices within their companies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="For.C3.A9tica">Forética</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Forética">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Forética claims to be global network of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish speaking</a> businesses and professionals whose mission is to promote an ethical management and corporate social responsibility by the establishment of a National standard in Spain known as SGE-21, (sistema de gestación ética para siglo 21) which is also Annex 1 to <a href="/wiki/ISO_26000" title="ISO 26000">ISO 26000</a> and supported by <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">CSR</a> Europe <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Membership is said to around 400 and includes companies of all sizes and sectors, as well as individual specialists, professionals and academics and it was incorporated into WBCSD on 2 September 2014 <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_balance">Geographic balance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geographic balance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While the majority of member companies are headquartered in developed countries, the STDs has a strong regional representation in developing countries through its Regional Network, a global network of almost 60 national and regional business councils (BCSDs) and partner organizations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Greenpeace the <i>World Business Council for Sustainable Development</i> is among the key players responsible for holding the world societies from tackling the <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> and energy management challenges for the past 20 years. The WBCSD Executive Committee was dominated by the largest nonrenewable energy and carbon-intensive companies in the world at least until 2011. According to Greenpeace the WBCSD executive committee has been a ‘Who’s Who’ of the world’s largest carbon-intensive companies.<sup id="cite_ref-GP2010_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP2010-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club" title="Sierra Club">Sierra Club</a> has collaborated with the World Business Council on a number of initiatives, as well as inviting its representatives to speak at Sierra Club events.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Defense_Fund" title="Environmental Defense Fund">Environmental Defense Fund</a> recommends the World Business Council's auditing methods to companies seeking to reduce greenhouse emissions,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Natural_Resources_Defense_Council" title="Natural Resources Defense Council">Natural Resources Defense Council</a> has drawn upon WBCSD guidelines in drawing up their own guidelines for determining biofuels sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The WBCSC's Vision 2020 report was highlighted by <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a> as "the largest concerted corporate sustainability action plan to date – include reversing the damage done to ecosystems, addressing rising greenhouse gas emissions and ensuring societies move to sustainable agriculture."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
