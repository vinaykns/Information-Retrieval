<p>The <b>Copenhagen Climate Council</b> is a global collaboration between international business and science founded by the leading independent think tank in Scandinavia, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/about-us/about-monday-morning.html">Monday Morning</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>. The councilors of the Copenhagen Climate Council have come together to create global awareness of the importance of the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> Climate Summit (<a href="/wiki/COP15" class="mw-redirect" title="COP15">COP15</a>) in Copenhagen, December 2009, and to ensure technical and public support and assistance to global decision makers when agreeing on a new climate treaty to replace the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> from 1997.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Copenhagen Climate Council was founded in 2007 by the leading independent think tank in Scandinavia, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/about-us/about-monday-morning.html"><i>Monday Morning</i></a>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The purpose of the Copenhagen Climate Council is to create global awareness of the importance of the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> Climate Summit (<a href="/wiki/COP15" class="mw-redirect" title="COP15">COP15</a>) in Copenhagen, December 2009. Leading up to this pivotal UN meeting, the Copenhagen Climate Council works on presenting innovative yet achievable solutions to climate change, as well as assess what is required to make a new global treaty effective. The Council will seek to promote constructive dialogue between government and business, so that when the world's political leaders and negotiators meet in Copenhagen, they will do so armed with the very best arguments for establishing a treaty that can be supported by global business. By promoting and demonstrating innovative, positive, and meaningful business leadership and ideas, the Copenhagen Climate Council aims to demonstrate that achieving an effective global climate treaty is not only possible, but necessary. The strategy is built upon the following principles:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Creating international awareness</b> of the importance of the Copenhagen UN Climate Summit and the successor treaty to the Kyoto Protocol.</li>
<li><b>Promoting constructive dialogue</b> between government, business, and science.</li>
<li><b>Inspiring global business leaders</b> by demonstrating that tackling climate change also has the potential to create huge opportunities for innovation and economic growth.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manifesto">Manifesto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manifesto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Published in November 2007, on the eve of the UN <a href="/wiki/COP13" class="mw-redirect" title="COP13">COP13</a> Climate Change Conference in Bali – the instigation night of the <a href="/wiki/Bali_Road_Map" title="Bali Road Map">Bali Road Map</a>. The document outlines what the Council believes is required to tackle climate change and how this can be achieved through a new global treaty. The Manifesto articulates a clear goal for the maximum level of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere by 2050. The document will serve as input at the <i>World Business Summit on Climate Change</i>, outlining key elements for further discussion and inclusion in the recommendations to be delivered to the UN Summit.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Copenhagen Climate Council comprises 30 global climate leaders <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> representing <i>business</i>, <i>science</i>, and <i>public policy</i> from all parts of the world.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Business leaders</b> are selected to represent global companies and innovative entrepreneurs, who, through their actions, reveal that sustainable, climate-responsible business is both necessary and profitable.</li>
<li><b>Scientists</b> are gathered to ensure that the work of the Council is underpinned by rigorous analysis.</li>
<li><b>Policy makers</b> with experience in public policy are included in the Council to ensure that the work is informed by knowledge of what is required to assist high-level, complex policy negotiations.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The central aim of the Copenhagen Climate Council is to create global awareness to the urgency of reaching a global agreement on how to tackle climate change at the UN Climate Conference in Copenhagen, December 2009. To achieve this end, the Copenhagen Climate Council provides a <a href="/wiki/Web_2.0" title="Web 2.0">Web 2.0</a> climate website – '<b>The Climate Community'</b> – which features latest climate news, intelligence, solutions and points of view, an online climate community, as well as the rest of the Copenhagen Climate Council activities, such as the '<b>World Business Summit on Climate Change'</b>; launching the '<b>Thought Leadership Series'</b>; launching the '<b>Climate LIFE'</b> film, book, and digital exhibition; co-hosting with CITRIS the scientific conference '<b>Unlocking the Climate Code: Innovation in Climate and Energy'</b>; and the <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Poznań</a> side event '<b>Business Requirements of a Post-2012 Climate Treaty'</b>. Recently, the Copenhagen Climate Council has also hosted a <a href="/wiki/Business_Roundtable" title="Business Roundtable">Business Roundtable</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Climate_Community">The Climate Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Climate Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-involved.html"><b>Climate Community</b></a> is the official website of the Copenhagen Climate Council. The website is based on <a href="/wiki/Web_2.0" title="Web 2.0">Web 2.0</a> principles, and hooks the user up with the world's leading climate <a href="/wiki/Stakeholder_(corporate)" title="Stakeholder (corporate)">stakeholders</a> and offers possibility for the user to give voice and influence the global climate agenda. The Climate Community aims to bring the latest and most relevant news, insights, and intelligence that equips the user to navigate the climate challenges and turn risks into opportunities. The Climate Community features an extensive <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/news.html">news section</a> with <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/news/channel/top-stories.html">Top Stories</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/news/channel/news-flashes.html">Daily News Summaries</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/points-of-view.html">Points of Views</a>, and a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/news/channel/weekly-roundups.html">Weekly Roundup</a>, searchable by date, region and sector.</p>
<p>Exclusive news features so far include interviews with U.S. Energy Secretary <a href="/wiki/Steve_Chu" class="mw-redirect" title="Steve Chu">Steve Chu</a>, UN Climate Chief <a href="/wiki/Yvo_de_Boer" title="Yvo de Boer">Yvo de Boer</a>, the Danish Climate minister <a href="/wiki/Connie_Hedegaard" title="Connie Hedegaard">Connie Hedegaard</a>, IPCC Chairman <a href="/wiki/Rajendra_Pachauri" class="mw-redirect" title="Rajendra Pachauri">Rajendra Pachauri</a>, Professor <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Kammen" title="Daniel Kammen">Daniel Kammen</a>, Lars Josefsson, CEO of <a href="/wiki/Vattenfall" title="Vattenfall">Vattenfall</a>. The Climate Community also features regular updates on the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/climate-negotiations-updates.html"></a><a href="/wiki/COP15" class="mw-redirect" title="COP15">COP15</a> negotiation process and important <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/events.html">upcoming events</a>. The unique content on Community also includes selected and in-depth descriptions of innovative <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/solutions.html">business solutions</a>. A valuable feature on the Community is the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/climate-intelligence.html">Climate Intelligence Archive</a>, which selects and list key international policies, research reports, government agencies, NGOs, inspiring media sources, and upcoming climate events.</p>
<p>The Climate Community also hosts an online <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-involved.html">Virtual Summit</a>, which is an integral part of the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/world-business-summit.html">World Business Summit on Climate Change</a> to take place in May 2009. The Virtual Summit will facilitate knowledge sharing and collaboration, as well as be a testing ground for new ideas and partnerships through interactive web 2.0 tools.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Business_Summit_on_Climate_Change">World Business Summit on Climate Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: World Business Summit on Climate Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/world-business-summit.html"><b>World Business Summit on Climate Change</b></a> takes place six months prior to the pivotal UN climate change conference (<a href="/wiki/COP15" class="mw-redirect" title="COP15">COP15</a>) in Copenhagen, December 2009. The summit brings together business chief executives with the world's top scientists, economists, civil society, media leaders, government representatives and other leading thinkers to put forward recommendations for the next international framework on climate change to replace the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> after 2012. Among the prominent participants so far are <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">Al Gore</a>, Chairman of Generation Investment Management; <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a>, Prime Minister of Denmark; and Sir <a href="/wiki/Richard_Branson" title="Richard Branson">Richard Branson</a>, Founder and CEO of the Virgin Group.</p>
<p>At the summit, chief executives will discuss how business can help solve the <a href="/wiki/Climate_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate crisis">climate crisis</a> through innovative business models, new partnerships and the development of low carbon technologies. They will send a message to the negotiating governments on how to remove barriers and create incentives for implementation of new solutions in a post-Kyoto. The results of the World Business Summit on Climate Change will be presented to the Danish government, host of COP15,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and to world leaders negotiating the terms of the next international climate treaty.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thought_Leadership_Series">Thought Leadership Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Thought Leadership Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Copenhagen Climate Council <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/get-informed/thought-leadership-series.html"><b>Thought Leadership Series on Climate Change</b></a> is a publication that will be published in hardcopy and on the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/">Copenhagen Climate Council Home Page</a> in the lead up to the World Business Summit on Climate Change in May 2009. The Thought Leadership Series presents a collection of inspirational, concise and clearly argued pieces from some of the world's most renowned thinkers and business leaders on climate change. The objective of the pieces is to assist in enhancing the public and political awareness of the actions that could have a significant impact on global emissions growth and to disseminate the message that it is time to act hence a new UN climate treaty will be developed in December 2009. The Thought Leadership Series is aimed at elucidating and creating awareness of the key elements in the business and policy response to the climate problem. The rationale for the Thought Leadership Series is firstly to change the focus from stating we have a problem to communicating the solutions to the problem, and secondly to show the potential and opportunities inherent in tackling climate change.</p>
<p><i>The themes of the Thought Leadership series are:</i></p>
<ul>
<li>01 Tackling Emissions Growth: The Role of Markets and Government Regulation</li>
<li>02 Achieving low emissions energy systems in rapidly developing economies</li>
<li>03 Drawing down CO2 from the atmosphere</li>
<li>04 The role of city planning and buildings in tackling emissions growth</li>
<li>05 Achieving the capital investment required to tackle climate change</li>
<li>06 The CEO's survival guide to climate change</li>
<li>07 Adapting to the impacts of climate change</li>
<li>08 Role of Information and Communications Technology in Addressing Climate Change</li>
<li>09 Beyond a global agreement: Scenarios from the future</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_LIFE">Climate LIFE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Climate LIFE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/about-us/climate-life.html"><b>Climate LIFE</b></a> is a film, book and digital exhibition project initiated by the Copenhagen Climate Council. Climate LIFE is intended to be “a virtual tour of how communities across the globe can both fight climate change and adapt to a warming world”. The purpose of Climate LIFE is to encourage awareness of and appreciation for the human and commercial potential in a low carbon future. The Copenhagen Climate Council With hopes for <i>Climate LIFE</i> to act as a catalyst for a new public discourse on climate change.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="FILM_:_.E2.80.9CClimate_LIFE_-_the_5th_revolution.E2.80.9D">FILM&#160;: “Climate LIFE - the 5th revolution”</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: FILM : “Climate LIFE - the 5th revolution”">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i>Climate LIFE - the 5th revolution</i> is an “emotional and strong story” of a journey across the world in search of the solutions so urgently needed for <a href="/wiki/Avoiding_dangerous_climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Avoiding dangerous climate change">avoiding a world climate life gone a wreck</a>. It is produced in the realisation that we need a new climate agenda in order to achieve a transition to a sustainable society. The Copenhagen Climate Council has stated it is “necessary” to tell the story of climate change using a new positive language that can appeal to new audiences. Particularly, the Copenhagen Climate Council wishes to use “evocative and emotional storytelling” to get behind the real motivations, which has made pioneers, community leaders and others act on climate changes. Through compelling and evocative story-telling, the audiences will themselves feel the urgency of the quest and be inspired to take action. Climate LIFE will be a follow-up to Al Gore's 'An inconvenient truth'. Where Al Gore opened the world's eyes to the massiveness of climate change, Climate LIFE intends to tell the new convenient truth of climate change - that the knowledge and solutions we know today give us the opportunity to create communities that enhance quality of life; that it is possible to build a greener, safer and more sustainable Earth. <i>Climate Life - the 5th revolution</i> aims to show that the precondition for the success is already present. A short feature will be launched at the World Business Summit on Climate Change and subsequently it will be shown at events, on the web, and will also be distributed to TV broadcasters across the world. The film is produced in collaboration with Koncern Film and TV.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_LIFE_Digital_exhibition">The LIFE Digital exhibition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The LIFE Digital exhibition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i>LIFE digital Exhibition</i> is intended to demonstrate what makes Climate LIFE possible. When launched on the web, it will explore the delivery model necessary to achieve the vision of Climate LIFE. Looking at the political, economic and cultural systems as well as the technological and biological process that will underpin low carbon living in the future, the exhibition will present a variety of practical solutions and their implications, highlighting the state of the art in movement, energy production and efficient consumption, water and waste management etc. The exhibition aims to use the latest social software advances and interactive tools to illustrate the challenges, how they affect people, and the possibilities for getting involved.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unlocking_the_Climate_Code:_Innovation_in_Climate_and_Energy">Unlocking the Climate Code: Innovation in Climate and Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Unlocking the Climate Code: Innovation in Climate and Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 19, 2008, Copenhagen Climate Council and <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Information_Technology_Research_in_the_Interest_of_Society" title="Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society">Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society</a> (CITRIS) co-hosted an energy conference named <b>Unlocking the Climate Code: Innovation in Climate and Energy</b>. The aim of the conference was to identify the critical research and development achievements necessary for a successful transition to a low carbon economy. Conference participants will present and debate relevant policy and business models that can support technology innovation in carbon emissions reduction. In an effort to create models of the relationships in business, policy, and technology to help guide innovative and rational decision making at the 2009 UN Summit, a suite of tools was developed, better known as the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.citris-uc.org/publications/articles/navigating_climate_change_tools_un_summit_and_beyond">Climate Navigator</a>. According to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.citris-uc.org/people/researchers/gary_baldwin">Gary Baldwin, Director of Special Projects at CITRIS</a>, the Climate Navigator will have several interrelated parts and functions. It will serve as an Internet-based community forum for researchers, policy makers, and business leaders, allowing politicians and others to direct questions to experts or open on-line discussions about specific proposals. It will also be a digital library, organizing the growing base of knowledge about how business models and policy can influence technology. In addition, the Navigator will employ new technology itself, including computer modeling applications developed by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/about-us/councillors/daniel-m-kammen.html">Dan Kammen's</a> lab at <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">Berkeley</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_Requirements_of_a_Post-2012_Climate_Treaty">Business Requirements of a Post-2012 Climate Treaty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Business Requirements of a Post-2012 Climate Treaty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 8, 2008, the Copenhagen Climate Council hosted an official side event <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> <a href="/wiki/COP14" class="mw-redirect" title="COP14">COP14</a> Summit on <a href="/wiki/Climate_Change" class="mw-redirect" title="Climate Change">Climate Change</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Poznań">Poznań</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> from December 1–10, 2008. The theme was <b>Business Requirements to a Post-2012 Climate Treaty</b>. At the event, Council representatives from business and science presented their key principles for a new treaty. The thoughts presented at the event will feed into the development of the final recommendations delivered by international business leaders at the <i>World Business Summit on Climate Change</i>, to be held in Copenhagen in May, 2009.</p>
<p>The speakers delivered their views on what they would toast to in Copenhagen. They included: Copenhagen Climate Council Chairman <b>Tim Flannery</b>; <b>Robert Purves</b> from <a href="/wiki/World_Wildlife_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wildlife Fund">World Wildlife Fund</a> International; <b>Jerry Stokes</b>, president of Suntech Europe; <b>Dr. Zhengrong Shi</b>, Founder and CEO of Suntech; <b>Steve Harper</b> of <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>; <b>Susanne Stormer</b> from <a href="/wiki/Novo_Nordisk" title="Novo Nordisk">Novo Nordisk</a>; <b>Michael Zarin</b> of Vestas; and <b>Thomas Becker</b>, the lead climate negotiator for the Danish government that will host the <a href="/wiki/UN" class="mw-redirect" title="UN">UN</a> <a href="/wiki/COP15" class="mw-redirect" title="COP15">COP15</a> climate summit in December, 2009. The session was moderated by <b>Nick Rowley</b>, strategic director at Copenhagen Climate Council.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_Roundtable_in_Beijing">Business Roundtable in Beijing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Copenhagen_Climate_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Business Roundtable in Beijing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 11, 2008, the Copenhagen Climate Council hosted a roundtable meeting<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with some of the most prominent business leaders in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and the Danish Minister for Climate and Energy <a href="/wiki/Connie_Hedegaard" title="Connie Hedegaard">Connie Hedegaard</a>. According to the Copenhagen Climate Council, conclusion of the summit was clear: “Climate change is becoming an important issue for Chinese CEOs, and opportunities in energy-efficient products and renewable energy are a driver for change.”</p>
<p>"Chinese business leaders recognize that sustainable development is a corporate responsibility, and that the need for creating economic growth in China should meet the needs of sound environment protection. I encourage all industries to respond and to collaborate – hand in hand – on tackling the challenge. I want to make sure that our children can live on a beautiful planet with blue sky and clean air. We are dedicated to this," said <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.copenhagenclimatecouncil.com/about-us/councillors/li-xiaolin.html">Li Xiaolin</a>, chairwoman and CEO of China Power International, one of Chinas five-biggest energy suppliers.</p>
