<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_and_Social_Council" title="United Nations Economic and Social Council">United Nations Economic and Social Council</a> has given a General Comment<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> that defines "<b>decent work</b>" and requires satisfaction of Article 7 of the <a href="/wiki/International_Covenant_on_Economic,_Social_and_Cultural_Rights" title="International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights">International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights</a>: decent work is <a href="/wiki/Employment" title="Employment">employment</a> that "respects the fundamental rights of the human person as well as the rights of workers in terms of conditions of work safety and remuneration. ... respect for the physical and mental integrity of the worker in the exercise of his/her employment."</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a> (ILO), decent work involves opportunities for work that are productive and deliver a fair income, security in the workplace and social protection for families, better prospects for personal development and <a href="/wiki/Social_integration" title="Social integration">social integration</a>, freedom for people to express their concerns, organize and participate in the decisions that affect their lives and <a href="/wiki/Equality_of_opportunity" class="mw-redirect" title="Equality of opportunity">equality of opportunity</a> and treatment for all women and men.</p>
<p>The ILO is developing an agenda for the community of work, represented by its tripartite constituents, to mobilize their considerable resources to create those opportunities and to help <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">reduce and eradicate poverty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ilo_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ilo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ILO Decent Work Agenda<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is the balanced and integrated programmatic approach to pursue the objectives of full and productive employment and decent work for all at global, regional, national, sectoral and local levels. It has four pillars: standards and rights at work,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> employment creation and enterprise development,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> social protection<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and social dialogue.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a> also proclaims decent work for sustainable <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Challenges">Challenges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Decent_work&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Challenges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although few disagree with the Decent Work Agenda in principle,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> actually achieving Decent Work poses challenges and controversies. The Decent Work Agenda requires national and international actors to commit to the objective of creating quality jobs globally and to pursue cooperative solutions to this challenge. However, governments struggle to convince their publics that development and job creation abroad is imperative to prosperity and employment at home. Some governments also face the temptation to close <a href="/wiki/Markets" class="mw-redirect" title="Markets">markets</a> and lower labor standards to remain competitive in a <a href="/wiki/World_economy" title="World economy">world economy</a> that is blamed for depressing wages and working conditions.</p>
<p>Various actors can affect the provision of Decent Work, although existing conditions and incentives do not always lend themselves to advancing the Decent Work Agenda. To illustrate:</p>
<ul>
<li>National governments create Decent Work through economic and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_policy" title="Industrial policy">industrial policies</a>. However, the forces of globalization – such as downward pressures on wages and reduced macroeconomic policy flexibility – have diminished the ability of national governments to achieve this goal on their own.</li>
<li>Businesses create jobs from the local to international levels, and those operating across borders can affect international wages and working conditions. Multinational enterprises typically locate operations in countries where wages are at their lowest and so called "worker's rights" are less prominent. This is antithetical to the Decent Work Agenda, although it does contribute to economic development.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trade_unions" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade unions">Trade unions</a> assist employees in advocating for elements of Decent Work, from a so-called "living wage" to health insurance to workplace safety standards. Trade unions face the challenge of meeting their members’ immediate needs at home while supporting job creation and "workers’ rights" around the globe.</li>
<li>International financial institutions provide loans or other assistance to national governments, and require loan recipients to implement certain policy measures. Existing programs generally exclude employment targets and have even been known to reduce job creation in the short term, as jobs which exist only through government <a href="/wiki/Market_distortion" title="Market distortion">market distortions</a> are replaced with economically viable employment.</li>
<li>Trade negotiators can forward the Decent Work Agenda globally by including labor standards in trade agreements, while legislators (among others) can support their implementation. However, many countries view the campaign for labor standards as an effort by other countries to make their own industries more competitive.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Day_for_Decent_Work">World Day for Decent Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Decent_work&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Day for Decent Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>October 7, is the World Day for Decent Work. During that day trade unions, union federations and other workers associations develop their actions to promote the idea of Decent Work. Actions vary from street demonstrations to music events or conferences held in many countries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decent_Work.2C_Decent_Life_Campaign">Decent Work, Decent Life Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Decent_work&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Decent Work, Decent Life Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Five organizations, <a href="/wiki/Solidar" title="Solidar">Solidar</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITUC" class="mw-redirect" title="ITUC">ITUC</a>, <a href="/wiki/ETUC" class="mw-redirect" title="ETUC">ETUC</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Social_Alert_International&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Social Alert International (page does not exist)">Social Alert International</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Progressive_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Global Progressive Forum (page does not exist)">Global Progressive Forum</a>, launched the Decent Work, Decent Life campaign at the <a href="/wiki/World_Social_Forum" title="World Social Forum">World Social Forum</a> in Nairobi in January 2007, and has since then worked in an alliance to promote decent work for decent life as solution to poverty. The idea to run a Campaign on Decent Work was conceived at the World Social Forum, 2005, in Porto Alegre. The Campaign targets young people, trade union activists, NGOs and decision makers in developed and developing countries.</p>
<p>The Campaign’s objectives focus on building awareness of Decent work and on promoting Decent work as the only sustainable way out of <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_cohesion" class="mw-redirect" title="Social cohesion">social cohesion</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Decent_work&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2007, decision makers from European governments and institutions signed the Call to Action of the Decent Work, Decent Life Campaign<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> adding up to the recognition of the Decent Work Agenda. "There is also a growing interest on the part of the EU and international civil society in decent work, as illustrated for instance by: the launch of the Decent Work/Decent Life [Campaign]…".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Campaign’s Call to Action focuses on seven issues, namely; decent work, <a href="/wiki/Workers%E2%80%99_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Workers’ rights">workers’ rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_protection" title="Social protection">social protection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fair_trade" title="Fair trade">fair trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_financial_institutions" title="International financial institutions">international financial institutions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Development_aid" title="Development aid">development aid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">migration</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decent_Work.2C_Decent_Life_for_Women_Campaign">Decent Work, Decent Life for Women Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Decent_work&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Decent Work, Decent Life for Women Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Decent Work, Decent Life for Women Campaign is a two years campaign launched on <a href="/wiki/International_Women%E2%80%99s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="International Women’s Day">International Women’s Day</a> 2008 (March 8) by the <a href="/wiki/International_Trade_Union_Confederation" title="International Trade Union Confederation">International Trade Union Confederation</a> (ITUC) and the <a href="/wiki/Global_union_federation" title="Global union federation">Global union federations</a> (GUF). The campaign aims to advocate decent work for women and gender equality in labour policies and agreements and to seek gender equality in trade union structures, policies and activities. The second objective aims at increasing number of women members in trade unions and women in elected positions.</p>
<p>The Campaign’s necessity stems from multiple forms of discrimination in both policy and practice on a daily basis women are facing such as the gender pay gap,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> the lack of maternity protection and the higher unemployment rates among women.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the moment 81 national centers in 56 countries participate with various events in this Campaign.</p>
