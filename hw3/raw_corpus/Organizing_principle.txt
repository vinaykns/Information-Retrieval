			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Organizing principle</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p>An <b>organizing principle</b> is a core assumption from which everything else by proximity can derive a classification or a value. It is like a central reference point that allows all other objects to be located, often used in a <a href="/wiki/Conceptual_framework" title="Conceptual framework">conceptual framework</a>. Having an organizing principle might help one simplify and get a handle on a particularly complicated domain or phenomenon. On the other hand, it might create a deceptive prism that colors one's judgment.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organizing_principle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In a <a href="/wiki/The_Brookings_Institution" class="mw-redirect" title="The Brookings Institution">Brookings Institution</a> article, <a href="/wiki/James_Steinberg" title="James Steinberg">James Steinberg</a> describes how <a href="/wiki/Counter_terrorism" class="mw-redirect" title="Counter terrorism">Counter terrorism</a> has become the organizing principle of U.S. national security.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The idea of the <a href="/wiki/Solar_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Solar system">solar system</a> is based on the organizing principle that the sun is located at a central point, and all planets rotate around it.</li>
<li>Most modern <a href="/wiki/Cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Cities">cities</a> are based on the organizing principle of the <a href="/wiki/Grid_plan" title="Grid plan">Grid plan</a> in order to better manage transportation and <a href="/wiki/Address_(geography)" title="Address (geography)">addressing</a>.</li>
<li>Most religions (as opposed to <a href="/wiki/Cult" title="Cult">cults</a>) can be described by social scientists as built around an organizing principle (for example, the divinity of Christ) that allows for the sustainable or improvable <a href="/wiki/Recursion" title="Recursion">recursion</a> of a unique population.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Organizations can be constructed around a set of organizing principles, such as concepts, priorities, or goals. For example, an organization may intend to be innovative, international, quality and agile.</li>
<li>The central organising principle of the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Government" title="Welsh Government">Welsh Government</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">Sustainability</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sociology_of_knowledge#Legitimation_Code_Theory" title="Sociology of knowledge">Legitimation Code Theory</a> is an explanatory framework in the <a href="/wiki/Sociology_of_knowledge" title="Sociology of knowledge">sociology of knowledge</a> and education that seeks to understand different social fields of practices in terms of their operating principles, which determines the basis of success and failure. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
