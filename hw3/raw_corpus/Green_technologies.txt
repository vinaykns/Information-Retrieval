<p><b>Environmental technology</b> (<i>envirotech</i>), green technology (<i>greentech</i>) or clean technology (<i>cleantech</i>) is the application of one or more of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_chemistry" title="Green chemistry">green chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_monitoring" title="Environmental monitoring">environmental monitoring</a> and electronic devices to monitor, model and conserve the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a> and resources, and to curb the negative impacts of human involvement. The term is also used to describe sustainable energy generation technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">photovoltaics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioreactors" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioreactors">bioreactors</a>, etc. <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">Sustainable development</a> is the core of <i>environmental technologies</i>. The term <i>environmental technologies</i> is also used to describe a class of electronic devices that can promote sustainable management of resources.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">Renewable energy</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> that can be replenished easily. For years we have been using sources such as <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">sun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, etc. for means for producing energy. Energy that can be produced by natural objects like wood, sun, wind, etc. is considered to be renewable.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_purification">Water purification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Water purification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">Water purification</a>: The whole idea/concept of having dirt/germ/pollution free water flowing throughout the environment. Many other phenomena lead from this concept of purification of water. Water pollution is the main enemy of this concept, and various campaigns and activists have been organized around the world to help purify water.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_purification">Air purification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Air purification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Air purification: Basic and common green plants can be grown indoors to keep air fresh because all plants remove CO<sub>2</sub> and convert it into <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. The best examples are: <i><a href="/wiki/Dypsis_lutescens" title="Dypsis lutescens">Dypsis lutescens</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sansevieria_trifasciata" title="Sansevieria trifasciata">Sansevieria trifasciata</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Epipremnum_aureum" title="Epipremnum aureum">Epipremnum aureum</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sewage_treatment">Sewage treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sewage treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sewage_treatment" title="Sewage treatment">Sewage treatment</a> is conceptually similar to water purification. Sewage treatments are very important as they purify water per levels of its pollution. The most polluted water is not used for anything, and the least polluted water is supplied to places where water is used affluently. It may lead to various other concepts of environmental protection, sustainability etc.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_remediation">Environmental remediation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Environmental remediation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Environmental_remediation" title="Environmental remediation">Environmental remediation</a> is the removal of pollutants or contaminants for the general protection of the environment. This is accomplished by various chemical, biological, and bulk movements. (encyclopedia of medical concepts)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Solid_waste_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Solid waste management">Solid waste management</a> is the purification, consumption, reuse, disposal and treatment of <a href="/wiki/Solid_waste" class="mw-redirect" title="Solid waste">solid waste</a> that is undertaken by the government or the ruling bodies of a city/town.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="eGain_forecasting">eGain forecasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: eGain forecasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Egain forecasting is a method using forecasting technology to predict the future <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a>'s impact on a building.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> By adjusting the heat based on the weather forecast, the system eliminates redundant use of heat, thus reducing the energy consumption and the emission of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_conservation">Energy conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Energy conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">Energy conservation</a> is the utilization of devices that require smaller amounts of energy in order to reduce the consumption of electricity. Reducing the use of electricity causes less fossil fuels to be burned to provide that electricity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_and_clean_power">Alternative and clean power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Alternative and clean power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

The <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Roadster" title="Tesla Roadster">Tesla Roadster</a> was the first all-electric sports car for sale and in serial production. It can be plugged into conventional outlets and can be charged fully or partially on <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geothermal" class="mw-redirect" title="Geothermal">geothermal</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a>.</div>

<p>Principles:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Green_syndicalism" title="Green syndicalism">Green syndicalism</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">Sustainability</a></li>

<p>Scientists continue to search for <a href="/wiki/Clean_energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean energy">clean energy</a> alternatives to our current power production methods. Some technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_digestion" title="Anaerobic digestion">anaerobic digestion</a> produce renewable energy from <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">waste</a> materials. The global reduction of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" class="mw-redirect" title="Greenhouse gases">greenhouse gases</a> is dependent on the adoption of energy conservation technologies at industrial level as well as this clean energy generation. That includes using unleaded <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gasoline</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_fuel" title="Alternative fuel">alternative fuel</a> vehicles, including <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_hybrid" title="Plug-in hybrid">plug-in hybrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_electric_vehicle" title="Hybrid electric vehicle">hybrid electric</a> vehicles.</p>
<p>Since industrial use of energy accounts for 51% of worldwide energy usage<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> improving energy efficiency in this field is a top priority for environmental technology companies around the globe. Advanced energy efficient <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">electric motor</a> (and <a href="/wiki/Electric_generator" title="Electric generator">electric generator</a>) technology that are cost effective to encourage their application, such as the <a href="/wiki/Brushless_wound-rotor_doubly_fed_electric_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Brushless wound-rotor doubly fed electric machine">brushless wound-rotor doubly fed electric machine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy_saving_module" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy saving module">energy saving module</a>, can reduce the amount of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>) and <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide" title="Sulfur dioxide">sulfur dioxide</a> (SO<sub>2</sub>) that would otherwise be introduced to the atmosphere, if electricity is generated using fossil fuels. <a href="/wiki/Greasestock" title="Greasestock">Greasestock</a> is an event held yearly in <a href="/wiki/Yorktown_Heights,_New_York" title="Yorktown Heights, New York">Yorktown Heights</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="New York (state)">New York</a> which is one of the largest showcases of environmental technology in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Courses aimed at developing graduates with specific skills in environmental systems or environmental technology are becoming more common and fall into three broads classes:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Environmental_Engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Engineering">Environmental Engineering or Environmental Systems</a></i> courses oriented towards a civil engineering approach in which structures and the landscape are constructed to blend with or protect the environment;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Environmental_chemistry" title="Environmental chemistry">Environmental chemistry</a></i>, <i>sustainable chemistry</i> or <i>environmental chemical engineering</i> courses oriented towards understanding the effects (good and bad) of chemicals in the environment. Such awards can focus on mining processes, pollutants and commonly also cover biochemical processes;</li>
<li><i>Environmental technology</i> courses oriented towards producing electronic, electrical or electrotechnology graduates capable of developing devices and artefacts able to monitor, measure, model and control environmental impact, including monitoring and managing energy generation from renewable sources, and developing novel energy generation technologies.</li>
</ul>
