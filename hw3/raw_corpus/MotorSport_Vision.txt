<p><b>MotorSport Vision</b> (MSV) is a motorsport organisations and an operator of UK motor racing venues. MSV has a portfolio ranging from major two and four wheel championships to organising the <a href="/wiki/PalmerSport" title="PalmerSport">PalmerSport</a> corporate driving event.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MotorSport Vision was founded by John Britten, Sir <a href="/wiki/Peter_Ogden" title="Peter Ogden">Peter Ogden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a>.</p>
<p>MSV purchased the <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park" title="Oulton Park">Oulton Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snetterton_Motor_Racing_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Snetterton Motor Racing Circuit">Snetterton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadwell_Park" title="Cadwell Park">Cadwell Park</a> motor racing circuits in 2004. Brands Hatch, the former venue for the British Grand Prix, has begun hosting international car racing again. Events either joining or back on its calendar include the DTM German touring car championship, the FIA World Touring Car Championship and the A1GP World Cup of Motorsport.</p>
<p>In 2006, MSV formed <a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision_Racing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MotorSport Vision Racing (page does not exist)">MotorSport Vision Racing</a> (MSVR), providing a complete car and bike racing organisation capability to the MotorSport Vision group. MSVR organises over 30 events annually across 10 UK circuits. MotorSport Vision's trackday division, <a href="/wiki/Club_MSV" class="mw-redirect" title="Club MSV">Club MSV</a>, has grown to become one of the UK's biggest trackday companies in the past four years and organises novice events up to advanced driver training days. Alongside <a href="/wiki/PalmerSport" title="PalmerSport">PalmerSport</a>, MSV also run driving experiences at Brands Hatch and Oulton Park.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>MSV also ran the <a href="/wiki/Formula_Palmer_Audi" title="Formula Palmer Audi">Formula Palmer Audi</a> championship from 1998 to 2010 which advanced the careers of hundreds of young drivers, nine of whom have made it all the way to <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>. In 2008 MSV also acquired the rights to the <a href="/wiki/British_Superbike_Championship" title="British Superbike Championship">British Superbike Championship</a>.</p>
<p>In September 2008 MotorSport Vision won an <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> tender to supply cars and operate the <a href="/wiki/FIA_Formula_Two_Championship_(2009%E2%80%932012)" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA Formula Two Championship (2009–2012)">FIA Formula Two Championship</a>, a new international single seater series derived from an FIA initiative to provide young drivers with the opportunity to compete in top-level racing on a competitive budget. The Formula Two cars, like Formula Palmer Audi, were assembled and prepared in a dedicated facility at <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Autodrome" title="Bedford Autodrome">Bedford Autodrome</a>, also home to PalmerSport and FPA. This category was discontinued after 2010.</p>
<p>One of its founders, John Britten, died on 25 November 2009, after a long period of illness.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuits">Circuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Circuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands_Hatch"><a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Brands Hatch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1986, John Foulston bought Brands Hatch, Oulton Park and Snetterton circuits from Grovewood Securities and established the Brands Hatch Leisure (BHL) company. The following year, the company acquired Cadwell Park before Foulston died testing a McLaren CanAm car at Silverstone. Ownership of BHL passed to his wife Mary, but the company was run by John Webb until 1990, when daughter Nicola Foulston took over the running. In that time, Brands Hatch hosted higher-profile series such as Formula 3000 and Superbike World Championship racing. 1988 saw further changes to the circuit layout, with a chicane added at Dingle Dell Corner, while Westfield Bend and Graham Hill Bend were tightened. New pits and a corporate entertainment facility were added in the late-1990s.</p>
<p>In 1999, Foulston announced that Brands Hatch had acquired the rights to the <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> from 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Whilst discussions were ongoing with regards to planning permission to bring the circuit up to F1 requirements, Foulston sold BHL to Octagon Motorsports (a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Interpublic" class="mw-redirect" title="Interpublic">Interpublic</a>) for £120&#160;m.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Octagon, however, failed to obtain the necessary planning permission and instead decided to lease <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Silverstone circuit">Silverstone</a> in order to host the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, high-profile single-seater racing did return to Brands Hatch in 2003, when a round of the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">CART</a> series was held at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Despite attracting around 40,000 spectators<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the race was not retained for subsequent seasons.</p>
<p>With financial pressures stemming from running the British Grand Prix,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Octagon sold off the group of four circuits,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> including Brands Hatch, to the MotorSport Vision group headed by ex-F1 driver <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oulton_Park"><a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park" title="Oulton Park">Oulton Park</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Oulton Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After many years of decay, Oulton was given new life when it was bought by MotorSport Vision. MSV redeveloped the circuit.</p>
<p>In 2007 Oulton park was voted the best UK circuit by British Superbike fans,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> whilst a BTCC meeting on Sunday 27 July 2008 saw a crowd of 35,000 attend - a record not only for the circuit but the highest crowd at any venue of the BTCC for a decade.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Snetterton"><a href="/wiki/Snetterton_Motor_Racing_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Snetterton Motor Racing Circuit">Snetterton</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Snetterton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Snetterton has undergone perhaps the most radical transformation of all the MotorSport Vision circuits since being taken over in 2004.</p>
<p>New spectator banking has been added to the track to improve circuit views, whilst the restaurant and toilet facilities have been updated or replaced. A new scrutineering bay has also been added.</p>
<p>Snetterton is regarded as being one of the fastest tracks in the UK thanks to its combination of several long straights and relatively few corners. The lap record currently stands at 56.095 seconds, or an average speed of 125.27&#160;mph.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cadwell_Park"><a href="/wiki/Cadwell_Park" title="Cadwell Park">Cadwell Park</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cadwell Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1953 the track was lengthened to 1.3 miles (2.1&#160;km), upon the invitation of the 500 cc motorcycle-engined Formula 3 to race in a traditional bike meeting. Around 30,000 spectators attended that particular race. The original track grew to the current 2.25 miles (3.62&#160;km) layout in 1962 and hosted the British F3 series the next May. Some of the bends are named after family members e.g. Mansfield, Charlie and Chris. Cadwell is now considered too narrow for high level car races, although club motorsport associations such as the BARC and 750MC still hold meetings. The layout is ideal for bikes however, with the British Superbike Championship round being the most popular event on the championship's calendar. Several tests and feature clips for the motoring program Fifth Gear have been filmed here.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bedford_Autodrome"><a href="/wiki/Bedford_Autodrome" title="Bedford Autodrome">Bedford Autodrome</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bedford Autodrome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The track, in Thurleigh near Bedford is owned by MotorSport Vision. No official motorsport events are held at the circuit but it is a popular public track day circuit, it is also the home of the Jaguar SlikCat which is used for corporate events and for ride alongs during the track days. It is served by <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Aerodrome" title="Bedford Aerodrome">Bedford Aerodrome</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Donington_Park"><a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Donington Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2017, the Donington circuit business was taken over by MotorSport Vision, with the Donington Park Estate on a 38 year lease, until 2038.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Laon">Laon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Laon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>MSV will build a circuit at the former <a href="/wiki/Laon-Couvron_Air_Base" class="mw-redirect" title="Laon-Couvron Air Base">Laon-Couvron Air Base</a> in France, 120&#160;km northeast of Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Series">Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012 MotorSport Vision will host several prestigious international events such as the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">DTM</a> German touring cars and the revived <a href="/wiki/FIA_Formula_Two_Championship_(2009%E2%80%932012)" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA Formula Two Championship (2009–2012)">FIA Formula Two Championship</a>.</p>
<p>Alongside these, MotorSport Vision's circuits will host the <a href="/wiki/British_Superbike_Championship" title="British Superbike Championship">British Superbike Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Touring_Car_Championship" title="British Touring Car Championship">British Touring Car Championship</a>, the <a href="/wiki/British_Formula_Three_Championship" title="British Formula Three Championship">British F3</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_GT_Championship" title="British GT Championship">GT</a> championships, and a range of historic events including the <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park_International_Gold_Cup" title="Oulton Park International Gold Cup">Oulton Park Gold Cup</a>.</p>
<p>There will also be numerous bike and car club meetings throughout the year, including the Britcar 'Into The Night' race at Brands Hatch. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Superbikes"><a href="/wiki/British_Superbike" class="mw-redirect" title="British Superbike">British Superbikes</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: British Superbikes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 MotorSport Vision took over the responsibility for the organisation and promotion of the British Superbike Championship from Dorna.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2008_British_Superbike_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 British Superbike season">2008 season</a> was MSV's first in charge, and saw Shane Byrne take the title. The championship was screened live by <a href="/wiki/British_Eurosport" class="mw-redirect" title="British Eurosport">British Eurosport</a> and highlights were shown later on <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_Palmer_Audi"><a href="/wiki/Formula_Palmer_Audi" title="Formula Palmer Audi">Formula Palmer Audi</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Formula Palmer Audi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Formula Palmer Audi, commonly known as FPA, was founded in 1998 by Jonathan Palmer. The ethos of FPA is to keep costs at a minimum whilst at all times promoting competitive and professional racing. The series is known for producing ex-F1 driver and IndyCar race-winner Justin Wilson and helping the career of triple World Touring Car Champion Andy Priaulx.</p>
<p>In order to keep a level playing field as much as possible, all the Formula Palmer Audi cars are run by a specialist team of FPA mechanics, rather than having separate teams. This concept of a large group of deliberately equalised cars has since been adopted by A1GP.</p>
<p>FPA announced a scholarship prize with the BRDC Stars of Tomorrow KF2 karting championship,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and with the newly revived FIA Formula Two Championship,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> positioning the series as a fantastic intermediary step between karts and top-level single seaters.</p>
<p>FPA folded after the 2010 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FIA_Formula_Two_Championship"><a href="/wiki/FIA_Formula_Two_Championship_(2009%E2%80%932012)" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA Formula Two Championship (2009–2012)">FIA Formula Two Championship</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: FIA Formula Two Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> Formula Two Championship was the revival of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> series that was highly successful from 1967 to 1985. It saw 24 drivers competing in identical MotorSport Vision-run cars with a 400&#160;bhp <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a> engine over 16 rounds.</p>
<p>The FIA's overall concern was that the cost of competing in motor racing at a level to progress directly to Formula One today is so high that it prevents many talented drivers from having the opportunity to participate.</p>
<p>In September 2008 the FIA tender to supply the cars and operate the FIA Formula Two Championship was awarded to MotorSport Vision. Compared to rival series, Formula Two will cost significantly less per season whilst allowing drivers to prove their skill and develop their racecraft in identical vehicles designed by a six-man team from <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">WilliamsF1</a>, led by Director of Engineering <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a>. The series was marred by the tragic death of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Surtees" title="Henry Surtees">Henry Surtees</a>, who died in an accident during a race at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>.</p>
<p>The series folded in 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MotorSport_Vision_Formula_Three_Cup"><a href="/wiki/MotorSport_Vision_Formula_Three_Cup" title="MotorSport Vision Formula Three Cup">MotorSport Vision Formula Three Cup</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: MotorSport Vision Formula Three Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>F3 Cup</b> is a national motor racing series that takes place primarily in the United Kingdom with a small number of events in mainland Europe. It is a junior-level feeder formula that uses small single seater Formula Three built after 1980 and before 2007. The championship comprises nine rounds, each with two races. Each race weekend will comprise one 25-minute qualifying session and two 20-minute races.</p>
<p>The 2011 season saw rounds held in England at Snetterton 300 (Two rounds), Donington Park, Brands Hatch Indy, Brands Hatch GP and Oulton Park International. There is one round held in Wales at Anglesey Circuit International and one round in Belgium at Spa Francorchamps. A test drive of a Formula 2 was awarded to the most meritorious driver of the year, which was Aaron Steele.</p>
<p>In 2012, F3 Cup has been awarded championship status, so the F2 prize will be awarded to the driver of any class that has the most points. There is also a team championship for MSA Entrants fielding two or more cars.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BRDC_British_Formula_4_Championship"><a href="/wiki/BRDC_British_Formula_4_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="BRDC British Formula 4 Championship">BRDC British Formula 4 Championship</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: BRDC British Formula 4 Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally operated by the 750 Motor Club, a deal was reached in September 2012 to pass the name onto MotorSport Vision (MSV) to create the new BRDC Formula 4 Championship.</p>
<p>The championship featured a mix of professional motor racing teams and privately funded drivers using identical 2.0-litre cars built by Ralph Firman’s RFR company.</p>
<p>The series was rebranded as the BRDC British Formula 3 Championship to reflect the pace of the new cars in use from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_British_Formula_3_Championship_season&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2016 British Formula 3 Championship season (page does not exist)">2016 British Formula 3 Championship season</a> and beyond.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BRDC_British_Formula_3_Championship"><a href="/wiki/British_Formula_Three_Championship" title="British Formula Three Championship">BRDC British Formula 3 Championship</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MotorSport_Vision&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: BRDC British Formula 3 Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The British Formula Three Championship, officially named the BRDC British Formula 3 Championship, is a national motor racing series that takes place in the United Kingdom, and previously with a small number of events held in mainland Europe.</p>
<p>The series uses an elevated pace Formula 4 chassis as opposed to a current FIA specification Formula 3 car. Despite this, the car is closer to the pace of a pre-2014 F3 car. The series will begin in March 2016.</p>
