<p><b>Circular flow land use management</b>, or <i>CircUse</i>, is a name for a particular process in which neglected land in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> is put to better uses. <i>CircUse</i> as a concept aims to be integrated with existing structures and uses, and is put into practice on a broad scale. The concept also looks to reduce the <a href="/wiki/Land_consumption" title="Land consumption">consumption of un-built land</a> through prioritizing inner development over outer development. In Germany the approach of circular flow land use management has been developed and tested by the German Institute for Urban Affairs (Deutsches Institut f√ºr Urbanistik) on behalf of the German Federal Ministry of Transport, Building and Urban Affairs (BMVBS) and German Federal Office for Building and Regional Planning (BBR).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_of_Land_Use">Concept of Land Use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Circular_flow_land_use_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept of Land Use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The concept of circular flow land use management can be described with the slogan ‚Äúreduce - recycle ‚Äì avoid‚Äù. To create <a href="/wiki/Sustainable" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">land uses</a>, actions have to be supported to find new innovative ways to ‚Äúreduce‚Äù the consumption of land by new development ‚Äúrecycle‚Äù or put back into use abandoned and derelict sites, and ‚Äúavoid‚Äù future land use decisions that are not sustainable. The concept puts a high value upon the development of inner districts through infill measures.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This approach takes into consideration issues related to <a href="/wiki/Urban_sprawl" title="Urban sprawl">urban sprawl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brownfield_land" title="Brownfield land">brownfield land</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Land_use_planning" class="mw-redirect" title="Land use planning">land use planning</a>. The concept was developed by the German Institute for Urban Affairs (Difu) and the Federal Office for Building and Regional Planning (BBR) between 2003 and 2007 during the ExWoSt research field ‚Äúcircular land use management in cities and urban regions‚Äù (Kreislaufwirtschaft in der st√§dtischen/stadtregionalen Fl√§chennuntzung -. Fl√§che im Kreis)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practical_Application">Practical Application</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Circular_flow_land_use_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practical Application">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To incorporate the circular flow land use management concept into the practice of city planning in Central Europe, the project ‚ÄúCircular Flow Land Use Management (CircUse)‚Äù was initiated by the European Union Central Europe organization <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> as well as co-financed by the <a href="/wiki/European_Regional_Development_Fund" title="European Regional Development Fund">European Regional Development Fund</a>. The CircUse project involves 12 partner organizations and 3 associated organizations in the six countries of Austria, Czech Republic, Germany, Italy, Poland and Slovakia. The lead organization is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Institute_for_the_Ecology_of_Urban_Areas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Institute for the Ecology of Urban Areas (page does not exist)">Institute for the Ecology of Urban Areas</a> (IETU), located in <a href="/wiki/Katowice" title="Katowice">Katowice</a>, Poland.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The main core outputs of the CircUse project will be:</p>
<ul>
<li>A Circular Flow Land Use Management Strategy</li>
<li>Position paper on existing and new instruments</li>
<li>Separate training materials for professionals and school children</li>
<li>Management structures (one newly formed and another modified from its previous form) that incorporate the CircUse principles</li>
<li>Developed action plans for six pilot regions</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Land_management" title="Land management">land management</a> data tool available as a software package</li>
<li>A compendium summarizing the entire project</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeframe">Timeframe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Circular_flow_land_use_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Timeframe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The CircUse project was initiated in March 2010 and is scheduled to run until the end of February 2013.</p>
