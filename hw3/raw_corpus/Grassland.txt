<p><b>Grasslands</b> are areas where the <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a> is dominated by <a href="/wiki/Grass" class="mw-redirect" title="Grass">grasses</a> (<a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">Poaceae</a>), however sedge (<a href="/wiki/Cyperaceae" title="Cyperaceae">Cyperaceae</a>) and rush (<a href="/wiki/Juncaceae" title="Juncaceae">Juncaceae</a>) families can also be found. Grasslands occur naturally on all continents except <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. Grasslands are found in most <a href="/wiki/Ecoregion" title="Ecoregion">ecoregions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>. For example, there are five <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_ecoregion" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrestrial ecoregion">terrestrial ecoregion</a> classifications (subdivisions) of the <a href="/wiki/Temperate_grasslands,_savannas,_and_shrublands" title="Temperate grasslands, savannas, and shrublands">temperate grasslands, savannas, and shrublands</a> <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>), which is one of eight <a href="/wiki/Terrestrial_ecozone" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrestrial ecozone">terrestrial ecozones</a> of the Earth's surface.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation">Vegetation</span></h2>
<p>Grassland vegetation can vary in height from very short, as in <a href="/wiki/Calcareous_grassland" title="Calcareous grassland">chalk grassland</a>, to quite tall, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Tallgrass_prairie" title="Tallgrass prairie">tallgrass prairie</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> grasslands and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Konza1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Konza1.jpg/220px-Konza1.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Konza1.jpg/330px-Konza1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Konza1.jpg/440px-Konza1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1520" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Konza1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Konza_Prairie" class="mw-redirect" title="Konza Prairie">Konza</a> <a href="/wiki/Tallgrass_prairie" title="Tallgrass prairie">tallgrass prairie</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Flint_Hills" title="Flint Hills">Flint Hills</a> of northeastern <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Woody plants, shrubs or trees, may occur on some grasslands – forming <a href="/wiki/Savannas" class="mw-redirect" title="Savannas">savannas</a>, scrubby grassland or semi-wooded grassland, such as the African savannas or the <a href="/wiki/Iberia" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia">Iberian</a> <a href="/wiki/Dehesa_(pastoral_management)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dehesa (pastoral management)">dehesa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As flowering <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plants</a> and trees, grasses grow in great concentrations in <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climates</a> where annual <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rainfall</a> ranges between 500 and 900&#160;mm (20 and 35&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-2">[2]</a></sup> The root systems of perennial grasses and <a href="/wiki/Forb" title="Forb">forbs</a> form complex mats that hold the <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soil</a> in place.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evolution">Evolution</span></h2>
<p>Graminoids are among the most versatile <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">life forms</a>. They became widespread toward the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> period, and fossilized <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> feces (<a href="/wiki/Coprolite" title="Coprolite">coprolites</a>) have been found containing <a href="/wiki/Phytolith" title="Phytolith">phytoliths</a> of a variety of grasses that include grasses that are related to modern <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bamboo" title="Bamboo">bamboo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The appearance of mountains in the <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">western United States</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> epochs, a period of some 25 million years, created a continental climate favorable to the evolution of grasslands. Existing forest biomes declined, and grasslands became much more widespread. Following the <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice ages</a>, grasslands expanded in range in the hotter, drier climates, and began to become the dominant land feature worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climates">Climates</span></h2>
<p>Grasslands often occur in areas with annual precipitation between 600&#160;mm (24&#160;in) and 1,500&#160;mm (59&#160;in) and average mean annual temperatures ranges from −5 and 20&#160;°C (Woodward et al. 2004). However, some grasslands occur in colder (−20&#160;°C) and hotter (30&#160;°C) climatic conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Grassland can exist in habitats that are frequently disturbed by grazing or fire, as such disturbance prevents the encroachment of woody species. Species richness is particularly high in grasslands of low soil fertility such as <a href="/wiki/Serpentine_barrens" class="mw-redirect" title="Serpentine barrens">serpentine barrens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calcareous" title="Calcareous">calcareous</a> grasslands, where woody encroachment is prevented as low nutrient levels in the soil may inhibit the growth of forest and shrub species.</p>

<p>Grasslands dominated by unsown wild-plant communities ("unimproved grasslands") can be called either natural or 'semi-natural' habitats. The majority of grasslands in temperate climates are 'semi-natural'. Although their plant communities are natural, their maintenance depends upon anthropogenic activities such as low-intensity farming, which maintains these grasslands through grazing and cutting regimes. These grasslands contain many species of wild plants – grasses, sedges, rushes and herbs – 25 or more species per square metre is not unusual.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Chalk downlands in England can support over 40 species per square metre. In many parts of the world, few examples have escaped agricultural improvement (fertilising, weed killing, ploughing or re-seeding). For example, original North American prairie grasslands or lowland wildflower meadows in the UK are now rare and their associated wild flora equally threatened. Associated with the wild-plant diversity of the "unimproved" grasslands is usually a rich invertebrate fauna; also there are many species of birds that are grassland "specialists", such as the <a href="/wiki/Snipe" title="Snipe">snipe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Great_bustard" title="Great bustard">great bustard</a>. Agriculturally improved grasslands, which dominate modern intensive agricultural landscapes, are usually poor in wild plant species due to the original diversity of plants having been destroyed by cultivation, the original wild-plant communities having been replaced by sown monocultures of cultivated varieties of grasses and clovers, such as <a href="/wiki/Perennial_ryegrass" class="mw-redirect" title="Perennial ryegrass">perennial ryegrass</a> and <a href="/wiki/White_clover" class="mw-redirect" title="White clover">white clover</a>. In many parts of the world "unimproved" grasslands are one of the most threatened types of habitat, and a target for acquisition by wildlife conservation groups or for special grants to landowners who are encouraged to manage them appropriately.</p>

<p><br />
Grassland vegetation often remains dominant in a particular area usually due to <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">grazing</a>, cutting, or natural or manmade fires, all discouraging colonisation by and survival of tree and shrub <a href="/wiki/Seedling" title="Seedling">seedlings</a>. Some of the world's largest expanses of grassland are found in African savanna, and these are maintained by wild <a href="/wiki/Herbivore" title="Herbivore">herbivores</a> as well as by nomadic <a href="/wiki/Pastoralism" title="Pastoralism">pastoralists</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a>, sheep or goats.</p>
<p>Grasslands may occur naturally or as the result of human activity. Grasslands created and maintained by human activity are called <b>anthropogenic grasslands</b>. Hunting peoples around the world often set regular fires to maintain and extend grasslands, and prevent fire-intolerant trees and shrubs from taking hold. The tallgrass <a href="/wiki/Prairie" title="Prairie">prairies</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">U.S. Midwest</a> may have been extended eastward into <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> by human agency. Much grassland in <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Northwest Europe">northwest Europe</a> developed after the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Neolithic Period">Neolithic Period</a>, when people gradually cleared the forest to create areas for raising their livestock.</p>
<p>The professional study of grasslands falls under the category of <a href="/wiki/Rangeland_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Rangeland Management">Rangeland Management</a> which focuses on ecosystem services associated with the grass-dominated arid and semi-arid rangelands of the world; rangelands account for an estimated 70% of the earths landmass, thus many cultures including those of the United States are indebted to the economics that the worlds grasslands have to offer albeit from producing grazing animals, tourism, ecosystems services such as clean water and air, or from energy extraction.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_and_Subtropical">Tropical and Subtropical</span></h4>
<p>These grasslands are classified with tropical and subtropical <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrubland" title="Shrubland">shrublands</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_grasslands,_savannas,_and_shrublands" title="Tropical and subtropical grasslands, savannas, and shrublands">tropical and subtropical grasslands, savannas, and shrublands biome</a>. Notable tropical and subtropical grasslands include the <a href="/wiki/Los_Llanos,_Venezuela" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Llanos, Venezuela">Llanos</a> grasslands of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Temperate">Temperate</span></h4>
<p>Mid-latitude grasslands, including the <a href="/wiki/Prairie" title="Prairie">prairie</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_California_native_plants#Selected_grasses" title="List of California native plants">Pacific grasslands</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pampas" title="Pampas">Pampas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, calcareous <a href="/wiki/Downland" title="Downland">downland</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. They are classified with temperate savannas and shrublands as the <a href="/wiki/Temperate_grasslands,_savannas,_and_shrublands" title="Temperate grasslands, savannas, and shrublands">temperate grasslands, savannas, and shrublands biome</a>. Temperate grasslands are the home to many large <a href="/wiki/Herbivores" class="mw-redirect" title="Herbivores">herbivores</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Bison" title="Bison">bison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gazelle" title="Gazelle">gazelles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zebra" title="Zebra">zebras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhinoceros" title="Rhinoceros">rhinoceroses</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wild_horse" title="Wild horse">wild horses</a>. <a href="/wiki/Carnivores" class="mw-redirect" title="Carnivores">Carnivores</a> like <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wolf" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolf">wolves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheetah" title="Cheetah">cheetahs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leopard" title="Leopard">leopards</a> are also found in temperate grasslands. Other animals of this region include: <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prairie_dog" title="Prairie dog">prairie dogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_rabbit" class="mw-redirect" title="Jack rabbit">jack rabbits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skunk" title="Skunk">skunks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coyote" title="Coyote">coyotes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fox" title="Fox">fox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badger" title="Badger">badgers</a>, blackbirds (both <a href="/wiki/Common_blackbird" title="Common blackbird">Old</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_World_blackbird" title="New World blackbird">New World</a> varieties), <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper" title="Grasshopper">grasshoppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meadowlark" title="Meadowlark">meadowlarks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sparrow" title="Sparrow">sparrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quail" title="Quail">quails</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawk" title="Hawk">hawks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hyena" title="Hyena">hyenas</a>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Flooded">Flooded</span></h4>
<p>Grasslands that are flooded seasonally or year-round, like the <a href="/wiki/Everglades" title="Everglades">Everglades</a> of <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pantanal" title="Pantanal">Pantanal</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Esteros_del_Ibera" class="mw-redirect" title="Esteros del Ibera">Esteros del Ibera</a> in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, are classified with flooded savannas as the <a href="/wiki/Flooded_grasslands_and_savannas" title="Flooded grasslands and savannas">flooded grasslands and savannas biome</a> and occur mostly in the tropics and subtropics.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Watermeadow" class="mw-redirect" title="Watermeadow">Watermeadows</a> are grasslands that are deliberately flooded for short periods.</p>

<p>High-altitude grasslands located on high <a href="/wiki/Mountain_ranges" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountain ranges">mountain ranges</a> around the world, like the <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1ramo" title="Páramo">Páramo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Andes_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Andes Mountains">Andes Mountains</a>. They are part of the <a href="/wiki/Montane_grasslands_and_shrublands" title="Montane grasslands and shrublands">montane grasslands and shrublands biome</a> and also constitute <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tundra">Tundra</span></h4>
<p>Similar to montane grasslands, polar <a href="/wiki/Arctic_tundra" class="mw-redirect" title="Arctic tundra">Arctic tundra</a> can have grasses, but high soil moisture means that few tundras are grass-dominated today. However, during the <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice ages</a>, a polar grassland known as <a href="/wiki/Steppe-tundra" class="mw-redirect" title="Steppe-tundra">steppe-tundra</a> occupied large areas of the Northern Hemisphere. These are in the <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra biome</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Desert_and_Xeric">Desert and Xeric</span></h4>
<p>Also called desert grasslands, this is composed of sparse grassland ecoregions located in the <a href="/wiki/Deserts_and_xeric_shrublands" title="Deserts and xeric shrublands">deserts and xeric shrublands biome</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Animals">Animals</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mite" title="Mite">Mites</a>, insect <a href="/wiki/Larva" title="Larva">larvae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nematode" title="Nematode">nematodes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earthworm" title="Earthworm">earthworms</a> inhabit deep soil, which can reach 6 metres (20&#160;ft) underground in undisturbed grasslands on the richest soils of the world. These invertebrates, along with symbiotic <a href="/wiki/Fungi" class="mw-redirect" title="Fungi">fungi</a>, extend the root systems, break apart hard soil, enrich it with <a href="/wiki/Urea" title="Urea">urea</a> and other natural fertilizers, trap minerals and water and promote growth. Some types of fungi make the plants more resistant to insect and microbial attacks.</p>
<p>Grassland in all its form supports a vast variety of mammals, reptiles, birds, and insects. Typical large mammals include the <a href="/wiki/Blue_wildebeest" title="Blue wildebeest">blue wildebeest</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_bison" title="American bison">American bison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giant_anteater" title="Giant anteater">giant anteater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Przewalski%27s_horse" title="Przewalski's horse">Przewalski's horse</a>.</p>
<p>While grasslands in general support diverse wildlife, given the lack of hiding places for predators, the African <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> regions support a much greater diversity in wildlife than do temperate grasslands.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is evidence for grassland being much the product of animal behaviour and movement;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> some examples include migratory herds of antelope trampling vegetation and <a href="/wiki/African_bush_elephant" title="African bush elephant">African bush elephants</a> eating <a href="/wiki/Acacia" title="Acacia">acacia</a> saplings before the plant has a chance to grow into a mature tree.</p>
