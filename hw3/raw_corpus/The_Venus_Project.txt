<p><b>Jacque Fresco</b> (born March 13, 1916), is an American <a href="/wiki/Futurologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Futurologist">futurist</a><sup id="cite_ref-futurist_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-futurist-1">[1]</a></sup> and self-described <a href="/wiki/Social_engineering_(political_science)" title="Social engineering (political science)">social engineer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-socialeng_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-socialeng-2">[2]</a></sup> Fresco is <a href="/wiki/Autodidacticism" title="Autodidacticism">self-taught</a> and has worked in a variety of positions related to <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a>.</p>
<p>Fresco writes and lectures his views on <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable cities">sustainable cities</a>, <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, natural-resource management, <a href="/wiki/Cybernetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Cybernetic">cybernetic</a> technology, <a href="/wiki/Automation" title="Automation">automation</a>, and the role of science in society. Fresco directs the Venus Project.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Venus_Project_Inc._5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Venus_Project_Inc.-5">[5]</a></sup> Fresco advocates global implementation of a socioeconomic system which he refers to as a "resource-based economy."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Born March 13, 1916,<sup id="cite_ref-1930_Census_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1930_Census-8">[8]</a></sup> Jacque Fresco grew up in a Sephardi Jewish<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> home in <a href="/wiki/Bensonhurst" class="mw-redirect" title="Bensonhurst">Bensonhurst</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-orlando_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-10">[10]</a></sup> Fresco was a teenager during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>.</p>
<p>Fresco spent time with friends discussing <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a>, science, and the future.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup> Fresco attended the <a href="/wiki/Young_Communist_League_USA" title="Young Communist League USA">Young Communist League</a>. After a discussion with the league president during a meeting Fresco was 'physically ejected' after loudly stating that 'Karl Marx was wrong!'<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_158-161_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_158-161-12">[12]</a></sup> Fresco left home at the age of 14, hitchhiking and 'jumping' trains as one of the so-called "<a href="/wiki/Wild_Boys_of_the_Road" title="Wild Boys of the Road">Wild Boys of the Road</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Fresco later turned his attention to <a href="/wiki/Technocracy" title="Technocracy">technocracy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft_industry">Aircraft industry</span></h3>
<p>Fresco worked at <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Aircraft_Company" title="Douglas Aircraft Company">Douglas Aircraft Company</a> in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> during the late 1930s.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_158-161_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_158-161-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup> He presented designs including a <a href="/wiki/Flying_wing" title="Flying wing">flying wing</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and a disk-shaped aircraft. Some of his designs were considered impractical at the time and Fresco's design ideas were not adopted.<sup id="cite_ref-Rejected_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rejected-16">[16]</a></sup> Fresco resigned from Douglas because of design disagreements.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_158-161_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_158-161-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rejected_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rejected-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1942, Fresco was drafted into the United States Army.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Jacque_Army_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jacque_Army-17">[17]</a></sup> He was assigned technical design duties for the <a href="/wiki/Army_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Army Air Force">Army Air Force</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wright_Field" class="mw-redirect" title="Wright Field">Wright Field</a> design laboratories in <a href="/wiki/Dayton,_Ohio" title="Dayton, Ohio">Dayton, Ohio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rejected_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rejected-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Scully_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scully-18">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Trip_Moon_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trip_Moon-19">[19]</a></sup> One design he produced was a "radical variable camber wing" with which he attempted to optimize flight control by allowing the pilot to adjust the thickness of the wings during lift and flight.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Fresco did not adjust to military life and was discharged.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trend_Home">Trend Home</span></h3>
<p>Fresco was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Earl_Muntz" class="mw-redirect" title="Earl Muntz">Earl Muntz</a>, to design housing that was low cost. Muntz invested $500,000 seed money in the project. Fresco along with his associates Harry Giaretto and Eli Catran conceived, designed and engineered a project house called the <i>Trend Home</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-brochure_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brochure-22">[22]</a></sup> Fresco, 32 years old at the time, came closest to traditional career success with this project. Built mostly of aluminum and glass, it was on prominent display at Stage 8 of the Warner Bros. Sunset Lot in Hollywood for three months. The home could be toured for one dollar, with proceeds going to the Cancer Prevention Society. In the summer of 1948, a <a href="/wiki/Federal_Housing_Administration" title="Federal Housing Administration">Federal Housing Administration</a> official met Muntz about the project. The official's proposal, according to Muntz, would add a bureaucratic overhead negating the low production costs. Without Federal or further private funding the project did not make mass production. This experience led Fresco to the conclusion that society would have to be changed for his inventions to reach their potential.<sup id="cite_ref-orlando_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-trend_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trend-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_Research_Laboratories">Scientific Research Laboratories</span></h3>
<p>In the late 1940s, Fresco created and was director of Scientific Research Laboratories in Los Angeles.<sup id="cite_ref-Trip_Moon_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trip_Moon-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Scientific_Research_Laboratories_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scientific_Research_Laboratories-24">[24]</a></sup> Here he also gave lectures, and taught technical design,<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_158-161_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_158-161-12">[12]</a></sup> meanwhile researching and working on inventions as a freelance inventor and scientific consultant.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> During this period, Fresco struggled to get his research funded<sup id="cite_ref-Frustrated_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Frustrated-26">[26]</a></sup> and faced setbacks and financial difficulties. In 1955, Fresco left California after his lab was removed to build the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Freeway" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden State Freeway">Golden State Freeway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_158-161_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_158-161-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Midlife">Midlife</span></h2>
<p>In 1955 Fresco moved to <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. He opened a business as a psychological consultant but had no formal schooling in the subject.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup> Receiving a 'barrage of criticism' from the <a href="/wiki/American_Psychological_Association" title="American Psychological Association">American Psychological Association</a> Fresco stopped that business.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup> In a newspaper article from that time period Fresco claimed to have a degree from <a href="/wiki/Sierra_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Sierra University">Sierra University</a>, Los Angeles California, which is unverified.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fresco described white supremacist organizations he joined to test the feasibility of changing people. He tells of joining a local <a href="/wiki/Ku_Klux_Klan" title="Ku Klux Klan">Ku Klux Klan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Citizens%27_Councils" title="Citizens' Councils">White Citizens Council</a> in an attempt to change their views about racial discrimination.<sup id="cite_ref-TVPmag_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TVPmag-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Miami Fresco presented designs of a circular city.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Fresco made his living working as an <a href="/wiki/Industrial_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial designer">industrial designer</a> for various companies such as <a href="/wiki/Alcoa" title="Alcoa">Alcoa</a> and the Major Realty Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1961, with <a href="/wiki/Pietro_Belluschi" title="Pietro Belluschi">Pietro Belluschi</a> and C. Frederick Wise,<sup id="cite_ref-House_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-House-30">[30]</a></sup> Fresco collaborated on a project, known as the Sandwich House.<sup id="cite_ref-Smith_2_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith_2-14">[14]</a></sup> Consisting of mostly prefabricated components, partitions, and aluminum, it sold for $2,950, or $7,500 with foundation and all internal installations.<sup id="cite_ref-House_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-House-30">[30]</a></sup> During this period, Fresco supported his projects by designing prefabricated aluminum devices through Jacque Fresco Enterprises Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1955 to 1969 Fresco named his social ideas "Project Americana".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Looking_Forward">Looking Forward</span></h3>
<p><i>Looking Forward</i> was published in 1969. Author <a href="/wiki/Ken_Keyes,_Jr." title="Ken Keyes, Jr.">Ken Keyes Jr.</a>, and Jacques Fresco coauthored the book. <i>Looking Forward,</i> is a speculative look at the future. The authors picture an ideal 'cybernetic society in which want has been banished and work and personal possessions no longer exist; individual gratification is the total concern'.<sup id="cite_ref-Lib_Journ_1971_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lib_Journ_1971-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sociocyberneering.2C_Inc.">Sociocyberneering, Inc.</span></h3>
<p>Fresco formed "Sociocyberneering", a membership organization claiming 250 members, according to an interview with Fresco.<sup id="cite_ref-Hagan_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hagan-33">[33]</a></sup> He hosted lectures in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Beach">Miami Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coral_Gables" class="mw-redirect" title="Coral Gables">Coral Gables</a><sup id="cite_ref-Hewitt_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hewitt-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gold_Coast_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gold_Coast-35">[35]</a></sup> Fresco promoted his organization by lecturing at universities<sup id="cite_ref-Intro_Socio_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Intro_Socio-36">[36]</a></sup> and appearing on radio and television.<sup id="cite_ref-King_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King-37">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Montage_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Montage-38">[38]</a></sup> Although Fresco is presented as a 'Doctor' on the Larry King show there is no evidence of that being the case. Fresco did not complete high school.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Fresco's "sociocyberneering" as a membership group was discontinued and land was purchased at another location in rural <a href="/wiki/Venus,_Florida" title="Venus, Florida">Venus, Florida</a>. He established his home and research center there.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Venus_Project_and_later_career">The Venus Project and later career</span></h2>

<p>Fresco, with Meadows, supported the project in the 1990s through freelance inventing,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> industrial engineering, conventional architectural modeling, and invention consultations.<sup id="cite_ref-orlando_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, Fresco published his main work <i>The Best That Money Can't Buy</i>. In 2006, <a href="/wiki/William_Gazecki" title="William Gazecki">William Gazecki</a> directed the semi-biographical film about Fresco, <i><a href="/wiki/Future_by_Design" class="mw-redirect" title="Future by Design">Future by Design</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Future_By_Design_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Future_By_Design-42">[42]</a></sup> In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Joseph" title="Peter Joseph">Peter Joseph</a> featured Fresco in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Zeitgeist_Addendum" class="mw-redirect" title="Zeitgeist Addendum">Zeitgeist Addendum</a></i> where his ideas of the future were given as possible alternatives. Peter Joseph, founder of <a href="/wiki/The_Zeitgeist_Movement" title="The Zeitgeist Movement">the Zeitgeist Movement</a> began advocating Fresco's approach. In April 2012, the two groups disassociated due to disagreements regarding goals and objectives.<sup id="cite_ref-orlando_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Fresco attempted to trademark the phrase "<a href="/wiki/Resource-based_economy" title="Resource-based economy">resource-based economy</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The phrase was reviewed and found to be too generic, and the trademark was denied.</p>
<p>Throughout 2010, Fresco traveled with Meadows, worldwide to promote interest in the Venus Project.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On January 15, 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/Zeitgeist:_Moving_Forward" class="mw-redirect" title="Zeitgeist: Moving Forward">Zeitgeist: Moving Forward</a></i> was released in theaters, featuring Fresco.<sup id="cite_ref-Zeitgeist:_Moving_Forward_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeitgeist:_Moving_Forward-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, Fresco spoke to protesters at the <a href="/wiki/Occupy_movement" title="Occupy movement">"occupy Miami"</a> site at <a href="/wiki/Stephen_P._Clark_Government_Center" title="Stephen P. Clark Government Center">Government Center</a> in Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In April 2012, Roxanne Meadows released a film, <i>Paradise or Oblivion</i>, summarizing the goals and proposals of the Venus Project.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In June 2012, Maja Borg screened her film, <i>Future My Love</i>, at the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Film_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Film Festival">Edinburgh International Film Festival</a> featuring the work of Fresco and Roxanne Meadows.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fresco holds lectures and tours at the Venus Project location.<sup id="cite_ref-New_American_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_American-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family">Personal life and family</span></h2>
<p>Fresco was born to immigrants from the Middle East, Isaac and Lena Fresco.<sup id="cite_ref-1930_Census_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1930_Census-8">[8]</a></sup> His father was born in 1880<sup id="cite_ref-Isaac_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Isaac-52">[52]</a></sup> and around 1905 immigrated from <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> to New York where he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Horticulturalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Horticulturalist">horticulturalist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1930_Census_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1930_Census-8">[8]</a></sup> He died in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-Isaac_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Isaac-52">[52]</a></sup> Fresco's mother was born in 1887<sup id="cite_ref-Lena_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lena-53">[53]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> and also migrated to New York around 1904.<sup id="cite_ref-1930_Census_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1930_Census-8">[8]</a></sup> She died in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-Lena_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lena-53">[53]</a></sup> Fresco was brother to two siblings,<sup id="cite_ref-1930_Census_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1930_Census-8">[8]</a></sup> a sister, Freda, and a brother, David.</p>
<p>Fresco had two marriages when he lived in Los Angeles and carried his second marriage through his first couple years in Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-Trip_Moon_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trip_Moon-19">[19]</a></sup> He divorced his second wife in 1957 and remained unmarried thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> His second wife, Patricia, gave birth to a son, Richard, in 1953 and a daughter, Bambi, in 1956. Richard was an army private<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> and died in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-Richard_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Richard-56">[56]</a></sup> Bambi died of cancer in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>Roxanne Meadows has assisted Fresco since 1976. As Fresco's <a href="/wiki/Domestic_partner" class="mw-redirect" title="Domestic partner">domestic partner</a> and administrative colleague, she oversees much of the management of the Venus Project.<sup id="cite_ref-orlando_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orlando-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_appraisals">Critical appraisals</span></h2>
<p>It’s a 'lack of professional engagement', <a href="/wiki/William_Gazecki" title="William Gazecki">William Gazecki</a> who in 2006 completed <i>Future by Design</i>, a feature-length profile of Jacque Fresco says, that has hurt Fresco the most. “The real missing link in Jacque’s world is having put Jacque to work,” Gazecki says, “[It’s] exemplified when people say: ‘Well, show me some buildings he’s built. And I don’t mean the domes out in Venus. I mean, let’s see an office building, let’s see a manufacturing plant, let’s see a circular city.’ And that’s where he should have been 30 years ago. He should have been applying his work, in the real world … [but] he’s not a collaborator, and I think that’s why he’s never had great public achievements.”<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fresco on Fresco: when asked by a reporter why he has such difficulty actualizing his many ideas, Fresco responded, "Because I can't get to anybody."<sup id="cite_ref-7_News_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7_News-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Views_on_Fresco">Views on Fresco</span></h3>
<p>Fresco's critical view of modern economics has been compared to <a href="/wiki/Thorstein_Veblen" title="Thorstein Veblen">Thorstein Veblen</a>'s concept of "the predatory phase in human development," according to an article in the journal of Society and Business Review.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Grønborg has labeled other facets of Fresco's ideology a "<a href="/wiki/Tabula_rasa" title="Tabula rasa">tabula rasa</a> approach."<sup id="cite_ref-According_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-According-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>Synergetics theorist, Arthur Coulter, calls Fresco's city designs 'organic' and 'evolutionary' (rather than revolutionary).<sup id="cite_ref-Synergetics_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Synergetics-63">[63]</a></sup> Coulter posits such cities as the answer to <a href="/wiki/Walter_B._Cannon" class="mw-redirect" title="Walter B. Cannon">Walter B. Cannon</a>'s idea of achieving <a href="/wiki/Homeostasis" title="Homeostasis">homeostasis</a> for society.<sup id="cite_ref-Synergetics_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Synergetics-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hypothetical_form_of_government">Hypothetical form of government</span></h3>
<p>Fresco describes his form of <a href="/wiki/Governance" title="Governance">governance</a> in this way: "The aims of The Venus Project have no parallel in history, not with communism, socialism, fascism or any other political ideology. This is true because cybernation is of recent origin. With this system, the system of financial influence and control will no longer exist."<sup id="cite_ref-According_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-According-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ludwig_von_Mises_Institute" title="Ludwig von Mises Institute">Ludwig von Mises Institute</a> scholar <a href="/wiki/Robert_P._Murphy" title="Robert P. Murphy">Robert P. Murphy</a> has raised the <a href="/wiki/Calculation_problem" class="mw-redirect" title="Calculation problem">calculation problem</a> against a resource-based economy.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> In a resource-based economy, Murphy claims there is no ability to calculate the availability and desirability of resources because the price mechanism is not utilized. Addressing this aspect, another article in the <i><a href="/wiki/Quarterly_Journal_of_Austrian_Economics" title="Quarterly Journal of Austrian Economics">Quarterly Journal of Austrian Economics</a></i>, states criticism of 'central plannings' computation problem applies to the ideas of Fresco.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Question_of_utopianism">Question of utopianism</span></h3>
<p>The Venus Project states on its website states that it is not <a href="/wiki/Utopia" title="Utopia">utopian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-about_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-66">[66]</a></sup> Nikolina Olsen-Rule, writing for the <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Institute_for_Futures_Studies" title="Copenhagen Institute for Futures Studies">Copenhagen Institute for Futures Studies</a>, supports this idea,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"For most people, the promise of the project sounds like an unattainable utopia, but if you examine it more closely, there are surprisingly many scientifically founded arguments that open up an entire new world of possibilities."<sup id="cite_ref-Spaces_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spaces-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Morten Grønborg, also of Copenhagen Institute for Futures Studies, points out the Venus Project is,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"... this visionary idea of a future society has many characteristics in common with the utopia. ... the word utopia carries a double meaning, since in Greek it can mean both the good place (eutopia) and the nonexisting place (outopia). A good place is precisely what Fresco has devoted his life to describing and fighting for."<sup id="cite_ref-morten_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-morten-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comments_on_Fresco">Comments on Fresco</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hans-Ulrich_Obrist" title="Hans-Ulrich Obrist">Hans-Ulrich Obrist</a> notes, "Fresco's future may, of course, seem outmoded and his writings have been subject to critique for their fascistic undertones of order and similitude, but his contributions are etched in the popular psyche and his eco-friendly concepts continue to influence our present generation of progressive architects, city planners and designers."<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fresco's work gained the attention of science fiction enthusiast and critic, <a href="/wiki/Forrest_J_Ackerman" title="Forrest J Ackerman">Forrest J Ackerman</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolfe_168.2C_169.2C_170-11">[11]</a></sup> Fresco would later attract <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> animator, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Drexler" title="Doug Drexler">Doug Drexler</a>, who worked with Fresco to produce several computer renderings of his designs.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>Commenting on Fresco, physicist, <a href="/wiki/Paul_G._Hewitt" title="Paul G. Hewitt">Paul G. Hewitt</a>, wrote that Fresco inspired him toward a career in physical science.<sup id="cite_ref-Hewitt_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hewitt-34">[34]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span></h3>
<p>In July 2016, Jacque Fresco received a NOVUS Summit award for City Design/Community. NOVUS Summit is supported by UN DESA (United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs).<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
