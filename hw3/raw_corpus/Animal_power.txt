<p>A <b>working animal</b> is an animal, usually <a href="/wiki/Domestication" title="Domestication">domesticated</a>, that is kept by humans and trained to perform tasks. They may be close members of the family, such as <a href="/wiki/Guide_dog" title="Guide dog">guide dogs</a> or other <a href="/wiki/Assistance_dog" title="Assistance dog">assistance dogs</a>, or they may be animals trained to provide <a href="/wiki/Tractive_force" title="Tractive force">tractive force</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Draft_horse" title="Draft horse">draft horses</a> or logging elephants. The latter types of animals are called <b>draft animals</b> (<b>draught animals</b>) or <b>beasts of burden</b>. Most working animals are either <a href="/wiki/Service_animal" title="Service animal">service animals</a> or draft animals. They may also be used for <a href="/wiki/Milk" title="Milk">milking</a> or <a href="/wiki/Herding" title="Herding">herding</a>, jobs that require human training to encourage the animal to cooperate. Some, at the end of their working lives, may also be used for meat or other products such as leather.</p>
<p>The history of working animals may predate agriculture, with dogs used by our <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherer</a> ancestors. Around the world, millions of animals work in relationship with their owners. Domesticated species are often <a href="/wiki/Breed" title="Breed">bred</a> to be suitable for different uses and conditions, especially horses and <a href="/wiki/Working_dog" title="Working dog">working dogs</a>. Working animals are usually raised on farms, though some are still captured from the wild, such as dolphins and some <a href="/wiki/Asian_elephant" title="Asian elephant">Asian elephants</a>.</p>

<p>People have found uses for a wide variety of abilities found in animals and even in industrialized society many animals are still used for work. The strength of horses, elephants and <a href="/wiki/Ox" title="Ox">oxen</a> is used in pulling carts and logs. The keen sense of smell of dogs is used to search for drugs and explosives as well helping to find game while hunting and to search for missing or trapped people. Several animals including <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donkey" title="Donkey">donkeys</a>, horses and dogs are used for transport, either riding or to pull wagons and sleds. Other animals including dogs and <a href="/wiki/Monkey" title="Monkey">monkeys</a> provide assistance to blind or disabled people.</p>
<p>On rare occasions, wild animals may be not only tamed, but <a href="/wiki/Animal_training" title="Animal training">trained</a> to perform work, though often solely for novelty or entertainment purposes, as such animals tend to lack the traits of trustworthiness and mild temper that characterize the true domesticated working animal. Conversely, not all domesticated animals are working animals. For example, while cats may perform work catching mice, it is an instinctive behavior, not one that can be trained by human intervention. Other domesticated animals, such as sheep, or rabbits, may have agricultural uses for meat, <a href="/wiki/Hide_(skin)" title="Hide (skin)">hides</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>, but are not suitable for work. Finally, small domestic pets such as most birds (other than certain types of <a href="/wiki/Carrier_pigeon" class="mw-redirect" title="Carrier pigeon">pigeon</a>) or <a href="/wiki/Hamster" title="Hamster">hamsters</a> are generally incapable of performing work other than that of providing simple companionship.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roles_and_specializations">Roles and specializations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Roles and specializations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Some animals are used due to sheer physical strength in tasks such as ploughing or logging. Such animals are grouped as a <b>draught</b> or <b>draft animal</b>. Others may be used as <a href="/wiki/Pack_animal" title="Pack animal">pack animals</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Animal-powered_transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Animal-powered transport">animal-powered transport</a>, the movement of people and goods. People ride some animals directly as mounts; Alternatively, one or more animals in <a href="/wiki/Horse_harness" title="Horse harness">harness</a> may be used to pull vehicles.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Riding_animals_or_mounts">Riding animals or mounts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Riding animals or mounts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>They include <a href="/wiki/Equine" class="mw-redirect" title="Equine">equines</a> such as horses, <a href="/wiki/Pony" title="Pony">ponies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donkey" title="Donkey">donkeys</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mule" title="Mule">mules</a>; <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephants</a>; <a href="/wiki/Yaks" class="mw-redirect" title="Yaks">yaks</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camels</a>. <a href="/wiki/Dromedaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Dromedaries">Dromedary camels</a> in arid areas of Australia, North Africa and the Middle East; the less common <a href="/wiki/Bactrian_camel" title="Bactrian camel">Bactrian camel</a> inhabits <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">central</a> and East Asia; both are used as working animals. On occasion, <a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">reindeer</a>, though usually driven, may be ridden.</p>
<p>Certain wild animals have been tamed and used for riding, usually for novelty purposes, including the <a href="/wiki/Zebra" title="Zebra">zebra</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ostrich" title="Ostrich">ostrich</a>. Some <a href="/wiki/Mythical_creature" class="mw-redirect" title="Mythical creature">mythical creatures</a> are believed to act as divine mounts, such as <a href="/wiki/Garuda" title="Garuda">garuda</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> and the winged horse <a href="/wiki/Pegasus" title="Pegasus">Pegasus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pack_animals">Pack animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pack animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg/220px-Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg/330px-Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg/440px-Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lloyd_the_Llama.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A pack <a href="/wiki/Llama" title="Llama">llama</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pack_animal" title="Pack animal">Pack animal</a></div>
<p>Pack animals may be of the same species as mounts or harness animals, though animals such as horses, mules, donkeys, reindeer and both types of camel may have individual bloodlines or <a href="/wiki/Breed" title="Breed">breeds</a> that have been <a href="/wiki/Selective_breeding" title="Selective breeding">selectively bred</a> for packing. Additional species are only used to carry loads, including <a href="/wiki/Llama" title="Llama">llamas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Andes_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Andes Mountains">Andes</a>.</p>
<p>Domesticated <a href="/wiki/Ox" title="Ox">oxen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">bullocks</a>, and yaks are also used as pack animals. Other species used to carry cargo include dogs and <a href="/wiki/Pack_goat" title="Pack goat">pack goats</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Harness_animals">Harness animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Harness animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mule_in_Morocco.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mule_in_Morocco.JPG/220px-Mule_in_Morocco.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mule_in_Morocco.JPG/330px-Mule_in_Morocco.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mule_in_Morocco.JPG/440px-Mule_in_Morocco.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mule_in_Morocco.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Donkey used to pull a wheeled vehicle in Morocco</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>An intermediate use is to <a href="/wiki/Horse_harness" title="Horse harness">harness</a> animals, singly or in <a href="/wiki/Team" title="Team">teams</a>, to pull (or haul) <a href="/wiki/Sled" title="Sled">sleds</a>, wheeled vehicles or <a href="/wiki/Plough" title="Plough">plough</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oxen" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxen">Oxen</a> are slow but strong, and have been used in a yoke since ancient times: the earliest surviving vehicle, Puabi's Sumerian sledge, was ox-drawn; an acre was originally defined as the area a span of oxen could plow in a day. The <a href="/wiki/Domestic_buffalo" class="mw-redirect" title="Domestic buffalo">Water buffalo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carabao" title="Carabao">Carabao</a>, domesticated water buffalo, pull wagons and ploughs in Southeast Asia and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Draft_horse" title="Draft horse">Draught or Draft horses</a> are commonly used in harness for heavy work. Several breeds of medium-weight horses are used to pull lighter wheeled carts, carriages and buggies when a certain amount of speed or style is desirable.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mule" title="Mule">Mules</a> are considered to be very tough and strong, with harness capacity dependent on the type of horse <a href="/wiki/Mare" title="Mare">mare</a> used to produce the mule <a href="/wiki/Foal" title="Foal">foal</a>. Because they are a <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybrid</a> animal and usually are infertile, separate breeding programs must also be maintained.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pony" title="Pony">Ponies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donkey" title="Donkey">donkeys</a> are often used to pull carts and small wagons, historically, ponies were commonly used in mining to pull ore carts.</li>
<li>Dogs are used for pulling light carts or, particularly, sleds. (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Sled_dog" title="Sled dog">sled dogs</a> such as Huskies) for both recreation and working purposes.</li>
<li>Goats also can perform light harness work in front of carts</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Reindeer" title="Reindeer">Reindeer</a> are used in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> and sub-Arctic Nordic countries and Siberia. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> deployed <a href="/wiki/Deer_transportation_battalion" title="Deer transportation battalion">deer transportation battalions</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Front_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Front of World War II">Eastern Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the twenty-first century, Russian soldiers continue to train with reindeer sleds in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Elephants are still used for logging in South-east Asia.</li>
<li>Less often, camels and llamas have been trained to harness. According to <a href="/wiki/Juan_Ignacio_Molina" title="Juan Ignacio Molina">Juan Ignacio Molina</a> the Dutch captain <a href="/wiki/Joris_van_Spilbergen" title="Joris van Spilbergen">Joris van Spilbergen</a> observed the use of <a href="/wiki/Llama" title="Llama">chiliquenes</a> (a llama type) by native <a href="/wiki/Mapuche" title="Mapuche">Mapuches</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mocha_Island" title="Mocha Island">Mocha Island</a> as <a href="/wiki/Plough" title="Plough">plough animals</a> in 1614.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Assorted wild animals have, on occasion, been tamed and trained to harness, including zebras and even moose .</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Searching_and_retrieving">Searching and retrieving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Searching and retrieving">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hunting">Hunting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hunting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg/220px-Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg/330px-Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/38/Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="299" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Working_cocker_spaniel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A dog working as a retriever</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>As predatory species are naturally equipped to catch prey, this is a further use for animals and birds. This can be done either for sustenance, sport, or to reduce the population of <a href="/wiki/Pest_(organism)" title="Pest (organism)">undesired animals</a> that are considered harmful to crops, livestock or the environment.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hound" title="Hound">Hounds</a> and other dogs are used to kill and fetch prey. Certain breeds have been bred for this task such as <a href="/wiki/Pointer_(dog)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pointer (dog)">pointers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Setter" title="Setter">setters</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cat#Domestication" title="Cat">Mousers</a> (domestic cats used for hunting small rodents and birds) are one of the oldest working animals having protected food supplies from pests since the foundation of human agriculture.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Caracal" title="Caracal">Caracals</a> are sometimes used as hunting animals in some parts of the Middle East, although they are normally kept as pets.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cheetah#Taming" title="Cheetah">Cheetahs</a> that have been tamed but not domesticated have been used by humans for chasing down prey.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ferret" title="Ferret">Ferrets</a> prey on creatures living in burrows, such as rabbits.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Falconry" title="Falconry">falconry</a>, birds of prey are used as hunters in the air.</li>
<li>Aquatic birds, such as <a href="/wiki/Cormorant" title="Cormorant">cormorants</a> in China, can be used to catch fish.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Humans">Humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Dogs, with their highly developed sense of smell, are used to locate humans, such as escaped prisoners or people lost in remote areas. They are used also to find people who are trapped, such as in avalanches or collapsed buildings.</li>
<li>Horses are used in remote areas to help human searchers cover large areas of rugged terrain. Their natural awareness of their surroundings will often alert human handlers to the presence of anything unusual, including lost hikers, hunters or other. Like some dogs, some horses are trained to follow scent. See <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue_horse" class="mw-redirect" title="Search and rescue horse">Search and rescue horse</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sniffing_Animals">Sniffing Animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sniffing Animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Dogs and pigs, with better smell sense than humans, can assist with gathering by finding valuable products, such as <a href="/wiki/Truffle" title="Truffle">truffles</a> (a very expensive subterranean mushroom). In France mainly pigs are used, in Italy mainly dogs. See <a href="/wiki/Truffle_hog" title="Truffle hog">Truffle hog</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interfacing_and_organization">Interfacing and organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Interfacing and organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Assistance_animals">Assistance animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Assistance animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Service_animal" title="Service animal">Service animal</a></div>
<ul>
<li>The best-known example is the <a href="/wiki/Guide_dog" title="Guide dog">guide dog</a> or seeing eye dog for blind people. See also <a href="/wiki/Service_dog" title="Service dog">service dog</a>. <a href="/wiki/Miniature_horse" title="Miniature horse">Miniature horses</a> are also occasionally used for this purpose as well.</li>
<li>Trained African monkeys and dogs have been taught to provide other functions for impaired people, such as opening mail and minor household tasks of the same like.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Herding">Herding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Herding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg/220px-Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg" width="220" height="217" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg/330px-Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg/440px-Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg 2x" data-file-width="448" data-file-height="441" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Australian_Cattle_Dog_Herding.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A dog herding sheep</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>A very close working relationship exists between a <a href="/wiki/Stockman_(Australia)" title="Stockman (Australia)">stockman</a> or <a href="/wiki/Shepherd" title="Shepherd">shepherd</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Herding_dog" title="Herding dog">herding dog</a>, and the herd (or mob) of sheep or cattle. Cattle and sheep herders in other parts of the world also use various dog breeds. See <a href="/wiki/Mountain_dog" title="Mountain dog">Mountain dogs</a>.</li>
<li>Certain breeds of horses also have an innate "cow sense" that allows them to effectively carry a rider to the right place at the right time to <a href="/wiki/Muster_(livestock)" title="Muster (livestock)">muster</a> (gather or round up) livestock. See <a href="/wiki/Stock_horse" title="Stock horse">stock horse</a>; <a href="/wiki/Cutting_horse" class="mw-redirect" title="Cutting horse">cutting horse</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Employers">Employers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Employers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_animals">Military animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Military animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Military_animal" title="Military animal">Military animal</a></div>
<p>The defensive and offensive capabilities of animals (such as fangs and claws) can be used to protect or to attack humans.</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Guard_dog" title="Guard dog">guard dog</a> barks or attacks, to warn of an intruder</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/War_elephant" title="War elephant">War elephants</a> were trained for battle in ancient times and are still used for military transport today.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Horses_in_warfare" title="Horses in warfare">Military uses of horses</a> have changed over the millennia but still continue, including for <a href="/wiki/Police_horse" class="mw-redirect" title="Police horse">police</a> work.</li>
<li>Dolphins and sea lions carry markers to attach to <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">mines</a> as well as patrolling harbors.</li>
<li>On land, dogs can be trained to find <a href="/wiki/Landmine" class="mw-redirect" title="Landmine">landmines</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">Rats</a>, which are lighter and less of a risk to set the mines off, have recently been used more frequently.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Detection rats such as those trained by <a href="/wiki/APOPO" title="APOPO">APOPO</a> can also be taught to identify diseases, especially pulmonary tuberculosis.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Homing_pigeon" title="Homing pigeon">Homing pigeons</a> transport material, usually messages on small pieces of paper, by air.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_status">Legal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Working_animal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Legal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In some jurisdictions, certain working animals are afforded greater legal rights than other animals. One such common example is <a href="/wiki/Police_dog" title="Police dog">police dogs</a>, which are often afforded additional protections and the same memorial services as human officers.</p>
