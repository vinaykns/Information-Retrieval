<p><b>Veolia Environnement S.A.</b>, branded as <b>Veolia</b>, is a French <a href="/wiki/Transnational_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Transnational company">transnational company</a> with activities in four main service and utility areas traditionally managed by public authorities – <a href="/wiki/Water_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Water management">water management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a>, public transport and energy services. In 2012, Veolia employed 318,376 employees in 48 countries. Its revenue in that year was recorded at €29.4 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is quoted on <a href="/wiki/Euronext" title="Euronext">Euronext Paris</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>. It is headquartered in the <a href="/wiki/16th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="16th arrondissement of Paris">16th arrondissement</a> of Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 2000 and 2003 the company was known as Vivendi Environnement, having been <a href="/wiki/Spin_out" class="mw-redirect" title="Spin out">spun off</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Vivendi" title="Vivendi">Vivendi</a> <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a>, most of the rest of which became Vivendi. Prior to 1998 Vivendi was known as <a href="/wiki/Compagnie_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale_des_Eaux" class="mw-redirect" title="Compagnie Générale des Eaux">Compagnie Générale des Eaux</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, following a major restructuring, the company adopted the unaccompanied Veolia name across its businesses.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1853.E2.80.931997:_Compagnie_G.C3.A9n.C3.A9rale_des_Eaux">1853–1997: Compagnie Générale des Eaux</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1853–1997: Compagnie Générale des Eaux">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 December 1853, a water company named <a href="/wiki/Compagnie_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale_des_Eaux" class="mw-redirect" title="Compagnie Générale des Eaux">Compagnie Générale des Eaux</a> (CGE) was created by an Imperial decree of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_III_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon III of France">Napoleon III</a>. In 1853, CGE obtained a concession in order to supply water to the public in <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, serving in this capacity for over a hundred years. In 1860, it obtained a 50-year concession with the City of Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-veolia.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-veolia.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>For a hundred years, Compagnie Générale des Eaux remained largely focused on the water sector. However, following the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Guy_Dejouany" title="Guy Dejouany">Guy Dejouany</a> as <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> in 1976, CGE extended its activities into other sectors with a series of takeovers. Beginning in 1980, CGE began diversifying its operations from water into waste management, energy, <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">transport</a> services, and <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Property" title="Property">property</a>. It acquired the "Compagnie Générale d'Entreprises Automobiles" (CGEA), specialized in industrial vehicles, which was later divided into two branches: Connex and Onyx Environnement. CGE then acquired the "Compagnie Générale de Chauffe", and later the Montenay group. The Energy Services division these companies became part of was later (1998) renamed "<a href="/wiki/Dalkia" title="Dalkia">Dalkia</a>".</p>
<p>CGE's expansion into communication commenced with the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Canal%2B" title="Canal+">Canal+</a> in 1983, the first Pay-TV channel in France. This expansion was accelerated after <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Messier" title="Jean-Marie Messier">Jean-Marie Messier</a> succeeded Guy Dejouany on 27 June 1996. In 1996, CGE created <a href="/wiki/Neuf_Cegetel" title="Neuf Cegetel">Cegetel</a> to take advantage of the 1998 deregulation of the French telecommunications market, accelerating the move into the media sector which would culminate in the 2000 demerger into Vivendi Universal and Vivendi Environnement.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998.E2.80.932003:_Vivendi">1998–2003: Vivendi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998–2003: Vivendi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Vivendi_SA" class="mw-redirect" title="Vivendi SA">Vivendi SA</a></div>
<p>In 1998, <a href="/wiki/Compagnie_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale_des_Eaux" class="mw-redirect" title="Compagnie Générale des Eaux">Compagnie Générale des Eaux</a> changed its name to <i>Vivendi</i>, and sold off its property and construction divisions the following year.</p>
<p>Vivendi went on to list on the New York Stock Exchange (as "V"), and in December, announced a major merger with Canal+ and <a href="/wiki/Seagram" title="Seagram">Seagram</a>, the owner of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> film company, to become <a href="/wiki/Vivendi_Universal" class="mw-redirect" title="Vivendi Universal">Vivendi Universal</a> and now named Vivendi.</p>
<p>In July 2000, Vivendi spun off the remaining water and waste companies into Vivendi Environnement (<a href="/wiki/Initial_Public_Offering" class="mw-redirect" title="Initial Public Offering">IPO</a> in Paris in July 2000 and in New York in October 2001), later (2003) renamed Veolia Environnement.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003_.E2.80.93_present:_Veolia">2003 – present: Veolia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2003 – present: Veolia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003, Vivendi Environnement became Veolia Environnement. In 2005, the name “Veolia” was established as an umbrella brand for all of the Group’s divisions (water, environmental services, energy services and transport) and a new logo was created.<sup id="cite_ref-veolia.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-veolia.com-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2009, Antoine Frérot has become the Chairman and the CEO of the Group after succeeding <a href="/wiki/Henri_Proglio" title="Henri Proglio">Henri Proglio</a> who has been appointed CEO of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lectricit%C3%A9_de_France" title="Électricité de France">Électricité de France</a>. The change has been part of a huge <a href="/wiki/Scandal" title="Scandal">politico-financial scandal</a> in France<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> as Proglio kept executive positions – and subsequent salary – in both companies until public criticism forced him to give up his Veolia revenues.</p>
<p>Its Veolia Water division remains the largest private operator of water services in the world.</p>
<p>In March 2011 the company announced the formation of <a href="/wiki/Transdev" title="Transdev">Veolia Transdev</a>, the result of the combination of its transport subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Veolia_Transport" title="Veolia Transport">Veolia Transport</a> with <a href="/wiki/Transdev_(historic)" title="Transdev (historic)">Transdev</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Caisse_des_D%C3%A9p%C3%B4ts" class="mw-redirect" title="Caisse des Dépôts">Caisse des Dépôts</a>. Veolia Transdev is the world's private-sector leader in sustainable mobility with more than 110,000 employees in 28 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-Transdev_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Transdev-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2011, amid disappointing financial results, the company announced the launch of new restructuring plans and redeployment of assets and businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In December 2011, Veolia announced a €5bn divestment program over 2012–2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The company would comprise only three divisions (Water, Environmental Services and Energy Services). The transport businesses Veolia Transdev would be divested.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Veolia is established in 48 countries, with employees across the globe in 2012:</p>

<li>France: 31%</li>
<li>Europe (not including France, Central and Eastern European countries): 23%</li>
<li>Central and Eastern Europe countries&#160;: 13%</li>
<li>China and Asia-Pacific: 12%</li>
<li>South America: 10%</li>
<li>Africa and Middle-East: 6%</li>

<p>The company has 2,573 subsidiaries around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water">Water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Veolia_Water" title="Veolia Water">Veolia Water</a></div>
<p>Veolia Water is the world leader in water services. It handles water and wastewater services for clients in the public sector and in various industries. It also creates and constructs the required technology and infrastructure. In 2012, Veolia Water employed 89,094 people and recorded revenue of €12.078 billion<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> 37.2% of its revenue comes from France, 30.2 from other European countries, 8.8% from Americas, 16.2% from Asia and 7.6% from Africa and the Middle East.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Waste_management">Waste management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Waste management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Veolia_Environmental_Services" title="Veolia Environmental Services">Veolia Environmental Services</a></div>
<p>Veolia operates waste management services around the world, including the treatment of hazardous and non-hazardous liquid and solid waste. It also deals with the collection and recycling of waste. <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy">Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Veolia bought <a href="/wiki/Dalkia" title="Dalkia">Dalkia</a> in 2001, and ran the business as a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lectricit%C3%A9_de_France" title="Électricité de France">Électricité de France</a> (EDF) until 2014, when it purchased Dalkia's international operations in full, and EDF bought its French operations. The company's services include maintaining and managing heating and cooling systems, making plants more energy efficient, and selecting the most adapted energies. In 2012, Dalkia employed 49,824 employees and recorded revenue of €7.664 billion<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> (France 42%, Continental Europe 24%, Southern Europe 14.5%, Northern Europe 8%, North America 3%, China 1.5%, other countries 7%).</p>
<p>In North America, Veolia Energy traded under the Trigen Energy name until February 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It is the largest operator and developer of efficient <a href="/wiki/District_energy_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="District energy (disambiguation)">district energy</a> (heating, cooling, and <a href="/wiki/Cogeneration" title="Cogeneration">cogeneration</a>) systems in North America, located in ten major U.S. cities. It also provides facility operations, energy management, and advisory services.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Transdev" title="Transdev">Transdev</a></div>
<p>Transdev (formerly Veolia Transdev) was formed in 2011 from a merger of <a href="/wiki/Veolia_Transport" title="Veolia Transport">Veolia Transport</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Transdev_(historic)" title="Transdev (historic)">old Transdev</a>, a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Caisse_des_D%C3%A9p%C3%B4ts" class="mw-redirect" title="Caisse des Dépôts">Caisse des Dépôts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Transdev_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Transdev-8">[8]</a></sup> Currently, Veolia owns 40% of the company's shares.<sup id="cite_ref-transdevshares_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transdevshares-17">[17]</a></sup> Before the merger, Veolia Transport recorded revenues of €7.863 billion in 2011 (For 2010&#160;: Europe 83%, included France 37.1%, North America 13.2%, Asia-Pacific 3.7%). It employed 101,798 people. It worked with public authorities under public-private partnerships to manage public transit systems (buses, trains, metros, ferries, etc.).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 6 December 2011 Veolia Environment, seeking to reduce debt and focus on its core businesses of water, waste and energy, announced that it will eventually sell its share in Veolia Transdev, within a two-year time frame, by when its own activities will have been reorganized. After this announcement, the Caisse des Dépôts et Consignations, for its part, officially reiterated its commitment to Veolia Transdev and its continued support as a shareholder to the group's development.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2012 it was reported that Cube Infrastructure, a fund controlled by the French bank <a href="/wiki/Natixis" title="Natixis">Natixis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Groupe_BPCE" title="Groupe BPCE">Groupe BPCE</a>), was likely to acquire about half of Veolia's stake in Transdev. The Caisse des Dépôts would take over the other half.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> This was not implemented, but instead, Caisse des Dépôts acquired 10% of the shares from Veolia in October 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-transdevshares_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transdevshares-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of January 2016, Transdev employed 83,000 people and recorded a revenue of €6.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_information">Financial information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Financial information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 31 December 2012, shares in Veolia Environnement were held as follows: 9.3% by Caisse des Dépôts et Consignations (primary shareholder), followed by Groupe Industriel Marcel Dassault (6.3%), Groupama (5.42%), Velo Investissement (4.73%), Électricité de France (4.22%), Veolia Environment (2.73%). Public and other institutional investors the remaining 67.3%.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

The company and its top executives were facing the prospect of a U.S. class-action lawsuit in January 2012 over allegations that they made “misleading” statements between 2007 and 2011 about its financial well-being. The company, which was described as "struggling" by the <i>Financial Times</i>, said that a complaint had been filed against it in New York for violation of U.S. federal securities laws. Veolia's shares were the worst performer on France’s CAC 40 index in 2011, falling 60%.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_social_responsibility">Corporate social responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Corporate social responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company’s sustainable development activities are diverse. Because it operates in four sectors with a huge potential impact on the environment, both the risks and opportunities presented by sustainable development activities are substantial. The company’s sustainability efforts are furthered by its Foundation and Institute which emphasize innovation and research.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Veolia_Foundation">The Veolia Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Veolia Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Veolia Foundation supports non-profit activities related to sustainable development, professional continuous development and the protection of the environment in France and overseas. The Foundation supports projects through financial aid and voluntary services provided by its employees. It also supports emergency relief operations in collaboration with humanitarian organisations.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2010_Haiti_earthquake" title="2010 Haiti earthquake">2010 Haiti earthquake</a>, the Veolia Foundation dispatched 30 tons of emergency supplies (mainly water treatment units) via French Red Cross air transportation. The Foundation also sent Veolia technical experts to provide water to the disaster’s victims.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Institut_Veolia">Institut Veolia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Institut Veolia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Institut Veolia was created in 2001 to provide insights into major global challenges such as climate change, urbanisation and various economic, social and cultural issues related to the environment. The institute is built around a committee that brings together seven experts and an international network of researchers and experts. Its activities include organising conferences and publishing articles and reports.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_development">Research and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Research and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 31 December 2009, the Group’s research and development investments reached €89.8 million (€92.1 million in 2008, €84.6 million in 2007).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Research and Innovation division includes 850 experts and supports around 200 scientific partnerships with private and public organisations. The division focuses on four main issues:<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Manage and preserve natural resources</li>
<li>Control impacts on natural environments</li>
<li>Care for health and living environments</li>
<li>Develop alternative sources of energy</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operating_events">Operating events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Operating events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Carrollton_plant_explosion">West Carrollton plant explosion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: West Carrollton plant explosion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 May 2009, a Veolia Environmental Service's plant in <a href="/wiki/West_Carrollton,_Ohio" title="West Carrollton, Ohio">West Carrollton, Ohio</a>, United States, exploded. The blast leveled two buildings on the property which were a laboratory and a building that had several 200,000 gallon chemical tanks. This particular plant handles fuel blending among other services. Two workers at the plant were injured in the blast.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The explosion caused $50 million in damage to the plant itself. More than a dozen homes up to a mile radius from the blast were also damaged due to the explosion.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fatal_accident_in_Gatlinburg">Fatal accident in Gatlinburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Fatal accident in Gatlinburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two workers died after a catastrophic mechanical failure in April 2011 at a waste water treatment plant in <a href="/wiki/Gatlinburg,_Tennessee" title="Gatlinburg, Tennessee">Gatlinburg, Tennessee</a>, United States, owned by the local municipality and operated by Veolia Water. At least 1.5 million gallons of a mix of storm and sewage water were spilled into a nearby river after a sewage-holding wall collapsed.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relations_with_Israel">Relations with Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Relations with Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2011 the <a href="/wiki/Tower_Hamlets_London_Borough_Council" title="Tower Hamlets London Borough Council">Tower Hamlets London Borough Council</a> of the London borough of <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">Tower Hamlets</a>, voted to review its position with Veolia and place no further contracts with it, after claiming that Veolia's work for the Israeli government assisted the "continued oppression of the Palestinian people".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Justice and Peace Commission, part of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> in England, urged London municipalities to stop doing business with Veolia because of its involvement with illegal settlements.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Veolia denied wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Palestinian human-rights organization <a href="/wiki/Al-Haq" title="Al-Haq">Al-Haq</a> instructed lawyers in the Netherlands to submit a formal objection against the decision of <a href="/wiki/Stadsregio_Arnhem_Nijmegen" class="mw-redirect" title="Stadsregio Arnhem Nijmegen">Stadsregio Arnhem Nijmegen</a>, a municipality, to award a public transport concession to Hermes, the Dutch subsidiary of Veolia Transdev. The objection was based on Veolia’s involvement in what Al-Haq claims are Israel’s violations of international law.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a 2012 interview with the Israeli press, Veolia's Denis Gasquet, senior executive vice president, admitted that Veolia had been under pressure from pro-Palestinian groups in Europe, particularly over the <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem_Light_Rail" title="Jerusalem Light Rail">Jerusalem Light Rail</a>. Parties within Veolia had argued that the group was losing tenders as a result, but Gasquest said he did not know of any tenders lost due to Veolia's activities in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. He confirmed Veolia's intention to stay in Israel and occupied Palestine, while exiting the transport business.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 1, 2015, the company announced on its website "Veolia closes the sale of its activities in Israel."<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> This was taken by supporters of the BDS (Boycott Divestment and Sanctions) movement to signify a success for their campaigning efforts: "The sale follows a worldwide campaign against the company’s role in illegal Israeli settlements that cost the firm billions of dollars of lost contracts."<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unpaid_internships">Unpaid internships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Unpaid internships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Veolia offered unpaid <a href="/wiki/Internship" title="Internship">internship</a> positions under the Irish government's <a href="/wiki/JobBridge" title="JobBridge">JobBridge</a> initiative. This scheme was opposed by left-wing political groups, who claim it amounts to free labour and can cause a claimant who refuses many such offers to fall foul of a provision in the Irish Social Welfare that allows benefits to be withdrawn from unemployed who repeatedly refuse work.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flint_water_crisis">Flint water crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veolia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Flint water crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Flint_water_crisis" title="Flint water crisis">Flint water crisis</a></div>
<p>On June 22, 2016, Michigan Attorney General Bill Schuette began its first civil action in the <a href="/wiki/Flint,_Michigan" title="Flint, Michigan">Flint</a> <a href="/wiki/Water_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Water crisis">water crisis</a> by filing suit against Veolia North America and <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_Andrews_%26_Newnam" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockwood, Andrews &amp; Newnam">Lockwood, Andrews &amp; Newnam</a> (LAN) who were hired to consult Flint <a href="/wiki/Water_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Water plant">water plant</a> officials after the switch to the <a href="/wiki/Flint_River_(Michigan)" title="Flint River (Michigan)">Flint River</a> in April 2015. The lawsuit accuses Veolia and LAN of professional <a href="/wiki/Negligence" title="Negligence">negligence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Public_nuisance" title="Public nuisance">public nuisance</a>. Veolia is also accused of <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">fraud</a>. Veolia called the accusations "baseless, entirely unfounded and appears to be intended to distract from the troubling and disturbing realities that have emerged as a result of this tragedy". And added: "In fact, when Veolia raised potential <a href="/wiki/Lead" title="Lead">lead</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> issues, city officials and representatives told us to exclude it from our scope of work because the city and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">EPA</a> were just beginning to conduct lead and copper testing." <sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
