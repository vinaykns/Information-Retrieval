			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Open source appropriate technology</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Open source appropriate technology</b> (<b>OSAT</b>) is <a href="/wiki/Appropriate_technology" title="Appropriate technology">appropriate technology</a> designed in the same fashion as <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source_software" title="Free and open-source software">free and open-source software</a>. OSAT refers to, on the one hand, technology designed with special consideration to the environmental, ethical, cultural, social, political, and economic aspects of the community it is intended for. On the other hand, OSAT is developed in the open and licensed in such a way as to allow their designs to be used, modified and distributed freely.<sup id="cite_ref-Pearce_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pearce-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Open source is a development method for <a href="/wiki/Appropriate_technology" title="Appropriate technology">appropriate technology</a> that harnesses the power of distributed peer review and transparency of process. <a href="/wiki/Appropedia" title="Appropedia">Appropedia</a> is an example of open-source appropriate technology. There anyone can both learn how to make and use AT free of concerns about patents. At the same time anyone can also add to the collective open-source knowledge base by contributing ideas, observations, experimental data, deployment logs, etc. It has been claimed that the potential for open-source-appropriate technology to drive <a href="/wiki/Applied_sustainability" title="Applied sustainability">applied sustainability</a> is enormous.<sup id="cite_ref-buitenhuis_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buitenhuis-3">[3]</a></sup> The built in continuous <a href="/wiki/Peer-review" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer-review">peer-review</a> can result in better quality, higher reliability, and more flexibility than conventional design/patenting of technologies. The free nature of the knowledge also obviously provides lower costs, particularly for those technologies that do not benefit to a large degree from scale of manufacture. Finally, OSAT also enables the end to predatory intellectual property lock-in. This is particularly important in the context of technology focused on relieving suffering and saving lives in the <a href="/wiki/Developing_world" class="mw-redirect" title="Developing world">developing world</a>.</p>
<p>The "open-source" model can act as a driver of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. There are (at least) three good reasons:<sup id="cite_ref-Cascio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cascio-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>It enables production as well as consumption;</li>
<li>It enables localization for communities that do not have the resources to tempt commercial developers to provide local versions of their products;</li>
<li>It can be free as in "gratis" as well as free as in "libre", an important consideration for developing communities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> following the lateral scaling concepts of <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Rifkin" title="Jeremy Rifkin">Jeremy Rifkin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethical_considerations">Ethical considerations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ethical considerations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>for solutions, many researchers, companies, and academics do work on products meant to assist <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. <a href="/wiki/Vinay_Gupta" title="Vinay Gupta">Vinay Gupta</a> has suggested that those developers agree to three principles:<sup id="cite_ref-Gupta_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gupta-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li>I will not permit any human being to be deprived of life-giving technology by the profit motive.</li>
<li>Any works that I patent I will make available to others who are engaged in humanitarian activity for free, except where this would breach other contractual responsibilities.</li>
<li>I will not use patent law to slow the pace of innovation or service delivery to the needy under any circumstances.</li>
</ol>
<p>The ethics of information sharing in this context has been explored in depth.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Support_in_the_literature">Support in the literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Support in the literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>It has been investigated how open sharing of designs, specifications, and technical information can enhance effectiveness, widespread use, and innovation of appropriate technology.<sup id="cite_ref-buitenhuis_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buitenhuis-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>OSAT has been proposed as a new model of enabling innovation for <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zelenika_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zelenika-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>OSAT has been claimed to assist in development of medical technology particularly for the developing world.<sup id="cite_ref-Kapczynski_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kapczynski-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sali_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sali-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>It has been claimed that the sharing of design processes, appropriate tools, and technical information enables more effective and rapid development of appropriate technologies for both industrialized and non-industrialized regions.<sup id="cite_ref-pearce-mushtaq_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pearce-mushtaq-14">[14]</a></sup> In addition, it is claimed that this sharing will require the appropriate-technology community to adopt open standards/licenses, document knowledge, and build on previous work.<sup id="cite_ref-pearce-mushtaq_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pearce-mushtaq-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li>OSAT can be used to generate <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>OSAT in <a href="/wiki/Information_and_communication_technologies" class="mw-redirect" title="Information and communication technologies">ICT</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>OSAT and <a href="/wiki/Peer_production" title="Peer production">peer production</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_education">In education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>At the university level, the use of open-source-appropriate technology classroom projects has been shown to be successful in forging the connection between physics and social benefit:<sup id="cite_ref-Pearce-2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pearce-2-18">[18]</a></sup> This approach has the potential to use university students' access to resources and testing equipment in furthering the development of appropriate technology. Similarly OSAT has been used as a tool for improving <a href="/wiki/Service_learning" class="mw-redirect" title="Service learning">service learning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pearce-3_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pearce-3-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Saleh_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saleh-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> has completed a study looking at the usefulness of using <a href="/wiki/Appropriate_technology" title="Appropriate technology">appropriate technology</a> education and its relation to OSAT.<sup id="cite_ref-Grue_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grue-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>It has been proposed that the evolution of the open-source <a href="/wiki/3D_printer" class="mw-redirect" title="3D printer">3D printers</a> can enable a new method of development for OSAT<sup id="cite_ref-Villumsen_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Villumsen-22">[22]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Zelenika-Zovko_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zelenika-Zovko-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_source_scientific_equipment">Open source scientific equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Open source scientific equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Open_source_hardware" class="mw-redirect" title="Open source hardware">Open source hardware</a> for science can also be considered OSAT. Hundreds of scientific tools have already been developed and shared. Recently (2014) <a href="/wiki/Open_Source_Lab_(book)" class="mw-redirect" title="Open Source Lab (book)">open source labs</a> have been proposed.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evaluation">Evaluation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Open_source_appropriate_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Evaluation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Appropriate technology is designed to promote <a href="/wiki/Decentralization" title="Decentralization">decentralized</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labor-intensive" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor-intensive">labor-intensive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy-efficient</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmentally_sound" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmentally sound">environmentally sound</a> businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-BrownU_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrownU-25">[25]</a></sup> Carroll Pursell says that the movement declined from 1965 to 1985, due to an inability to counter advocates of agribusiness, large private utilities, and multinational construction companies.<sup id="cite_ref-Pursell_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pursell-26">[26]</a></sup> Recently (2011), several barriers to OSAT deployment have been identified:<sup id="cite_ref-Zelenika-Pearce_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zelenika-Pearce-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>AT seen as inferior or "poor person's" technology</li>
<li>Technical transferability and robustness of AT</li>
<li>Insufficient funding</li>
<li>Weak institutional support</li>
<li>The challenges of distance and time in tackling rural poverty.</li>
</ul>
