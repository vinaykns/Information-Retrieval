<p><b>Greater Gabbard</b> is a 504&#160;MW <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> on sandbanks 23 kilometres (14&#160;mi) off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> at a cost of £1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-4cUK-GG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4cUK-GG-2">[2]</a></sup> Onshore construction activities commenced in early July 2008 at Sizewell. It was completed on 7 September 2012 with all of the <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">Siemens</a> SWT3.6-107 turbines connected.<sup id="cite_ref-windpowermonthly.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-windpowermonthly.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Gabbard_wind_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The project was originally developed by Greater Gabbard Offshore Winds Limited (GGOWL) which was a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Airtricity" title="Airtricity">Airtricity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fluor_Corp." class="mw-redirect" title="Fluor Corp.">Fluor</a>. Airtricity was subsequently bought by <a href="/wiki/Scottish_and_Southern_Energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish and Southern Energy">Scottish and Southern Energy</a> who have bought out Fluor's 50% stake for £40m which is expected to cost £1.3b. Fluor are now contracted to design, supply, installation and commissioning of the balance of the plant.<sup id="cite_ref-projectPage_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-projectPage-4">[4]</a></sup> Scottish and Southern sold a 50% stake to <a href="/wiki/RWE" title="RWE">RWE</a>, the owners of <a href="/wiki/Npower_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Npower (UK)">Npower (UK)</a>, in November 2008 for £308m.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The project was given the go-ahead in May 2008 and work started in June.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In July 2011 erection of the turbines was two thirds complete, with all the pile foundations installed.</p>
<p>In October 2009 Seajacks Ltd delivered its 7,000 tonne Leviathan vessel to Fluor Ltd which sailed to <a href="/wiki/Harwich" title="Harwich">Harwich</a> to prepare the hook-up and commissioning of an in-field substation and then installation of the turbines.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The first foundations were installed in autumn 2009 with the first of a total of 140&#160;turbines installed in the spring 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Electricity generation began on 29 December 2010 and construction was completed on 7 September 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-windpowermonthly.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-windpowermonthly.com-3">[3]</a></sup> During April 2014 to March 2015 the wind farm produced 1.7 TWh, corresponding to a <a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">capacity factor</a> of 39%.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wind farm may be seen by the public from the <a href="/wiki/Stena_Line" title="Stena Line">Stena Line</a> <a href="/wiki/Harwich" title="Harwich">Harwich</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hook_of_Holland" title="Hook of Holland">Hook of Holland</a> car &amp; passenger ferry, the route passing within a few kilometres.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Galloper_extension">Galloper extension</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Gabbard_wind_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Galloper extension">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An extension of the project, called Galloper, was agreed in May 2013. The proposal was to add up to 140 turbines to the development, producing up to 504&#160;MW of electricity. The wind farm was expected to be completed in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-gov24may13_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gov24may13-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc24may13_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc24may13-11">[11]</a></sup> The project was being developed in partnership by <a href="/wiki/RWE_Innogy" class="mw-redirect" title="RWE Innogy">RWE Innogy</a> and <a href="/wiki/SSE_plc" title="SSE plc">SSE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ren1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren1-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013 the project capacity was reduced to 68 turbines producing 340 MW. In March 2014, SSE announced that it would be pulling out of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-ren1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren1-12">[12]</a></sup> In October 2014 RWE Innogy announced that it too was abandoning the project,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> but in December the company revealed that it was still seeking potential investment partners.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Greater_Gabbard_specifications">Greater Gabbard specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Gabbard_wind_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Greater Gabbard specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Number of turbines: 140<sup id="cite_ref-4cUK-GG_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4cUK-GG-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LORC_data_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LORC_data-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>Power rating: 504&#160;MW<sup id="cite_ref-LORC_data_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LORC_data-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">Load factor</a>: 39.6% (estimated)<sup id="cite_ref-LORC_data_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LORC_data-15">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li>Estimated output: 1.75&#160;<a href="/wiki/TWh" class="mw-redirect" title="TWh">TWh</a> per year<sup id="cite_ref-LORC_data_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LORC_data-15">[15]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents">Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Gabbard_wind_farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 12 November 2009, a man was killed and a woman injured after a chain broke and the two people were hit with pieces of the chain. Police responded to the incident, and an investigation was launched. The casualties were on board a <a href="/wiki/Tugboat" title="Tugboat">tugboat</a>, the <i>Typhoon</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 May 2010, a man died and another suffered serious injuries following an accident at <a href="/wiki/Harwich_International_Port" title="Harwich International Port">Parkeston Quay</a>, Harwich. A Siemens engineer from Norresundby, Denmark, died in the incident. A 43-year-old German national was taken to Addenbrooke’s Hospital. The incident happened at about 7.50 am while loading a wind turbine blade on the vessel <i>Seajack</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TerpVertikal_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TerpVertikal-20">[20]</a></sup> Siemens and Fluor were ordered to pay £1 million for the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2013 remedial work was begun on the export cables close to shore as the cables were not buried sufficiently deeply.<sup id="cite_ref-eadt1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eadt1-22">[22]</a></sup> The work which was meant to take three weeks was only finished in September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-eadt1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eadt1-22">[22]</a></sup> The work caused problems for local fishermen who asked the Greater Gabbard Offshore Wind Farm for a disruption payment.<sup id="cite_ref-eadt1_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eadt1-22">[22]</a></sup> A spokesman for SSE denied that the work was responsible for the snagging of fishing nets.<sup id="cite_ref-eadt1_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eadt1-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
