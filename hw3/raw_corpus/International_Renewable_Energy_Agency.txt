<p>The <b>International Renewable Energy Agency</b> (<b>IRENA</b>) is an intergovernmental organization to promote adoption and sustainable use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>. It was founded in 2009 and its statute entered into force on 8 July 2010. The agency is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gulf2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gulf2-1">[1]</a></sup> The Director-General of IRENA is Adnan Amin, a national of Kenya.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Renewable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first suggestions for an international renewable agency is based on the 1980 <a href="/wiki/Brandt_Report" title="Brandt Report">Brandt Report</a> activities. NGOs and industry lobbying groups like <a href="/wiki/Eurosolar" title="Eurosolar">Eurosolar</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/World_Council_for_Renewable_Energy" title="World Council for Renewable Energy">World Council for Renewable Energy</a> (WCRE) and the <a href="/wiki/World_Wind_Energy_Association" title="World Wind Energy Association">World Wind Energy Association</a> have promoted IRENA since several decades.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 1990 the Austrian government of <a href="/wiki/Franz_Vranitzky" title="Franz Vranitzky">Franz Vranitzky</a> suggested a reneweables agency to the UN.<sup id="cite_ref-lw_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lw-5">[5]</a></sup> One of the drivers was <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Scheer" title="Hermann Scheer">Hermann Scheer</a>, a German politician and lobbyist which was acting as president of <a href="/wiki/Eurosolar" title="Eurosolar">EUROSOLAR</a> and chair of WCRE.<sup id="cite_ref-lw_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lw-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 June, at their annual event, the WWEA gave their 2010 World Wind Energy Award to the Founding member States of IRENA. They stated: "The creation of IRENA can be seen as the most important decision ever taken on the global level in favour of renewable energy. The founding of IRENA sent out a very strong signal to the world community that renewable energy will have to play and will play a key role in the future energy supply all over the world. With the Award, WWEA would also like to indicate that WWEA is committed to work closely with IRENA and will continue to give its full support."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>54&#160;countries participated in the Preparatory Conference for the Foundation of the International Renewable Energy Agency in Berlin, held on 10 and 11 April 2008. This meeting followed several other meetings since 1981 which also discussed the formation of IRENA. At Berlin, government representatives met to discuss the objectives, activities, finances, and organizational structure of IRENA. Participants expressed a need to begin a swift transition to a more secure, sustainable renewable energy economy with the assistance of an international body.<sup id="cite_ref-ren_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ren-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Founding Conference of the International Renewable Energy Agency was held in <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a>, Germany, on 26 January 2009. 75 countries signed the Agency's statute. The statute entered into force on 8 July 2010, thirty days after the twenty-fifth country deposits its instrument of ratification. The Founding Conference established the Preparatory Commission for IRENA, which consists of all signatory states.</p>
<p>During the first session of the Preparatory Commission in Bonn on 27 January 2009, the signatory countries adopted criteria and procedures for selecting IRENA's Interim Director-General and its interim headquarters. An Administrative Committee was created to assist the Commission in preparing its second session. The Administrative Committee prepared draft proposals for an interim work programme and budget as well as for interim staff regulations and interim financial rules.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Nominations for the Interim Director-General and the interim headquarters were submitted by 30 April 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second session of the Preparatory Commission met in <a href="/wiki/Sharm_el-Sheikh" class="mw-redirect" title="Sharm el-Sheikh">Sharm el-Sheikh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, on 29–30 June 2009, to elect the Interim Director General and decide the location of IRENA's <a href="/wiki/Interim" title="Interim">interim</a> headquarters. It was decided, that the interim headquarters will be located in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. The UAE thus became the first developing country to host a major international organisation. In addition, an innovation and technology center will be located in Bonn, and an office dedicated to liaising with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and other international institutions will be located in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gulf1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gulf1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dw1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dw1-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9l%C3%A8ne_Pelosse" title="Hélène Pelosse">Hélène Pelosse</a> was elected as the Interim Director-General.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The second session of the Preparatory Commission also adopted an interim work programme and budget as well as for interim staff regulations and interim financial rules.</p>
<p>The fourth session of the Preparatory Commission on 24–25 October 2010 in Abu Dhabi appointed Kenyan representative Adnan Amin, Deputy Interim Director-General, to perform functions of Interim Director-General after resignation of Hélène Pelosse.<sup id="cite_ref-recharge261010_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-recharge261010-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 4 April 2011, Adnan Amin was sworn in as the first Director-General.</p>
<p>The agency has staged a number of events bringing together member states for interaction on ways and means of furthering renewable energy, and conducted significant research and development into viable solutions for the future. On 8 September 2014, IRENA published a notable report on its works titled <i>REthinking energy</i>, which encouraged "speedier adoption of renewable energy technologies," as "the most feasible route to reduce carbon emissions and avoid catastrophic climate change." The study set out to gauge the global power sector and establish how technological advances, economic growth and climate change are transforming it. "A convergence of social, economic and environmental forces are transforming the global energy system as we know it. But if we continue on the path we are currently on and fuel our growing economies with outmoded ways of thinking and acting, we will not be able avoid the most serious impacts of climate change," Director-General Amin said at a function to release the report.<sup id="cite_ref-REthinkingEnergy_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-REthinkingEnergy-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ninth Meeting of International Renewable Energy Agency Council would be held on 10–11 June 2015 at Abu Dhabi.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">Aims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Renewable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>IRENA aims to become the main driving force in promoting a transition towards the use of renewable energy on a global scale:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Acting as the global voice for renewable energies, IRENA will provide practical advice and support for both industrialised and developing countries, help them improve their regulatory frameworks and build capacity. The agency will facilitate access to all relevant information including reliable data on the potential of renewable energy, best practices, effective financial mechanisms and state-of-the-art technological expertise.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>IRENA provides advice and support to governments on <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Renewable energy policy">renewable energy policy</a>, capacity building, and technology transfer. IRENA will also co-ordinate with existing renewable energy organizations, such as <a href="/wiki/REN21" title="REN21">REN21</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_states">Member states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Renewable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Member states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of July 2015, 143 States are members of IRENA, and further 29 are in the process of accession. Two notable exceptions from the participating states and applicants are Brazil and Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-IRENA_States_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IRENA_States-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Nations">United Nations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Renewable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United Nations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Various UN organisations work in the field of renewable energy. But IRENA is the only one dedicated to the promotion of 100% renewable energy worldwide. IRENA and the UN will join forces to speed up the transformation of the global energy sector.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Hélène Pelosse, former Interim Director General of IRENA, met with UN Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> during 2009 Climate week in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and together with Mr Ban explored future fields of cooperation between IRENA and various UN bodies. IRENA also seeks to cooperate with the UN and associated organisations like the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_University" title="United Nations University">United Nations University</a>, UNESCO, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, GEF, <a href="/wiki/UNIDO" class="mw-redirect" title="UNIDO">UNIDO</a>, <a href="/wiki/UNDP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNDP">UNDP</a>, <a href="/wiki/UNEP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNEP">UNEP</a>, and <a href="/wiki/WTO" class="mw-redirect" title="WTO">WTO</a> in the areas of education and training, financing, access to energy, potential studies and trade.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Director-General">Director-General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Renewable_Energy_Agency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Director-General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first director-general of IRENA is the current Director General <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adnan_Z._Amin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adnan Z. Amin (page does not exist)">Adnan Z. Amin</a>. He was elected to the post in April 2011. He previously spent more than 25 years in the development of international environment and sustainable development policy, and worked in political, management, and interagency coordination areas of the United Nations. He was Head of the UN System Chief Executives Board for Coordination (CEB) Secretariat.<sup id="cite_ref-IRENAstates_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IRENAstates-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
