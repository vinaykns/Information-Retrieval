<div role="note" class="hatnote">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Environmental_studies" title="Environmental studies">Environmental studies</a>.</div>
<p><b>Environmental science</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinary" class="mw-redirect" title="Interdisciplinary">interdisciplinary</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic field">academic field</a> that integrates physical, biological and <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information sciences</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zoology" title="Zoology">zoology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mineralogy" title="Mineralogy">mineralogy</a>, oceanology, <a href="/wiki/Limnology" title="Limnology">limnology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soil_science" title="Soil science">soil science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Atmospheric science">atmospheric science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Geodesy" title="Geodesy">geodesy</a>) to the study of the environment, and the solution of environmental problems. Environmental science emerged from the fields of <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">natural history</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment" title="Age of Enlightenment">Enlightenment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Today it provides an integrated, quantitative, and <a href="/wiki/Interdisciplinary" class="mw-redirect" title="Interdisciplinary">interdisciplinary</a> approach to the study of <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">environmental systems</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Related areas of study include <a href="/wiki/Environmental_studies" title="Environmental studies">environmental studies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_engineering" title="Environmental engineering">environmental engineering</a>. Environmental studies incorporates more of the social sciences for understanding human relationships, perceptions and policies towards the environment. Environmental engineering focuses on design and technology for improving environmental quality in every aspect.</p>
<p>Environmental scientists work on subjects like the understanding of earth processes, evaluating <a href="/wiki/Alternative_energy" title="Alternative energy">alternative energy</a> systems, <a href="/wiki/Pollution_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Pollution control">pollution control</a> and mitigation, <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource_management" title="Natural resource management">natural resource management</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_global_warming" title="Effects of global warming">effects of global climate change</a>. <a href="/wiki/Environmental_issues" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental issues">Environmental issues</a> almost always include an interaction of physical, chemical, and biological processes. Environmental scientists bring a systems approach to the analysis of environmental problems. Key elements of an effective environmental scientist include the ability to relate space, and time relationships as well as quantitative analysis.</p>
<p>Environmental science came alive as a substantive, active field of scientific investigation in the 1960s and 1970s driven by (a) the need for a <a href="/wiki/Multi-disciplinary" class="mw-redirect" title="Multi-disciplinary">multi-disciplinary</a> approach to analyze complex environmental problems, (b) the arrival of substantive environmental laws requiring specific environmental protocols of investigation and (c) the growing public awareness of a need for action in addressing environmental problems. Events that spurred this development included the publication of Rachel Carson's landmark environmental book <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Spring" title="Silent Spring">Silent Spring</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></i> along with major environmental issues becoming very public, such as the <a href="/wiki/1969_Santa_Barbara_oil_spill" title="1969 Santa Barbara oil spill">1969 Santa Barbara oil spill</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cuyahoga_River" title="Cuyahoga River">Cuyahoga River</a> of Cleveland, Ohio, "catching fire" (also in 1969), and helped increase the visibility of environmental issues and create this new field of study.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_environmental_science" title="Glossary of environmental science">Glossary of environmental science</a></div>
<p>In common usage, "environmental science" and "ecology" are often used interchangeably, but technically, ecology refers only to the study of organisms and their interactions with each other and their environment. Ecology could be considered a subset of environmental science, which also could involve purely chemical or <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> issues (for example) ecologists would be unlikely to study. In practice, there is considerable overlap between the work of ecologists and other environmental scientists.</p>
<p>The National Center for Education Statistics in the United States defines an academic program in environmental science as follows:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>A program that focuses on the application of biological, chemical, and physical principles to the study of the physical environment and the solution of environmental problems, including subjects such as abating or controlling environmental pollution and degradation; the interaction between human society and the natural environment; and natural resources management. Includes instruction in biology, chemistry, physics, geosciences, climatology, statistics, and mathematical modeling.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atmospheric_sciences">Atmospheric sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Atmospheric sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_sciences" title="Atmospheric sciences">Atmospheric sciences</a></div>
<p>Atmospheric sciences focus on the Earth's atmosphere, with an emphasis upon its interrelation to other systems. Atmospheric sciences can include studies of <a href="/wiki/Meteorological" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorological">meteorology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> phenomena, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_dispersion_modeling" title="Atmospheric dispersion modeling">atmospheric dispersion modeling</a> of airborne contaminants,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> sound propagation phenomena related to <a href="/wiki/Noise_pollution" title="Noise pollution">noise pollution</a>, and even <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a>.</p>
<p>Taking the example of the <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> phenomena, physicists create <a href="/wiki/Computer_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer model">computer models</a> of atmospheric circulation and <a href="/wiki/Infra-red_radiation" class="mw-redirect" title="Infra-red radiation">infra-red radiation</a> transmission, chemists examine the inventory of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atmospheric_chemical&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Atmospheric chemical (page does not exist)">atmospheric chemicals</a> and their reactions, biologists analyze the plant and animal contributions to <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> fluxes, and specialists such as <a href="/wiki/Meteorologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Meteorologist">meteorologists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oceanographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceanographer">oceanographers</a> add additional breadth in understanding the <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_dynamics" class="mw-redirect" title="Atmospheric dynamics">atmospheric dynamics</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">Ecology</a></div>
<p>Ecology is the study of the interactions between organisms and their environment. Ecologists might investigate the relationship between a population of organisms and some physical characteristic of their environment, such as concentration of a chemical; or they might investigate the interaction between two populations of different organisms through some symbiotic or competitive relationship.</p>
<p>For example, an interdisciplinary analysis of an ecological system which is being impacted by one or more stressors might include several related environmental science fields. In an estuarine setting where a proposed industrial development could impact certain species by <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biologist" title="Biologist">biologists</a> would describe the flora and fauna, <a href="/wiki/Chemist" title="Chemist">chemists</a> would analyze the transport of <a href="/wiki/Water_pollutant" class="mw-redirect" title="Water pollutant">water pollutants</a> to the marsh, <a href="/wiki/Physicist" title="Physicist">physicists</a> would calculate air pollution emissions and <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologists</a> would assist in understanding the marsh soils and bay muds.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_chemistry">Environmental chemistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environmental chemistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Environmental_chemistry" title="Environmental chemistry">Environmental chemistry</a></div>
<p>Environmental chemistry is the study of chemical alterations in the environment. Principal areas of study include <a href="/wiki/Soil_contamination" title="Soil contamination">soil contamination</a> and water pollution. The topics of analysis include chemical degradation in the environment, multi-phase transport of chemicals (for example, evaporation of a <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">solvent</a> containing lake to yield solvent as an air pollutant), and chemical effects upon <a href="/wiki/Biota" class="mw-disambig" title="Biota">biota</a>.</p>
<p>As an example study, consider the case of a leaking solvent tank which has entered the habitat soil of an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> of amphibian. As a method to resolve or understand the extent of <a href="/wiki/Soil_contamination" title="Soil contamination">soil contamination</a> and subsurface transport of solvent, a <a href="/wiki/Computer_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer model">computer model</a> would be implemented. Chemists would then characterize the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Molecular_bonding&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Molecular bonding (page does not exist)">molecular bonding</a> of the solvent to the specific soil type, and biologists would study the impacts upon soil <a href="/wiki/Arthropod" title="Arthropod">arthropods</a>, plants, and ultimately pond-dwelling organisms that are the food of the endangered amphibian.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geosciences">Geosciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Geosciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Geosciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Geosciences">Geosciences</a></div>
<p>Geosciences include <a href="/wiki/Environmental_geology" title="Environmental geology">environmental geology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_soil_science" title="Environmental soil science">environmental soil science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volcanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Volcanic">volcanic</a> phenomena and evolution of the Earth's crust. In some classification systems this can also include <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>.</p>
<p>As an example study of soils <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a>, calculations would be made of <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">surface runoff</a> by soil scientists. Fluvial geomorphologists would assist in examining <a href="/wiki/Sediment_transport" title="Sediment transport">sediment transport</a> in overland flow. Physicists would contribute by assessing the changes in light transmission in the receiving waters. Biologists would analyze subsequent impacts to aquatic flora and fauna from increases in water turbidity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulations_driving_the_studies">Regulations driving the studies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_science&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Regulations driving the studies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In the U.S. the <a href="/wiki/National_Environmental_Policy_Act" title="National Environmental Policy Act">National Environmental Policy Act</a> (NEPA) of 1969 set forth requirements for analysis of major projects in terms of specific environmental criteria. Numerous state laws have echoed these mandates, applying the principles to local-scale actions. The upshot has been an explosion of documentation and study of environmental consequences before the fact of development actions.</p>
<p>One can examine the specifics of environmental science by reading examples of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Impact_Statement" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Impact Statement">Environmental Impact Statements</a> prepared under NEPA such as: <i>Wastewater treatment expansion options discharging into the San Diego/<a href="/wiki/Tijuana" title="Tijuana">Tijuana</a> <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">Estuary</a></i>, <i>Expansion of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_International_Airport" title="San Francisco International Airport">San Francisco International Airport</a></i>, <i>Development of the <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Metro Transportation system</i>, <i>Expansion of the metropolitan <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> MBTA transit system</i>, and <i>Construction of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_66" title="Interstate 66">Interstate 66</a> through <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Virginia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arlington, Virginia">Arlington, Virginia</a></i>.</p>
<p>In England and Wales the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a> (EA),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> formed in 1996, is a public body for protecting and improving the environment and enforces the regulations listed on the communities and local government site.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> (formerly the office of the deputy prime minister). The agency was set up under the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Act_1995" title="Environment Act 1995">Environment Act 1995</a> as an independent body and works closely with UK Government to enforce the regulations.</p>
