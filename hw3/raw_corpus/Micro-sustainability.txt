			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Micro-sustainability</h1>

<p><b>Micro-sustainability</b> focuses on the small <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environmental</a> actions that collectively result in a large <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental impact">environmental impact</a>. Micro-sustainability centers on individual efforts, <a href="/wiki/Behavior_modification" title="Behavior modification">behavior modification</a> and creating attitudinal changes, which result in an environmentally conscious individual or community. Micro-sustainability encourages sustainable changes through "change agents", which are individuals that are encouraged; and therefore, foster positive environmental action inside their <a href="/wiki/Sphere_of_influence" title="Sphere of influence">sphere of influence</a>. Examples of micro-sustainability include <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">recycling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Power_saving" class="mw-redirect" title="Power saving">power saving</a> by turning off unused lights, programming <a href="/wiki/Thermostat" title="Thermostat">thermostats</a> for efficient <a href="/wiki/Energy_use" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy use">use of energy</a>, reducing <a href="/wiki/Water_usage" class="mw-redirect" title="Water usage">water usage</a>, changing <a href="/w/index.php?title=Driving_habits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Driving habits (page does not exist)">driving habits</a> or patterns in order to use less gasoline or modifying <a href="/w/index.php?title=Buying_habits&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Buying habits (page does not exist)">buying habits</a> to reduce waste and <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a>. The focus is on individual actions, rather than organizational practices. These narrow, small ticket, community level actions have immediate local benefits. If widely imitated, they have a cumulative,<sup id="cite_ref-transformative_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transformative-1">[1]</a></sup> broader impact.</p>
<p>The remaining large-scale plans for <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, categorized under the term <i>macro-sustainability</i>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> are in most cases addressed by governments, multi-national corporations or companies. They combat global issues including <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, and reliance upon <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>-based energy sources. Businesses primarily focus on the business case and return on investment of changes such as their source of energy or the way they transport or manufacture products. Governments confront these larger issues through increased regulation, subsidies, and investment in new technologies and energy sources.</p>
