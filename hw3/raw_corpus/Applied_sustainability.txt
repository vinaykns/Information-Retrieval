			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Applied sustainability</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Applied sustainability</b> is the application of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">innovation</a>, including the insights of the social sciences, to meet human needs while indefinitely preserving the <a href="/wiki/Life_support" title="Life support">life support</a> systems of the <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planet</a>.</p>
<p>Note that this is a significant difference from the standard definition of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> that normally is encapsulated by some version of the <a href="/wiki/Brundtland_Commission" title="Brundtland Commission">Brundtland Commission</a>'s concept: “development that meets the needs and aspirations of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs”<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Just_applied_sustainability">Just applied sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_sustainability&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Just applied sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A more refined definition would be called <b>just applied sustainability</b>: the just and equitable application of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">innovation</a>, including the social sciences, to ensure a better quality of life for all, now and into the future whilst living within the limits of supporting <a href="/wiki/Ecosystems" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecosystems">ecosystems</a>. This comes from the definition of Just Sustainability, which is “the <a href="/wiki/Egalitarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Egalitarian">egalitarian</a> conception of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>"(Jacobs, 1999:32).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It generates an improved definition of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> as “the need to ensure a better <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a> for all, now and into the future, in a <a href="/wiki/Justice" title="Justice">just</a> and equitable manner, whilst living within the limits of supporting <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a>” (Agyeman, et al., 2003:5).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This new form of sustainable development prioritizes justice and equity, while maintaining the importance of the environment and the global life support system.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synonymous_with_applied_science">Synonymous with applied science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_sustainability&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Synonymous with applied science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The relationship between <b>applied sustainability</b> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_science" title="Sustainability science">sustainability science</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> is analogous relationship between <a href="/wiki/Applied_science" title="Applied science">applied science</a> (<a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Basic_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Basic science">basic science</a>. Whereas science is the effort to discover, understand, or to understand better, how the physical world works, with observable physical evidence as the basis of that understanding. Applied science is the application of knowledge from one or more natural scientific fields to solve practical problems.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainable_engineering">Sustainable engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_sustainability&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sustainable engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Applied sustainability is essentially <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_engineering" title="Sustainable engineering">sustainable engineering</a> – by utilizing natural laws and physical resources in order to design and implement materials, <a href="/wiki/Structures" class="mw-redirect" title="Structures">structures</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machines" class="mw-redirect" title="Machines">machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gadget" title="Gadget">devices</a>, <a href="/wiki/Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Systems">systems</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Process_(engineering)" title="Process (engineering)">processes</a> that meets human need while preserving the <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a> forever. Applied sustainability is made up of work in engineering, policy, and education – whatever methods are necessary to conserve the world for our children. A recent study has shown that <a href="/wiki/Open_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Open source">open source</a> principles <sup id="cite_ref-ray2000_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ray2000-5">[5]</a></sup> can be used to accelerate deployment of sustainable technologies <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> such as <a href="/wiki/Open_source_appropriate_technologies" class="mw-redirect" title="Open source appropriate technologies">open source appropriate technologies</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clarification_regarding_terminology">Clarification regarding terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Applied_sustainability&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Clarification regarding terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">Sustainability</a>, itself, is a term that is often confused because in its most basic form it is a characteristic of a process or state that can be maintained at a certain level indefinitely. When used in the context of development, as <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>, it is a pattern of resource use that aims to meet human needs while preserving the environment so that these needs can be met not only in the present, but in the indefinite future. The most evolved definition of sustainability is that of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Just_sustainability&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Just sustainability (page does not exist)">just sustainability</a> - “the need to ensure a better quality of life for all, now and into the future, in a just and equitable manner, whilst living within the limits of supporting ecosystems”.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
