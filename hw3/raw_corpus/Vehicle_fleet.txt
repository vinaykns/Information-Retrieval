<p><b>Fleet vehicles</b> are groups of <a href="/wiki/Motor_vehicle" title="Motor vehicle">motor vehicles</a> owned or leased by a business, government agency or other organization rather than by an individual or family. Typical examples are vehicles operated by <a href="/wiki/Car_rental" title="Car rental">car rental companies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taxicab" title="Taxicab">taxicab companies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_utility" title="Public utility">public utilities</a>, public bus companies, and police departments. In addition, many businesses purchase or lease fleet vehicles to deliver <a href="/wiki/Good_(economics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Good (economics)">goods</a> to customers, or for sales representatives to travel to clients.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_vehicle_fleet">Federal vehicle fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fleet_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Federal vehicle fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Presidential_State_Car_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential State Car (United States)">Presidential State Car (United States)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Federal_vehicle_fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal vehicle fleet">Federal vehicle fleet</a></div>
<p>In the United States, Federal Vehicle fleets refers to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">federal government</a>'s vehicles.</p>
<p>Fleet leasing in the UK is very much the same as in the USA; business van leasing is popular among the construction industry as a valuable asset. Fleet leasing is popular with much larger businesses with the ability to get great deals and discounts on multi leasing vehicles.</p>
