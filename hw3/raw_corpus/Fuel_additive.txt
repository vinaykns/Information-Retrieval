<p><b>Gasoline additives</b> increase <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">gasoline</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Octane_rating" title="Octane rating">octane rating</a> or act as <a href="/wiki/Corrosion_inhibitor" title="Corrosion inhibitor">corrosion inhibitors</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lubricant" title="Lubricant">lubricants</a>, thus allowing the use of higher <a href="/wiki/Compression_ratio" title="Compression ratio">compression ratios</a> for greater efficiency and power. Types of additives include <a href="/wiki/Metal_deactivator" title="Metal deactivator">metal deactivators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corrosion_inhibitor" title="Corrosion inhibitor">corrosion inhibitors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxygenate" title="Oxygenate">oxygenates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antioxidant" title="Antioxidant">antioxidants</a>.</p>
<p>Some additives are harmful and are regulated or banned in some countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislation">Legislation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_gasoline_additives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_gasoline_additives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fuel additives in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> are regulated under <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/7545">section 211</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Clean_Air_Act_(United_States)" title="Clean Air Act (United States)">Clean Air Act</a> (as amended in January 1995). The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) requires the registration of all fuel additives which are commercially distributed for use in highway motor vehicles in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and may require testing and ban harmful additives. The EPA also regularly reviews the health and net economic benefits of Clean Air Act policies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The act also requires <a href="/w/index.php?title=Deposit_control_additive&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Deposit control additive (page does not exist)">deposit control additives</a> (DCAs) be added to all gasolines. This type of additive is a <a href="/wiki/Detergent" title="Detergent">detergent</a> additive that acts as a cleansing agent in small passages in the <a href="/wiki/Carburetor" title="Carburetor">carburetor</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fuel_injector" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuel injector">fuel injectors</a>. This in turn serves to ensure a consistent <a href="/wiki/Air-fuel_ratio" class="mw-redirect" title="Air-fuel ratio">air and fuel mixture</a> that will contribute to better <a href="/wiki/Gas_mileage" class="mw-redirect" title="Gas mileage">gas mileage</a>.</p>
