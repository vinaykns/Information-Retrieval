<p>The <b>Waldpolenz Solar Park</b> is a 52-<a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">megawatt</a> (MW) <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_power_station" title="Photovoltaic power station">photovoltaic power station</a> built by German developer and operator <a href="/wiki/Juwi" title="Juwi">Juwi</a> at a former military air base near <a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Germany">Eastern Germany</a>. When completed by the end of 2008, it was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_photovoltaic_power_stations" title="List of photovoltaic power stations">world's largest</a> <a href="/wiki/Thin_film_solar_cell" class="mw-redirect" title="Thin film solar cell">thin-film</a> solar park using <a href="/wiki/Cadmium_telluride_photovoltaics" title="Cadmium telluride photovoltaics">CdTe-modules</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initially, the <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar power</a> plant's <a href="/wiki/Nameplate_capacity" title="Nameplate capacity">nameplate capacity</a> was 40 MW, consisting of 500,000 state-of-the-art <a href="/wiki/Solar_panels" class="mw-redirect" title="Solar panels">solar panels</a> provided by U.S. manufacturer <a href="/wiki/First_Solar" title="First Solar">First Solar</a>, and generated 40,000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Megawatt-hour" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt-hour">MWh</a> of electricity per year.<sup id="cite_ref-solarbuzz.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-solarbuzz.com-2">[2]</a></sup> The solar park was then extended with another 153,650 panels, also provided by First Solar, to a final capacity of 52&#160;MW<sub>P</sub> in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The installation is located in the <a href="/wiki/Muldentalkreis" title="Muldentalkreis">Muldentalkreis</a> district in the state of <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> in eastern Germany, built on half of the location’s 220 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectares</a> (543.6 acres)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in the townships of <a href="/wiki/Brandis,_Germany" title="Brandis, Germany">Brandis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bennewitz" title="Bennewitz">Bennewitz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The investment costs for the Waldpolenz solar park have amounted to some 130 million euro.<sup id="cite_ref-solarbuzz.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-solarbuzz.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
