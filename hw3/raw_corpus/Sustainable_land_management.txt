			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sustainable land management</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Sustainable land management</b> (SLM<sup id="cite_ref-worldbank_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldbank-1">[1]</a></sup>) refers to practices and technologies that aim to integrate the <a href="/wiki/Land_management" title="Land management">management of land</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Water management">water</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Environmental_resource_management" title="Environmental resource management">environmental resources</a> to meet human needs while ensuring the long-term <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a> services and livelihoods. The term sustainable land management is used, for example, in <a href="/wiki/Regional_planning" title="Regional planning">regional planning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">soil</a> or <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">environmental protection</a>, as well as in property and estate management.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_land_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> defines sustainable land management as a process in a charged environment between <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">environmental protection</a> and the guarantee claim of <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">ecosystem services</a> on the one hand. On the other hand, it is about productivity of <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> with respect to <a href="/wiki/Demographic_growth" class="mw-redirect" title="Demographic growth">demographic growth</a> and increasing pressure in <a href="/wiki/Land_consumption" title="Land consumption">land use</a>.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>SLM is defined as a knowledge-based procedure that helps integrate land, water, biodiversity, and environmental management (including input and output externalities) to meet rising food and fiber demands while sustaining ecosystem services and livelihoods. SLM is necessary to meet the requirements of a growing population. Improper land management can lead to <a href="/wiki/Land_degradation" title="Land degradation">land degradation</a> and a significant reduction in the productive and service (biodiversity niches, <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">carbon sequestration</a>) functions of watersheds and landscapes."<sup id="cite_ref-worldbank_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldbank-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="templatequotecite"><cite>— The World Bank</cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe" title="United Nations Economic Commission for Europe">United Nations Economic Commission for Europe</a> (UNECE) applies the term in a much wider context. Besides agriculture and forestry they include the mineral extraction sector, property and estate management.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Land management is the process by which the resources of land are put to good effect. It covers all activities concerned with the management of land as a resource both from an environmental and from an economic perspective. It can include farming, mineral extraction, property and estate management, and the physical planning of towns and the countryside.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In the course of national politics and programmes, few <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> states use the terminology "sustainable land management". Here <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> are to be mentioned, as both countries have agreed on sustainable land management with respect to climate change as part of their government programmes.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the European context, the definition of the European Network for Land Use Management for Sustainable European Cities (LUMASEC)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> may be used as a reference. It emphasizes the inter- and transdisciplinary cooperation on sustainable land management:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>As management is the human activity meaning the action of people working together in the aim to accomplish desired goals, land use management is a process of managing use and development of land, in which spatial, sector-oriented and temporary aspects of urban policy are coordinated. Resources of land are used for different purposes, which may produce conflicts and competitions, and land use management has to see those purposes in an integrated way. Therefore, land management covers the debate about norms and visions driving the policy-making, sector-based planning both in the strategic and more operative time spans, spatial integration of sectoral issues, decision-making, budgeting, implementation of plans and decisions and the monitoring of results and evaluation of impacts.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_examples">Research examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_land_management&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2010, the <a href="/wiki/German_Federal_Ministry_of_Education_and_Research" class="mw-redirect" title="German Federal Ministry of Education and Research">German Federal Ministry of Education and Research</a> (BMBF) funds an international research program on "sustainable land management".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Module A of the program investigates interactions between land management, <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, and ecosystem services.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It includes projects operating in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, Europe, <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. Module B seeks "innovative system solutions" in 13 projects with a Central European focus.</p>
<p>Furthermore, <a href="/wiki/Economics_of_Land_Degradation_Initiative" title="Economics of Land Degradation Initiative">the Economics of Land Degradation (ELD) Initiative</a> seeks to establish a cost benefit analysis on the practice of sustainable land management highlighting its economic benefits. This economic analysis will enable decision makers to take appropriate measures combat <a href="/wiki/Land_degradation" title="Land degradation">land degradation</a> globally. Additionally, the Initiative supports regional case studies focusing on <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.</p>
