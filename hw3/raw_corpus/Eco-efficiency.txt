			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Eco-efficiency</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>Over the years, as countries and regions around the world began to <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">develop</a>, it slowly became evident that <a href="/wiki/Industrialization" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialization">industrialization</a> and economic growth come hand in hand with environmental degradation.<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>Eco-Efficiency</b> has been proposed as one of the main tools to promote a transformation from unsustainable development to one of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-yadong_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yadong-2">[2]</a></sup> It is based on the concept of creating more goods and services while using fewer resources and creating less waste and <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>. “It is measured as the ratio between the (added) value of what has been produced (e.g. GDP) and the (added) environment impacts of the product or service (e.g. S02 emissions).”<sup id="cite_ref-yadong_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yadong-2">[2]</a></sup> The term was coined by the <a href="/wiki/World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development" title="World Business Council for Sustainable Development">World Business Council for Sustainable Development</a> (WBCSD) in its 1992 publication “Changing Course,” and at the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a>, eco-efficiency was endorsed as a new business concept and means for companies to implement <a href="/wiki/Agenda_21" title="Agenda 21">Agenda 21</a> in the private sector.<sup id="cite_ref-oecd_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oecd-3">[3]</a></sup> Ergo the term has become synonymous with a management philosophy geared towards <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, combing <a href="/wiki/Ecological" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological">ecological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic_efficiency" title="Economic efficiency">economic efficiency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oecd_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oecd-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eco-efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although eco-efficiency is a rather new method, the idea is not. In the early 1970s <a href="/wiki/Paul_R._Ehrlich" title="Paul R. Ehrlich">Paul R. Ehrlich</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Holdren" title="John Holdren">John Holdren</a> developed the lettering formula <a href="/wiki/I_%3D_PAT" title="I = PAT">I = PAT</a> to describe the <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">impact of human activity on the environment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ehren_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ehren-4">[4]</a></sup> Furthermore, the concept of eco-efficiency was first described by McIntyre and Thornton in 1978,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but it wasn’t until 1992, when the term was formally coined and widely publicized by <a href="/wiki/Stephan_Schmidheiny" title="Stephan Schmidheiny">Stephan Schmidheiny</a> in ‘’Changing Course’’.<sup id="cite_ref-ehren_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ehren-4">[4]</a></sup> Schmidheiny set out “to change the perception of industry as being part of the problem of environmental degradation to the reality of its becoming part—a key part—of the solution for sustainability and global development.”<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> The major drivers in the early phase of eco-efficiency’s development were the “forward-looking managers and thinkers in 3M and Dow.”<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> It was their involvement which catapulted eco-efficiency from a brilliant idea to a workable concept. The results of the <a href="/wiki/World_Business_Council_for_Sustainable_Development" title="World Business Council for Sustainable Development">WBCSD</a>’s work creating the “linkage between environmental performance and the bottom line was published in 1997 in its report Environmental Performance and Shareholder Value.”<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methods">Methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eco-efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the WBCSD definition, eco-efficiency is achieved through the delivery of "competitively priced goods and services that satisfy human needs and bring <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a> while progressively reducing <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental impact">environmental impacts</a> of goods and <a href="/wiki/Resource_intensity" title="Resource intensity">resource intensity</a> throughout the entire life-cycle to a level at least in line with the Earth's estimated <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> It works by implementing 4 main types of <a href="/wiki/Ratios" class="mw-redirect" title="Ratios">ratios</a>.</p>
<dl>
<dd>“The first two are environmental productivity and its inverse, environmental intensity of production, referring to the realm of production. The second pair, environmental improvement cost and its inverse, environmental <a href="/wiki/Cost-effectiveness" class="mw-redirect" title="Cost-effectiveness">cost-effectiveness</a>, are defined from an environmental improvements measures point-of-view.”<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>The ratios may be applied to any unit comprising economic activities because such activities always relate to cost and value, “and having some physical substrate, always influence the environment.”<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> Furthermore, there are two different levels upon which to orchestrate the ratios: ‘’micro’’ and ‘’macro’’. There are three different methods to determine eco-efficiency at the micro-level. First, ‘’incremental eco-efficiency’’, which “specifies the effects of the total value of a product system or sector and its total concomitant environmental effects.”<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup> Second, an analysis method nicknamed ‘’win-win’’, which “gives a comparison between a historical reference situation and potentially new situations based on the use of new technologies.”<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guns_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guns-7">[7]</a></sup> It should be noted that the win-win micro-method is limited because it cannot give a concrete answer on the question of whether it improves overall environmental performance. And the third is ‘’difference eco-efficiency’’, which is similar to the win-win variant, but removes all irrelevant alternatives to heighten potential for optimal technologies while comparing two alternatives.<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup> Now the macro-level is much less defined and has shown less accurate results. However, “the ultimate aim of eco-efficiency analysis is to help move micro-level decision making into macro-level optimality.”<sup id="cite_ref-huppes_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huppes-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-oecd_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oecd-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> The main goal in years to come is to create headline indicators to carry out macro-level analysis at a country/world scale.<sup id="cite_ref-oecd_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oecd-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-iisd_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iisd-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are two <a href="/wiki/Life-cycle_assessment" title="Life-cycle assessment">life-cycle assessment</a> (LCA) -based calculation systems on eco-efficiency: the Analysis Method of BASF, and the method of the <a href="/wiki/Eco-costs_value_ratio" title="Eco-costs value ratio">Eco-costs value ratio</a> of the Delft University of Technology.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eco-efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The reduction in ecological impacts translates into an increase in <a href="/wiki/Resource_productivity" title="Resource productivity">resource productivity</a>, which in turn can create a <a href="/wiki/Competitive_advantage" title="Competitive advantage">competitive advantage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-iisd_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iisd-8">[8]</a></sup> According to the WBCSD, critical aspects of eco-efficiency are:<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hunter_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunter-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>A reduction in the material intensity of goods or services;</li>
<li>A reduction in the <a href="/wiki/Energy_intensity" title="Energy intensity">energy intensity</a> of goods or services;</li>
<li>Reduced dispersion of toxic materials;</li>
<li>Improved <a href="/wiki/Recyclability" class="mw-redirect" title="Recyclability">recyclability</a>;</li>
<li>Maximum use of renewable resources;</li>
<li>Greater <a href="/wiki/Durable_goods" class="mw-redirect" title="Durable goods">durability</a> of products;</li>
<li>Increased service intensity of goods and services.</li>
</ul>
<p>Strategies that have been linked to eco-efficiency include “Factor 4” and “<a href="/wiki/Factor_10" title="Factor 10">Factor 10</a>”, which call for specific reductions in resource use, “natural capitalism”, which incorporates eco-efficiency as part of a broader strategy, and the “<a href="/wiki/Cradle-to-cradle" class="mw-redirect" title="Cradle-to-cradle">cradle-to-cradle</a>” movement, which claims to go beyond eco-efficiency in abolishing the very idea of waste. According to Boulanger,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> all versions of eco-efficiency share four key characteristics:</p>
<ul>
<li>Confidence in <a href="/wiki/Technological_innovation" class="mw-redirect" title="Technological innovation">technological innovation</a> as the main solution to un-sustainability;</li>
<li>Reliance on business as the principal <a href="/wiki/Actor%E2%80%93network_theory" title="Actor–network theory">actor</a> of transformation. The emphasis is on firms designing new products, shifting to new production processes, and investing in R&amp;D, etc., more than on the retailer or the consumer, let alone the citizen.</li>
<li>Trust in <a href="/wiki/Market_economy" title="Market economy">markets</a> (if they are functioning well);</li>
<li>“Growthphilia”: there is nothing wrong with <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">growth</a> as such.</li>
</ul>
<p>The view that improvements in eco-efficiency are sufficient for achieving sustainability has been challenged by Huesemann and Huesemann,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> who demonstrate using extensive historical evidence that increases in technological efficiency have not reduced overall resource use and pollution. Moreover, with “cradle-to-cradle”, growth is conducive to sustainability per se. This broader concept is called ‘’Sustainable Production and Consumption’’ (SPC). “This concept involves changes in production and consumption patterns that lead to sustainable use of <a href="/wiki/Natural_resources" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural resources">natural resources</a>;” <sup id="cite_ref-iisd_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iisd-8">[8]</a></sup> business has taken a key role in accelerating the use of this concept because businesses both consume and produce. Eco-efficiency is routinely a concept used because it combines performance along two of the three axes of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ehren_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ehren-4">[4]</a></sup> making it easier for academics and leading thinkers to tease out the associated social issues.<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eco-efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Furthermore, eco-efficiency is also a very useful tool because it can adapt and flex to be fit different sizes of companies, while also maintaining relevance with the larger scale of government and national policies.<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> For example, larger national players such as the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_for_Economic_Co-operation_and_Development" title="Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development">Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development</a> (OECD 2002), <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> (EU 2005), <a href="/wiki/European_Environment_Agency" title="European Environment Agency">European Environment Agency</a> (EEA) and the <a href="/wiki/National_Round_Table_on_the_Environment_and_the_Economy" title="National Round Table on the Environment and the Economy">National Round Table on the Environment and the Economy</a> (NRTEE) have all recognized that eco-efficiency is a practical approach that businesses should adopt in setting and achieving their environmental performance objectives.<sup id="cite_ref-yadong_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yadong-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-iisd_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iisd-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-baker_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baker-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nrtee_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrtee-13">[13]</a></sup> It has be proven to heighten market values for firms,<sup id="cite_ref-guns_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guns-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-accounting_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-accounting-14">[14]</a></sup> serve as an effective management tool for governments, benefit civil society, and increase quality of life. “It does this by changing industrial processes, creating new products and changing and influencing markets with new ideas and with new rules.”<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup> More people aim to get more value for their money in the market, while also enjoying a better environment.</p>
<p>Recently, there has also been use of eco-efficiency in more non-traditional ways, such as a use in banks to integrate environmental criteria into their credit-approval process; looking at “eco-integrated economic risks of a customer.”<sup id="cite_ref-iisd2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iisd2-15">[15]</a></sup> And is also being implemented as marketing advantages where, “eco-efficient choices are always preferred,”<sup id="cite_ref-ehren_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ehren-4">[4]</a></sup> especially in service sectors such as <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wbcsd_6-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wbcsd-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
