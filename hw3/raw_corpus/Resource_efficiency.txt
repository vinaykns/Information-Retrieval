			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Resource efficiency</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Resource <a href="/wiki/Efficiency" title="Efficiency">efficiency</a></b> is about maximising the supply of money, materials, staff, and other assets that can be drawn on by a person or organization in order to function effectively, with minimum <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">wasted</a> (<a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural</a>) <a href="/wiki/Resource_intensity" title="Resource intensity">resource expenses</a>. It means using the Earth's <a href="/wiki/Non-renewable_resource" title="Non-renewable resource">limited resources</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Sustainable" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> manner while minimising <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental impact">environmental impact</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motivation">Motivation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Motivation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 2014 report by <a href="/wiki/The_Carbon_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="The Carbon Trust">The Carbon Trust</a> suggested that resource challenges are intensifying rapidly - for example, there could be a 40% gap between available water supplies and water needs by 2030, and some critical materials could be in short supply as soon as 2016. These challenges could lead to disruptions to supply, growing regulatory requirements, volatile fluctuation of prices, and may ultimately threaten the viability of existing <a href="/wiki/Business_model" title="Business model">business models</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_concepts">Related concepts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Related concepts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Resource efficiency measures, methods, and aims are quite similar to those of <a href="/wiki/Resource_productivity" title="Resource productivity">resource productivity</a>/<a href="/wiki/Resource_intensity" title="Resource intensity">resource intensity</a> and of the slightly more environment-inclined concept of <a href="/wiki/Ecological_efficiency" title="Ecological efficiency">ecological efficiency</a>/<a href="/wiki/Eco-efficiency" title="Eco-efficiency">eco-efficiency</a>.</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_measurement" title="Sustainability measurement">Sustainability measurement</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_efficiency">Energy efficiency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Energy efficiency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">Efficient energy use</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_approaches">Possible approaches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Possible approaches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To achieve and optimize natural resource and energy effiency, several <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable economical or production schemes</a> have been proposed over the course of the last 50 years: <a href="/wiki/Circular_economy" title="Circular economy">circular economy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cradle-to-cradle" class="mw-redirect" title="Cradle-to-cradle">cradle-to-cradle</a>- or <a href="/wiki/Regenerative_design" title="Regenerative design">regenerative design</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Biomimetics" title="Biomimetics">biomimetics</a> principles, just to name a few. Common to all of them is built-in sustainability, in which (<a href="/wiki/Non-renewable" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-renewable">non-renewable</a>) resource-wasting is ruled out by design. They are generally built to be <a href="/wiki/Holistic" class="mw-redirect" title="Holistic">holistic</a>, robustly <a href="/wiki/Self-sustaining" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-sustaining">self-sustaining</a> and respecting the <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a> of the economic / <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecological</a> system.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Resource_Use_Measurement_and_Identification_of_Hotspots">Resource Use Measurement and Identification of Hotspots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Resource Use Measurement and Identification of Hotspots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A key tool in resource efficiency is measuring different aspects of resource use (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_footprint" class="mw-redirect" title="Water footprint">water footprint</a>, <a href="/wiki/Land_footprint" title="Land footprint">land footprint</a> or <a href="/wiki/Material_flow_accounting" title="Material flow accounting">material use</a>), then identifying 'hot spots' where the most resources are used and/or where there are the best opportunities to reduce this resource use. For example, <a href="/wiki/Waste_%26_Resources_Action_Programme" title="Waste &amp; Resources Action Programme">WRAP</a> has published information on hotspots for 50 grocery products likely to contribute most to the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental impact">environmental impacts</a> associated with UK <a href="/wiki/Household_consumption" class="mw-redirect" title="Household consumption">household consumption</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> WRAP have created a range of tools and guides to help improve business resource efficiency.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Initiatives_and_programmes">Initiatives and programmes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Initiatives and programmes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UNEP">UNEP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: UNEP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/UNEP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNEP">UNEP</a> works to promote resource efficiency and <i><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_consumption" title="Sustainable consumption">sustainable consumption</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable production">production</a></i> (SCP) in both developed and <a href="/wiki/Developing_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a>. The focus is on achieving increased understanding and implementation by public and private decision makers, as well as civil society, of policies and actions for resource efficiency and SCP. This includes the promotion of sustainable <a href="/wiki/Resource_management" title="Resource management">resource management</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Product_life_cycle" class="mw-redirect" title="Product life cycle">life cycle</a> perspective for goods and services.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_2020">Europe 2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Europe 2020">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The resource-efficient Europe flagship initiative is part of the <a href="/wiki/Europe_2020" title="Europe 2020">Europe 2020</a> Strategy, the EU's growth strategy for a smart, inclusive and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_economy" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable economy">sustainable economy</a>. It supports the shift towards <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_growth" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable growth">sustainable growth</a> via a resource-efficient, <a href="/wiki/Low-carbon_economy" title="Low-carbon economy">low-carbon economy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resource_Efficiency_in_Tomsk_Polytechnic_University">Resource Efficiency in Tomsk Polytechnic University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Resource Efficiency in Tomsk Polytechnic University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2012 <a href="/wiki/Tomsk_Polytechnic_University" title="Tomsk Polytechnic University">Tomsk Polytechnic University</a> (TPU) launched the Development Program of Resource Efficient Technologies for the period 2013-2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> That program was presented by TPU in 2009 at the Russian federal competition "National Research University". A key point of the program of TPU was announced the formation of high school as a world-class university-based staffing and development of technologies for resource-efficient economy.</p>
<p>TPU developed educational module "Resource Efficiency", prepared and published a textbook "Principals of resource efficiency", optional subject matter of the same name introduced in the curriculum (for all disciplines and areas of undergraduate).</p>
<p>TPU envisages university development in the field of resource-efficient technologies that unites six research and educational clusters:</p>

<li>Safe Environment
<ol>
<li>Non-destructive testing and diagnostics</li>
<li>Materials for extreme conditions</li>
<li>Domestic and industrial <a href="/wiki/Waste_recycling" class="mw-redirect" title="Waste recycling">waste recycling</a></li>
</ol>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Sustainable Energy">Sustainable Energy</a>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/High-temperature_superconductivity" title="High-temperature superconductivity">High-temperature superconductivity</a> technologies for <a href="/wiki/Energy_production" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy production">energy production</a></li>
<li>Nuclear and hydrogen fuel of the new generation</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hybrid_simulation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hybrid simulation (page does not exist)">Hybrid simulation</a> in energy production</li>
<li>Resource-efficient generation</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medical_Engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical Engineering">Medical Engineering</a>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bioengineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioengineering">Bioengineering</a> materials and technologies</li>

</ol>
</li>
<li>Planet Resources

</ol>
</li>
<li>Cognitive Systems and Telecommunications
<ol>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resource_Efficient_Scotland">Resource Efficient Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Resource_efficiency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Resource Efficient Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
