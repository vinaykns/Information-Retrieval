			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Distributed manufacturing</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Distributed manufacturing</b> also known as <b>distributed production</b>, <b>cloud producing</b> and <b>local manufacturing</b> is a form of decentralized manufacturing practiced by enterprises using a network of geographically dispersed manufacturing facilities that are coordinated using <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a>. It can also refer to local manufacture via the historic <a href="/wiki/Cottage_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Cottage industry">cottage industry</a> model, or manufacturing that takes place in the homes of consumers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consumer">Consumer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Consumer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Maker_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Maker movement">maker movement</a> and <a href="/wiki/DIY" class="mw-redirect" title="DIY">DIY</a> culture, small scale production by consumers often using <a href="/wiki/Peer_to_peer" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer to peer">peer to peer</a> resources is being referred to as distributed manufacturing. Consumers download digital designs from an <a href="/wiki/Open_design" title="Open design">open design</a> repository website like <a href="/w/index.php?title=Youmagine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Youmagine (page does not exist)">Youmagine</a> or <a href="/wiki/Thingiverse" title="Thingiverse">Thingiverse</a> and produce a product for low costs through a distributed network of 3D printing services such as <a href="/wiki/3D_Hubs" title="3D Hubs">3D Hubs</a> or at home with an <a href="/wiki/Open-source_3-D_printer" class="mw-redirect" title="Open-source 3-D printer">open-source 3-D printer</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/RepRap" class="mw-redirect" title="RepRap">RepRap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Distributed manufacturing with <a href="/wiki/Distributed_generation" title="Distributed generation">distributed generation</a> using <a href="/wiki/Solar_photovoltaic" class="mw-redirect" title="Solar photovoltaic">solar photovoltaic</a> cells and <a href="/wiki/3-D_printers" class="mw-redirect" title="3-D printers">3-D printers</a> has been proposed as a means for <a href="/wiki/Off-grid" class="mw-redirect" title="Off-grid">off-grid</a> rural area residents to manufacture themselves out of <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Recent advances in solar-powered 3-D printers have made manufacturing of a wide range of products possible in isolated communities.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>An example of such an application are <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">spectacles</a>. As Gwamuri et al. point out that while it is "still not yet feasible to print the lenses (the most critical component of the eyeglasses)" and in current prototypes "only the frames and syringe are printed" and that "aesthetics is another challenge" the "primary cost of the glasses could be reduced to about one dollar for a highly customized/individualized design, which could be printed on site in under an hour" (presumably excluding the lenses) and "it seems clear that other products could benefit from the same approach and that distributed manufacturing can assist in sustainable development, particularly in isolated rural regions".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initial life cycle analysis indicates that distributed production can have a smaller impact on the environment than conventional manufacturing and shipping because of reductions in transportation embodied energy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> There are now several types of open-source solar-powered 3-D printers,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> which can be used for production in <a href="/wiki/Off_grid" class="mw-redirect" title="Off grid">off grid</a> locations.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This can be taken to the extreme with <a href="/wiki/RV" class="mw-redirect" title="RV">RV</a> owners using mobile <a href="/wiki/3-D_printers" class="mw-redirect" title="3-D printers">3-D printers</a> to make fixtures for RV rooftops for solar <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaic" class="mw-redirect" title="Photovoltaic">photovoltaic</a> panels.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enterprise">Enterprise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Enterprise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The primary attribute of distributed manufacturing is the ability to create value at geographically dispersed locations via manufacturing. For example, <a href="/wiki/Shipping" class="mw-redirect" title="Shipping">shipping</a> costs are minimized when products are built geographically close to their intended markets. Also, products manufactured in a number of small facilities distributed over a wide area can be customized with details adapted to individual or regional tastes. Manufacturing components in different physical locations and then managing the supply chain to bring them together for final assembly of a product is also considered a form of distributed manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-Chrisman_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chrisman-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-K.C3.BChnle2010_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-K.C3.BChnle2010-12">[12]</a></sup> Digital networks combined with additive manufacturing allow companies a decentralized and geographically independent distributed production (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Cloud_Producing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cloud Producing (page does not exist)">Cloud Producing</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Bopp2010_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bopp2010-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_change">Social change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Distributed_manufacturing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Social change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some<sup id="cite_ref-triple-c.at_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triple-c.at-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> call attention to the conjunction of <a href="/wiki/Commons-based_peer_production" title="Commons-based peer production">Commons-based peer production</a> with distributed manufacturing techniques. The self-reinforced fantasy of a system of eternal growth can be overcome with the development of economies of scope, and here, the civil society can play an important role contributing to the raising of the whole productive structure to a higher plateau of more sustainable and customised productivity.<sup id="cite_ref-triple-c.at_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triple-c.at-14">[14]</a></sup> Further, it is true that many issues, problems and threats rise due to the large <a href="/wiki/Democratization" title="Democratization">democratization</a> of the means of production, and especially regarding the physical ones.<sup id="cite_ref-triple-c.at_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triple-c.at-14">[14]</a></sup> For instance, the recyclability of advanced nanomaterials is still questioned; weapons manufacturing could become easier; not to mention the implications on counterfeiting<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and on "<a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It might be maintained that in contrast to the industrial paradigm whose competitive dynamics were about economies of scale, <a href="/wiki/Commons-based_peer_production" title="Commons-based peer production">Commons-based peer production</a> and distributed manufacturing could develop economies of scope. While the advantages of scale rest on cheap global transportation, the economies of scope share infrastructure costs (intangible and tangible productive resources), taking advantage of the capabilities of the fabrication tools.<sup id="cite_ref-triple-c.at_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triple-c.at-14">[14]</a></sup> And following <a href="/wiki/Neil_Gershenfeld" title="Neil Gershenfeld">Neil Gershenfeld</a><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> in that “some of the least developed parts of the world need some of the most advanced technologies”, commons-based peer production and distributed manufacturing may offer the necessary tools for thinking globally but act locally in response to certain problems and needs. This can take the most radical form --individual personal manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
