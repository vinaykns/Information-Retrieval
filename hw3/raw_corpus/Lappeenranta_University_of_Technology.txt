<p><b>Lappeenranta University of Technology</b> (<b>LUT</b>) was established in 1969. The university campus is situated on the shore of lake <a href="/wiki/Saimaa" title="Saimaa">Saimaa</a> – the 4th biggest lake in Europe.</p>
<p>LUT is a community where curious people inspire curious people. The curious dive deep into their passion, and develop their thinking daily. Without curiosity, the world cannot be changed.</p>
<p>LUT addresses issues that matter to the world and that others may consider impossible to solve. The LUT campus is the capital city of curious people, but the state of mind can be achieved anywhere.</p>
<p>LUT’s strategic focus areas are clean energy and water, circular economy and sustainable business. Those are the key questions of humankind to which LUT seeks solutions through technology and business.</p>
<p>The university’s expertise in energy is reflected in Green Campus,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> a unique research and educational environment where its own innovations are put to practical use. Green Campus is an example on how science and technology can be used to solve environmental problems and build a sustainable world.</p>
<p>LUT takes part in university rankings to compare the university's research performance, education quality and international relations with those of other universities. Since 2014 LUT has been ranked among the world’s top 600 universities (Times Higher Education and QS rankings) as well as one of the world's 20 most promising and rapidly developing challenger universities (a report by the Firetail<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> consulting firm).</p>
<p>Furthermore, LUT has a tradition of strong links with the business community. The Finnish business journal "Talouselämä" has ranked LUT as the best university in business technology cooperation in Finland.</p>
<p>There are about 890 staff members and 6,900 students in the university. In LUT one can graduate in the following degrees: Bachelor of Science (Econ), Bachelor of Science (Tech), Master of Science (Tech), Master of Science (Econ), Licentiate of Science (Econ), Licentiate of Science (Tech), Doctor of Science (Tech), Doctor of Science (Econ), and Doctor of Philosophy.</p>
<p>LUT has several internationally accredited Master´s programmes. Master's degree programme in International Marketing Management was awarded EFMD`s EPAS-accreditation for five years in 2012. EUR-ACE and ASIIN-accredited programmes LUT has in Chemical Engineering, in Energy Technology, in Environmental Technology,in Mechanical Engineering, in Electrical Engineering and in Industrial Management.</p>
<p>LUT is state run and state funded, like all <a href="/wiki/Universities_in_Finland" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities in Finland">universities in Finland</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_in_breef">History in breef</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lappeenranta_University_of_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History in breef">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Combining technology and business, LUT (in Finnish: until 2003 Lappeenrannan teknillinen korkeakoulu, LTKK, and from 2003 Lappeenrannan teknillinen yliopisto, LTY) has served as an academic forerunner since 1969. In the 1950s and 1960s, the Finnish government made plans to establish the University of Eastern Finland in Lappeenranta, but it was ultimately decentralized in three cities: Lappeenranta, Kuopio, and Joensuu. Only the departments of engineering were located in Lappeenranta at that time. The Department of Business Administration was established and teaching of economics began in 1991.</p>

<p>The Viipuri Prize, established by the Society for Viipuri School of Economics, is awarded for outstanding achievements in the field of strategy research.<sup id="cite_ref-viipuri_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-viipuri-3">[3]</a></sup> The Prize is given every second year to a scholar who is among the most prominent in his or her field of study on a global scale, and whose work powerfully affects the research agenda at LUT School of Business.<sup id="cite_ref-viipuri_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-viipuri-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following scholars have been awarded the Viipuri Prize:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Teece" title="David Teece">David Teece</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> (2003)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_G._March" title="James G. March">James G. March</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> (2004)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Buckley_(academic)" title="Peter Buckley (academic)">Peter Buckley</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leeds" title="University of Leeds">University of Leeds</a> (2006)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sidney_G._Winter" title="Sidney G. Winter">Sidney G. Winter</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> (2008)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/C._K._Prahalad" title="C. K. Prahalad">C. K. Prahalad</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> (2010)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Kay_(economist)" title="John Kay (economist)">John Kay</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a> (2012)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Constance E. Helfat,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Tuck School of Business (2014)</li>
</ul>
