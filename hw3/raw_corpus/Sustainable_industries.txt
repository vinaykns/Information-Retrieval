<p>The phrase <b>sustainable industries</b> is related to the development of <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a> processes in a <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> way.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earliest mention of the phrase "sustainable industries" appeared in 1990 in a story about a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> group <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforesting</a> a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_moist_broadleaf_forests" title="Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests">tropical forest</a> to help create sustainable industries for the local populace.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Soon after, a study entitled “Jobs in a Sustainable Economy” by Michael Renner of the <a href="/wiki/Worldwatch_Institute" title="Worldwatch Institute">Worldwatch Institute</a> was published, using the term "sustainable industries".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This 1991 report concluded, "Contrary to the jobs-versus-owls rhetoric that blames environmental restrictions for layoffs, the movement toward an environmentally sustainable <a href="/wiki/World_economy" title="World economy">global economy</a> will create far more jobs than it eliminates. The chief reason: non-<a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">polluting</a>, environmentally sustainable industries tend to be intrinsically more labour intensive and less resource intensive than traditional processes." Among the features of sustainable industry offered in the paper were <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, resource conservation to meet the needs of future generations, safe and skill-enhancing working conditions, <a href="/wiki/Waste_minimisation" title="Waste minimisation">low waste production</a> processes, and the use of safe and environmentally compatible materials. Some of the benefits, however would be offset by higher prices (due to labor costs) and a theoretically larger population needed to perform the same amount of work, increasing the <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> and other loads on the system.</p>
<p>In February 2003 the business magazine Sustainable Industries was first published, offering news and analysis of core industries such as <a href="/wiki/Clean_energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean energy">clean energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a>. By 2012 the company had expanded to include digital media and event production with the Sustainable Industries Economic Forum in the U.S. Sustainable Industries merged with Triple Pundit in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Then in 2013 Sustainable Industries stopped publishing under its masthead.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
