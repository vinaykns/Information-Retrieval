<p><b>David Albert Charles Armstrong-Jones, 2nd Earl of Snowdon</b> (born 3 November 1961), styled as <b>Viscount Linley</b> until 2017 and known professionally as <b>David Linley</b>, is an English furniture maker and chairman of the auction house <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK. The son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon</a>, he is a grandson of King <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a> and is 18th in <a href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" class="mw-redirect" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line to succeed</a> his maternal aunt Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, and the first in line who is not her descendant.<sup id="cite_ref-RoyalSuccession_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoyalSuccession-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_2nd_Earl_of_Snowdon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of five, Snowdon started lessons in the Buckingham Palace schoolroom with his cousin <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">Prince Andrew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-M.R._2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M.R.-2">[2]</a></sup> He went to several <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent schools</a>: first, to Gibbs Pre-Preparatory School in <a href="/wiki/Kensington" title="Kensington">Kensington</a> in London,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> followed by the pre-preparatory section of <a href="/wiki/Ashdown_House,_East_Sussex" title="Ashdown House, East Sussex">Ashdown House School</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Sussex" title="East Sussex">East Sussex</a>, then on to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.education.gov.uk/edubase/establishment/summary.xhtml?urn=123305">Millbrook House School</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Abingdon,_Oxfordshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Abingdon, Oxfordshire">Abingdon</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and finally to <a href="/wiki/Bedales_School" title="Bedales School">Bedales School</a>, where he developed a passion for arts and crafts. From 1980 to 1982 he studied at Parnham House in the small town of <a href="/wiki/Beaminster" title="Beaminster">Beaminster</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, for craftsmen in wood.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_2nd_Earl_of_Snowdon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Snowdon opened a workshop in <a href="/wiki/Dorking" title="Dorking">Dorking</a>, where he designed and made furniture for three years before setting up his own company, David Linley Furniture Ltd (now known as LINLEY) where he makes <a href="/wiki/Bespoke" title="Bespoke">bespoke</a> furniture, upholstery, and interior design products known for their neoclassical appearance and use of inlaid woods. He has written numerous books and lectured around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Christ_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christ-6">[6]</a></sup> His work is sold in retail stores in <a href="/wiki/Belgravia" title="Belgravia">Belgravia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrods" title="Harrods">Harrods</a> and overseas including the <a href="/wiki/Bespoke_Collection" title="Bespoke Collection">Bespoke Collection</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Schneider_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schneider-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 December 2006, Snowdon took up the post of chairman of <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie's">Christie's</a> UK, having joined the board in 2005 as a non-executive director.<sup id="cite_ref-Christ_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christ-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family">Personal life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_2nd_Earl_of_Snowdon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 8 April 2002, Snowdon, along with <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">the Prince of Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">the Duke of York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Earl_of_Wessex" title="Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex">the Earl of Wessex</a>, stood guard at the lying-in-state of their grandmother, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_The_Queen_Mother" title="Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth, The Queen Mother</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This was a mark of respect unofficially known as the <a href="/wiki/Vigil_of_the_Princes" title="Vigil of the Princes">Vigil of the Princes</a>, which had taken place only once before, during the lying-in-state of <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">King George V</a> in 1936.</p>
<p>Snowdon had romances with <a href="/wiki/Susannah_Constantine" title="Susannah Constantine">Susannah Constantine</a>, Kate Menzies and Nicola Formby. On 8 October 1993 he married <a href="/wiki/Serena_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscountess_Linley" class="mw-redirect" title="Serena Armstrong-Jones, Viscountess Linley">the Hon. Serena Stanhope</a>, a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stanhope,_12th_Earl_of_Harrington" title="Charles Stanhope, 12th Earl of Harrington">Viscount Petersham (later the 12th Earl of Harrington)</a> at <a href="/wiki/St._Margaret%27s,_Westminster" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Margaret's, Westminster">St. Margaret's Church, Westminster</a>.</p>
<p>Lord Snowdon and his wife have two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Armstrong-Jones,_Viscount_Linley" title="Charles Armstrong-Jones, Viscount Linley"><b>Charles</b> Patrick Inigo Armstrong-Jones</a>, Viscount Linley (born 1 July 1999)</li>
<li>Lady <a href="/wiki/Lady_Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Lady Margarita Armstrong-Jones"><b>Margarita</b> Elizabeth Rose Alleyne Armstrong-Jones</a> (born 14 May 2002)</li>
</ul>
<p>Snowdon has one full sister, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Sarah_Chatto" title="Lady Sarah Chatto">Lady Sarah Chatto</a> (<i>née</i> Armstrong-Jones); and one paternal half-sister, Lady Frances von Hofmannsthal (<i>née</i> Armstrong-Jones). Lord Snowdon also has a half-brother, Jasper Cable-Alexander, son of his father and Melanie Cable-Alexander, an editor at <i><a href="/wiki/Country_Life_(magazine)" title="Country Life (magazine)">Country Life</a></i> magazine.</p>
<p>From 2000 until 2002, Snowdon, his wife and son lived at <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Palace" title="Kensington Palace">Kensington Palace</a> with his mother, Princess Margaret, in her declining years.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Snowdon's daughter, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Margarita_Armstrong-Jones" title="Lady Margarita Armstrong-Jones">Margarita Armstrong-Jones</a>, was a bridesmaid at the wedding of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Duke of Cambridge">Duke of Cambridge</a>.</p>
<p>The Snowdons have three homes: a flat in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea, London</a>; a cottage on the <a href="/wiki/Daylesford,_Gloucestershire" title="Daylesford, Gloucestershire">Daylesford estate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Chateau_d%27Autet" class="mw-redirect" title="Chateau d'Autet">Chateau d'Autet</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Luberon" title="Luberon">Luberon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>.</p>
<p>His father, Anthony Armstrong-Jones, the 1st Earl of Snowdon, died on 13 January 2017 and he assumed the title, thus becoming the 2nd Earl of Snowdon.</p>

<dt>Crest</dt>
<dd>A stag statant gules attired collared and unguled Or between two arms embowed in armour the hands proper each grasping a fleur-de-lis gold.<sup id="cite_ref-DIP_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIP-12">[12]</a></sup></dd>
<dt>Escutcheon</dt>
<dd>Quarterly 1st and 4th sable on a chevron argent, between in chief two fleurs-de-lis Or, and in base an eagle displayed Or, four pallets gules, 2nd and 3rd grandquarterly in the 1st and 4th quarters gules three lions passant guardant or in the 2nd quarter or a lion rampant gules within a double tressure flory counterflory gules 3rd azure a harp or stringed argent, the whole differenced by a label of three points Argent, first and third charged with a Tudor rose the second with a thistle proper.</dd>
<dt>Supporters</dt>
<dd>Dexter, a griffin, and sinister, an eagle, each with wings elevated and addorsed Or.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancestry">Ancestry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Armstrong-Jones,_2nd_Earl_of_Snowdon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ancestry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Snowdon is the son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Antony_Armstrong-Jones,_1st_Earl_of_Snowdon" title="Antony Armstrong-Jones, 1st Earl of Snowdon">1st Earl of Snowdon</a>; thus he is the nephew of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, and a grandson of <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>. At the time of his birth he was fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" class="mw-redirect" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line of succession to the British throne</a>; he is currently in eighteenth place and is the first person in the line of succession who is not a descendant of the Queen.<sup id="cite_ref-RoyalSuccession_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoyalSuccession-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
