<p><b>BARD Offshore 1</b> is a 400 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Megawatt">megawatt</a> (MW) <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> <a href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Offshore wind farm">offshore wind farm</a> with 80 BARD 5.0 turbines. Construction was finished in July 2013<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and the wind farm was officially inaugurated in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The wind farm is located 100 kilometres (62&#160;mi) northwest of the isle <a href="/wiki/Borkum" title="Borkum">Borkum</a> in 40 metres (130&#160;ft) deep water.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Laying of cables to connect the wind farm started on 23 July 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The 200&#160;km connection is the longest of its kind in the world. It is also the first connection of an offshore wind park realized as <a href="/wiki/HVDC_BorWin1" title="HVDC BorWin1">HVDC</a>-transmission. Construction of the wind turbines began in March 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The first turbine became operational at the beginning of December 2010. Construction was assisted by the purpose-built <i>Wind Lift&#160;1</i> barge / platform, which placed the 470&#160;ton, 21 meter foundations on the sea bed.</p>
<p>The project has run into serious and unclear problems, including being three years behind schedule and, at a cost of €3 billion, run significantly over budget. A <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">diver</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and a worker died during construction.<sup id="cite_ref-Pressemitteilung_27._Januar_2012_BARD_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pressemitteilung_27._Januar_2012_BARD-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mitteilung_der_DGzRS.2C_retrieved.2C_4_February_2012_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mitteilung_der_DGzRS.2C_retrieved.2C_4_February_2012-8">[8]</a></sup> The farm was supposed to go online in August 2013, but a series of setbacks, including a fire at a transmission station in March 2014, have delayed its activation.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> BARD went <a href="/wiki/Bankrupt" class="mw-redirect" title="Bankrupt">bankrupt</a> in November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Problems include <a href="/wiki/Overvoltage" title="Overvoltage">overvoltage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harmonics_(electrical_power)" title="Harmonics (electrical power)">harmonics</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> between BARD and the <a href="/wiki/HVDC_BorWin1" title="HVDC BorWin1">BorWin 1 grid link</a> and <a href="/wiki/BorWin_Alpha" title="BorWin Alpha">BorWin Alpha HVDC converter platform</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wpm2015-05-29_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpm2015-05-29-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>BARD's original owner had decided to make all components within the company, and troubles from the custom transformer are unrelated to standard equipment used elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of January 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=BARD_Offshore_1&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, most of the turbines are not supplying power to shore, costing ratepayers €2million per day.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2015, StatKraft and Ocean Breeze extended their contract for two years.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>By May 2016, the company website stated that the wind farm had produced 3 TWh, and was running stable at full capacity.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
