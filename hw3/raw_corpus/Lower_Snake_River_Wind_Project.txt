			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Lower Snake River Wind Project</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p>The <b>Lower Snake River Wind Project</b> is under construction in Washington’s <a href="/wiki/Garfield_County,_Washington" title="Garfield County, Washington">Garfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_County,_Washington" title="Columbia County, Washington">Columbia counties</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> is being developed in phases with the first phase consisting of 149 Siemens <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbines</a> rated at 2.3 MW each for a maximum generating capacity of 343 MW.<sup id="cite_ref-PSElowersnake_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSElowersnake-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lowers_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lowers-2">[2]</a></sup> The project will also create up to 150 temporary jobs and the installation will create up to 25 permanent jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-lowers_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lowers-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PSEpdf_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSEpdf-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
