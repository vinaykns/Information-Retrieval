			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Industrial metabolism</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Industrial metabolism</b> is a concept to describe the material and energy turnover of industrial systems. It was proposed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ayres_(scientist)" title="Robert Ayres (scientist)">Robert Ayres</a> in analogy to the biological <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a> as "the whole integrated collection of physical processes that convert <a href="/wiki/Raw_material" title="Raw material">raw materials</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, plus <a href="/wiki/Manual_labour" title="Manual labour">labour</a>, into finished <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">products</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">wastes</a>..."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In analogy to the biological concept of metabolism, which is used to describe the whole of chemical reactions in, for example, a cell to maintain its functions and reproduce itself, the concept of industrial metabolism describes the chemical reactions, transport processes, and manufacturing activities in industry. Industrial metabolism presupposes a connection between different industrial activities by seeing them as part of a larger system, such as a material cycle or the supply chain of a commodity. System scientists, for example in <a href="/wiki/Industrial_ecology" title="Industrial ecology">industrial ecology</a>, use the concept as paradigm to study the <a href="/wiki/Material_flow" title="Material flow">flow of materials</a> or energy through the industrial system in order to better understand <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">supply chains</a>, the sources and causes of <a href="/wiki/Pollutant" title="Pollutant">emissions</a>, and the linkages between the industrial and the wider <a href="/wiki/Socio-technological" class="mw-redirect" title="Socio-technological">socio-technological</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Industrial metabolism is a subsystem of the <a href="/wiki/Anthropogenic_metabolism" title="Anthropogenic metabolism">anthropogenic</a> or socioeconomic metabolism, which also comprises non-industrial human activities in households or the public sector.</p>
