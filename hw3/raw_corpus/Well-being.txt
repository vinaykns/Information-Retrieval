			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Well-being</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div role="note" class="hatnote">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Welfare_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Welfare (disambiguation)">Welfare (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Well-being</b>, <b>wellbeing</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>welfare</b> or <b>wellness</b> is a general term for the condition of an individual or group, for example their social, <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic</a>, psychological, spiritual or medical state; a high level of well-being means in some sense the individual or group's condition is positive, while low well-being is associated with negative happenings.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, the term 'well-being' (and 'welfare', 'utility', etc.) refers to the manner in which an individual's life manifests desires, objectives, and needs—among myriad more diverse variables—and how these affect the individual's perspective.</p>
<p>Philosophers, such as Fred Feldman and Brad Hooker, have suggested we should think of well-being in terms of a parent's expectations for a child (aka 'crib test')<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Reliable source needed for the whole sentence (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Philosophical study of well-being identifies a number of different kinds of theory, such as: hedonism, desire-fulfillment theory, objective-list theory, perfectionism, and some 'mixed' or 'hybrid' views of well-being. Well-being features in normative ethical theories, most notably <a href="/wiki/Utilitarianism" title="Utilitarianism">utilitarianism</a>; one need not be a utilitarian, or a <a href="/wiki/Consequentialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Consequentialist">consequentialist</a>, more generally, to think that well-being is a moral matter. Any plausible ethical theory will give at least some role to well-being.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, the term is used for one or more <a href="/wiki/Numerical_data" class="mw-redirect" title="Numerical data">quantitative</a> measures intended to assess the <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a> of a group, for example, in the <a href="/wiki/Capabilities_approach" class="mw-redirect" title="Capabilities approach">capabilities approach</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Happiness_economics" title="Happiness economics">economics of happiness</a>. As with the related <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> terms '<a href="/wiki/Wealth" title="Wealth">wealth</a>' and 'welfare', economics sources often contrast the state with its opposite.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The study of well-being is divided into <a href="/wiki/Subjective_well-being" title="Subjective well-being">subjective well-being</a> and objective well-being.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although there has not been a clear definition established for well-being, it can be defined as “...a special case of attitude”.<sup id="cite_ref-Guttman.2C_Levy_1982_159_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guttman.2C_Levy_1982_159-3">[3]</a></sup> This definition serves two purposes of well-being: developing and testing a [systematic] theory for the structure of [interrelationships] among varieties of well-being, and integration of well-being theory with the ongoing<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2015)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> cumulative theory <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> development in the fields of attitude of related research”.<sup id="cite_ref-Guttman.2C_Levy_1982_159_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guttman.2C_Levy_1982_159-3">[3]</a></sup> One’s well-being develops through assessments of their environment and emotions and then developing an interpretation of their own personal self. There are two different types of well-being: <a href="/wiki/Cognitive" class="mw-redirect" title="Cognitive">cognitive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Affective" class="mw-redirect" title="Affective">affective</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (February 2015)">according to whom?</span></a></i>]</sup>. Social well-being is mentioned in Canadian law.<sup id="cite_ref-OGCA_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OGCA-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A more current definition of wellness and its distinction from <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> is the following: "Wellness refers to diverse and interconnected dimensions of physical, mental, and social well-being that extend beyond the traditional definition of health. It includes choices and activities aimed at achieving physical vitality, mental alacrity, social satisfaction, a sense of accomplishment, and personal fulfillment."<sup id="cite_ref-JAMA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JAMA-5">[5]</a></sup> In other words, although health is part of wellbeing, some people are able to maintain satisfactory wellbeing despite the presence of psychological symptoms.<sup id="cite_ref-Bos_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bos-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cognitive">Cognitive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cognitive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cognitive well-being is developed through assessing one’s interactions with their environment and other people. “<a href="/wiki/Welfare" title="Welfare">Welfare</a> <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a> ultimately deals with cognitive concepts such as well-being, <a href="/wiki/Happiness" title="Happiness">happiness</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Contentment" title="Contentment">satisfaction</a>. These relate to notions such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aspirations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aspirations (page does not exist)">aspirations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Needs" class="mw-redirect" title="Needs">needs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contentment" title="Contentment">contentment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disappointment" title="Disappointment">disappointment</a>”.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> People tend to assess their cognitive well-being based on the social classes that are in their community. In communities with a wide variety of social statuses, the lower class will tend to compare their lifestyle to those of higher class and assess what they do and do not have that could lead to a higher level of well-being. Whenever someone interprets their needs and wants as to being satisfied or not, they then develop their cognitive well-being.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Affective">Affective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Affective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These are the different levels of <a href="/wiki/Affect_(psychology)" title="Affect (psychology)">affect</a> on well-being: “...high negative affect is represented by anxiety and [hostility]; low negative affect is represented by calmness and relaxation; high positive affect is represented by a state of pleasant <a href="/wiki/Arousal" title="Arousal">arousal</a> <a href="/wiki/Enthusiasm" title="Enthusiasm">enthusiasm</a> and low positive affect is represented by a state of unpleasantness and low arousal (dull, sluggish)”.<sup id="cite_ref-Daniels_2000_277_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Daniels_2000_277-8">[8]</a></sup> Well-being is most usefully thought of as the dynamic process that gives people a sense of how their lives are going, through the interaction between their circumstances, activities and psychological resources or sense of their own ‘mental capital’." Or, as has been recorded elsewhere, it could be said that well-being "...is a state of complete wellness".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Psychology">Psychology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Psychology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to McNulty (2012),<sup id="cite_ref-McNulty_2011_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McNulty_2011-10">[10]</a></sup>“...positive <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a> at the subjective level is about valued subjective experiences”. Well-being is an important factor in this subjective experience, as well as <a href="/wiki/Contentment" title="Contentment">contentment</a>, satisfaction with the past, <a href="/wiki/Optimism" title="Optimism">optimism</a> for the future and <a href="/wiki/Happiness" title="Happiness">happiness</a> in the present. People are more likely to experience positive psychology if they take in the good things in each experience or situation. Even with regard to the past, if a person only focuses on the negative the <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a> will only be able to recognise the negative. The more the <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a> has access to the negative, the easier it becomes, because that is what is more memorable. It takes more effort for the brain to remember the positive experiences because typically it is the smaller actions and experiences that are the positive ones. James McNulty's (2012) <sup id="cite_ref-McNulty_2011_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McNulty_2011-10">[10]</a></sup> research examines this idea further. He argues that, “...well-being is not determined solely by people’s psychological characteristics but instead is determined jointly by the interplay between those characteristics and qualities of people's social environments”. When people experience well-being, they are experiencing a sense of <a href="/wiki/Emotional" class="mw-redirect" title="Emotional">emotional</a> freedom – there is nothing negative that is holding them back from experiencing positive emotions. This is true if a person is in a certain setting, because it has been demonstrated in previous research that particular environments can hold a lot of memories for an individual just because of what was shared there and the meaning of it (source?). Therefore, “...well-being is often equated with the experience of pleasure and the absence of [pain] over time”.<sup id="cite_ref-tamir_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tamir-11">[11]</a></sup> The less psychological pain an individual is experiencing the more he or she is going to experience well-being.</p>
<p>When someone is experiencing well-being they are also experiencing several other things. It involves a sense of <a href="/wiki/Self-fulfillment" title="Self-fulfillment">self-fulfillment</a>, which is the feeling of being happy and satisfied because one is doing something that fully uses one's abilities and talents (Merriam-Webster). The feeling of having a purpose in life and connection with others are also contributors to the idea of well-being.<sup id="cite_ref-tamir_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tamir-11">[11]</a></sup> When people feel as though they have a [purpose] in the world, they feel like they belong. They feel like they matter.</p>
<p>The tripartite model of mental well-being is one of the most comprehensive models of well-being in psychology. This model views mental well-being as having three components of hedonic (or subjective), psychological, and social well-being.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Hedonic well-being concerns emotional aspects of well-being, whereas psychological and social well-being concerns skills, abilities, and optimal functioning.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">[13]</a></sup> The tripartite model of mental well-being has received extensive empirical support across cultures.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When talking about the school system, the idea of well-being is not as well defined. It is argued that school should only be about <a href="/wiki/Learning" title="Learning">learning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a> but children also learn about social skills and themselves. When the group norm is performing good in school and a child feels like they belong they are more likely to perform better in school. However, if the class is performing under standards and the child already didn't feel belonged but does perform good, it will continue to perform good, because that stresses its role as the outsider. As well as accessing an <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, ideally they need to learn how to believe in themselves and create purpose for themselves. If well-being is established in kids at a young age then it is more likely to play a part in their life as they get older. John White (2013) looked at public schools in Britain now and in the past. In the past schools only focused on <a href="/wiki/Knowledge" title="Knowledge">knowledge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a> but now <a href="/wiki/Britain" class="mw-disambig" title="Britain">Britain</a> has moved to a broader direction. Their Every Child Matters initiative seeks to enhance children's well-being across the range of children's services.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial">Financial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Financial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>“Wellbeing” has traditionally focused on improving physical, <a href="/wiki/Emotional" class="mw-redirect" title="Emotional">emotional</a> and mental quality of life with little understanding of how dependent they all are on financial health.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, financial stress often manifests itself in physical and <a href="/wiki/Emotional" class="mw-redirect" title="Emotional">emotional</a> difficulties that lead to increased healthcare costs and reduced productivity.<sup id="cite_ref-rodalenews.com_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rodalenews.com-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> A more inclusive paradigm for well-being would acknowledge money as a source of <a href="/wiki/Empowerment" title="Empowerment">empowerment</a> that maximizes physical and <a href="/wiki/Emotional" class="mw-redirect" title="Emotional">emotional</a> health by reducing financial <a href="/wiki/Stress" class="mw-disambig" title="Stress">stress</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rodalenews.com_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rodalenews.com-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Such a model would provide individuals with the financial knowledge they need, as well enable them to gain valuable insight and understanding regarding their financial habits, as well as their thoughts, feelings, fears and attitudes about money. Through this work, individuals would be better equipped to manage their money and achieve the financial wellness that is essential for their overall well-being.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subjective">Subjective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Subjective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Subjective_well-being" title="Subjective well-being">Subjective well-being</a> is “...based on the idea that how each person thinks and feels about his or her life is important”.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This idea is developed specifically in a person’s culture. People base their own well-being in relation to their environment and the lives of others around them. Well-being is also subject to how one feels other people in their environment perceive them, whether that positively or negatively. New researches are increasingly focusing on the <a href="/wiki/Perceptual" class="mw-redirect" title="Perceptual">perceptual</a> aspect of well-being. The research program ‘Understanding Positive Emotions’ under the HSL Human Well-being project at Human Science Lab investigates how <a href="/wiki/Material" title="Material">material</a> well-being and <a href="/wiki/Perceptual" class="mw-redirect" title="Perceptual">perceptual</a> well-being works as relative determinants in conditioning our <a href="/wiki/Mind" title="Mind">mind</a> for positive <a href="/wiki/Emotions" class="mw-redirect" title="Emotions">emotions</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Well-being is also subject to pleasure and whether or not basic human needs are fulfilled, although it could be said one’s needs and wants are never fully satisfied. The quality of life of an individual and a society is dependent on the amount of happiness and <a href="/wiki/Pleasure" title="Pleasure">pleasure</a> the experience, as well as their relationship to human health. Whether or not other cultures are subject to internal culture appraisal is based on that culture's type. “<a href="/wiki/Collectivistic" class="mw-redirect" title="Collectivistic">Collectivistic</a> cultures are more likely to use norms and the social appraisals of others in evaluating their subjective well-being, whereas those [individualistic] societies are more likely to heavily weight the internal [frame of reference] arising from one’s own happiness”.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_identity">Ethnic identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ethnic identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ethnic_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic Group">Ethnic identity</a> may play a role in an individual's cognitive well-being. Studies have shown that “...both social psychological and developmental perspectives suggest that a strong, secure ethnic identity makes a positive contribution to <a href="/wiki/Cognitive" class="mw-redirect" title="Cognitive">cognitive</a> well-being”.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Those in an acculturated society may feel more equal as a human being within their culture, therefore experiencing increased well-being.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Individual_roles">Individual roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Individual roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Individual roles play a part in cognitive well-being. Not only does having social ties improve cognitive well-being, it also improves psychological health.<sup id="cite_ref-Thoits_1992_237_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thoits_1992_237-28">[28]</a></sup> Having multiple identities and roles helps individuals to relate to their society and provide the opportunity for each to contribute more as they increase their roles, therefore creating enhanced levels of cognitive well-being. Each individual role is ranked internally within a <a href="/wiki/Hierarchy" title="Hierarchy">hierarchy</a> of salience. <a href="/wiki/Salience_(neuroscience)" title="Salience (neuroscience)">Salience</a> is “...the subjective importance that a person attaches to each identity”.<sup id="cite_ref-Thoits_1992_237_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thoits_1992_237-28">[28]</a></sup> Different roles an individual has have a different impact on their well-being. Within this hierarchy, higher roles offer more of a source to their well-being and define more meaningfulness to their overall role as a human being.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Bloodworth and McNamee sports and physical activities are a key contributor to the development of people's well-being. The influence of sports on well-being is conceptualized within a framework which includes impermanence, its <a href="/wiki/Hedonistic" class="mw-redirect" title="Hedonistic">hedonistic</a> shallowness and its <a href="/wiki/Epistemological" class="mw-redirect" title="Epistemological">epistemological</a> inadequacy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Researching the effect of sport on well-being is difficult as some societies are unable to access sports, a deficiency in studying this phenomenon.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Well-being&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Living in an environment with more green spaces is associated with higher well-being, partly due to the beneficial effects on psychological relaxation, stress alleviation, increased physical activity, and reduced exposure to air pollutants and noise, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-who_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-who-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BosGr_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BosGr-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
