<p>The <b>London Array</b> is a 175 turbine 630&#160;MW <a href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Round 2 wind farm">Round 2 offshore wind farm</a> located 20&#160;km off the <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a> coast in the outer <a href="/wiki/Thames_Estuary" title="Thames Estuary">Thames Estuary</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It is the largest offshore wind farm in the world, and the largest wind farm in Europe by megawatt capacity.</p>
<p>Construction of phase 1 of the wind farm began in March 2011 and was completed by mid 2013, being formally inaugurated by the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a> on 4 July 2013.</p>
<p>The second phase of the project was refused planning consent in 2014 due to concerns on the impact to sea birds.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The wind farm site is more than 20 kilometres (12&#160;mi) off the <a href="/wiki/North_Foreland" title="North Foreland">North Foreland</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a> coast in the area between Long Sand and <a href="/wiki/Kentish_Knock_(England)" title="Kentish Knock (England)">Kentish Knock</a>, between <a href="/wiki/Margate" title="Margate">Margate</a> in Kent and <a href="/wiki/Clacton" class="mw-redirect" title="Clacton">Clacton</a> in Essex.<sup id="cite_ref-pim_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pim-3">[3]</a></sup> The site has water depths of no more than 25&#160;m<sup id="cite_ref-4coffshore_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4coffshore-1">[1]</a></sup> and is mostly away from shipping lanes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first phase consisted of 175 <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">Siemens Wind Power</a> SWT-3.6 turbines and two offshore substations, giving a wind farm with a peak rated power of 630&#160;MW.<sup id="cite_ref-nseng_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nseng-5">[5]</a></sup> Each turbine and offshore substation is erected on a <a href="/wiki/Monopile_foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Monopile foundation">monopile foundation</a>, and connected together by 210&#160;km (130&#160;mi) of 33&#160;kV array cables. The two offshore substations are connected to an onshore substation at Cleve Hill (near <a href="/wiki/Graveney" title="Graveney">Graveney</a>) on the north Kent coast, by four 150&#160;kV subsea export cables, in total 220&#160;km (140&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-nseng_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nseng-5">[5]</a></sup> It is named after London because the power goes to the London grid.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The smaller <a href="/wiki/Thanet_Wind_Farm" title="Thanet Wind Farm">Thanet Wind Farm</a> is to the south of it.</p>
<p>The array is intended to reduce annual <a href="/wiki/CO2" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2">CO<sub>2</sub></a> emissions by about 900,000 tons, equal to the emissions of 300,000 passenger cars.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001 environmental studies identified areas of the outer <a href="/wiki/Thames_Estuary" title="Thames Estuary">Thames Estuary</a> as potential sites for offshore wind farm;<sup id="cite_ref-la6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la6-8">[8]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Trade_and_Industry_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department of Trade and Industry (United Kingdom)">Department of Trade and Industry</a> published the paper <i>Future Offshore — A Strategic Framework for the Offshore Wind Industry</i>, which identified the outer Thames Estuary as one of three potential areas for future wind farm development (<a href="/wiki/Round_2_wind_farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Round 2 wind farm">Round 2 wind farms</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Crown_Estate" title="Crown Estate">Crown Estate</a> awarded a 50-year lease to <i>London Array Ltd.</i> (a consortium of E.ON UK Renewables, Shell WindEnergy, and CORE Limited.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[note 1]</a></sup>) in December 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-la6_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-la6-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc2005_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2005-13">[12]</a></sup> A planning application was submitted in 2005,<sup id="cite_ref-bbc2005_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2005-13">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> which was approved in December 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup> Planning permission for the onshore electricity substation was received in November 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-onfarm1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-onfarm1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2008, Shell announced that it was pulling out of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> It was announced in July 2008 that E.ON UK and DONG Energy would buy Shell's stake.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> Subsequently on 16 October 2008, London Array announced the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> based Masdar would join E.ON as a joint venture party in the scheme. Under the agreement, Masdar purchased 40% of E.ON's half share of the scheme, giving Masdar a 20% stake in the project overall.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-masdar_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-masdar-19">[18]</a></sup> The resultant ownership was 50% <a href="/wiki/DONG_Energy" title="DONG Energy">DONG Energy</a>, 30% <a href="/wiki/E.ON_UK" title="E.ON UK">E.ON UK Renewables</a> and 20% <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Future_Energy_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Abu Dhabi Future Energy Company">Masdar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ingdong_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ingdong-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2009, the backers agreed on an initial investment of €2.2&#160;billion.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup> Financing of phase 1 was achieved through the <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">European Investment Bank</a> and the Danish Export Credit Fund with £250 million.<sup id="cite_ref-ingdong_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ingdong-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, in response to <a href="/wiki/Ofgem" class="mw-redirect" title="Ofgem">Ofgem</a> "Offshore Transmission Owner" regulations, the consortium divested the electrical transmission assets of the wind farm (valued at £459 million) to "Blue Transmission London Array Limited" – an entity incorporated by Barclays Infrastructure Funds Management Limited (<a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">Barclays</a>) and Diamond UK Transmission Corporation (<a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Corporation" title="Mitsubishi Corporation">Mitsubishi Corporation</a> subsidiary).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>In January 2014 Dong sold half its stake to Quebec public pension plan manager <span lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/Caisse_de_d%C3%A9p%C3%B4t_et_placement_du_Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec">Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec</a></span> ("<span lang="fr">La Caisse</span>").<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of its construction it was the largest offshore wind farm in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_commissioning">Construction and commissioning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Construction and commissioning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Offshore work began in March 2011;<sup id="cite_ref-offshore051112_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-offshore051112-25">[24]</a></sup> the first foundation was installed in March 2011<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Turbines were supplied by <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">Siemens Wind Power</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-siemens_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siemens-27">[26]</a></sup> Their foundations were built by the joint-venture between Per Aarsleff and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bilfinger_Berger_Ingenieurbau_GmbH&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bilfinger Berger Ingenieurbau GmbH (page does not exist)">Bilfinger Berger Ingenieurbau GmbH</a>. The same company supplied and installed the monopiles.<sup id="cite_ref-upstream141209_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upstream141209-28">[27]</a></sup> Generators were installed by MPI and <a href="/wiki/A2SEA" title="A2SEA">A2SEA</a> by using an installation vessel <a href="/wiki/TIV_MPI_Adventure" title="TIV MPI Adventure">TIV&#160;<i>MPI Adventure</i></a> and a <a href="/wiki/Jack-up" class="mw-redirect" title="Jack-up">jack-up</a> barge <i>Sea Worker</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-array040210_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-array040210-29">[28]</a></sup> Two offshore substations were designed, fabricated and installed by Future Energy, a joint venture between Fabricom, Iemants and Geosea, while electrical systems and onshore substation work was undertaken by Siemens Transmission &amp; Distribution. The subsea export cable was supplied by <a href="/wiki/Nexans" title="Nexans">Nexans</a> and array cables by JDR Cable Systems. The array cables and the export cables were installed by VSMC.<sup id="cite_ref-upstream141209_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-upstream141209-28">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wind farm started producing electricity at the end of October 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-offshore051112_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-offshore051112-25">[24]</a></sup> All 175 turbines of phase 1 were confirmed fully operational on 8 April 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-renewableuk_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-renewableuk-30">[29]</a></sup> and the wind farm was formally inaugurated by the Prime minister <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a> on 4 July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-20130704gulfnews_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20130704gulfnews-31">[30]</a></sup> In December 2015 it produced 369 GWh; a monthly <a href="/wiki/Capacity_factor" title="Capacity factor">capacity factor</a> of 78.9%. It produced 2.5 TWh in 2015. During two days of January 2016, production varied from 3 MW to 619 MW.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_2">Phase 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Array&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Phase 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A second phase was planned which would have seen a further 166 turbines installed to increase the capacity to 1000&#160;MW.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc140219_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc140219-34">[33]</a></sup> However, the second phase was scaled back and finally cancelled in February 2014 after concerns were raised by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_for_the_Protection_of_Birds" title="Royal Society for the Protection of Birds">Royal Society for the Protection of Birds</a> about its effect on a local population of <a href="/wiki/Red-throated_diver" class="mw-redirect" title="Red-throated diver">red-throated divers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc140219_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc140219-34">[33]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup></p>
