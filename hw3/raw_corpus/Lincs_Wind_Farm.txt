<p>The <b>Lincs Wind Farm</b> is a 270&#160;MW offshore <a href="/wiki/Wind_farm" title="Wind farm">wind farm</a> 8 kilometres (5.0&#160;mi) off <a href="/wiki/Skegness" title="Skegness">Skegness</a> on the east coast of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The total cost of the project is estimated at £1&#160;billion including electrical transmission links. The farm was completed in 2013. It is adjacent to the smaller <a href="/wiki/Lynn_and_Inner_Dowsing_Wind_Farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Lynn and Inner Dowsing Wind Farm">Lynn and Inner Dowsing Wind Farm</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincs_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Centrica" title="Centrica">Centrica</a> acquired the Lincs Wind Farm project in 2004 from <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Energy_Systems" title="Renewable Energy Systems">Renewable Energy Systems</a>, in 2008 the company obtained planning permission for the project.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2009 <a href="/wiki/DONG_Energy" title="DONG Energy">DONG Energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens Project Ventures</a> jointly acquired 50% of the project (25% share each) for £50&#160;million, plus 50% of the capital cost of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Preparatory construction work for the development included the extension of the <a href="/wiki/National_Grid_(Great_Britain)" title="National Grid (Great Britain)">national-grid</a> electricity substation at <a href="/wiki/Walpole,_Norfolk" title="Walpole, Norfolk">Walpole, Norfolk</a>, which began in April 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-centrica032012_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centrica032012-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shared_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shared-7">[note 1]</a></sup> In June 2010 Siemens obtained the contract for the offshore electrical substation (£101&#160;million).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup> Transmission cables from offshore to onshore <a href="/wiki/Electrical_substation" title="Electrical substation">electrical substations</a> were installed in 2010–2011.<sup id="cite_ref-centrica032012_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-centrica032012-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pp1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pp1-9">[8]</a></sup> Construction of the offshore facilities of the wind farm began in 2011, with the installation of the offshore <a href="/wiki/Electrical_substation" title="Electrical substation">electricity substation</a> foundations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Hull" title="Port of Hull">Port of Hull</a> was selected as one of the logistics bases for the wind farm turbine installation, supplying wind tower transition pieces. <a href="/wiki/Jackup_rig" title="Jackup rig">Jack-up barge</a> <i>JB114</i> arriving at the port in March 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a> was also used as a base for the wind farm installation.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Construction of the offshore 33&#160;kV to 132&#160;kV substation (structure sub-contracted to <a href="/w/index.php?title=McNulty_Offshore_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="McNulty Offshore Construction (page does not exist)">McNulty Offshore Construction</a>/<a href="/wiki/Atkins_(company)" title="Atkins (company)">Atkins PLC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup>) and of the onshore 132&#160;kV to 400&#160;kV substation was completed by April 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2011, the consortia of DONG, Siemens, and Centrica began to seek financing for the £1&#160;billion project, committing to providing £166.7, £75 and £333.3&#160;million respectively (£575&#160;million total). In June 2012 the consortia obtained financing for the project with £425&#160;million of <a href="/wiki/Nonrecourse_debt" title="Nonrecourse debt">nonrecourse debt</a> facilities provided from a consortium of 10 banks. Approximately 75%, of the project cost was for the wind farm installation, the remainder for the high voltage electricity transmission link.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Construction began in 2010; installation of the 75-monopile tower foundation by <a href="/wiki/MT_H%C3%B8jgaard" title="MT Højgaard">MT Højgaard</a> was completed by June 2012, with the underwater installation phase completed by September 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> The first generator had been installed by 14 July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> The wind farm was scheduled become operational in the second half of 2012, and be complete by 2013:<sup id="cite_ref-pr1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr1-18">[17]</a></sup> the final turbine was installed by March 2013;<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> and the wind farm officially opened by Deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Nick_Clegg" title="Nick Clegg">Nick Clegg</a> in August 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup> with commissioning complete in September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> The license for the electrical transmission link was awarded to TC Lincs OFTO Limited for £308&#160;million in late 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_description">Technical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincs_Wind_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Technical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The scheme has 250&#160;MW of generating capacity, with an additional 20&#160;MW of turbine generating capacity constructed within the nearby <a href="/wiki/Lynn_and_Inner_Dowsing_Wind_Farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Lynn and Inner Dowsing Wind Farm">Lynn Wind Farm and Dowsing Wind Farm</a> but connected to the Lincs project transmission system.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup> The project uses 75 three blade 3.6&#160;MW Siemens wind turbines. The wind turbines have 120 metres (390&#160;ft) rotor diameter machines with 58.5 metres (192&#160;ft) blades with a hub height of 100 metres (330&#160;ft) on cylindrical steel towers. The turbines were installed in 10 to 15 metres (33 to 49&#160;ft) water depth, with a spacing of approximately 500 metres (1,600&#160;ft) within a 35 square kilometres (14&#160;sq&#160;mi) wind farm.<sup id="cite_ref-pp1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pp1-9">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Transmission of electrical power to land is by 145&#160;kV cables supplied by <a href="/wiki/Nexans" title="Nexans">Nexans</a>. Transmission voltage is 132&#160;kV to land, being increased to 400&#160;kV at Walpole substation for grid connection.<sup id="cite_ref-pp1_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pp1-9">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The operation and maintenance base for the wind farm is based at <a href="/wiki/Grimsby" title="Grimsby">Grimsby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wind farm has an expected lifespan of 40 years, with electrical equipment and wind turbines expected to be replaced after 20 years. After the end of the project's lifespan the farm is to be decommissioned, including removal of turbines and electrical equipment, and removal of offshore foundations or cut off to <a href="/wiki/Seabed" title="Seabed">seabed</a> level or lower. Connecting cables are not scheduled to be removed.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup></p>
