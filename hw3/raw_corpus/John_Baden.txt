			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">John Baden</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>John A. Baden</b> is founder and chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Foundation_for_Research_on_Economics_and_the_Environment" title="Foundation for Research on Economics and the Environment">Foundation for Research on Economics and the Environment</a> (FREE) based in <a href="/wiki/Bozeman,_Montana" title="Bozeman, Montana">Bozeman, Montana</a>.</p>
<p>In 1977 Baden co-authored <i>Managing the Commons</i><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ecobooks.com/commons.htm">[1]</a> with <a href="/wiki/Garrett_Hardin" title="Garrett Hardin">Garrett Hardin</a>, the author of the essay "<a href="/wiki/The_Tragedy_of_the_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="The Tragedy of the Commons">The Tragedy of the Commons</a>". The book, which is currently<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=John_Baden&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> out of print, is a collection of articles exploring the themes raised in Hardin's original essay.</p>
