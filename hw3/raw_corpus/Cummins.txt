<p><b>Cummins, Inc.</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> corporation that designs, manufactures, and distributes engines, filtration, and power generation products.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Cummins also services engines and related equipment, including <a href="/wiki/Fuel_injection" title="Fuel injection">fuel systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Control_system" title="Control system">controls</a>, air handling, <a href="/wiki/Filtration" title="Filtration">filtration</a>, emission control and <a href="/wiki/Power_generation" class="mw-redirect" title="Power generation">electrical power generation systems</a>. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Indiana" title="Columbus, Indiana">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Cummins sells in approximately 190 countries and territories through a network of more than 600 company-owned and independent distributors and approximately 6,000 dealers. Cummins reported net income of $1.65 billion on sales of $19.2 billion in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Investor_Relations_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Investor_Relations-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Cummins Engine Company was founded in 1919 by namesake <a href="/wiki/Clessie_Cummins" title="Clessie Cummins">Clessie Cummins</a>, an Indiana mechanic. The <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Indiana" title="Columbus, Indiana">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> company focused on developing the engine invented 20 years earlier by <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Diesel" title="Rudolf Diesel">Rudolf Diesel</a>, but in spite of several well publicized endurance trials, it was not until 1933 that they introduced the successful Model H, used in small railroad switchers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cummins_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cummins-7">[7]</a></sup> In World War II, U.S. road building boom Cummins, with their N Series engines, became the leader in heavy duty truck engines, from 1952 to 1959 they had more than half the market.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWren1979232_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWren1979232-8">[8]</a></sup> In the 1960s the company opened an assembly plant in <a href="/wiki/Shotts" title="Shotts">Shotts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. By 2013 they had operations in 197 countries and territories.<sup id="cite_ref-Cummins_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cummins-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_units">Business units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business units">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Engine_Business">Cummins Engine Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cummins Engine Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Engine Business Unit consists of Aftermarket support, Mid-Range, Heavy-Duty, and High-Horsepower Engines. Cummins manufactures and markets a complete line of diesel and natural gas-powered engines for on-highway and off-highway use. Its markets include heavy-and medium-duty truck, bus, recreational vehicle (RV), light-duty automotive and a number of industrial uses including, construction, mining, marine, oil and gas, railroad and military equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-FactSheet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FactSheet-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the general public, the most visible Cummins product may be the 5.9-liter in-line six-cylinder engine used in the <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Ram" class="mw-redirect" title="Dodge Ram">Dodge Ram</a> light duty pickups starting in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2007, a 6.7-liter version of the Cummins straight six engine became optional on the RAM pickup.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2008, Cummins was a named defendant in a class action suit related to 1998-2001 model year Chrysler Dodge Ram trucks, model 2500 or 3500, originally equipped with a Cummins ISB 5.9 liter diesel engine built using a pattern 53 Block.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The case has been settled, but some qualified Chrysler owners may receive $500 for repairs to the block, which was alleged to crack and create a coolant leak.</p>
<p>In April 2013, Cummins utilized technology developed by <a href="/wiki/Westport_Innovations" title="Westport Innovations">Westport Innovations</a> to begin shipping large natural gas fueled engines to truck manufacturers in the United States as trucking companies began converting portions of their fleets to natural gas and the natural gas distribution network in the United States began to expand.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT42213_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT42213-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cummins has a Technical Centre in <a href="/wiki/Darlington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Darlington (UK Parliament constituency)">Darlington UK</a>, where it develops products for the European, Middle Eastern and Asian markets.</p>

<p>Cummins Power Generation Business Unit consists of Alternators, Automatic Transfer Switches, Commercial Power Systems, Consumer Systems, Engines, and Paralleling Systems. Cummins is a global provider of power generation systems, components and services in standby power, distributed power generation, as well as auxiliary power in mobile applications to meet the needs of a diversified customer base.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Component_Business">Cummins Component Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cummins Component Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Component Business Unit consists of Emission Solutions, Filtration (Fleetguard), Fuel Systems, Turbo Technologies (Holset), and Electronics. With regards to Turbo Technologies, Cummins designs and manufactures <a href="/wiki/Turbochargers" class="mw-redirect" title="Turbochargers">turbochargers</a> and related products, on a global scale, for diesel engines above 3 liters. In regard to Emission Solutions, Cummins develops and supplies catalytic exhaust systems and related products to the medium-and heavy-duty commercial diesel engine markets. In regard to Filtration, Cummins designs, manufactures and distributes heavy-duty and light-duty air, fuel, hydraulic and lube filtration, chemicals and exhaust system technology products for diesel and gas-powered equipment. With regards to Electronics, Cummins designs engine control units and sensors for Cummins diesel engines.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Distribution_Business">Cummins Distribution Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cummins Distribution Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Distribution Business consists of Engine and Power Generation Distribution as well as Service and Parts. The distribution unit of Cummins consists of 17 Cummins owned distributors and 10 joint ventures, covering 90 countries and territories through 234 locations.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Turbo_Technologies">Cummins Turbo Technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cummins Turbo Technologies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Holset Engineering Co.</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> company that produced <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbochargers</a>, primarily for <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel</a> and heavy duty application</p>
<p>In 1973 the company was purchased by Cummins after briefly being owned by the Hanson Trust. Holset now operates facilities in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>
<p>In 2006, the division officially changed its name to Cummins Turbo Technologies to be identified more closely with its parent company. The turbocharger products still use the Holset brand name.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Power_Generation">Cummins Power Generation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cummins Power Generation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1986, Cummins began acquisition of Onan and completed it in 1992. Since then, Onan has evolved into Cummins Power Generation, a wholly invested division of Cummins. The Onan name continues to be used for modern versions of their traditional engine-driven generators for RV, marine, commercial mobility, home standby, and portable use.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Emission_Solutions">Cummins Emission Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cummins Emission Solutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Exhaust and emissions after-treatment company Nelson Industries was purchased in 1999, due to the increasing importance of exhaust after-treatment systems for meeting future emissions standards. The division officially changed its name to <i>Cummins Emission Solutions</i>, in order to be identified more closely with their parent company.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India_operations">India operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: India operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cummins began its <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> operations on 17 February 1962 in a joint venture with the <a href="/wiki/Kirloskar_Group" title="Kirloskar Group">Kirloskar Group</a>. The ownership structure of the joint venture was divided as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cummins - 50%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kirloskar_Group" title="Kirloskar Group">Kirloskar Group</a> - 25.5%</li>
<li>Retail Investors - 24.5%</li>
</ul>
<p>As of 2013, the Cummins group had revenues of over $1.5 billion, 20 factories and 9000 employees in India.</p>
<p>Cummins does a significant part of its R&amp;D in India at the Cummins Research and Technology centre that was set up in 2003. Also, Cummins is building an advanced technical centre in <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a> which will house over 2000 engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cummins India has also made significant contributions to local skill development by establishing the <a href="/wiki/MKSSS%27s_Cummins_College_of_Engineering_for_Women" title="MKSSS's Cummins College of Engineering for Women">MKSSS's Cummins College of Engineering for Women</a>, a women-only engineering college in Pune.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
