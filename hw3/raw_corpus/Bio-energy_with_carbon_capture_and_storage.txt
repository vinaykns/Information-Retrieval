			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Mitigation_of_global_warming" class="mw-redirect" title="Mitigation of global warming">Mitigation of global warming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biochar" title="Biochar">Biochar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_removal" title="Carbon dioxide removal">Carbon dioxide removal</a></div>
<p><b>Bio-energy with carbon capture and storage</b> (<b>BECCS</b>) is a future <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">greenhouse gas mitigation</a> technology which produces <a href="/wiki/Negative_carbon_dioxide_emission" title="Negative carbon dioxide emission">negative carbon dioxide emissions</a> by combining <a href="/wiki/Bioenergy" title="Bioenergy">bioenergy</a> (energy from <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>) use with <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geologic</a> <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Obersteiner_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obersteiner-1">[1]</a></sup> The concept of BECCS is drawn from the integration of trees and crops, which extract carbon dioxide (CO<sub>2</sub>) from the atmosphere as they grow, the use of this biomass in processing industries or power plants, and the application of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> via CO<sub>2</sub> injection into geological formations.<sup id="cite_ref-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010-2">[2]</a></sup> There are other non-BECCS forms of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_removal" title="Carbon dioxide removal">carbon dioxide removal</a> and storage that include technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Biochar" title="Biochar">biochar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_air_capture" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon dioxide air capture">carbon dioxide air capture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration#Burial" title="Carbon sequestration">biomass burial</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rhodes_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rhodes-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to a recent <a href="/w/index.php?title=Biorecro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Biorecro (page does not exist)">Biorecro</a> report, there is 550 000 tonnes CO<sub>2</sub>/year in total BECCS capacity currently operating, divided between three different facilities (as of January 2012).<sup id="cite_ref-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ECN_Global_Technology_Roadmap_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECN_Global_Technology_Roadmap-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Fourth_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Fourth Assessment Report">IPCC Fourth Assessment Report</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a> (IPCC), BECCS was indicated as a key technology for reaching low carbon dioxide atmospheric concentration targets.<sup id="cite_ref-IPCC2007_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IPCC2007-8">[8]</a></sup> The negative emissions that can be produced by BECCS has been estimated by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> to be equivalent to a 50 to 150 <a href="/wiki/Parts_per_million" class="mw-redirect" title="Parts per million">ppm</a> decrease in <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_in_the_Earth%27s_atmosphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon dioxide in the Earth's atmosphere">global atmospheric carbon dioxide concentrations</a><sup id="cite_ref-RoyalSociety_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RoyalSociety-9">[9]</a></sup> and according to the <a href="/wiki/International_Energy_Agency" title="International Energy Agency">International Energy Agency</a>, the BLUE map <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation_scenarios" title="Climate change mitigation scenarios">climate change mitigation scenario</a> calls for more than 2 <a href="/wiki/Giga" class="mw-redirect" title="Giga">giga</a><a href="/wiki/Tonnes" class="mw-redirect" title="Tonnes">tonnes</a> of negative CO<sub>2</sub> emissions per year with BECCS in 2050.<sup id="cite_ref-IEA_Technology_Roadmap_Carbon_Capture_and_Storage_2009_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEA_Technology_Roadmap_Carbon_Capture_and_Storage_2009-10">[10]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, 10 <a href="/wiki/Giga" class="mw-redirect" title="Giga">giga</a><a href="/wiki/Tonnes" class="mw-redirect" title="Tonnes">tonnes</a> is achievable by this date.<sup id="cite_ref-Going_negative:_Stanford_scientists_explore_new_ways_to_remove_atmospheric_CO2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Going_negative:_Stanford_scientists_explore_new_ways_to_remove_atmospheric_CO2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a>, the UK <a href="/wiki/Met_Office" title="Met Office">Met Office</a> <a href="/wiki/Hadley_Centre_for_Climate_Prediction_and_Research" title="Hadley Centre for Climate Prediction and Research">Hadley Centre for Climate Prediction and Research</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Tyndall_Centre_for_Climate_Change_Research" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyndall Centre for Climate Change Research">Tyndall Centre for Climate Change Research</a>, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.walker-institute.ac.uk/">Walker Institute for Climate System Research</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Grantham_Institute_for_Climate_Change" class="mw-redirect" title="Grantham Institute for Climate Change">Grantham Institute for Climate Change</a> issued a joint report on carbon dioxide removal technologies as part of the <i>AVOID: Avoiding dangerous climate change</i> research program, stating that "Overall, of the technologies studied in this report, BECCS has the greatest maturity and there are no major practical barriers to its introduction into today’s energy system. The presence of a primary product will support early deployment."<sup id="cite_ref-AVOID_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AVOID-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/OECD" class="mw-redirect" title="OECD">OECD</a>, "Achieving lower concentration targets (450 ppm) depends significantly on the use of BECCS".<sup id="cite_ref-OECD_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OECD-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Negative_emission">Negative emission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Negative emission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sink" title="Carbon sink">Carbon sink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Negative_carbon_dioxide_emission" title="Negative carbon dioxide emission">Negative carbon dioxide emission</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carbon_flow.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Carbon_flow.jpg/400px-Carbon_flow.jpg" width="400" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Carbon_flow.jpg/600px-Carbon_flow.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Carbon_flow.jpg/800px-Carbon_flow.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="600" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carbon_flow.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Carbon flow schematic for different energy systems.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The main appeal of BECCS is in its ability to result in negative emissions of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a>. The capture of carbon dioxide from <a href="/wiki/Bioenergy" title="Bioenergy">bioenergy</a> sources effectively removes CO<sub>2</sub> from the atmosphere.<sup id="cite_ref-read_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-read-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bio-energy is derived from biomass which is a <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Renewable energy source">renewable energy source</a> and serves as a carbon sink during its growth. During industrial processes, the biomass combusted or processed re-releases the CO<sub>2</sub> into the atmosphere. The process thus results in a net zero emission of CO<sub>2</sub>, though this may be positively or negatively altered depending on the carbon emissions associated with biomass growth, transport and processing, see below under environmental considerations.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">Carbon capture and storage</a> (CCS) technology serves to intercept the release of CO<sub>2</sub> into the atmosphere and redirect it into geological storage locations.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> CO<sub>2</sub> with a biomass origin is not only released from biomass fuelled power plants, but also during the production of <a href="/wiki/Pulp_(paper)" title="Pulp (paper)">pulp</a> used to make paper and in the production of biofuels such as <a href="/wiki/Biogas" title="Biogas">biogas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bioethanol" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioethanol">bioethanol</a>. The BECCS technology can also be employed on such industrial processes.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is argued that through the BECCS technology, carbon dioxide is trapped in geologic formations for very long periods of time, whereas for example a tree only stores its carbon during its lifetime. In its report on the CCS technology, IPCC projects that more than 99% of carbon dioxide which is stored through geologic sequestration is likely to stay in place for more than 1000 years. While other types of carbon sinks such as the ocean, trees and soil may involve the risk of negative feedback loops at increased temperatures, BECCS technology is likely to provide a better permanence by storing CO<sub>2</sub> in geological formations.<sup id="cite_ref-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global_Status_of_BECCS_Projects_2010-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IPCC5_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IPCC5-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The amount of CO<sub>2</sub> that has been released to date is believed to be too much to be able to be absorbed by conventional sinks such as trees and soil in order to reach low emission targets.<sup id="cite_ref-hare_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hare-19">[19]</a></sup> In addition to the presently accumulated emissions, there will be significant additional emissions during this century, even in the most ambitious low-emission scenarios. BECCS has therefore been suggested as a technology to reverse the emission trend and create a global system of net negative emissions.<sup id="cite_ref-Obersteiner_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obersteiner-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IPCC2007_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IPCC2007-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hare_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hare-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Azar_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Azar-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This implies that the emissions would not only be zero, but negative, so that not only the emissions, but the absolute amount of CO<sub>2</sub> in the atmosphere would be reduced.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">Carbon capture and storage</a></div>
<p>The main technology for CO<sub>2</sub> capture from biotic sources generally employs the same technology as carbon dioxide capture from conventional fossil fuel sources. Broadly, three different types of technologies exist: <a href="/wiki/Post_combustion_capture" class="mw-redirect" title="Post combustion capture">post-combustion</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-combustion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pre-combustion (page does not exist)">pre-combustion</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oxy-fuel_combustion_process" title="Oxy-fuel combustion process">oxy-fuel combustion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cost">Cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> technical potential for net negative emissions with BECCS has been estimated to 10 <a href="/wiki/Gigatonne" class="mw-redirect" title="Gigatonne">Gt</a> of CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent annually, with an economic potential of up to 3.5 Gt of CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent annually at a cost of less than 50 €/tonne, and up to 3.9 Gt of CO<sub>2</sub> equivalent annually at a cost of less than 100 €/tonne.<sup id="cite_ref-IEA-GHG_Ecofys_BECCS_report_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IEA-GHG_Ecofys_BECCS_report-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy">Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Based on the current <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> agreement, carbon capture and storage projects are not applicable as an emission reduction tool to be used for the <a href="/wiki/Clean_Development_Mechanism" title="Clean Development Mechanism">Clean Development Mechanism</a> (CDM) or for <a href="/wiki/Joint_Implementation" title="Joint Implementation">Joint Implementation</a> (JI) projects.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Recognising CCS technologies as an emission reduction tool is vital for the implementation of such plants as there is no other financial motivation for the implementation of such systems. There has been growing support to have fossil CCS and BECCS included in the protocol. Accounting studies on how this can be implemented, including BECCS, have also been done.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Techno-economics_of_BECCS_and_the_TESBiC_Project">Techno-economics of BECCS and the TESBiC Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Techno-economics of BECCS and the TESBiC Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The largest and most detailed techno-economic assessment of BECCS was carried out by cmcl innovations and the TESBiC<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> group (Techno-Economic Study of Biomass to CCS) in 2012. This project recommended the most promising set of biomass fuelled power generation technologies coupled with carbon capture and storage (CCS). The project outcomes lead to a detailed “biomass CCS roadmap” for the U.K..</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_considerations">Environmental considerations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bio-energy_with_carbon_capture_and_storage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Environmental considerations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Issues_relating_to_biofuels" title="Issues relating to biofuels">Issues relating to biofuels</a></div>
<p>Some of the environmental considerations and other concerns about the widespread implementation of BECCS are similar to those of CCS. However, much of the critique towards CCS is that it may strengthen the dependency on depletable fossil fuels and environmentally invasive coal mining. This is not the case with BECCS, as it relies on renewable biomass. There are however other considerations which involve BECCS and these concerns are related to the possible increased use of <a href="/wiki/Biofuels" class="mw-redirect" title="Biofuels">biofuels</a>.</p>
<p>Biomass production is subject to a range of sustainability constraints, such as: scarcity of arable land and fresh water, loss of biodiversity, competition with food production, <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> and scarcity of phosphorus.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It is important to make sure that biomass is used in a way that maximizes both energy and climate benefits. There has been criticism to some suggested BECCS deployment scenarios, where there would be a very heavy reliance on increased biomass input.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>These systems may have other negative side effects. There is however presently no need to expand the use of biofuels in energy or industry applications to allow for BECCS deployment. There is already today considerable emissions from point sources of biomass derived CO<sub>2</sub>, which could be utilized for BECCS. Though, in possible future bio-energy system upscaling scenarios, this may be an important consideration.</p>
<p>The BECCS process allows CO<sub>2</sub> to be collected and stored directly from the atmosphere, rather than from a fossil source. This implies that any eventual emissions from storage may be recollected and restored simply by reiterating the BECCS-process. This is not possible with CCS alone, as CO<sub>2</sub> emitted to the atmosphere cannot be restored by burning more fossil fuel with CCS.</p>
