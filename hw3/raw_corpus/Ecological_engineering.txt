			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ecological engineering</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Environmental_engineering" title="Environmental engineering">Environmental engineering</a></div>
<p><b>Ecological engineering</b> is an emerging study of integrating <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, concerned with the design, monitoring, and construction of <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a>. According to Mitsch (1996) "the design of sustainable ecosystems intends to integrate <a href="/wiki/Human_society" class="mw-redirect" title="Human society">human society</a> with its <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a> for the benefit of both".<sup id="cite_ref-Mi_98_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mi_98-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecological_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ecological engineering emerged as a new idea in the early 1960s, but its definition has taken several decades to refine, its implementation is still undergoing adjustment, and its broader recognition as a new paradigm is relatively recent. Ecological engineering was introduced by <a href="/wiki/Howard_T._Odum" title="Howard T. Odum">Howard Odum</a> and others<sup id="cite_ref-Od_63_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Od_63-2">[2]</a></sup> as utilizing <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">natural energy</a> sources as the predominant input to manipulate and control environmental systems.</p>
<p>Mitsch and Jorgensen<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_89_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_89-3">[3]</a></sup> wrote that ecological engineering is designing societal services such that they benefit society and nature, and later noted<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the design should be systems based, sustainable, and integrate society with its natural environment. Odum<sup id="cite_ref-Od_89_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Od_89-6">[6]</a></sup> emphasized that <a href="/wiki/Self-organization" title="Self-organization">self-organizational</a> properties were a central feature to ecological engineering.</p>
<p>Mitsch and Jørgensen<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_89_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_89-3">[3]</a></sup> were the first to define ecological engineering and provide ecological engineering principles. Later they refined the definition and increased the number of principles.<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_03_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_03-7">[7]</a></sup> They defined and characterized ecological engineering in a 1989 book and clarified it further in their 2004 book (see Literature). They suggest the goal of ecological engineering is:</p>
<ol>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/Restoration_ecology" title="Restoration ecology">restoration of ecosystems</a> that have been substantially disturbed by human activities such as <a href="/wiki/Environmental_pollution" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental pollution">environmental pollution</a> or <a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_disturbance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Land disturbance (page does not exist)">land disturbance</a>, and</li>
<li>the development of new sustainable ecosystems that have both human and ecological values.</li>
</ol>
<p>They summarized the five concepts key to ecological engineering as:</p>
<ol>
<li>it is based on the self-designing <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">capacity of ecosystems</a>,</li>
<li>it can be a field test of ecological theory,</li>
<li>it relies on integrated system approaches,</li>
<li>it conserves <a href="/wiki/Non-renewable_energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-renewable energy">non-renewable energy</a>, and</li>
<li>it supports <a href="/wiki/Biological_conservation" class="mw-redirect" title="Biological conservation">biological conservation</a>.</li>
</ol>
<p>Bergen et al.<sup id="cite_ref-Be_01_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Be_01-8">[8]</a></sup> defined ecological engineering as:</p>
<ol>
<li>utilizing ecological science and theory,</li>
<li>applying to all types of ecosystems,</li>
<li>adapting engineering design methods, and</li>
<li>acknowledging a guiding value system.</li>
</ol>
<p>Barrett (1999)<sup id="cite_ref-Ba_99_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ba_99-9">[9]</a></sup> offers a more literal definition of the term: "the design, construction, operation and management (that is, engineering) of landscape/aquatic structures and associated plant and animal communities (that is, ecosystems) to benefit humanity and, often, nature." Barrett continues: "other terms with equivalent or similar meanings include <a href="/wiki/Ecotechnology" title="Ecotechnology">ecotechnology</a> and two terms most often used in the <a href="/wiki/Erosion_control" title="Erosion control">erosion control</a> field: soil bioengineering and biotechnical engineering. However, ecoengineering should not be confused with '<a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a>' when describing genetic engineering at the cellular level, or '<a href="/wiki/Bioengineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioengineering">bioengineering</a>' meaning construction of artificial body parts."</p>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> discipline combines basic and applied science from engineering, ecology, economics, and natural sciences for the restoration and construction of aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems. The field of ecological engineering is increasing in breadth and depth as more opportunities to design and use ecosystems as interfaces between technology and environment are explored.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Implementation of ecological engineering has focused on the creation or restoration of ecosystems, from <a href="/wiki/Land_degradation" title="Land degradation">degraded</a> <a href="/wiki/Wetlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetlands">wetlands</a> to multi-celled tubs and <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse" title="Greenhouse">greenhouses</a> that integrate microbial, fish, and plant services to process human <a href="/wiki/Wastewater" title="Wastewater">wastewater</a> into products such as fertilizers, flowers, and <a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">drinking water</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Potential applications of ecological engineering in cities have included the field of <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Urban_horticulture" title="Urban horticulture">urban horticulture</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Be_01_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Be_01-8">[8]</a></sup> which can be synthesized into urban <a href="/wiki/Stormwater_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Stormwater management">stormwater management</a>. Potential applications of ecological engineering in rural landscapes have included wetland treatment<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and community <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a> through traditional ecological knowledge.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Recent lifestyle and habitat planning examples include the <a href="/wiki/Permaculture" title="Permaculture">permaculture</a> movement.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_guidelines">Design guidelines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecological_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design guidelines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ecological engineering design will follow a cycle similar to <a href="/wiki/Engineering_design" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering design">engineering design</a> - problem formulation (goal), problem analysis (constraints), alternative solutions search, decision among alternatives, and specification of a complete solution.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Elements that distinguish ecological engineering design are elaborated by many authors, however a singular approach is still absent. Typically, the design goal involves protecting an at-risk ecosystem, restoring a degraded ecosystem, or creating a new sustainable ecosystem to satisfy needs of nature and society.<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_03_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_03-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A temporal framework is provided by Matlock et al.,<sup id="cite_ref-Ma_01_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ma_01-15">[15]</a></sup> stating the design solutions are considered in ecological time. In selecting between alternatives, the design should incorporate <a href="/wiki/Ecological_economics" title="Ecological economics">ecological economics</a> in design evaluation<sup id="cite_ref-Ma_01_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ma_01-15">[15]</a></sup> and acknowledge a guiding value system which promotes biological conservation.<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_03_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_03-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Be_01_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Be_01-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>applying to all types of ecosystems,</li>
<li>adapting engineering design methods, and</li>
<li>Design steps should be based on utilizing ecological science and theory,<sup id="cite_ref-Be_01_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Be_01-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>the self-designing capacity of ecosystems;<sup id="cite_ref-Od_89_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Od_89-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>accept the <a href="/wiki/Adaptive_management" title="Adaptive management">adaptive management</a> theory of learning from mistakes<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> as the design will field test <a href="/wiki/Ecological_theory" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological theory">ecological theory</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_03_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_03-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>utilize integrated <a href="/w/index.php?title=System_approach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="System approach (page does not exist)">system approaches</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-MJ_03_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MJ_03-7">[7]</a></sup> and</li>
<li>conserve non-renewable energy.<sup id="cite_ref-Od_63_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Od_63-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_curriculum">Academic curriculum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecological_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academic curriculum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An academic curriculum has been proposed for ecological engineering,<sup id="cite_ref-Ma_01_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ma_01-15">[15]</a></sup> and institutions around the world are starting programs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Key elements of this curriculum are:</p>
<ul>
<li>quantitative ecology,</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systems_ecology" title="Systems ecology">systems ecology</a>,</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Restoration_ecology" title="Restoration ecology">restoration ecology</a>,</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ecological_modeling" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological modeling">ecological modeling</a>,</li>
<li>ecological engineering,</li>
<li>economics of ecological engineering, and</li>
<li>technical <a href="/wiki/Course_(education)#Elective_and_required_courses" title="Course (education)">electives</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Complementing this set of courses are prerequisites courses in physical, biological, and chemical subject areas, and integrated design experiences. According to Matlock et al.,<sup id="cite_ref-Ma_01_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ma_01-15">[15]</a></sup> the design should identify constraints, characterize solutions in ecological time, and incorporate ecological economics in design evaluation. Economics of ecological engineering has been demonstrated using energy principles for a wetland.,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and using nutrient valuation for a dairy farm <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
