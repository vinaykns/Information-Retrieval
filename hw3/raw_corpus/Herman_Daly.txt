<p><b>Herman Edward Daly</b> (born July 21, 1938) is an American <a href="/wiki/Ecological_economist" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological economist">ecological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgism#Georgism_and_environmental_economics" title="Georgism">Georgist</a> economist<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and emeritus professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland_School_of_Public_Policy" title="University of Maryland School of Public Policy">School of Public Policy</a> of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland,_College_Park" title="University of Maryland, College Park">University of Maryland, College Park</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_Daly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before joining the World Bank, Daly was a Research Associate at Yale University,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and Alumni Professor of Economics at <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_State_University" title="Louisiana State University">Louisiana State University</a>.</p>
<p>Daly was Senior Economist in the Environment Department of the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, where he helped to develop policy guidelines related to <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. While there, he was engaged in environmental operations work in Latin America. He is closely associated with theories of a <a href="/wiki/Steady-state_economy#Herman_Daly.27s_concept_of_a_steady-state_economy" title="Steady-state economy">steady-state economy</a>. He was a co-founder and associate editor of the journal, <i><a href="/wiki/Ecological_Economics_(journal)" title="Ecological Economics (journal)">Ecological Economics</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1989 Daly and <a href="/wiki/John_B._Cobb" title="John B. Cobb">John B. Cobb</a> developed the <a href="/wiki/Measure_of_Economic_Welfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Measure of Economic Welfare">Index of Sustainable Economic Welfare (ISEW)</a>, which they proposed as a more valid measure of socio-economic progress than <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a>.</p>
<p>Daly is a recipient of an Honorary <a href="/wiki/Right_Livelihood_Award" title="Right Livelihood Award">Right Livelihood Award</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Dr_A.H._Heineken_Prize" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr A.H. Heineken Prize">Heineken Prize</a> for Environmental Science from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences">Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences</a>, the 1992 <a href="/wiki/University_of_Louisville" title="University of Louisville">University of Louisville</a> <a href="/wiki/Grawemeyer_Award" title="Grawemeyer Award">Grawemeyer Award</a> for Ideas Improving World Order,<sup id="cite_ref-grawemeyer.org_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grawemeyer.org-5">[5]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Prize" title="Sophie Prize">Sophie Prize</a> (Norway), the <a href="/wiki/Leontief" class="mw-redirect" title="Leontief">Leontief</a> Prize from the <a href="/wiki/Global_Development_and_Environment_Institute" title="Global Development and Environment Institute">Global Development and Environment Institute</a> and was chosen as Man of the Year 2008 by <a href="/wiki/Adbusters" title="Adbusters">Adbusters</a> magazine. He is widely credited with having originated the idea of <a href="/wiki/Uneconomic_growth" title="Uneconomic growth">uneconomic growth</a>, though some credit this to <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Waring" title="Marilyn Waring">Marilyn Waring</a> who developed it more completely in her study of the <a href="/wiki/UN_System_of_National_Accounts" class="mw-redirect" title="UN System of National Accounts">UN System of National Accounts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2014, Daly was the recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Planet_Prize" title="Blue Planet Prize">Blue Planet Prize</a> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> of the Asahi Glass Foundation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toward_a_Steady-State_Economy">Toward a Steady-State Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_Daly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toward a Steady-State Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daly was the editor of a long-lived and influential anthology, originally published in 1973 as <i>Toward a Steady-state Economy</i>, and twice revised (under different titles; see bibliography), in 1980 and 1993. Writers and topics in the original 1973 edition included:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Georgescu-Roegen" title="Nicholas Georgescu-Roegen">Nicholas Georgescu-Roegen</a> on <i>The Entropy Law and the Economic Problem</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Preston_Cloud" title="Preston Cloud">Preston Cloud</a> on mineral resources</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_R._Ehrlich" title="Paul R. Ehrlich">Paul R. Ehrlich</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Holdren" title="John Holdren">John Holdren</a> on population</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Leon_R._Kass" class="mw-redirect" title="Leon R. Kass">Leon R. Kass</a> on bioethics</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenneth_E._Boulding" title="Kenneth E. Boulding">Kenneth E. Boulding</a> on the "Economics of the Coming <a href="/wiki/Spaceship_Earth" title="Spaceship Earth">Spaceship Earth</a>"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Garrett_Hardin" title="Garrett Hardin">Garrett Hardin</a>'s 1968 article, "<a href="/wiki/The_Tragedy_of_the_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="The Tragedy of the Commons">The Tragedy of the Commons</a>"</li>
<li>Daly on the steady-state economy</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warren_A._Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Warren A. Johnson (page does not exist)">Warren A. Johnson</a> on the guaranteed income as an environmental measure</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_England_(ecologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Richard England (ecologist) (page does not exist)">Richard England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barry_Bluestone" title="Barry Bluestone">Barry Bluestone</a> on ecology and social conflict</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Ophuls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="William Ophuls (page does not exist)">William Ophuls</a> on political economy ("Leviathan or oblivion?")</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/E.F._Schumacher" class="mw-redirect" title="E.F. Schumacher">E.F. Schumacher</a> on "<a href="/wiki/Small_is_Beautiful" class="mw-redirect" title="Small is Beautiful">Small is Beautiful</a>" (title of his book, also published in 1973)</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walter_A._Weisskopf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Walter A. Weisskopf (page does not exist)">Walter A. Weisskopf</a> on economic growth versus existential balance</li>
<li>Daly's essay, "Electric power, employment, and economic growth: a case study in growthmania"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J%C3%B8rgen_Randers" title="Jørgen Randers">Jørgen Randers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donella_Meadows" title="Donella Meadows">Donella Meadows</a> on the carrying capacity of the environment</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_B._Cobb" title="John B. Cobb">John B. Cobb</a> on "ecology, ethics, and theology</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/C.S._Lewis" class="mw-redirect" title="C.S. Lewis">C.S. Lewis</a> on "<a href="/wiki/The_Abolition_of_Man" title="The Abolition of Man">The Abolition of Man</a>" (an extract from his 1943 book of the same name)</li>
</ul>
