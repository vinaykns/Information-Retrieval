			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Biodiversity loss</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Loss of biodiversity</b> or <b>biodiversity loss</b> refers to either</p>
<ul>
<li>the ongoing <i><a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinction</a></i> of species worldwide or</li>
<li>the local reduction or loss of species in a certain <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ecological_niche" title="Ecological niche">ecological niche</a> or <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>The latter phenomenon can be temporary or permanent, depending on whether the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a> that leads to the loss is reversible through <a href="/wiki/Ecological_restoration" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecological restoration">ecological restoration</a> / <a href="/wiki/Ecological_resilience" title="Ecological resilience">ecological resilience</a> or effectively permanent (e.g. through <a href="/wiki/Land_loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Land loss">land loss</a>). Global extinction has so far been proven to be irreversible.</p>
<p>Even though permanent <a href="/wiki/Global_biodiversity" title="Global biodiversity">global</a> <a href="/wiki/Species_loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Species loss">species loss</a> is a more dramatic phenomenon than regional changes in <a href="/wiki/Species_composition" class="mw-redirect" title="Species composition">species composition</a>, even minor changes from a healthy stable state can have dramatic influence on the <a href="/wiki/Food_web" title="Food web">food web</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Food_chain" title="Food chain">food chain</a> insofar as reductions in only one species can adversely affect the entire chain (<a href="/wiki/Coextinction" title="Coextinction">coextinction</a>), leading to an overall reduction in <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>, possible <a href="/wiki/Alternative_stable_states" class="mw-redirect" title="Alternative stable states">alternative stable states</a> of an ecosystem notwithstanding. <a href="/wiki/Ecological_effects_of_biodiversity" title="Ecological effects of biodiversity">Ecological effects of biodiversity</a> are usually counteracted by its loss. Reduced biodiversity in particular leads to reduced <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">ecosystem services</a> and eventually poses an immediate danger for <a href="/wiki/Food_security" title="Food security">food security</a>, also for humankind.<sup id="cite_ref-cardinale2012_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cardinale2012-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_rate">Loss rate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biodiversity_loss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Loss rate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current <a href="/wiki/Rate_(mathematics)" title="Rate (mathematics)">rate</a> of <i>global</i> diversity loss is estimated to be a 1000 times higher than the (naturally occurring) <a href="/wiki/Background_extinction_rate" title="Background extinction rate">background extinction rate</a> and expected to still grow in the upcoming years.</p>
<p>Locally bounded loss rates can be <a href="/wiki/Measurement" title="Measurement">measured</a> using <a href="/wiki/Species_richness" title="Species richness">species richness</a> and its variation <a href="/wiki/Time_series" title="Time series">over time</a>. Raw <a href="/wiki/Count_data" title="Count data">counts</a> may not be as ecologically relevant as relative or absolute <a href="/wiki/Abundance_(ecology)" title="Abundance (ecology)">abundances</a>. Taking into account the <a href="/wiki/Relative_frequencies" class="mw-redirect" title="Relative frequencies">relative frequencies</a>, a considerable number of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity_index" class="mw-redirect" title="Biodiversity index">biodiversity indexes</a> has been developed. Besides richness, evenness and heterogeneity are considered to be the main dimensions along which diversity can be measured.<sup id="cite_ref-cardinale2012_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cardinale2012-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As with all diversity measures, it is essential to accurately classify the spatial and temporal scope of the observation. "Definitions tend to become less precise as the complexity of the subject increases and the associated spatial and temporal scales widen.<sup id="cite_ref-tagliapietra_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tagliapietra-2">[2]</a></sup> Biodiversity itself is not a single concept but can be split up into various scales (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_diversity" title="Ecosystem diversity">ecosystem diversity</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> diversity or even biodiversity vs. habitat d.<sup id="cite_ref-tagliapietra_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tagliapietra-2">[2]</a></sup>) or different subcategories (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_diversity" title="Phylogenetic diversity">phylogenetic diversity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Species_diversity" title="Species diversity">species diversity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Genetic_diversity" title="Genetic diversity">genetic diversity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nucleotide_diversity" title="Nucleotide diversity">nucleotide diversity</a>). The question of net loss in confined regions is often a matter of debate but longer observation times are generally thought to be beneficial to loss estimates.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>To compare rates between different geographic regions <a href="/wiki/Latitudinal_gradients_in_species_diversity" title="Latitudinal gradients in species diversity">latitudinal gradients in species diversity</a> should also be considered.</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Alpha_diversity" title="Alpha diversity">alpha diversity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beta_diversity" title="Beta diversity">beta diversity</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gamma_diversity" title="Gamma diversity">gamma diversity</a></div>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Factors">Factors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biodiversity_loss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Factors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Major factors for <a href="/wiki/Biotic_stress" title="Biotic stress">biotic stress</a> and the ensuing accelerating loss rate are, <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity#Threats" title="Biodiversity">amongst other threats</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat loss">Habitat loss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Habitat_degradation" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat degradation">degradation</a>
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">Land use</a> intensification (and ensuing <a href="/wiki/Land_loss" class="mw-redirect" title="Land loss">land loss</a>/habitat loss) has been identified to be a significant factor in loss of ecological services due to direct effects as well as biodiversity loss.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">Climate change</a> through <a href="/wiki/Heat_stress" class="mw-redirect" title="Heat stress">heat stress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drought_stress" class="mw-redirect" title="Drought stress">drought stress</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eutrophication" title="Eutrophication">Excessive nutrient load</a> and other forms of <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Over-exploitation" class="mw-redirect" title="Over-exploitation">Over-exploitation</a> and unsustainable use (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Unsustainable_fishing_methods" title="Unsustainable fishing methods">unsustainable fishing methods</a>) we are currently using 25% more natural resources than the planet</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Invasive_alien_species" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasive alien species">Invasive alien species</a> that effectively <a href="/wiki/Competition_(biology)" title="Competition (biology)">compete</a> for a niche, replacing <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_species" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous species">indigenous species</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
