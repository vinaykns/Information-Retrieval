			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Environmental engineering</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div role="note" class="hatnote">See also: <a href="/wiki/Ecological_engineering" title="Ecological engineering">Ecological engineering</a></div>
<p><b>Environmental engineering</b> is the branch of engineering concerned with the application of scientific and engineering principles for protection of human populations from the effects of adverse environmental factors; protection of environments, both local and global, from potentially deleterious effects of natural and human activities; and improvement of environmental quality.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Environmental engineering can also be described as a branch of applied science and technology that addresses the issues of energy preservation, production asset and control of waste from human and animal activities. Furthermore, it is concerned with finding plausible solutions in the field of public health, such as <a href="/wiki/Waterborne_diseases" title="Waterborne diseases">waterborne diseases</a>, implementing laws which promote adequate sanitation in urban, rural and recreational areas. It involves <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">waste water management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a> control, recycling, <a href="/wiki/Waste_disposal" class="mw-redirect" title="Waste disposal">waste disposal</a>, radiation protection, industrial hygiene, animal agriculture, environmental sustainability, <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_engineering_law" title="Environmental engineering law">environmental engineering law</a>. It also includes studies on the environmental impact of proposed construction projects.</p>
<p>Environmental engineers study the effect of technological advances on the environment. To do so, they conduct studies on hazardous-<a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a> to evaluate the significance of such hazards, advise on treatment and containment, and develop regulations to prevent mishaps. Environmental engineers design <a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">municipal water supply</a> and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_wastewater_treatment" title="Industrial wastewater treatment">industrial wastewater treatment</a> systems.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They address local and worldwide environmental issues such as the effects of <a href="/wiki/Acid_rain" title="Acid rain">acid rain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ozone_depletion" title="Ozone depletion">ozone depletion</a>, water pollution and air pollution from <a href="/wiki/Automobile_emissions_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile emissions control">automobile exhausts</a> and <a href="/wiki/AP_42_Compilation_of_Air_Pollutant_Emission_Factors" title="AP 42 Compilation of Air Pollutant Emission Factors">industrial sources</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics.2C_U.S._Department_of_Labor.U.S._Department_of_Labor.2C_Occupational_Outlook_Handbook.2C_2010-11_Edition_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bureau_of_Labor_Statistics.2C_U.S._Department_of_Labor.U.S._Department_of_Labor.2C_Occupational_Outlook_Handbook.2C_2010-11_Edition-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>At many universities environmental engineering programs are offered at either the department of <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineering</a> or the department of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> at engineering faculties. Environmental "civil" engineers focus on hydrology, water resources management, bioremediation, and water treatment plant design. Environmental "chemical" engineers, on the other hand, focus on environmental chemistry, advanced air and water treatment technologies and separation processes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Additionally, engineers are more frequently obtaining specialized training in law (<a href="/wiki/Juris_Doctor" title="Juris Doctor">J.D.</a>) and are utilizing their technical expertise in the practices of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_engineering_law" title="Environmental engineering law">environmental engineering law</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Most jurisdictions also impose licensing and registration requirements.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ever since people first recognized that their health is related to the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_quality" title="Environmental quality">quality of their environment</a>, they have applied principles to attempt to improve the quality of their environment. The ancient Indian <a href="/wiki/Harappan_civilization" class="mw-redirect" title="Harappan civilization">Harappan civilization</a> utilized early sewers in some cities more than 5000 years ago. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_aqueduct" title="Roman aqueduct">Romans constructed aqueducts</a> to prevent drought and to create a clean, healthful water supply for the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. In the 15th century, <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> created laws restricting the development and degradation of alpine country that constituted the region's water supply.</p>
<p>The field emerged as a separate environmental discipline during the middle third of the 20th century in response to widespread public concern about water and pollution and increasingly extensive environmental quality degradation. However, its roots extend back to early efforts in public health engineering.<sup id="cite_ref-National_Science_Foundation..28Dec._3.2C_2009.29.Environmental_Engineering_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-National_Science_Foundation..28Dec._3.2C_2009.29.Environmental_Engineering-8">[8]</a></sup> Modern environmental engineering began in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in the mid-19th century when <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Bazalgette" title="Joseph Bazalgette">Joseph Bazalgette</a> designed the first major <a href="/wiki/Sewage_collection_and_disposal" class="mw-redirect" title="Sewage collection and disposal">sewerage</a> system that reduced the incidence of waterborne diseases such as <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a>. The introduction of drinking water treatment and sewage treatment in industrialized countries reduced waterborne diseases from leading causes of death to rarities.<sup id="cite_ref-Water_industry_from_The_Oxford_Companion_to_British_History_online_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Water_industry_from_The_Oxford_Companion_to_British_History_online-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In many cases, as societies grew, actions that were intended to achieve benefits for those societies had longer-term impacts which reduced other environmental qualities. One example is the widespread application of the pesticide <a href="/wiki/DDT" class="mw-redirect" title="DDT">DDT</a> to control agricultural pests in the years following <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. While the <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> benefits were outstanding and crop yields increased dramatically thus reducing world hunger substantially, and <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> was controlled better than it ever had been, numerous species were brought to the verge of extinction due to the impact of the DDT on their reproductive cycles. The story of DDT as vividly told in <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Carson" title="Rachel Carson">Rachel Carson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Spring" title="Silent Spring">Silent Spring</a></i> (1962) is considered to be the birth of the modern environmental movement and of the modern field of "environmental engineering."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Conservation_movement" title="Conservation movement">Conservation movements</a> and <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">laws</a> restricting public actions that would harm the environment have been developed by various societies for millennia. Notable examples are the laws decreeing the construction of <a href="/wiki/Sanitary_sewer" title="Sanitary sewer">sewers</a> in <a href="/wiki/London_sewerage_system" title="London sewerage system">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris_Sewer_Museum" title="Paris Sewer Museum">Paris</a> in the 19th century and the creation of the U.S. national park system in the early 20th century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">Scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solid_waste_management">Solid waste management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solid waste management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_impact_assessment_and_mitigation">Environmental impact assessment and mitigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Environmental impact assessment and mitigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact_assessment" title="Environmental impact assessment">Environmental impact assessment</a></div>
<p>Scientists have air pollution dispersion models to evaluate the concentration of a pollutant at a receptor or the impact on overall air quality from vehicle exhausts and industrial flue gas stack emissions. To some extent, this field overlaps the desire to decrease carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions from combustion processes. They apply scientific and engineering principles to evaluate if there are likely to be any adverse impacts to water quality, air quality, <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat (ecology)">habitat</a> quality, <a href="/wiki/Flora_(plants)" class="mw-redirect" title="Flora (plants)">flora</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fauna_(animals)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fauna (animals)">fauna</a>, agricultural capacity, <a href="/wiki/Traffic" title="Traffic">traffic</a> impacts, social impacts, ecological impacts, noise impacts, visual (landscape) impacts, etc. If impacts are expected, they then develop mitigation measures to limit or prevent such impacts. An example of a mitigation measure would be the creation of <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetlands</a> in a nearby location to mitigate the filling in of wetlands necessary for a road development if it is not possible to reroute the road.</p>
<p>In the United States, the practice of environmental assessment was formally initiated on January 1, 1970, the effective date of the <a href="/wiki/National_Environmental_Policy_Act" title="National Environmental Policy Act">National Environmental Policy Act</a> (NEPA). Since that time, more than 100 developing and developed nations either have planned specific analogous laws or have adopted procedure used elsewhere. NEPA is applicable to all federal agencies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-McGraw-Hill.2C_Inc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McGraw-Hill.2C_Inc-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_supply_and_treatment">Water supply and treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Water supply and treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>They evaluate the water balance within a <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">watershed</a> and determine the available water supply, the water needed for various needs in that watershed, the seasonal cycles of water movement through the watershed and they develop systems to store, treat, and convey water for various uses. Water is treated to achieve water quality objectives for the end uses. In the case of a <a href="/wiki/Potable_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Potable water">potable water</a> supply, water is treated to minimize the risk of <a href="/wiki/Infectious_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Infectious disease">infectious disease</a> transmission, the risk of non-infectious illness, and to create a palatable water flavor. Water distribution systems are designed and built to provide adequate water pressure and flow rates to meet various end-user needs such as domestic use, fire suppression, and <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wastewater_treatment">Wastewater treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wastewater treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Water_pollution.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d6/Water_pollution.jpg" width="200" height="174" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="174" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Water_pollution.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Water pollution</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There are numerous wastewater treatment technologies. A wastewater treatment train can consist of a primary clarifier system to remove solid and floating materials, a secondary treatment system consisting of an <a href="/wiki/Aeration" title="Aeration">aeration</a> basin followed by <a href="/wiki/Flocculation" title="Flocculation">flocculation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Settling" title="Settling">sedimentation</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Activated_sludge" title="Activated sludge">activated sludge</a> system and a secondary clarifier, a tertiary biological <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a> removal system, and a final <a href="/wiki/Disinfection" class="mw-redirect" title="Disinfection">disinfection</a> process. The aeration basin/activated sludge system removes organic material by growing bacteria (activated sludge). The secondary clarifier removes the activated sludge from the water. The tertiary system, although not always included due to costs, is becoming more prevalent to remove nitrogen and <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus" title="Phosphorus">phosphorus</a> and to disinfect the water before discharge to a surface water stream or ocean outfall.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_pollution_management">Air pollution management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Air pollution management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scientists have developed <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_dispersion_modeling" title="Atmospheric dispersion modeling">air pollution dispersion models</a> to evaluate the concentration of a pollutant at a receptor or the impact on overall air quality from vehicle <a href="/wiki/Exhaust_system" title="Exhaust system">exhausts</a> and industrial <a href="/wiki/Flue_gas_stack" class="mw-redirect" title="Flue gas stack">flue gas stack</a> emissions. To some extent, this field overlaps the desire to decrease <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions from combustion processes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_Protection_Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Environmental Protection Agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Environmental_Protection_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Environmental Protection Agency">U.S. Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) is one of the many agencies that work with environmental engineers to solve key issues. An important component of EPA's mission is to protect and improve air, water, and overall environmental quality in order to avoid or mitigate the consequences of harmful effects.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecological_engineering_for_sustainable_agriculture_in_arid_and_semiarid_West_African_regions.5B13.5D">Ecological engineering for sustainable agriculture in arid and semiarid West African regions<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ecological engineering for sustainable agriculture in arid and semiarid West African regions[13]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ecological engineering offers new alternatives for the management of agricultural systems that are more tailored to the ever-changing social and environmental necessities in these regions. This requires managing the complexity of agrosystems, while striving to mimic the functioning of natural ecosystems of West African drylands and taking advantage of traditional practices and local know-how resulting from a long process of adaptation to environmental constraints.</p>
<ol>
<li>Acting on biodiversity. Biodiversity is essential to the productivity of ecosystems and their temporal stability under the impact of external disturbances. Several ecological processes related to biodiversity may be intensified for the benefit of agrosilvopastoral systems: promoting diversity and soil microorganism activity to benefit&#160;plants, associating and utilizing the mutual benefits of plants</li>
<li>Utilizing organic matter and nutrient cycles. The productivity of agrosystems with low chemical input use in dryland regions is primarily based on efficient organic resource management, and in turn on the nutrient and energy flows they induce. It is thus possible to intervene at several levels: enhancing crop-livestock farming integration to preserve natural resources, restoring the biological activity of soils via specific organic&#160;inputs, supplying nutrients to plants locally.</li>
<li>Enhancing available water use. Water supplies are limited and irregular in dryland areas. Current management of these supplies—which involves capturing rainwater and surface runoff—could be improved in several ways: adapting to erratic rainfall or drought risks by focusing on: (i) the organization of the farm and community (farm plot patterns in association with the random rainfall distribution, etc.), and on (ii) cropping techniques to reduce crop water needs (plant choices, weeding, etc.), preserving water in crop fields by hampering runoff, accounting for the essential role of trees regarding soil and water in drylands.</li>
<li>Managing landscapes and associated ecological processes. Ecological crop pest regulation by their natural enemies is one ecosystem service provided by biodiversity. Better pest management could be considered in association with promoting biodiversity at different scales, e.g. from the plant to the landscape.</li>
</ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Courses aimed at developing graduates with specific skills in environmental systems or environmental technology are becoming more common and fall into broad classes:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">Mechanical engineering</a></i> courses oriented towards designing machines and mechanical systems for environmental use such as water treatment facilities, pumping stations, garbage segregation plants and other mechanical facilities;</li>
<li><i>Environmental engineering or environmental systems</i> courses oriented towards a civil engineering approach in which structures and the landscape are constructed to blend with or protect the environment;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Environmental_chemistry" title="Environmental chemistry">Environmental chemistry</a></i>, <i>sustainable chemistry</i> or <i>environmental <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineer" title="Chemical engineer">chemical</a> engineering</i> courses oriented towards understanding the effects (good and bad) of chemicals in the environment. Focus on mining processes, pollutants and commonly also cover biochemical processes;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Environmental_technology" title="Environmental technology">Environmental technology</a></i> courses oriented towards producing electronic or electrical graduates capable of developing devices and artifacts able to monitor, measure, model and control environmental impact, including monitoring and managing energy generation from renewable sources.</li>
</ul>
