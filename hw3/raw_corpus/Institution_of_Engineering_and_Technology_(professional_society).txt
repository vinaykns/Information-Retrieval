<p>The <b>Institution of Engineering and Technology</b> (<b>IET</b>) is the largest multidisciplinary professional engineering institution in the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The IET was formed in 2006 from two separate institutions: the <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Electrical_Engineers" title="Institution of Electrical Engineers">Institution of Electrical Engineers</a> (IEE), dating back to 1871,<sup id="cite_ref-ECUK_Institution_Details_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECUK_Institution_Details-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Incorporated_Engineers" title="Institution of Incorporated Engineers">Institution of Incorporated Engineers</a> (IIE) dating back to 1884. Its worldwide membership is currently in excess of 167,000. The IET's main offices are in <a href="/wiki/Savoy_Place" title="Savoy Place">Savoy Place</a> in <a href="/wiki/London,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="London, England">London, England</a> and at Michael Faraday House in <a href="/wiki/Stevenage,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Stevenage, England">Stevenage, England</a>.</p>
<p>The IET has the authority to establish professional registration for the titles of Chartered Engineer, Incorporated Engineer, Engineering Technician, and ICT Technician, as a Licensed Member institution of the <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Council" title="Engineering Council">Engineering Council</a>.</p>
<p>The IET is registered as a charity in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Discussions started in 2004 between the IEE and the IIE about merging to form a new institution. In September 2005, both institutions held votes of the merger and the members voted in favour (73.5% IEE, 95.7% IIE). This merger also needed government approval, so a petition was then made to the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Council of the United Kingdom</a> for a Supplemental Charter, to allow the creation of the new institution. This was approved by the Privy Council on December 14, 2005, and the new institution emerged on March 31, 2006.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_IEE">History of the IEE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History of the IEE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Electrical_Engineers" title="Institution of Electrical Engineers">Institution of Electrical Engineers</a></div>
<p>The Society of Telegraph Engineers (STE) was formed on May 17, 1871, and it published the <i>Journal of the Society of Telegraph Engineers</i> from 1872 through 1880. On December 22, 1880, the STE was renamed as the Society of Telegraph Engineers and of Electricians and, as part of this change, it renamed its journal the <i>Journal of the Society of Telegraph Engineers and of Electricians</i> (1881 – 82) and later the <i>Journal of the Society of Telegraph-Engineers and Electricians</i> (1883 – 88). Following a meeting of its Council on 10 November 1887, it was decided to adopt the name of the Institution of Electrical Engineers (IEE). The name of the Institution of Electrical Engineers remains engraved in the marble façade of its headquarters at Savoy Place. As part of this change, its Journal was renamed <i>Journal of the Institution of Electrical Engineers</i> in 1889, and it kept this title through 1963. In 1921, the Institution was Incorporated by royal charter and, following mergers with the Institution of Electronic and Radio Engineers (IERE) in 1988 and the Institution of Manufacturing Engineers (IMfgE) in 1990, it had a worldwide membership of around 120,000. The IEE represented the engineering profession, operated Professional Networks (worldwide groups of engineers sharing common technical and professional interests), had an educational role including the accreditation of degree courses and operated schemes to provide awards scholarships, grants and prizes. It was well known for publication of the <i>IEE Wiring Regulations</i> which now continue to be written by the IET and to be published by the <a href="/wiki/British_Standards_Institution" class="mw-redirect" title="British Standards Institution">British Standards Institution</a> as <a href="/wiki/BS_7671" title="BS 7671">BS 7671</a>.</p>
<p>The IET hosts the archive for the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Engineering_Society" title="Women's Engineering Society">Women's Engineering Society</a> (WES) and it has also provided office space for WES since 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_IIE">History of the IIE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of the IIE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Incorporated_Engineers" title="Institution of Incorporated Engineers">Institution of Incorporated Engineers</a></div>
<p>The modern <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Incorporated_Engineers" title="Institution of Incorporated Engineers">Institution of Incorporated Engineers</a> (IIE) traced its heritage to The Vulcanic Society that was founded in 1884 and became the Junior Institution of Engineers in 1902, which became the Institution of General Technician Engineers in 1970. It changed its name in 1976 to the Institution of Mechanical and General Technician Engineers. At this point it merged with the Institution of Technician Engineers in Mechanical Engineering and formed the Institution of Mechanical Incorporated Engineers in 1988. The Institution of Engineers in Charge, which was founded in 1895, was merged into the Institution of Mechanical Incorporated Engineers (IMechIE) in 1990.</p>
<p>The Institution of Electrical and Electronic Technician Engineers, the Society of Electronic and Radio Technicians, and the Institute of Practitioners in Radio and Electronics merged in 1990 to form the Institution of Electronics and Electrical Incorporated Engineers (IEEIE).</p>
<p>The IIE was formed in April 1998 by the merger of The Institution of Electronic and Electrical Incorporated Engineers (IEEIE), The Institution of Mechanical Incorporated Engineers (IMechIE), and The Institute of Engineers and Technicians (IET, not to be confused with the later-formed Institution of Engineering and Technology). In 1999 there was a further merger with The Institution of Incorporated Executive Engineers (IIExE). The IIE had a worldwide membership of approximately 40,000.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Journals">Journals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Journals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IET has a <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">journals</a> publishing programme,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> totalling 24 titles such as <i><a href="/wiki/IET_Software" title="IET Software">IET Software</a></i> as of August 2016 (with the addition of <i>IET Biometrics</i> and <i>IET Networks</i>). The journals contain both original and review-oriented papers relating to various disciplines in electrical, electronics, computing, control, biomedical and communications technologies.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Electronics_Letters" title="Electronics Letters">Electronics Letters</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is a peer-reviewed rapid-communication journal, which publishes short original research papers every two weeks. Its scope covers developments in all electronic and electrical engineering related fields. Also available to Electronics Letters subscribers are something called the <i>Insight Letters</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micro_%26_Nano_Letters&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Micro &amp; Nano Letters (page does not exist)">Micro &amp; Nano Letters</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> first published in 2016, specialises in the rapid online publication of short research papers concentrating on advances in miniature and ultraminiature structures and systems that have at least one dimension ranging from a few tens of micrometres to a few nanometres. It offers a rapid route for international dissemination of research findings generated by researchers from the micro and nano communities.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_scholarships">Awards and scholarships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and scholarships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Achievement_awards_and_medals">Achievement awards and medals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Achievement awards and medals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/IET_Achievement_Medals" title="IET Achievement Medals">IET Achievement Medals</a> are awarded to individuals who have made major and distinguished contributions in the various sectors of science, engineering and technology. The judging panel look for outstanding and sustained excellence in one or more activities. For example: research and development, innovation, design, manufacturing, technical management, and the promotion of engineering and technology.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Faraday_Medal"><a href="/wiki/Faraday_Medal" title="Faraday Medal">Faraday Medal</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Faraday Medal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Faraday_Medal" title="Faraday Medal">Faraday Medal</a> is a bronze medal awarded either for notable scientific or industrial achievement in engineering or for conspicuous service rendered to the advancement of science, engineering and technology without restriction as regards nationality, country of residence or membership of the Institution. It is awarded not more frequently than once a year. The award was established in 1922 to commemorate the 50th Anniversary of the first Ordinary Meeting of the Society of Telegraph Engineers and is named after <a href="/wiki/Michael_Faraday" title="Michael Faraday">Michael Faraday</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountbatten_Medal"><a href="/wiki/Mountbatten_Medal" title="Mountbatten Medal">Mountbat<dfn>ten Medal</dfn></a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mountbatten Medal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mountbatten_Medal" title="Mountbatten Medal">Mountbat<dfn>ten Medal</dfn></a> <dfn>celebrates individuals</dfn> who have made an outstanding contribution, over a period of time, to the promotion of electronics or information technology and their application. Contributions can be within the spheres of science, technology, industry or commerce and in the dissemination of the understanding of electronics and information technology, whether to young people, or adults. The Medal was founded by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Electronics_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Electronics Council (page does not exist)">National Electronics Council</a> in <dfn>1992 and named after</dfn> <a href="/wiki/Earl_Mountbatten_of_Burma" title="Earl Mountbatten of Burma">The Earl Mountbat<dfn>ten of Burma</dfn></a>, the first Chairman of the Council.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mensforth_Manufacturing_Gold_Medal"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mensforth_Manufacturing_Gold_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal (page does not exist)">Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mensforth_Manufacturing_Gold_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal (page does not exist)">Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal</a> is awarded to candidates who have made major and distinguished contributions to advancing the manufacturing sector. Like the Faraday Medal, the Mensforth Manufacturing Gold Medal is awarded without restriction regarding nationality, country of residence or membership of the IET.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="J_J_Thomson_Medal_for_Electronics"><a href="/w/index.php?title=J_J_Thomson_Medal_for_Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="J J Thomson Medal for Electronics (page does not exist)">J J Thomson Medal for Electronics</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: J J Thomson Medal for Electronics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=J_J_Thomson_Medal_for_Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="J J Thomson Medal for Electronics (page does not exist)">J J Thomson Medal for Electronics</a> was created in 1976 by the Electronics Divisional Board of the Institution of Electrical Engineers (IEE), and is awarded to candidates who have made major and distinguished contributions in electronics.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="IET_Volunteer_Medal"><a href="/w/index.php?title=IET_Volunteer_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IET Volunteer Medal (page does not exist)">IET Volunteer Medal</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: IET Volunteer Medal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Introduced in 2015, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=IET_Volunteer_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IET Volunteer Medal (page does not exist)">IET Volunteer Medal</a> is awarded to individuals for major and outstanding contributions voluntarily given to furthering the aims of the IET.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Young_Woman_Engineer_.28YWE.29"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Woman_Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Young Woman Engineer (page does not exist)">Young Woman Engineer</a> (YWE)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Young Woman Engineer (YWE)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Since 1978 the IET has awarded the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Young_Woman_Engineer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Young Woman Engineer (page does not exist)">Young Woman Engineer</a> award to top female engineers in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> to recognize the contribution they make and to encourage young women and girls to consider engineering as a career. The award was created as part of an initiative to address the shortage of women in engineering roles.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarships">Scholarships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Scholarships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Diamond_Jubilee_Scholarship"><a href="/w/index.php?title=IET_Diamond_Jubilee_Scholarship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IET Diamond Jubilee Scholarship (page does not exist)">Diamond Jubilee Scholarship</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Diamond Jubilee Scholarship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The IET offer Diamond Jubilee undergraduate scholarships for first year students studying an IET accredited degree. Winners receive between £1,000 to £2,000 per year, for up to four years to help with their studies. Eligibility is partially based on the exam results at the final year of school prior to university.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Postgraduate_scholarships">Postgraduate scholarships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Postgraduate scholarships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Intended for IET members carrying out doctoral research, the postgraduate scholarships offered by the IET assist members with awards of up to £10,000, to further research engineering related topics at universities.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="IET_Engineering_Horizons_Bursary">IET Engineering Horizons Bursary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: IET Engineering Horizons Bursary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bursaries are £1,000 per year for undergraduate students on IET accredited degree courses in the UK and apprentices starting an IET Approved Apprenticeship scheme. For those who have overcome personal challenges to pursue an engineering education. UK residents only.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose_and_function">Purpose and function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Purpose and function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IET represents the engineering profession in matters of public concern and assists governments to make the public aware of engineering and technological issues. It provides advice on all areas of engineering, regularly advising <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament</a> and other agencies.</p>
<p>The IET also grants <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer_(UK)" title="Chartered Engineer (UK)">Chartered Engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Incorporated Engineer">Incorporated Engineer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Technician" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering Technician">Engineering Technician</a>, and ICT Technician professional designations on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Council_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering Council UK">Engineering Council UK</a>. IEng is roughly equivalent to North American <a href="/wiki/Professional_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Engineer">Professional Engineer</a> designations and CEng is set at a higher level. Both designations have far greater geographical recognition.</p>
<p>This is made possible through a number of networks for engineers established by the IET including the Professional Networks, worldwide groups of engineers sharing common technical and professional interests. Through the IET website, these networks provide up-to-date sector-specific news, stock a library of technical articles and give members the opportunity to exchange knowledge and ideas with peer groups through dedicated discussion forums. Particular areas of focus include education, IT, energy and the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>.</p>
<p>The IET has an educational role, seeking to support its members through their careers by offering a professional home for life, producing advice and guidance at all levels to secure the future of engineering.</p>
<p>For instance, the IET accredits degree courses worldwide in subjects relevant to electrical, electronic, manufacturing and information engineering. In addition, it secures funding for professional development schemes for engineering graduates including awards <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarships</a>, grants and prizes.</p>
<p>For the public, the IET website provides <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.theiet.org/factfiles/index.cfm">factfiles</a> on topics such as solar power, nuclear power, fuel cells, micro-generation and the possible effects on health of mobile phones and power lines.</p>
<p>The IET runs the bibliographic information service <a href="/wiki/Inspec" title="Inspec">Inspec</a>, which is a major indexing database of scientific and technical literature and publishes books, journals such as Electronics Letters, magazines such as <a href="/wiki/Engineering_%26_Technology" title="Engineering &amp; Technology">Engineering &amp; Technology</a> and conference proceedings. Over 80,000 technical articles are available via the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ietdl.org/">IET Digital Library</a>.</p>
<p>IET.tv is one of the world’s largest collated resources of authoritative and multidisciplinary engineering and technology content. Comprising in excess of 6,500 presentation, lecture and training videos, this high quality engineering information offers research insight, workflow solutions and access to inspirational events and expert communities. With a range of search and user functionalities, IET.tv enables online video access to a range of topics and expertise. IET.tv also has an YouTube presence, where it publishes a wide variety of content related to engineering and technology.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Categories_of_membership">Categories of membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Categories of membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>IET</b> has several categories of membership, some with designatory <a href="/wiki/Postnominal" class="mw-redirect" title="Postnominal">postnominals</a>:</p>
<dl>
<dd><b>Honorary Fellow (HonFIET)</b>
<dl>
<dd>The highest membership category, awarded to persons of particular distinction.</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><b>Fellow (FIET)</b>
<dl>
<dd>The category of <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Fellow of the Institution of Engineering and Technology">Fellow of the Institution of Engineering and Technology</a> (<a href="/wiki/FIET" class="mw-redirect" title="FIET">FIET</a>) is open to persons who have demonstrated significant individual responsibility, sustained achievement and professionalism in areas relevant to the interests of the Institution.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><b>Member (MIET or TMIET)</b>
<dl>
<dd>This category is open to professional engineers (MIET) and technicians (TMIET) with suitable qualifications and involvement in areas relevant to the interests of the Institution.</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<p><b>MIET</b> is a regulated professional title recognised in Europe by the Directive 2005/36. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>MIET</b> is listed on the part 2 professions regulated by professional bodies incorporated by Royal Charter-Statutory Instruments 2007 No. 2781 Professional Qualifications-The European Communities (Recognition of Professional Qualifications) Regulations 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><b>Associate</b>
<dl>
<dd>Open to persons with an interest in areas relevant to the interests of the Institution who do not qualify for the <b>Member</b> category.</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd><b>Student</b>
<dl>
<dd>Open to persons studying to become professional engineers and technicians.</dd>
</dl>
</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_IET_outside_the_United_Kingdom">The IET outside the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: The IET outside the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IET refers to its region-specific branches as "Local Networks".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IET Australia is the Australian Local Network of the IET (Institution of Engineering and Technology). The Australian Local Network of the IET has representation in all the states and territories of Australia. These include the state branches, their associated Younger Members Sections, and university sections in Australia. The <b>Younger Members Sections</b> are divided in categories based on each state, e.g. IET YMS New South Wales (IET YMS NSW).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The IET Toronto Network covers IET activities in the Southern and Western areas of Ontario and has approximately 500 members. The first Canadian Branch of the IEE (now the IET) was inaugurated by John Thompson, FIEE, and Harry Copping, FIEE, in Toronto in the early 1950s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IET Hong Kong is the Hong Kong Local Network (formerly Branch) of the IET (Institution of Engineering and Technology). The Hong Kong Local Network of the IET has representations in the Asian region and provides a critical link into mainland China. It includes six sections, i.e. Electronics &amp; Communications Section (ECS); Informatics and Control Technologies Section (ICTS); Management Section(MS); Power and Energy Section (PES); Manufacturing &amp; Industrial Engineering (MIES); Railway Section( RS), as well as the Younger Members Section. It has over 5,000 members and activities are coordinated locally. It is one of the professional organisations for chartered engineers in Hong Kong.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IET Italy Local Network was established in 2007 by a group of active members led by Dr M Fiorini with the purpose to represent locally the aims and services of the IET. The vision of sharing and advancing knowledge throughout the global science, engineering and technology community to enhance people’s lives is achieved building-up an open, flexible and global knowledge network supported by individuals, companies and institutions and facilitated by the IET and its members.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An IET India Office was established in 2006. India has eight Local Networks: Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi,Kanyakumari, Kolkata, Mumbai, Nashik and Pune. The prestigious IET <a href="/wiki/Pinkerton_Lecture" title="Pinkerton Lecture">Pinkerton Lecture</a> is presented annually in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a>.<span style="padding: 0; font-size: x-small; font-weight: bold; color: #000000; background-color: #f9f9f9;"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Templates_for_discussion/Log/2017_March_9#Template:Dubious" title="Wikipedia:Templates for discussion/Log/2017 March 9">‹See TfD›</a></span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kenya">Kenya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Kenya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An IET in Kenya was established on 16th Nov.2011. It is registered under the Societies Act Section 10 Certificate of Registration No.35998.The Institution has been enacted in the Kenya National Assembly of the Engineering Technologists Act No. 23 of 2016. With support of Faculty at the newly established Technical University of Kenya(formerly the Kenya Polytechnic) and Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture &amp; Technology,the proposed bill seeks to register Technologists, Technicians and Craftsmen,who were previously excluded from registration by the Engineering Board of Kenya.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kuwait">Kuwait</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Kuwait">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IET Kuwait community was established in 2013 by Dr. Abdelrahman Abdelazim. The community is very active in the region, it oversees 4 students chapters in Kuwait universities. The community most notable event is the 2015 GCC robotics challenge, the challenge witnessed collaboration with many networks in the region.</p>
