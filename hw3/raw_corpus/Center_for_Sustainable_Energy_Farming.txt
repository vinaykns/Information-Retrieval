<p><b>Global Clean Energy Holdings</b> (OTC:GCEH) is a Southern California-based <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> company focused on the production and commercialization of non-food based <a href="/wiki/Feedstock" class="mw-redirect" title="Feedstock">feedstocks</a> used for the production of biofuels, biomass and renewable chemicals.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Clean_Energy_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, GCEH purchased approximately 5,000 acres of farmland in Mexico’s <a href="/wiki/Yucatan_peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Yucatan peninsula">Yucatan peninsula</a>. The farm is a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Stewart_A._Resnick" class="mw-redirect" title="Stewart A. Resnick">Stewart A. Resnick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selim_Zilkha" title="Selim Zilkha">Selim Zilkha</a>, two highly accomplished entrepreneurs with successful agricultural &amp; alternative energy companies.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company currently owns and manages two <a href="/wiki/Jatropha" title="Jatropha">Jatropha</a> farms in the Yucatán, the larger of the two, located in the county of <a href="/wiki/Tizimin" class="mw-redirect" title="Tizimin">Tizimin</a>, is nearly 9000-acres in size and is purportedly the largest single Jatropha farm in the Americas. The other is a small, research-based farm located near the city of Mérida.</p>
<p>On April 1, <a href="/wiki/Interjet" title="Interjet">Interjet</a> completed the first Jatropha-based biofuel test flight in Mexico. The fuel was manufactured by Honewell’s <a href="/wiki/UOP_LLC" title="UOP LLC">UOP LLC</a> from oil produced by three Mexican Jatropha produces including Globales Energia Renovables, a wholly owned subsidiary of Global Clean Energy Holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2011, GCEH, Emerald Biofuels and <a href="/wiki/Honeywell" title="Honeywell">Honeywell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UOP_LLC" title="UOP LLC">UOP LLC</a> submitted a joint <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Fuel_Standard" class="mw-redirect" title="Renewable Fuel Standard">Renewable Fuel Standard</a> pathway application to the U.S. EPA for Jatropha.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A pathway is necessary for Jatropha-based renewable fuels to qualify for generating <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Identification_Number" title="Renewable Identification Number">Renewable Identification Numbers</a> (RINS) under the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Policy_Act_of_2005" title="Energy Policy Act of 2005">Energy Policy Act of 2005</a>.</p>
<p>Two weeks after the U.S. EPA approved Camelina for RIN (<a href="/wiki/Renewable_Identification_Number" title="Renewable Identification Number">Renewable Identification Number</a>) generation under RFS2,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Global Clean Energy announced its purchase of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Oils" title="Sustainable Oils">Sustainable Oils</a>, LLC, a global leader in Camelina production and genetics.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Completed on February 13, 2013, the acquisition was trumpeted by Honewell's <a href="/wiki/UOP_LLC" title="UOP LLC">UOP LLC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a> and other public and private bio-energy stakeholders.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Biofuels_Certification">Roundtable on Sustainable Biofuels Certification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Clean_Energy_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Roundtable on Sustainable Biofuels Certification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2012, GCEH became the first company based in North America and only biodiesel feedstock producer to achieve <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_biofuel#Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Biofuels" title="Sustainable biofuel">Roundtable on Sustainable Biofuels</a> certification.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The RSB standard is the result of a 4-year effort building a global consensus of over 120 organizations from farmers and biofuel producers to refiners, regulators, civil society and inter-governmental organizations.</p>
<p>In February 2013, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Palmer_(executive)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Richard Palmer (executive) (page does not exist)">Richard Palmer</a>, CEO of GCEH, become a member of RSB Services Board of Directors.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> RSB Services is the implementing entity of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Roundtable_on_Sustainable_Biofuels&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Roundtable on Sustainable Biofuels (page does not exist)">Roundtable on Sustainable Biofuels</a> (RSB) sustainability certification.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="CFSEF">CFSEF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Clean_Energy_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CFSEF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2010, GCEH formed The <a href="/wiki/Center_for_Sustainable_Energy_Farming" class="mw-redirect" title="Center for Sustainable Energy Farming">Center for Sustainable Energy Farming</a> (CfSEF), a non-profit research institution dedicated to advancing the quality of plant-based feedstocks for <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">biofuel</a> production.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It is fueled by socially responsible clean energy produced from <a href="/wiki/Jatropha" title="Jatropha">Jatropha</a>.</p>
<p>The Center‘s mission is to perform plant science research in <a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">genetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plant_breeding" title="Plant breeding">breeding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">horticulture</a>, and further develop technologies to allow for the economic commercialization and sustainability of energy farms. The Center provides a common research platform to foster communication and cooperation among various national and international research institutions and organizations in the biofuel sector. From a global economics, environmental and social perspective, this collaboration will contribute to the development of more productive energy farms capable of supplying large quantities of plant-based (non-food) oils to replace the use of <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" class="mw-redirect" title="Fossil fuels">fossil fuels</a>. This initiative has the potential to create tens of thousands of jobs in the <a href="/wiki/Agri-business" class="mw-redirect" title="Agri-business">agri-business</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_energy" title="Alternative energy">alternative energy</a> industries, expand the use of non-productive land, generate local business-to-business opportunities, reduce global dependency on oil reserves, and contribute to the reduction of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Center is focused on developing high-yielding commercial varieties of <a href="/wiki/Jatropha_curcas" title="Jatropha curcas">Jatropha curcas</a> through interdisciplinary research cooperation across multiple scientific disciplines.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The center has a goal of improving yields 300 percent and reducing inputs by 30 percent within 10 years.</p>
