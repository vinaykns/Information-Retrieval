			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Environmental Performance Index</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p>The <b>Environmental Performance Index</b> (EPI) is a method of quantifying and numerically marking the <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> performance of a state's policies. This index was developed from the Pilot Environmental Performance Index, first published in 2002, and designed to supplement the environmental targets set forth in the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The EPI was preceded by the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Sustainability_Index" title="Environmental Sustainability Index">Environmental Sustainability Index</a> (ESI), published between 1999 and 2005. Both indexes were developed by <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yale_Center_for_Environmental_Law_and_Policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Yale Center for Environmental Law and Policy">Yale Center for Environmental Law and Policy</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Center_for_International_Earth_Science_Information_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Center for International Earth Science Information Network">Center for International Earth Science Information Network</a>) in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Research_Centre" title="Joint Research Centre">Joint Research Centre</a> of the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>. The ESI was developed to evaluate environmental sustainability relative to the paths of other countries. Due to a shift in focus by the teams developing the ESI, the EPI uses outcome-oriented indicators, then working as a benchmark index that can be more easily used by policy makers, environmental scientists, advocates and the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Other leading indices like the Global Green Economy Index (GGEI) <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> provide an integrated measure of the environmental, social and economic dynamics of national economies. The GGEI utilizes EPI data for the environmental dimension of the index while also providing a performance assessment of efficiency sectors (e.g. transport, buildings, energy), investment, green innovation and national leadership around climate change.</p>
<p>As of January 2012 four EPI reports have been released - the Pilot 2006 Environmental Performance Index,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and the 2008, 2010, and 2012 Environmental Performance Index.<sup id="cite_ref-EPI2010_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPI2010-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EPI2012_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPI2012-6">[6]</a></sup> For the 2012 report, a new "Pilot Trend EPI" was developed to rank countries based on the environmental performance changes occurred during the last decade, allowing to establish which countries are improving and which are declining.<sup id="cite_ref-Summary2012_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summary2012-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2014 EPI ranking, the top five countries were <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>. The bottom five countries in 2014 were <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. The United Kingdom was ranked in 12th place, Japan 26th place, the United States 33rd, Brazil 77th, China 118th, and India came in 155th.<sup id="cite_ref-Rank2014_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rank2014-8">[8]</a></sup> The top five countries based on their 2012 Pilot Trend EPI were <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Congo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Summary2014_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summary2014-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EPI_scores">EPI scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Performance_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: EPI scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Performance_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 January 2016, the <b>2016 Environmental Performance Index</b> was released at the World Economic Forum, with 180 countries being ranked.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Top 30 countries and score</b></p>
<div class="div-col columns column-count column-count-3" style="-moz-column-count: 3; -webkit-column-count: 3; column-count: 3;">

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Performance_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 January 2010 <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> released the <b>2010 Environmental Performance Index</b> at the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> ranking 163 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-EPI2010_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPI2010-5">[5]</a></sup> The top performer for 2010 is <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> due to its high scores on environmental public health, gets virtually all of its power from renewable sources (<a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geothermal" class="mw-redirect" title="Geothermal">geothermal</a> energy), and its control of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions. The <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> fell to the 61st position, as compared to 39th in the 2008 EPI, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> ranks 62nd, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> 69th, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> 121st, and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> ranks 123rd.<sup id="cite_ref-EPI2010_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EPI2010-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_Performance_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 January 2008 <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> released the <b>2008 Environmental Performance Index</b> at the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> ranking 149 countries. <p>On 26 January 2006 <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yale_Center_for_Environmental_Law_and_Policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Yale Center for Environmental Law and Policy">YCELP</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Center_for_International_Earth_Science_Information_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Center for International Earth Science Information Network">CIESIN</a>) released the <b>Pilot 2006 Environmental Performance Index</b> at the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> ranking 133 countries. It was done in collaboration with the World Economic Forum and the Joint Research Centre of the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>.</p>
