<p><b>Arno Tausch</b> (born February 11, 1951 in <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>) is an Austrian <a href="/wiki/Political_scientist" class="mw-redirect" title="Political scientist">political scientist</a>. His research program is focused on <a href="/wiki/World_systems_theory" class="mw-redirect" title="World systems theory">world systems theory</a>, development studies and <a href="/wiki/Dependency_theory" title="Dependency theory">dependency theory</a>, European studies in the framework of <a href="/wiki/Core-periphery" class="mw-redirect" title="Core-periphery">core-periphery</a> relationships, and quantitative <a href="/wiki/Peace_and_conflict_studies" title="Peace and conflict studies">Peace and conflict studies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_career">Academic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arno_Tausch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Academic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tausch received his <a href="/wiki/Ph.D." class="mw-redirect" title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> in political science from <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Salzburg University">Salzburg University</a> in 1976 <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Habilitation" title="Habilitation">habilitation</a> at the Department of Political Science at <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Innsbruck University">Innsbruck University</a> was concluded in 1988; the Chairperson of his habilitation commission was Professor <a href="/wiki/Anton_Pelinka" title="Anton Pelinka">Anton Pelinka</a>. Since then he has been adjunct professor (‘Universitätsdozent’) at that department.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is currently also associate visiting professor of Economics at <a href="/wiki/Corvinus_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Corvinus University">Corvinus University</a> Budapest.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tausch is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Editorial_board" title="Editorial board">editorial boards</a> of several scientific journals, among them <i>Innovation: The European Journal of Social Science Research</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>Society and Economy, (Journal of the Corvinus University of Budapest)</i>,;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>History &amp; Mathematics Almanac</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and the <i>Journal of Globalization Studies</i> (State University, Moscow) <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arno_Tausch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of November 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Arno_Tausch&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Tausch had authored or co-authored according to his academic CV at his website, 17 books in English, 2 in French, and 8 in German,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and a number of articles in English, Spanish, French, Russian, Polish and German in <a href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> scholarly journals and articles on current affairs.</p>
<p>He also has written articles for <i>Tiempo Argentino</i>, a newspaper based in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and in <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Standard" title="Der Standard">Der Standard</a></i> in Vienna <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarly_work">Scholarly work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arno_Tausch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scholarly work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the international press, Tausch's work is seen mostly in the framework of the efficiency and effectiveness of social spending.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Tausch also published critical studies of the <a href="/wiki/Political_economy" title="Political economy">political economy</a> of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a> <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and providing a link between <a href="/wiki/Dependency_theory" title="Dependency theory">dependency theory</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_system_theory" class="mw-redirect" title="World system theory">world system theory</a> with the empirical analysis of <a href="/wiki/Social_development" class="mw-redirect" title="Social development">social development</a> in Europe, especially <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_world" title="Muslim world">Muslim world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>His research started with a rethinking of <a href="/wiki/Dependency_theory" title="Dependency theory">dependency theory</a> in the framework of <a href="/wiki/Quantitative_data" class="mw-redirect" title="Quantitative data">quantitative data</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statistical_analysis" class="mw-redirect" title="Statistical analysis">statistical analysis</a> and work on poverty in the leading industrialized countries.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>His doctoral thesis at <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Salzburg University">Salzburg University</a> on "The limits to growth theory" <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> was dedicated to the empirical analysis of dependency, income inequality, and social development in up to 40 countries of the world, with country studies for <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Following the logic of trying to identify the structures, which block <a href="/wiki/Third-World" class="mw-redirect" title="Third-World">Third-World</a> development in the <a href="/wiki/Industrialized_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialized countries">industrialized countries</a>, he then researched on Austrian <a href="/wiki/Arms_exports" class="mw-redirect" title="Arms exports">arms exports</a> to Third World countries, based on United States <a href="/wiki/Arms_Control_and_Disarmament_Agency" title="Arms Control and Disarmament Agency">Arms Control and Disarmament Agency</a> figures.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>His <a href="/wiki/Habilitation_thesis" class="mw-redirect" title="Habilitation thesis">habilitation thesis</a> focused on <a href="/wiki/Multiple_regression" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiple regression">multiple regression</a> models of global development, based on data from the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> and Volker Bornschier as well as the <i>World Handbook of Political and Social Indicators III</i>, and tests the relevance of socio-liberal approaches to world development, already inherent in the writings of classical social democratic development theories in Europe in the 1930s with <a href="/wiki/Multiple_regression" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiple regression">multiple regression</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canonical_correlation" title="Canonical correlation">canonical correlation</a> models.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This approach was continued and expanded in <i>Socio-Liberal Theory of World Development</i> (1993).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later work dealt with Russia and the global <a href="/wiki/Kondratieff_cycle" class="mw-redirect" title="Kondratieff cycle">Kondratieff cycle</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> the effects of the <a href="/wiki/Arms_industry" title="Arms industry">arms trade</a> on social development, especially for the countries of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Former_USSR" class="mw-redirect" title="Former USSR">former USSR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Militarism" title="Militarism">Militarism</a> will grow in these countries because of the distribution coalition prone environment of "periphery socialism".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In the 1990s, Tausch analyzed migration processes and the transformation of post-communist countries.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later work also included studies of the effects of <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporations" class="mw-redirect" title="Multinational corporations">multinational corporations</a> and their <a <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critique">Critique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arno_Tausch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the concept of "<a href="/wiki/Unequal_exchange" title="Unequal exchange">unequal exchange</a>", as developed by Kohler and Tausch <sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> has become one of the standard definitions in "<a href="/wiki/World_system_theory" class="mw-redirect" title="World system theory">world system theory</a>" <sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> and in critical studies on <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> the Turkish economist <a href="/w/index.php?title=Turan_Subasat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Turan Subasat (page does not exist)">Turan Subasat</a> (<a href="/wiki/Izmir_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Izmir University">Izmir University</a>) recently criticized this concept by saying that international prices are formed in a complex manner and lower international prices for low income countries cannot simply be considered as evidence for unequal exchange.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Austrian political scientist <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Schmidinger&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Schmidinger (page does not exist)">Thomas Schmidinger</a>, writing in the official journal of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Austrian_Political_Science_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Austrian Political Science Association (page does not exist)">Austrian Political Science Association</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%96sterreichische_Zeitschrift_f%C3%BCr_Politikwissenschaft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Österreichische Zeitschrift für Politikwissenschaft (page does not exist)">Österreichische Zeitschrift für Politikwissenschaft</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> on Tausch's attempts to measure Muslim opinions in Europe <sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> comes to the conclusion:</p>
<p><i>"Tausch attempts to redress the lack of serious qualitative and quantitative social research by using data from the <a href="/wiki/World_Values_Survey" title="World Values Survey">World Values Survey</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Social_Survey" title="European Social Survey">European Social Survey</a>. He speaks even of a "forensic political science " and a "forensic sociology" (p 9). He rightly criticizes that neither the European Union nor the national states in Europe provide adequate resources for such research. Really to resolve this lack of data, a whole team of long-term research would have been necessary. Right here, however, Tausch shows that he [also] does not have enough data and therefore his approach - as he himself writes in the introduction - is "forensic". Who really wants to get more secure data in the debate on Islam in Europe should invest in a large-scale study conducted in a methodologically sound way, which should not examine only the Muslim population, but also appropriate comparison groups from the non-Muslim population."</i></p>
