<p>The <b>American Council of Engineering Companies</b> (ACEC) is a trade association founded in 1909 to promote the business interests of engineers in private practice in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. ACEC is a federation of 51 state and regional councils with national headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC traces its roots to the founding of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Institute_of_Consulting_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Institute of Consulting Engineers (page does not exist)">American Institute of Consulting Engineers</a> (AICE) in 1909. In its first years, AICE was largely a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-focused organization, with more than half of its members working in the New York City area. The organization was active in opposing the use of publicly employed engineers on public projects and advocated for engineers to be hired based on their qualifications rather than price.</p>
<p>In 1956, representatives from 10 state associations representing consulting engineers met in <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">Tulsa, Oklahoma</a> and created the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Consulting_Engineers_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Consulting Engineers Council (page does not exist)">Consulting Engineers Council</a>, a nationwide association to promote the business interests of engineers. The new organization had an initial membership of 494 individuals and firms. In 1959, it joined the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Consulting_Engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="International Federation of Consulting Engineers">International Federation of Consulting Engineers</a> (FIDIC). By 1960, CEC had 29 state-based member organizations, represented more than 1,000 firms, and had established a group liability insurance program.</p>
<p>It supported the passage of the A/E Selection Procedures Act, also known as the <a href="/wiki/Brooks_Act" title="Brooks Act">Brooks Act</a>, in 1972, which requires that the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Federal_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Federal Government">U.S. Federal Government</a> select engineering and architecture firms based upon their competency, qualifications and experience rather than by price. The Council has been a major supporter of the Act's Qualifications-Based Selection (QBS) rules ever since, repeatedly beating back efforts from federal agencies to use price in procuring engineering services.</p>
<p>In 1973, CEC and AICE merged to form the <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Consulting_Engineers_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Consulting Engineers Council (page does not exist)">American Consulting Engineers Council</a> (ACEC).</p>
<p>In 1986, ACEC founded the <a href="/wiki/American_Tort_Reform_Association" title="American Tort Reform Association">American Tort Reform Association</a> (ATRA) to address what it considers unfair liability statutes nationwide.</p>
<p>In 1998, it supported the inclusion of a provision in the <a href="/wiki/Transportation_Equity_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Transportation Equity Act">Transportation Equity Act</a> for the 21st Century (TEA-21) to require state transportation departments to follow QBS guidelines.</p>
<p>In 2000, the organization changed its name to the American Council of Engineering Companies to reflect its firm-based membership. All of the 51 state and regional councils have since followed suit.</p>
<p>Later that year, it backed the "Thomas Amendment" in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_Resources_and_Development_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Water Resources and Development Act (page does not exist)">Water Resources and Development Act</a>, which limited the ability of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Corps_of_Engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army Corps of Engineers">U.S. Army Corps of Engineers</a> to compete with private engineering firms in municipal works such as schools and utilities.</p>
<p>In 2004, it helped win passage of a 9 percent tax deduction for engineering firms as part of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Jobs_Creation_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Jobs Creation Act (page does not exist)">American Jobs Creation Act</a>.</p>
<p>In 2012, ACEC, along with the <a href="/wiki/American_Public_Works_Association" title="American Public Works Association">American Public Works Association</a> (APWA) and <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Civil_Engineers" title="American Society of Civil Engineers">American Society of Civil Engineers</a> (ASCE), founded the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Institute_for_Sustainable_Infrastructure&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Institute for Sustainable Infrastructure (page does not exist)">Institute for Sustainable Infrastructure</a> (ISI), which has developed the envision sustainability rating system for civil infrastructure.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advocacy">Advocacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Advocacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC advocates for the business interests of its member firms on both the federal and state levels.</p>
<p>Key Council issues include limiting government competition, increasing infrastructure funding, reforming the tax system, and improving risk management protections.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_Action_Committee">Political Action Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political Action Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies_Political_Action_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="American Council of Engineering Companies Political Action Committee (page does not exist)">American Council of Engineering Companies Political Action Committee</a> (ACEC/PAC) was established in 1975 to support federal candidates for office who promote a pro-business legislative agenda. ACEC/PAC is bipartisan and is funded solely by ACEC member contributions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Council holds more than 130 online educational <a href="/wiki/Webinars" class="mw-redirect" title="Webinars">webinars</a> annually, covering a wide range of business management and engineering topics.</p>
<p>Founded in 1995, ACEC's Senior Executives Institute provides advanced management, leadership and public policy training for emerging firm leaders. More than 400 executives have participated in the program.</p>
<p>In cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Highway_Administration" title="Federal Highway Administration">Federal Highway Administration</a>, ACEC holds regular on-site educational workshops on how engineering firms can better navigate Federal Acquisition Regulations (FAR).</p>
<p>Each fall, ACEC hosts two-day meetings for three educational forums: Information Technology; Financial; and Human Resources.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coalitions">Coalitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coalitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC has six coalitions—dedicated communities of ACEC members organized by practice area or firm size:</p>
<ul>
<li>Design Professionals Coalition</li>
<li>Council of American Structural Engineers</li>
<li>Council of American Mechanical and Electrical Engineers</li>
<li>Council of Professional Surveyors</li>
<li>Small Firm Council</li>
<li>Land Development Coalition</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC publishes <i>Engineering Inc.</i> magazine, a bi-monthly four-color journal that focuses on business and engineering industry issues. In 2014, the magazine won an APEX Grand Award, a national award for business communicators.</p>
<p><i>Last Word</i> is the Council's weekly electronic membership newsletter.</p>
<p>In 2014, the Council introduced the <i>ACEC Engineering Business Index</i> survey, which charts the health of the engineering industry.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EJCDC">EJCDC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: EJCDC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC, ASCE, and the <a href="/wiki/National_Society_of_Professional_Engineers" title="National Society of Professional Engineers">National Society of Professional Engineers</a> (NSPE) founded the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Engineers_Joint_Contract_Document_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Engineers Joint Contract Document Committee (page does not exist)">Engineers Joint Contract Document Committee</a> (EJCDC) in 1975 to develop objective standard contract documents that represent both parties in engineer-designed construction projects in the United States.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ACEC hosts two conferences per year. Each spring, the ACEC Annual Convention and Legislative Summit is held in Washington, D.C. and features political speakers and coordinated member visits to congressional offices. The ACEC Fall Conference is held in different locations each year and focuses on member education. The 2017 conference will be held in Orlando.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_Programs">Awards Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Council_of_Engineering_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Engineering Excellence Awards—Introduced in 1967, the annual Engineering Excellence Awards (EEA) program honors that year's most outstanding engineering accomplishments.</li>
<li>Community Service Awards—Given annually to member firm principals who have made outstanding contributions to the quality of life in their community.</li>
<li>Distinguished Award of Merit—The Council's highest award, given to individuals for exemplary achievement in the field of engineering, pure or applied natural science, or an educator in one of more of these fields, either American or foreign. Recipients include former Presidents <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a>, General Lucius Clay, Admiral Hyman G. Rickover, Carl Sagan, W. Edwards Deming, and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">Neil Armstrong</a>.</li>
<li>QBS Awards Program—Co-sponsored with <a href="/wiki/NSPE" class="mw-redirect" title="NSPE">NSPE</a>, the QBS Awards recognize public and private entities that make exemplary use of the <a href="/wiki/Qualifications-Based_Selection" title="Qualifications-Based Selection">qualifications-based selection</a> (QBS) process at the state and local levels.</li>
<li>Young Professional of the Year Award—This award promotes the accomplishments of young engineers by highlighting their engineering contributions and the resulting impact on society.</li>
</ul>
<p>ACEC also administers six scholarship programs.</p>
