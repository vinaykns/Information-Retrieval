			<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ecologically sustainable development</h1>
									<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
								<div id="contentSub"></div>
												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
				</div>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><p><b>Ecologically sustainable development</b> is the environmental component of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. It can be achieved partially through the use of the <a href="/wiki/Precautionary_principle" title="Precautionary principle">precautionary principle</a>; if there are threats of serious or irreversible environmental damage, lack of full scientific certainty should not be used as a reason for postponing measures to prevent <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a>. Also important is the principle of <a href="/wiki/Intergenerational_equity" title="Intergenerational equity">intergenerational equity</a>; the present generation should ensure that the health, diversity and productivity of the environment is maintained or enhanced for the benefit of future generations. In order for this movement to flourish, environmental factors should be more heavily weighed in the valuation of assets and services to provide more incentive for the conservation of biological diversity and ecological integrity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_considerations">Political considerations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecologically_sustainable_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Political considerations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Effective political support is necessary for ecologically sustainable development. The mobilization of governments can be translated into action plans that are crucial to <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development#Politics" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>. Development efforts can be influenced by patterns of family arrangements, work attitudes, <a href="/wiki/Morality" title="Morality">social morality</a>—particularly interpersonal responsibilities, hierarchy of authority, quality of scientific education and implementation, and degree of domestic stability—especially, freedom from <a href="/wiki/Social_conflict" title="Social conflict">social conflict</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> National policy and development planning requires three conditions to permit ecological <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>: action-oriented values to which individuals are committed, political authorities that favour long-term ecological benefits over immediate economic gains, and a policy with a politically competent <a href="/wiki/Constituency" class="mw-redirect" title="Constituency">constituency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><b>Canada</b> has taken an evidence based approach to sustainability development by using Environmental <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_measurement" title="Sustainability measurement">Sustainability Indicators</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as guiding tool for policy development. At the municipal level, many cities also use evidence based indicators<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and Ecological Indexes<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as a tools for policy development.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forums_for_Ecologically_Sustainable_Development">Forums for Ecologically Sustainable Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecologically_sustainable_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Forums for Ecologically Sustainable Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The World Conservation Strategy was published in 1980, becoming one of the most encouraging developments that uses a goal-oriented programme for political change concerning ecological sustainability.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The publication marked a fundamental policy shift for the global <a href="/wiki/Conservation_movement" title="Conservation movement">conservation movement</a>. The traditional focus became cure rather than <a href="/wiki/Prevention_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Prevention Science">Prevention</a>, confirming the growing trend on the assimilation of preservation and development aims that are key to an ecologically sustainable society. Specifically, the concentration on <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_conservation" title="Wildlife conservation">wildlife conservation</a> drifted into a concern for wider strains degrading the natural environment.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It promotes the principles of sustainable development and addresses the environmental concerns introduced by <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> decisions with a format that targets a wide audience. There are three chief conservation objectives:</p>
<ol>
<li>Maintaining essential <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">biogeochemical cycles</a> and life-support systems</li>
<li>Preserving <a href="/wiki/Genetic_diversity" title="Genetic diversity">genetic diversity</a></li>
<li>Establishing a sustainable use of <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystems</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<p>Other efforts such as the World Campaign for the Biosphere present environmental obstacles constantly before governmental and scientific authorities. Nicholas Polunin, former president of the Foundation for Environmental Conservation, believed the starting point for the World Campaign effort occurred in 1966 at an <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> conference in Finland.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The conference examined conditions that hinder ecologically sustainable development such as rapid <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a>, proliferation of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a>, and depletion of <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">natural resources</a>. Similarly, the <a href="/wiki/The_Global_2000_Report_to_the_President" title="The Global 2000 Report to the President">Global 2000 Report to the President</a>, presents environmental prospective conditions that are likely to worsen if public policies, institutions, and rates of technologic advancement do not change. Findings of this type prompted <a href="/wiki/Environmentalist" title="Environmentalist">environmentalists</a> and the Foundation for Environmental Conservation to initiate the World Campaign project and have included the following suggestions:</p>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li>Publication and broadcast of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_issue" title="Environmental issue">environmental issues</a></li>
<li>Use of traditional <a href="/wiki/Media_(communication)" title="Media (communication)">communication mediums</a> such as posters and automobile stickers</li>
<li>Distribution of increasing basic and applied environmental research results</li>
<li>Information about <a href="/wiki/Family_planning" title="Family planning">family planning</a> and the necessity to control population growth</li>
<li>Designating more area as <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national parks</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wilderness_area" title="Wilderness area">wilderness areas</a>, including the ocean</li>
<li>Organization of local, state, national, and international conferences to discuss environmental issues</li>
<li>Encouraging sustainable uses of our natural resources</li>
<li>Establishment of <a href="/wiki/Common_law" title="Common law">common laws</a> for the Earth and mankind</li>
<li>Obtaining support form <a href="/wiki/Nongovernmental_organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Nongovernmental organization">nongovernmental organizations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institutions</a> for the World Campaign</li>
<li>Attaining recognition such as "Guardians of the Biosphere Awards," for persons and/or groups that demonstrate actions to preserve the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
