<p><b>Rockingham Motor Speedway</b> is a modern <a href="/wiki/Motorsport" title="Motorsport">motorsport</a> venue in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, that hosts corporate driving days, driver training, conferencing and exhibitions, vehicle manufacturing events, track days, testing, driving experiences and motor racing. It claims to be Europe's fastest racing circuit,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was the first banked oval constructed in Britain since the closure of <a href="/wiki/Brooklands" title="Brooklands">Brooklands</a> in 1939.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>, Rockingham falls under the control of two local authorities.</p>
<p>Rockingham is easily accessible from the A1, M1, A14, A43 and A6116, and is located 30 minutes from <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peterborough" title="Peterborough">Peterborough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northampton" title="Northampton">Northampton</a>.</p>
<p>The nearest railway station is <a href="/wiki/Corby_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Corby station">Corby</a>, which is two miles from the circuit and runs an hourly direct service to <a href="/wiki/London_St_Pancras_International" class="mw-redirect" title="London St Pancras International">London St Pancras International</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Circuit">The Circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg/220px-Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg/330px-Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg/440px-Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2650" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rockingham_Motor_Speedway_aerial.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Aerial of Rockingham Motor Speedway</div>

<p>Rockingham has 13 configurations of track, which can be used for anything from <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">touring cars</a> to <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycles</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rally cars</a>. The circuit is overlooked by the 6280 seat Rockingham Building, a steel-framed, glass-fronted grandstand building containing suites, offices, bars and kitchens, and by four grandstands. Together the building and grandstands offer a total seating capacity of 52,000. The inner pit and paddock complex is accessed from the Rockingham Building via two pedestrian tunnels and there is a further spectator viewing area on top of the pit garages.<sup id="cite_ref-rockingham.co.uk_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rockingham.co.uk-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Oval_Circuit">The Oval Circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Oval Circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 1.48 mile American-style banked oval circuit is 18.3 metres wide and has a maximum bank angle of 7 degrees and comprises four very distinct corners. Rockingham's oval is unique in the UK and one of only two speedways in Europe. The oval circuit can also be converted to a road course layout for events by positioning temporary chicanes and curves both on the main area and apron of the circuit.</p>
<p>Over the weekend of 20–22 September 2001, the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">Champ cars</a> came to England for the first time to contest the Rockingham 500, a round of the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">CART</a> (Championship Auto Racing Teams) Fedex Championship Series. For various reasons the race distance was shortened to 300&#160;km and victory was snatched on the exit of Turn Four of the last lap by <a href="/wiki/Gil_de_Ferran" title="Gil de Ferran">Gil de Ferran</a> driving the <a href="/wiki/Team_Penske" title="Team Penske">Marlboro Team Penske</a> <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> –powered <a href="/wiki/Reynard" title="Reynard">Reynard</a> 01i at a race average speed of 153.41&#160;mph from <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Br%C3%A4ck" title="Kenny Bräck">Kenny Bräck</a> at the wheel the <a href="/wiki/Team_Rahal" class="mw-redirect" title="Team Rahal">Team Rahal</a> Lola-Ford Cosworth B1/00, and the <a href="/wiki/Newman/Haas_Racing" title="Newman/Haas Racing">Newman-Haas Racing</a> Lola-Toyota B1/00 driven by <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a>. The fastest lap, and therefore outright lap record was set by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Carpentier" title="Patrick Carpentier">Patrick Carpentier</a> in 25.551secs (210.59&#160;mph) in the <a href="/wiki/Forsythe_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Forsythe Racing">Player’s Forsythe Racing</a> Reynard-Cosworth. Carpentier became for first Canadian to ever hold the outright lap record at an English circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-Swinger_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swinger-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The US-based series returned in September 2002. This was the last time Champ Cars raced at this facility. The race ran over the full distance of 500&#160;km, Scotland’s <a href="/wiki/Dario_Franchitti" title="Dario Franchitti">Dario Franchitti</a> took the chequered flag in his <a href="/wiki/Andretti_Green_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Andretti Green Racing">Team KOOL Green</a> Lola-Honda, beating <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> by just 0.986 of a second .<sup id="cite_ref-champcarstats.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-champcarstats.com-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_International_Super_Sportscar_Circuit_.28ISSC.29">The International Super Sportscar Circuit (ISSC)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The International Super Sportscar Circuit (ISSC)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ISSC is 1.94 miles long and between 18.3 and 11 metres wide, with a maximum bank angle of 7 degrees at turn 1. Rockingham's International Super Sportscar Circuit is used by the Dunlop MSA British Touring Car Championship as well as for most other car race events. The circuit combines long straights, sweeping high-speed bends (including the infamous Turn 1 and Gracelands) with a lower speed, highly technical infield layout. Average lap speeds around the circuit range from 1m 24s in the BTCC to 1m 12s in British F3.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wet_Handling_Facility">Wet Handling Facility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wet Handling Facility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The site has a wet handling area with a kick plate which is used to simulate a variety of driving conditions including ice, rain and slippery wet leaves. These different road conditions are simulated at much slower speeds than in normal driving and in a fully controlled environment. The area is designed to give drivers a greater understanding of the latest ABS, traction control and crash mitigating technologies, and the way that they affect vehicle control.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction of Rockingham started late in 1999, with the opening meeting planned for May 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Swinger_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swinger-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rockingham Motor Speedway was constructed on a British Steel works <i><a href="/wiki/Brownfield_status" class="mw-redirect" title="Brownfield status">brown field</a></i> site as a banked oval with the intention of bringing the American oval racing across the Atlantic for the first time. The opportunity was taken to use the infield for further circuits.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Swinger_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swinger-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After almost ten years of planning and 23 months of construction work, Rockingham opened for business on Monday 15 January 2001. It was formally opened by the <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen</a> on 26 May 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oval_lap_records">Oval lap records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Oval lap records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rockingham is Europe's fastest banked oval racing circuit, with the lap record for the 1.47 miles (2.37&#160;km) oval set at 24.719 seconds by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Kanaan" title="Tony Kanaan">Tony Kanaan</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford">Ford</a> <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">Champ Car</a> on 22 September 2001 – an average speed of 215.397 miles per hour (346.648&#160;km/h). Rockingham is the first purpose-built banked oval in the UK since <a href="/wiki/Brooklands" title="Brooklands">Brooklands</a> in 1907. It has an all-seating capacity of 52,000, and has both an oval and a racing circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oval_racing">Oval racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Oval racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 2001 to the end of 2007, Rockingham organised and funded American-style Stockcar racing on the oval. Originally known as ASCAR, the series changed name to "Days of Thunder", and then to SCSA (Stock Car Speed Association), before re-branding to the MAC Tools V8 Trophy for its final season in 2007. All cars were UK variants of the American "ASA National-Tour" Howe Racing chassis, running with either Ford, Chevrolet or Pontiac bodies. A change of circuit ownership for 2006 saw the end of promotion and funding for the series, and low grids in 2007 led the organising club (BRSCC) to amalgamate it with a road course series, and eventually to the demise of both championships before the end of 2008.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The circuit is operational 357 days a year and the majority of events that take place at the venue are not motorsport competitions. Events range from manufacturer product launches to dealer training, track days and testing, and corporate and experience days.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Magazines_and_newspapers">Magazines and newspapers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockingham_Motor_Speedway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Magazines and newspapers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Telegraph</i> and Fiat Punto held a track day at Rockingham in 2012 and published a number of articles featuring the circuit. <i>Autosport</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> Caterham SP300R video review was made on the ISSC, and <i>Car Magazine</i> did a track test on the Nissan GT-R vs Porsche 911 Turbo in 2012 and a McLaren supercar group test in 2010. Car Dealer magazine reviewed the BMW M5 at Rockingham and compared it with a Mercedes E63 AMG, a Lexus ISF, a Porsche Panamera and an Infiniti M35h. The circuit also features on the Pistonheads website with videos showing the Caterham SP300 R and the new Astra VXR. Chris Harris on <i>Cars</i> showcased the BAC Mono.</p>
