<p><i><b>GAIA: Ecological Perspectives for Science and Society</b></i> (<i>Gaia: Ökologische Perspektiven für Wissenschaft und Gesellschaft</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> established in 1992. Its main focus is on background information, analyses, and solutions of environmental and <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> problems. Since 2001 it is published by oekom verlag. Articles are in English and German. The <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> is Verena Winiwarter (<a href="/wiki/Klagenfurt_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Klagenfurt University">Klagenfurt University</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sections">Sections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GAIA_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal contains the following sections:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Magazine</i>: reporting on ongoing environmental and sustainability research, environmental policy, and research promotion</li>
<li><i>Forum</i>: original papers, including essays, expressions of opinion, and reactions to articles that have appeared in recent issues</li>
<li><i>Research</i>: original scientific articles about environmental and sustainability research</li>
<li><i>Books</i>: reviews of new publications</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Masters_Student_Paper_Award">Masters Student Paper Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GAIA_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Masters Student Paper Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014 the <i>GAIA</i> Masters Student Paper Award was established. It recognizes outstanding results from young scientists in the field of environmental and sustainability research. The prize is awarded in cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/Leuphana_University_of_L%C3%BCneburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Leuphana University of Lüneburg">Leuphana University of Lüneburg</a> and the Selbach-Umwelt-Stiftung.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
